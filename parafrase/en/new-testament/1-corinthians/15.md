# 1 Corinthians 15

**1** Now, brothers and sisters, I want to remind you of the good news I preached to you — which you received and on which you've taken your stand. **2** By this good news you are being saved, if you hold firmly to the message I preached to you. Otherwise, you believed for nothing.

**3** For what I received I passed on to you as of first importance:ᵃ that Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures, **4** that he was buried, that he was raised on the third day according to the Scriptures, **5** and that he appeared to Cephas, and then to the Twelve. **6** After that, he appeared to more than five hundred brothers and sisters at once, most of whom are still alive, though some have fallen asleep. **7** Then he appeared to James, then to all the apostles. **8** And last of all he appeared to me too, as to one abnormally born.ᵇ

**9** For I am the least of the apostles, not even deserving to be called an apostle, because I persecuted the church of God. **10** But by the grace of God I am what I am, and his grace to me was not without effect. No, I worked harder than all of them — yet it wasn't I, but the grace of God working with me. **11** Whether it was I or they, this is what we preach, and this is what you believed.

**12** Now if it's preached that Christ has been raised from the dead, how can some of you say there's no resurrection of the dead? **13** If there's no resurrection of the dead, then not even Christ has been raised. **14** And if Christ hasn't been raised, our preaching is useless and so is your faith. **15** More than that, we'd be found to be false witnesses about God, because we testified that God raised Christ from the dead. But he didn't raise him if the dead aren't raised. **16** For if the dead aren't raised, then Christ hasn't been raised either. **17** And if Christ hasn't been raised, your faith is futile — you're still in your sins. **18** Then those who have fallen asleep in Christ are lost. **19** If only for this life we have hope in Christ, we are of all people most to be pitied.

**20** But Christ has indeed been raised from the dead — the firstfruitsᶜ of those who have fallen asleep. **21** For since death came through a human being, the resurrection of the dead also comes through a human being. **22** For just as in Adam everyone dies, so in Christ everyone will be made alive. **23** But each in their own order: Christ the firstfruits, then at his coming, those who belong to him. **24** Then the end will come, when he hands over the kingdomᵈ to God the Father after he has destroyed all dominion, authority, and power. **25** For he must reign until he has put all his enemies under his feet. **26** The last enemy to be destroyed is death. **27** For he "has put everything under his feet."ᵉ Now when it says "everything" has been put under him, obviously this doesn't include God himself, who put everything under Christ. **28** When everything has been made subject to him, then the Son himself will be made subject to the one who put everything under him, so that God may be all in all.

**29** Now if there's no resurrection, what will those who are baptized for the deadᶠ do? If the dead aren't raised at all, why are people baptized for them? **30** And why do we put ourselves in danger every hour? **31** I face death every single day — I mean that, brothers and sisters, just as surely as I take pride in you in Christ Jesus our Lord. **32** If I fought wild beasts in Ephesusᵍ with merely human motives, what did I gain? If the dead aren't raised, "Let's eat and drink, for tomorrow we die."ʰ

**33** Don't be deceived: "Bad company corrupts good character."ⁱ **34** Come back to your senses as you should, and stop sinning. Some of you don't know God — I say this to shame you.

**35** But someone will ask, "How are the dead raised? What kind of body will they have?" **36** How foolish! What you sow doesn't come to life unless it dies. **37** When you sow, you don't plant the full-grown body that will be, but just a seed — maybe wheat or something else. **38** But God gives it a body as he has determined, and to each kind of seed he gives its own body. **39** Not all flesh is the same: people have one kind of flesh, animals another, birds another, fish another. **40** There are heavenly bodies and earthly bodies. The glory of the heavenly is one kind, and the glory of the earthly is another. **41** The sun has one kind of glory, the moon another, the stars another — and star differs from star in glory.

**42** So it is with the resurrection of the dead. The body that is sown is perishable; it's raised imperishable. **43** It's sown in dishonor; it's raised in glory. It's sown in weakness; it's raised in power. **44** It's sown a natural bodyʲ; it's raised a spiritual body. If there is a natural body, there is also a spiritual body.

**45** So it is written: "The first man Adam became a living being"; the last Adam became a life-giving spirit. **46** The spiritual didn't come first — the natural did, and then the spiritual. **47** The first man was from the earth, made of dust. The second man is from heaven. **48** As was the man of dust, so are those of the dust. And as is the heavenly man, so are those who are of heaven. **49** And just as we have borne the image of the man of dust, we will also bear the image of the heavenly man.

**50** Now I declare this, brothers and sisters: flesh and blood cannot inherit the kingdom of God, and the perishable cannot inherit the imperishable. **51** Listen, I'm telling you a mystery:ᵏ we won't all sleep, but we will all be changed — **52** in a flash, in the blink of an eye, at the last trumpet. For the trumpet will sound, the dead will be raised imperishable, and we will be changed. **53** For the perishable must clothe itself with the imperishable, and the mortal with immortality. **54** When the perishable has been clothed with the imperishable, and the mortal with immortality, then the saying that is written will come true: "Death has been swallowed up in victory."ˡ **55** "Where, O death, is your victory? Where, O death, is your sting?"ᵐ **56** The sting of death is sin, and the power of sin is the law. **57** But thanks be to God! He gives us the victory through our Lord Jesus Christ.

**58** So then, my dear brothers and sisters, stand firm. Don't let anything move you. Always give yourselves fully to the work of the Lord, because you know that your labor in the Lord is not in vain.

## Notes
- v.3a: ἐν πρώτοις (en prōtois) — "among the first things," of first importance; what follows (vv.3-5) is an early Christian creed Paul received and transmitted
- v.8b: ἔκτρωμα (ektrōma) — a miscarriage, an abortive birth; Paul's startling self-description of his untimely, violent encounter with the risen Christ
- v.20c: ἀπαρχή (aparchē) — firstfruits; the first portion of a harvest, pledging the full harvest to come; Christ's resurrection guarantees ours
- v.24d: τὴν βασιλείαν (tēn basileian) — the kingdom, the reign; Paul presents Christ's present rule as moving toward a final consummation
- v.27e: Paul quotes Psalm 8:6, a psalm about humanity's dominion, applied to Christ as the true human
- v.29f: βαπτιζόμενοι ὑπὲρ τῶν νεκρῶν (baptizomenoi hyper tōn nekrōn) — "baptized on behalf of the dead"; one of the most obscure phrases in Paul's letters, with over 40 proposed interpretations; Paul neither endorses nor condemns the practice but uses it as an argument for resurrection
- v.32g: θηριομαχέω (thēriomacheō) — fought wild beasts; likely metaphorical for severe opposition in Ephesus
- v.32h: Paul quotes Isaiah 22:13
- v.33i: Paul quotes the Greek poet Menander (Thais, fragment 218)
- v.44j: σῶμα ψυχικόν (sōma psychikon) — a body animated by the soul/life-force; contrasted with σῶμα πνευματικόν (sōma pneumatikon), a body animated by the Spirit
- v.51k: μυστήριον (mystērion) — a truth now revealed: not all believers will die before Christ returns, but all will be transformed
- v.54l: Paul quotes Isaiah 25:8
- v.55m: Paul adapts Hosea 13:14, transforming a threat into a taunt of triumph over death
