# 1 Corinthians 4

**1** So here's how you should think of us: as servants of Christ and managersᵃ of the mysteries God has revealed. **2** Now, the main thing required of managers is that they be found trustworthy. **3** Personally, I couldn't care less if I'm judged by you or by any human court. I don't even judge myself. **4** My conscience is clear, but that doesn't mean I'm innocent — the Lord is the one who judges me. **5** So stop passing judgment before the right time. Wait until the Lord comes. He'll bring to light what's hidden in darkness and expose the motives of people's hearts. Then each person will receive their praise from God.

**6** Now, brothers and sisters, I've applied all this to myself and Apollos for your benefit, so you can learn from us the meaning of the saying, "Don't go beyond what is written."ᵇ That way, none of you will become arrogant, taking sides with one leader against another. **7** Who makes you different from anyone else? What do you have that you didn't receive? And if you received it, why do you boast as if you hadn't?

**8** You already have everything you need! You're already rich! You've become kings without us! I wish you really had become kings — so we could reign alongside you. **9** But it seems to me that God has put us apostles on display at the very end of the parade, like men sentenced to death in the arena. We've become a spectacleᶜ to the whole universe — to angels and to people alike. **10** We're fools for Christ, but you're so wise in Christ! We're weak, but you're strong! You're honored, we're dishonored! **11** Right up to this very moment, we're hungry and thirsty, wearing rags, getting beaten up, with no place to call home. **12** We wear ourselves out working with our own hands. When people curse us, we bless them. When we're persecuted, we endure it. **13** When we're slandered, we respond with kindness. We've become like the world's garbage, the scum of the earth — and it's still that way.

**14** I'm not writing this to shame you, but to warn you as my dear children. **15** Even if you had ten thousand tutorsᵈ in Christ, you don't have many fathers. In Christ Jesus I became your father through the good news. **16** So I'm urging you: imitate me. **17** That's exactly why I'm sending you Timothy, my dear and faithful child in the Lord. He'll remind you of how I live my life in Christ Jesus — the same way I teach everywhere in every church.

**18** Some of you have gotten pretty arrogant, as if I'm not actually coming to visit. **19** But I will come to you soon — if the Lord is willing — and then I'll find out not just what these arrogant people are saying, but what power they actually have. **20** Because the kingdom of God isn't about talk — it's about power. **21** What do you prefer? Should I come to you with a rod of discipline, or with loveᵉ and a gentle spirit?

---

## Notes
- v.1a: οἰκονόμους (oikonomous) — household managers, stewards; responsible for managing another's resources
- v.6b: This cryptic phrase τὸ μὴ ὑπὲρ ἃ γέγραπται (to mē hyper ha gegraptai) may refer to Scripture generally, or to the specific passages Paul has just quoted
- v.9c: θέατρον (theatron) — theater, spectacle; the image is of gladiators displayed last in a Roman procession, doomed to die in the arena
- v.15d: παιδαγωγούς (paidagōgous) — child-supervisors, guardians who accompanied children; not teachers in the modern sense
- v.21e: ἀγάπη (agapē) — selfless, committed love; the kind Paul will elaborate in chapter 13
