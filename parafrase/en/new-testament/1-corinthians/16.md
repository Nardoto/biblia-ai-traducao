# 1 Corinthians 16

**1** Now about the collectionᵃ for God's people: do what I told the churches in Galatia to do. **2** On the first day of every week, each one of you should set aside a sum of money in keeping with your income, saving it up, so that when I come no collections will need to be made. **3** Then, when I arrive, I'll give letters of introduction to the people you approve and send them with your gift to Jerusalem. **4** If it seems right for me to go too, they can travel with me.

**5** I'll visit you after I pass through Macedonia — I'm going through Macedonia — **6** and perhaps I'll stay with you for a while, or even spend the winter, so that you can help me on my journey, wherever I'm going. **7** I don't want to see you now just in passing. I hope to spend some time with you, if the Lord allows. **8** But I'll stay in Ephesus until Pentecost, **9** because a great and effective door has opened for me — and there are many who oppose me.

**10** When Timothy comes, make sure he has nothing to fear while he's with you, because he's doing the Lord's work, just as I am. **11** Nobody should look down on him. Send him on his way in peace so he can return to me, because I'm expecting him along with the brothers.

**12** Now about our brother Apollos: I strongly urged him to visit you with the brothers. He was quite unwillingᵇ to come right now, but he'll come when he has the opportunity.

**13** Stay alert. Stand firm in the faith. Be courageous. Be strong.ᶜ **14** Do everything in love.

**15** You know that the household of Stephanas were the first convertsᵈ in Achaia, and they've devoted themselves to serving God's people. I urge you, brothers and sisters, **16** to submit to people like them and to everyone who joins in the work and labors at it. **17** I'm glad Stephanas, Fortunatus, and Achaicus came, because they've made up for your absence. **18** They refreshed my spirit and yours too. Give people like them the recognition they deserve.

**19** The churches in the province of Asiaᵉ send you greetings. Aquila and Priscilla send you warm greetings in the Lord, together with the church that meets in their house. **20** All the brothers and sisters here send you greetings. Greet one another with a holy kiss.ᶠ

**21** I, Paul, write this greeting with my own hand.ᵍ **22** If anyone does not love the Lord, let them be under a curse.ʰ Come, Lord!ⁱ **23** The grace of the Lord Jesus be with you. **24** My love to all of you in Christ Jesus.

## Notes
- v.1a: λογείας (logeias) — collection, a gathering of funds; Paul organizes a major relief offering from Gentile churches for the impoverished Jerusalem church (cf. Rom 15:25-27; 2 Cor 8-9)
- v.12b: οὐκ ἦν θέλημα (ouk ēn thelēma) — "it was not [his] will"; could refer to Apollos's will or God's will; Apollos maintains his independence from Paul's urging
- v.13c: Four rapid imperatives: γρηγορεῖτε (grēgoreite, "be watchful"), στήκετε (stēkete, "stand firm"), ἀνδρίζεσθε (andrizesthe, "act courageously"), κραταιοῦσθε (krataiousthe, "be strong")
- v.15d: ἀπαρχή (aparchē) — firstfruits; the same word used of Christ's resurrection in 15:20, here applied to Stephanas's household as the first believers in the region
- v.19e: The Roman province of Asia, in modern-day western Turkey, where Ephesus was the capital
- v.20f: φιλήματι ἁγίῳ (philēmati hagiō) — a holy kiss; a standard greeting in early Christian worship, expressing familial bond
- v.21g: Paul typically dictated his letters but signed them personally, authenticating the letter (cf. Gal 6:11; 2 Thess 3:17)
- v.22h: ἀνάθεμα (anathema) — accursed, devoted to destruction; a solemn pronouncement
- v.22i: μαράνα θά (marana tha) — Aramaic: "Our Lord, come!" One of the earliest preserved Christian prayers, kept in its original Aramaic even in this Greek letter, testifying to its antiquity
