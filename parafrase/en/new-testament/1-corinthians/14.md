# 1 Corinthians 14

**1** Pursue love, and eagerly desireᵃ spiritual gifts — especially prophecy. **2** Because anyone who speaks in a tongue doesn't speak to people but to God. No one understands them — they're speaking mysteriesᵇ by the Spirit. **3** But the one who prophesies speaks to people for their strengthening, encouragement, and comfort.ᶜ **4** Whoever speaks in a tongue strengthens themselves, but whoever prophesies strengthens the church. **5** I'd like all of you to speak in tongues, but I'd rather you prophesy. The one who prophesies is greater than the one who speaks in tongues, unless they interpret, so that the church may be strengthened.

**6** Now, brothers and sisters, if I come to you speaking in tongues, what good will I do you unless I bring you some revelation, knowledge, prophecy, or teaching? **7** Even lifeless instruments that produce sound — like a flute or a harp — if they don't make distinct notes, how will anyone know what tune is being played? **8** And if the trumpet gives an unclear call, who will get ready for battle? **9** It's the same with you. Unless you use your tongue to speak words that are easy to understand, how will anyone know what you're saying? You'll just be talking to the air!

**10** There are all kinds of languages in the world, and none of them is without meaning. **11** But if I don't know the meaning of what someone is saying, I'll be a foreigner to the speaker, and the speaker a foreigner to me. **12** It's the same with you. Since you're eager for manifestations of the Spirit, strive to excel in those that build up the church.

**13** So the person who speaks in a tongue should pray for the ability to interpret. **14** Because if I pray in a tongue, my spirit prays, but my mindᵈ is unfruitful. **15** So what should I do? I'll pray with my spirit, but I'll also pray with my mind. I'll sing with my spirit, but I'll also sing with my mind. **16** Otherwise, if you give thanks with your spirit, how can someone who doesn't understand say "Amen"ᵉ to your thanksgiving? They don't know what you're saying! **17** You might be giving thanks well enough, but the other person isn't built up.

**18** I thank God that I speak in tongues more than all of you. **19** But in the church I'd rather speak five words with my mind, to instruct others, than ten thousand words in a tongue.

**20** Brothers and sisters, stop thinking like children. In evil be infants, but in your thinking be adults. **21** In the law it's written: "Through people of strange tongues and through the lips of foreigners I will speak to this people, but even then they will not listen to me,"ᶠ says the Lord. **22** So tongues are a sign, not for believers, but for unbelievers. Prophecy, however, is not for unbelievers but for believers. **23** So if the whole church comes together and everyone speaks in tongues, and outsiders or unbelievers walk in, won't they say you're out of your minds? **24** But if everyone is prophesying, and an unbeliever or outsider walks in, they'll be convicted by all, called to account by all. **25** The secrets of their heart will be laid bare, and they'll fall facedown and worship God, declaring, "God is really among you!"

**26** So what should you do, brothers and sisters? When you come together, each person has a hymn, a teaching, a revelation, a tongue, or an interpretation. Everything should be done for the building up of the church. **27** If anyone speaks in a tongue, limit it to two — or at most three — and one at a time, and someone must interpret. **28** If there's no interpreter, they should keep quiet in the church and speak to themselves and to God.

**29** Two or three prophets should speak, and the others should weigh carefullyᵍ what is said. **30** And if a revelation comes to someone who is sitting down, the first speaker should stop. **31** You can all prophesy one by one, so that everyone may learn and be encouraged. **32** The spirits of prophets are subject to the control of the prophets, **33** because God is not a God of disorder but of peace — as in all the churches of God's people.

**34** Womenʰ should be silent in the churches, for they are not permitted to speak. They should be in submission, as the law says. **35** If they want to learn anything, they should ask their own husbands at home, because it's disgraceful for a woman to speak in church.

**36** Or did the word of God originate with you? Or are you the only ones it has reached? **37** If anyone thinks they're a prophet or spiritually gifted, let them acknowledge that what I'm writing to you is the Lord's command. **38** If they ignore this, they will be ignored.

**39** So, my brothers and sisters, be eager to prophesy, and don't forbid speaking in tongues. **40** But everything should be done in a fitting and orderly way.

## Notes
- v.1a: ζηλοῦτε (zēloute) — be zealous for, eagerly desire; the same intensity redirected from rivalry (3:3) to spiritual service
- v.2b: μυστήρια (mystēria) — mysteries, hidden truths; the tongue-speaker communicates things concealed from human understanding
- v.3c: Three terms: οἰκοδομήν (oikodomēn, "building up"), παράκλησιν (paraklēsin, "encouragement/exhortation"), παραμυθίαν (paramythian, "consolation/comfort")
- v.14d: νοῦς (nous) — mind, understanding, rational faculty; Paul insists on intelligibility alongside spiritual experience
- v.16e: ἀμήν (amēn) — from Hebrew, meaning "so be it, truly"; congregational participation requires understanding
- v.21f: Paul quotes Isaiah 28:11-12, where God warns that foreign speech will be a sign of judgment, not blessing
- v.29g: διακρινέτωσαν (diakrinetōsan) — evaluate, discern, sift; the community exercises collective discernment over prophetic speech
- v.34h: These verses (34-35) are among the most debated in Paul's letters. Some scholars note they appear after v.40 in some manuscripts, suggesting a later insertion. Others argue Paul addresses a specific local disruption, not women's speech universally (cf. 11:5 where women pray and prophesy). The tension with 11:5 is widely acknowledged
