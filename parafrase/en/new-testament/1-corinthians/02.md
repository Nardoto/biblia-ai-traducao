# 1 Corinthians 2

**1** When I came to you, brothers and sisters, I didn't come with impressive eloquence or superior wisdomᵃ to announce God's testimonyᵇ to you. **2** I had decided to know nothing among you except Jesus Christ — and him crucified. **3** I came to you in weakness, with great fear and trembling. **4** My message and my preaching weren't delivered with persuasive words of wisdom, but with a demonstration of the Spirit and of power, **5** so that your faith wouldn't rest on human wisdom but on God's power.

**6** Now, we do speak a kind of wisdom among the mature — but it's not the wisdom of this age or of the rulers of this age, who are being brought to nothing. **7** No, we speak God's wisdom, hidden in a mysteryᶜ — the wisdom God planned before the ages for our glory. **8** None of the rulers of this age understood it. If they had, they wouldn't have crucified the Lord of glory. **9** But as it's written: "What no eye has seen, what no ear has heard, and what no human mind has conceived — these are the things God has prepared for those who love him."

**10** But God has revealed these things to us through his Spirit. The Spirit searches everything, even the deep things of God. **11** After all, who among humans knows a person's thoughts except that person's own spirit within them? In the same way, nobody knows God's thoughts except the Spirit of God. **12** And we haven't received the spirit of the world but the Spirit who is from God, so that we can understand the things God has freely given us.

**13** And that's exactly what we speak about — not in words taught by human wisdom, but in words taught by the Spirit, explaining spiritual realitiesᵈ with Spirit-given words. **14** The natural personᵉ doesn't accept the things that come from God's Spirit. They seem like foolishness to them, and they can't understand them because these things are discerned spirituallyᶠ. **15** But the spiritual person discerns all things, while they themselves aren't subject to anyone else's judgment. **16** For "who has known the mind of the Lord so as to instruct him?" But we have the mindᵍ of Christ.

---

## Notes
- v.1a: σοφία (sophia) — wisdom; Paul deliberately contrasts divine wisdom with the rhetorical sophistication prized in Corinth
- v.1b: Some manuscripts read μυστήριον (mystērion, "mystery") instead of μαρτύριον (martyrion, "testimony"); both are well-attested
- v.7c: μυστήριον (mystērion) — not a puzzle to be solved but a truth once hidden now revealed by God
- v.13d: πνευματικοῖς πνευματικά (pneumatikois pneumatika) — could mean "spiritual things to spiritual people" or "spiritual things with spiritual words"; the ambiguity may be intentional
- v.14e: ψυχικὸς ἄνθρωπος (psychikos anthrōpos) — the "soulish" or "natural" person; one operating by merely human faculties without the Spirit
- v.14f: ἀνακρίνεται πνευματικῶς (anakrinetai pneumatikōs) — examined, judged, discerned by the Spirit's work
- v.16g: νοῦς Χριστοῦ (nous Christou) — the mind, understanding, or perspective of Christ; Paul's bold claim for the Spirit-led community
