# 1 Corinthians 8

**1** Now about food sacrificed to idols. We know that "we all have knowledge."ᵃ But knowledge puffs up, while love builds up. **2** Anyone who thinks they know something doesn't yet know as they ought to know. **3** But whoever loves God is known by God.

**4** So about eating food sacrificed to idols: we know that "an idol is nothing at all in the world"ᵇ and that "there is no God but one." **5** Even if there are so-called gods, whether in heaven or on earth — as indeed there are many "gods" and many "lords" — **6** yet for us there is one God, the Father, from whom all things came and for whom we exist, and one Lord, Jesus Christ, through whom all things came and through whom we exist.ᶜ

**7** But not everyone has this knowledge. Some people, still accustomed to idols, eat this food as if it were actually sacrificed to a real god, and since their conscience is weak, it becomes stained. **8** Food doesn't bring us closer to God. We're no worse off if we don't eat, and no better off if we do.

**9** But be careful that this freedom of yours doesn't become a stumbling blockᵈ to the weak. **10** If someone with a weak conscience sees you — with all your knowledge — eating in an idol's temple, won't they be emboldened to eat what's been sacrificed to idols? **11** And so this weak brother or sister, for whom Christ died, is destroyed by your knowledge. **12** When you sin against your brothers and sisters like this, wounding their weak conscience, you sin against Christ.

**13** So if food causes my brother or sister to stumble, I'll never eat meat again — ever — so that I won't cause them to fall.ᵉ

---

## Notes
- v.1a: Paul again quotes Corinthian slogans — γνῶσιν ἔχομεν (gnōsin echomen, "we have knowledge") — then corrects their attitude
- v.4b: Another Corinthian slogan: οὐδὲν εἴδωλον ἐν κόσμῳ (ouden eidōlon en kosmō) — Paul agrees theologically but adds nuance about pastoral care
- v.6c: This verse contains an early Christian confession remarkably parallel to the Shema (Deut 6:4), expanded to include Christ alongside the Father
- v.9d: πρόσκομμα (proskomma) — a stumbling block, something that causes another to trip and fall spiritually
- v.13e: σκανδαλίζω (skandalizō) — to cause to stumble, to set a trap; Paul makes his personal commitment concrete and absolute
