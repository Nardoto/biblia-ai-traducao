# 1 Corinthians 10

**1** I don't want you to be unaware, brothers and sisters, that our ancestors were all under the cloud, and they all passed through the sea. **2** They were all baptized into Mosesᵃ in the cloud and in the sea. **3** They all ate the same spiritual food **4** and drank the same spiritual drink, because they drank from the spiritual rock that followed them — and that rock was Christ.ᵇ **5** Nevertheless, God was not pleased with most of them, and their bodies were scattered across the wilderness.

**6** Now these things happened as examplesᶜ for us, to keep us from craving evil things the way they did. **7** Don't be idolaters, as some of them were. As it's written: "The people sat down to eat and drink and got up to indulge in revelry." **8** And let's not commit sexual immorality, as some of them did — and twenty-three thousand fell in a single day. **9** Let's not test Christᵈ, as some of them did — and were killed by snakes. **10** And don't grumble, as some of them did — and were killed by the destroying angel.

**11** These things happened to them as examples, and they were written down as warnings for us, on whom the fulfillment of the ages has come. **12** So if you think you're standing firm, watch out — you might fall! **13** No temptation has seized you except what is common to humanity. And God is faithful — he won't let you be tempted beyond what you can handle. When you are tempted, he'll also provide a way outᵉ so you can endure it.

**14** Therefore, my dear friends, flee from idolatry. **15** I'm speaking to sensible people — judge for yourselves what I say. **16** The cup of blessing that we bless — isn't it a sharing inᶠ the blood of Christ? And the bread that we break — isn't it a sharing in the body of Christ? **17** Because there is one loaf, we who are many are one body, for we all share the one loaf.

**18** Consider the people of Israel: don't those who eat the sacrifices participate in the altar? **19** So what am I saying? That food sacrificed to an idol is anything? Or that an idol is anything? **20** No! But what the pagans sacrifice, they sacrifice to demonsᵍ, not to God. And I don't want you to be participants with demons. **21** You can't drink the cup of the Lord and the cup of demons too. You can't have a share in the Lord's table and the demons' table too. **22** Are we trying to provoke the Lord to jealousy? Are we stronger than he is?

**23** "I have the right to do anything"ʰ — but not everything is beneficial. "I have the right to do anything" — but not everything builds up. **24** Nobody should seek their own good, but the good of others.

**25** Eat anything sold in the meat market without raising questions of conscience, **26** because "the earth is the Lord's, and everything in it."ⁱ **27** If an unbeliever invites you to a meal and you want to go, eat whatever is put before you without raising questions of conscience. **28** But if someone tells you, "This has been offered in sacrifice," then don't eat it — for the sake of the one who told you, and for conscience's sake. **29** I mean the other person's conscience, not yours. For why should my freedom be judged by someone else's conscience? **30** If I eat with thankfulness, why am I criticized for food I give thanks for?

**31** So whether you eat or drink or whatever you do, do it all for the glory of God. **32** Don't cause anyone to stumble — not Jews, not Greeks, not God's church — **33** just as I try to please everyone in everything I do. I'm not seeking my own benefit but the benefit of the many, so that they may be saved.

## Notes
- v.2a: εἰς τὸν Μωϋσῆν ἐβαπτίσαντο (eis ton Mōysēn ebaptisanto) — "baptized into Moses"; Paul draws an audacious parallel between Israel's exodus and Christian baptism
- v.4b: Paul draws on Jewish traditions that the rock from which Israel drank (Ex 17:6; Num 20:11) followed them through the wilderness, and identifies it as Christ
- v.6c: τύποι (typoi) — types, patterns, examples; events in Israel's history prefigure and warn the church
- v.9d: Some manuscripts read κύριον (kyrion, "the Lord") or θεόν (theon, "God") instead of Χριστόν (Christon, "Christ")
- v.13e: ἔκβασιν (ekbasin) — a way out, an exit, an escape route; God actively provides a path through every trial
- v.16f: κοινωνία (koinōnia) — communion, participation, sharing; the Lord's Supper creates genuine connection with Christ's sacrifice
- v.20g: δαιμονίοις (daimoniois) — demons; Paul quotes Deuteronomy 32:17, asserting that real spiritual powers stand behind pagan worship even if the idols themselves are nothing
- v.23h: Paul again quotes and corrects the Corinthian slogan (cf. 6:12)
- v.26i: Paul quotes Psalm 24:1, grounding freedom in God's ownership of all creation
