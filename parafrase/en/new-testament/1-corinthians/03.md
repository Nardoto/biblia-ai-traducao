# 1 Corinthians 3

**1** Brothers and sisters, I couldn't talk to you as spiritual people — I had to talk to you as people still living by the fleshᵃ, as babies in Christ. **2** I gave you milk, not solid food, because you weren't ready for it. And honestly? You're still not ready, **3** because you're still living by the flesh. When there's jealousy and quarreling among you, aren't you living like everyone else? Aren't you just acting like ordinary people? **4** When one of you says, "I'm with Paul," and another says, "I'm with Apollos" — aren't you being merely human?

**5** So what is Apollos, really? And what is Paul? We're just servantsᵇ through whom you came to believe — each doing the task the Lord assigned. **6** I planted, Apollos watered, but God made it grow. **7** So the one who plants is nothing, and the one who waters is nothing — only God, who makes things grow, matters. **8** The planter and the waterer are one in purpose, and each will receive their own reward according to their own labor. **9** We're God's co-workersᶜ; you are God's field, God's building.

**10** By the grace God gave me, I laid a foundation like a skilled master builderᵈ, and someone else is building on it. But everyone should be careful how they build. **11** No one can lay a foundation other than the one already laid — that foundation is Jesus Christ. **12** Now if anyone builds on this foundation using gold, silver, precious stones, wood, hay, or straw, **13** their work will be shown for what it is. The Dayᵉ will bring it to light, because it will be revealed with fire, and the fire will test what kind of work each person has done. **14** If what someone has built survives, they'll receive a reward. **15** If it burns up, they'll suffer loss — though they themselves will be saved, but only like someone escaping through the flames.

**16** Don't you know that you are God's templeᶠ and that God's Spirit lives in you? **17** If anyone destroys God's temple, God will destroy them, because God's temple is sacred — and that's what you are.

**18** Don't fool yourselves. If any of you think you're wise by this age's standards, become a "fool" so you can become truly wise. **19** Because the wisdom of this world is foolishness in God's eyes. As it's written: "He catches the wise in their craftiness." **20** And again: "The Lord knows that the thoughts of the wise are futile." **21** So stop boasting about human leaders! Everything belongs to you — **22** whether Paul or Apollos or Cephas, whether the world or life or death, whether the present or the future — it all belongs to you, **23** and you belong to Christ, and Christ belongs to God.

---

## Notes
- v.1a: σαρκίνοις (sarkinois) — "fleshly," made of flesh; in v.3 Paul switches to σαρκικοί (sarkikoi), "characterized by flesh," suggesting ongoing choice rather than mere nature
- v.5b: διάκονοι (diakonoi) — servants, attendants; Paul deliberately uses a humble term for ministry
- v.9c: θεοῦ συνεργοί (theou synergoi) — co-workers belonging to God; the emphasis is that Paul and Apollos serve God together, not that humans are God's equal partners
- v.10d: σοφὸς ἀρχιτέκτων (sophos architektōn) — a skilled chief builder; origin of English "architect"
- v.13e: ἡ ἡμέρα (hē hēmera) — "the Day," referring to the day of the Lord's judgment
- v.16f: ναός (naos) — the inner sanctuary of the temple, the holiest part; Paul uses the plural "you" — the community together is God's temple
