# Matthew 18

**1** At that time, the disciples came to Jesus and asked, "So who's the greatest in the kingdom of heaven?"

**2** He called a little child over, set the child among them, **3** and said, "I'm telling you the truth, unless you turn around and become like little children, you'll never enter the kingdom of heaven.ᵃ **4** Whoever humbles themselves like this child — that's the person who's greatest in the kingdom of heaven. **5** And whoever welcomes one child like this in my name welcomes me.

**6** "But whoever causes one of these little ones who trust in me to stumble — it would be better for them to have a huge millstoneᵇ hung around their neck and be drowned in the open sea. **7** How terrible for the world because of the things that cause people to stumble! Those things have to come, but how terrible for the person through whom they come!

**8** "If your hand or foot causes you to stumble, cut it off and throw it away. It's better to enter life maimed or lame than to have two hands or two feet and be thrown into the eternal fire. **9** And if your eye causes you to stumble, tear it out and throw it away. It's better to enter life with one eye than to have two eyes and be thrown into the fire of Gehenna.

**10** "Make sure you don't look down on one of these little ones. I'm telling you, their angels in heaven are always seeing the face of my Father in heaven.ᶜ

**12** "What do you think? If a man has a hundred sheep and one of them wanders off, won't he leave the ninety-nine on the hills and go looking for the one that wandered away? **13** And if he finds it, I'm telling you the truth, he's happier about that one than about the ninety-nine that didn't wander off. **14** In the same way, it's not the will of your Father in heaven that a single one of these little ones should be lost.

**15** "If your brother or sister sins against you,ᵈ go and point out their fault — just between the two of you, privately. If they listen, you've won your brother or sister back. **16** But if they won't listen, take one or two others along, so that 'every matter may be established by the testimony of two or three witnesses.'ᵉ **17** If they refuse to listen to them, tell it to the communityᶠ. And if they refuse to listen even to the community, treat them as you would a Gentile or a tax collector.ᵍ

**18** "I'm telling you the truth, whatever you bind on earth will have been bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will have been loosed in heaven.

**19** "Again, I'm telling you the truth: if two of you on earth agree about anything you ask for, it'll be done for you by my Father in heaven. **20** Because where two or three are gathered in my name, I'm there among them."

**21** Then Peter came up and asked him, "Lord, how many times should I forgive my brother or sister who sins against me? Up to seven times?"ʰ

**22** Jesus said to him, "I'm not saying seven times — I'm saying seventy-seven times.ⁱ

**23** "That's why the kingdom of heaven is like a king who decided to settle accounts with his servants. **24** When he began the process, someone was brought to him who owed ten thousand talents.ʲ **25** Since he couldn't pay, the master ordered that he, his wife, his children, and everything he owned be sold to repay the debt.

**26** "The servant fell down before him. 'Be patient with me,' he begged, 'and I'll pay you everything!' **27** The master's heart went out to him. He released him and forgave the entire debt.

**28** "But when that servant went out, he found one of his fellow servants who owed him a hundred denarii.ᵏ He grabbed him, started choking him, and said, 'Pay what you owe!'

**29** "His fellow servant fell down and begged him, 'Be patient with me, and I'll pay you!'

**30** "But he refused. He went and had the man thrown into prison until he could pay the debt. **31** When the other servants saw what had happened, they were deeply disturbed. They went and reported everything to their master.

**32** "Then the master summoned him. 'You wicked servant!' he said. 'I forgave your entire debt because you begged me. **33** Shouldn't you have shown mercy to your fellow servant the way I showed mercy to you?' **34** In anger, his master handed him over to the torturers until he paid back everything he owed.

**35** "That's how my heavenly Father will treat each of you — unless you forgive your brother or sister from the heart."

## Notes

- v.3a: *straphēte kai genēsthe hōs ta paidia* — "turn and become like the children." Not childishness but the child's dependent posture, lack of status, and openness to receive.
- v.6b: *mylos onikos* (μύλος ὀνικός) — a "donkey millstone," a massive stone turned by a donkey, as opposed to the smaller hand-mill. This is extreme, deliberate imagery.
- v.10c: This is the clearest New Testament reference to guardian angels. The point: these "little ones" have direct heavenly representation before God.
- v.15d: Some manuscripts omit "against you," making this about any sin, not just personal offenses.
- v.16e: Quoting Deuteronomy 19:15.
- v.17f: *ekklēsia* (ἐκκλησία) — "the assembly/community." The second of only two uses in the Gospels (both in Matthew).
- v.17g: The irony is deliberate — Jesus himself was known as a "friend of tax collectors." The instruction may mean: treat them as people who need to be reached, not written off.
- v.21h: Seven was the Jewish number of completeness. Peter thinks he's being generous — rabbinic teaching typically required forgiving three times.
- v.22i: *hebdomēkontakis hepta* (ἑβδομηκοντάκις ἑπτά) — either "seventy-seven" or "seventy times seven" (490). Either way, the point is limitless forgiveness — a deliberate reversal of Lamech's seventy-sevenfold vengeance (Genesis 4:24).
- v.24j: *myriōn talantōn* (μυρίων ταλάντων) — ten thousand talents, an astronomically absurd sum. One talent was about 20 years' wages for a laborer. Ten thousand talents equals roughly 200,000 years of work. The debt is unpayable by design.
- v.28k: A hundred denarii was about 100 days' wages — a real debt, but trivially small compared to ten thousand talents. The contrast is the whole point.
