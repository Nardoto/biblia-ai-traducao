# Matthew 4

**1** Then the Spirit led Jesus out into the wilderness to be tested by the devil.ᵃ **2** After fasting forty days and forty nights, he was starving. **3** The tester came to him and said, "If you really are God's Son, tell these stones to become bread."

**4** But Jesus answered, "It's written: 'People don't live on bread alone, but on every word that comes from God's mouth.'"ᵇ

**5** Then the devil took him to the holy city and set him on the highest point of the temple. **6** "If you really are God's Son," he said, "throw yourself down. After all, it's written: 'He'll command his angels concerning you, and they'll carry you in their hands so you won't even stub your foot on a stone.'"

**7** Jesus told him, "It's also written: 'Don't put the Lord your God to the test.'"ᶜ

**8** Again, the devil took him to an extremely high mountain and showed him all the kingdoms of the world and their magnificence. **9** "I'll give you all of this," he said, "if you'll fall down and worship me."

**10** Then Jesus said to him, "Get out of here, Satan! It's written: 'Worship the Lord your God, and serve him only.'"ᵈ

**11** Then the devil left him, and angels came and took care of him.

**12** When Jesus heard that John had been arrested, he withdrew to Galilee. **13** He left Nazareth and settled in Capernaum, which sits by the sea in the territory of Zebulun and Naphtali — **14** so that what was spoken through the prophet Isaiah would come true:

**15** "Land of Zebulun and land of Naphtali, the road to the sea, beyond the Jordan, Galilee of the Gentiles — **16** the people sitting in darkness have seen a great light, and for those sitting in the shadow of death,ᵉ a light has dawned."

**17** From that point on, Jesus began to proclaim: "Turn your lives around — the kingdom of heaven has arrived!"

**18** As he was walking along the Sea of Galilee, he saw two brothers — Simon (called Peter) and his brother Andrew — throwing a net into the sea. They were fishermen. **19** He said to them, "Come follow me, and I'll make you fishers of people." **20** Immediately they dropped their nets and followed him.

**21** Going on from there, he saw two other brothers — James son of Zebedee and his brother John — in a boat with their father Zebedee, mending their nets. He called them, **22** and immediately they left the boat and their father and followed him.

**23** Jesus traveled throughout all of Galilee, teaching in their synagogues, proclaiming the good news of the kingdom, and healing every kind of disease and sickness among the people. **24** News about him spread through the whole region of Syria. People brought him everyone who was suffering from various diseases and intense pains — those tormented by demons, those with seizures, those who were paralyzed — and he healed them. **25** Large crowds followed him from Galilee, the Decapolis,ᶠ Jerusalem, Judea, and from beyond the Jordan.

## Notes

- v.1a: *peirasthēnai* (πειρασθῆναι) — "to be tested/tempted." The word carries both senses: a test to prove quality (positive) and a temptation to do wrong (negative).
- v.4b: Quoting Deuteronomy 8:3. Jesus responds to each temptation with Torah, specifically from Deuteronomy — Israel's wilderness failures are being reversed.
- v.7c: Quoting Deuteronomy 6:16, which refers to Israel's testing of God at Massah (Exodus 17:1-7).
- v.10d: Quoting Deuteronomy 6:13. The pattern is clear: Jesus succeeds where Israel failed during their forty years in the wilderness.
- v.16e: *skia thanatou* (σκιᾷ θανάτου) — "shadow of death," a powerful image from Isaiah 9:1-2 suggesting complete hopelessness being pierced by light.
- v.25f: *Dekapolis* (Δεκάπολις) — "Ten Cities," a league of Greco-Roman cities east of the Jordan, mostly Gentile territory. Their inclusion hints at Jesus's reach beyond Jewish borders.
