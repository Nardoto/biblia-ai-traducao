# Matthew 8

**1** When he came down from the mountain, large crowds followed him. **2** And right away, a man with a serious skin diseaseᵃ came up and knelt before him. "Lord," he said, "if you're willing, you can make me clean."

**3** Jesus reached out his hand and touched him.ᵇ "I'm willing," he said. "Be clean!" Immediately his skin disease was healed. **4** Then Jesus told him, "Don't tell anyone. But go show yourself to the priest and present the offering that Moses prescribed — as evidence for them."

**5** When Jesus entered Capernaum, a Roman centurion came to him, pleading with him. **6** "Lord," he said, "my servant is lying at home paralyzed, in terrible agony."

**7** Jesus said to him, "I'll come and heal him."

**8** But the centurion answered, "Lord, I don't deserve to have you come under my roof. Just say the word, and my servant will be healed. **9** I understand authority — I'm a man under authority myself, with soldiers under me. I tell one 'Go,' and he goes. I tell another 'Come,' and he comes. I tell my servant 'Do this,' and he does it."

**10** When Jesus heard this, he was amazed. He said to those following him, "I'm telling you the truth — I haven't found faith this strong anywhere in Israel.ᶜ **11** And I tell you, many will come from the east and the west and sit down at the feast with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob in the kingdom of heaven. **12** But the natural heirs of the kingdomᵈ will be thrown outside into the darkness, where there'll be weeping and grinding of teeth."

**13** Then Jesus said to the centurion, "Go. It'll be done just as you believed it would." And his servant was healed at that very moment.

**14** When Jesus arrived at Peter's house, he found Peter's mother-in-law lying in bed with a fever. **15** He touched her hand, and the fever left her. She got up and began serving them.

**16** When evening came, people brought him many who were demonized, and he drove out the spirits with a word. He healed all who were sick, **17** so that what was spoken through the prophet Isaiah would come true: "He took on our weaknesses and carried our diseases."ᵉ

**18** When Jesus saw a crowd gathering around him, he gave orders to cross to the other side of the lake. **19** A legal scholar came up and said, "Teacher, I'll follow you wherever you go."

**20** Jesus said to him, "Foxes have dens, and birds have nests, but the Son of Manᶠ has nowhere to lay his head."

**21** Another disciple said to him, "Lord, let me first go and bury my father."

**22** But Jesus told him, "Follow me, and let the dead bury their own dead."ᵍ

**23** Then he got into a boat, and his disciples followed him. **24** Suddenly a massive storm hit the lake — so violent that the waves were swamping the boat. But Jesus was sleeping. **25** His disciples came and woke him up. "Lord, save us!" they cried. "We're going to drown!"

**26** He said to them, "Why are you so afraid? You have so little faith!" Then he got up and rebuked the winds and the sea, and it became completely calm.

**27** The men were astonished. "What kind of person is this?" they said. "Even the winds and the sea obey him!"

**28** When he arrived on the other side, in the region of the Gadarenes, two men who were demonized came out from among the tombs to meet him. They were so dangerous that nobody could travel that road. **29** They screamed, "What do you want with us, Son of God? Have you come here to torment us before the appointed time?"ʰ

**30** Some distance away, a large herd of pigs was feeding. **31** The demons begged him, "If you're going to drive us out, send us into that herd of pigs."

**32** "Go!" he said. So they came out and went into the pigs — and the whole herd rushed down the steep bank into the lake and drowned in the water. **33** The herders ran off, went into the town, and reported everything, including what had happened to the demonized men. **34** The whole town came out to meet Jesus. And when they saw him, they begged him to leave their region.

## Notes

- v.2a: *lepros* (λεπρός) — traditionally "leper," but biblical *lepra* covered various skin conditions, not just Hansen's disease.
- v.3b: Touching someone with this condition made a person ritually unclean under Torah law. Jesus's touch is deliberately transgressive.
- v.10c: *pistis* (πίστις) — "faith/trust." The centurion, a Gentile, understood Jesus's authority better than anyone in Israel had.
- v.12d: *huioi tēs basileias* (υἱοὶ τῆς βασιλείας) — "sons of the kingdom," meaning those who were expected to inherit it by birthright.
- v.17e: Quoting Isaiah 53:4. Matthew applies the Suffering Servant passage to Jesus's healing ministry.
- v.20f: *huios tou anthrōpou* (υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου) — "Son of Man," Jesus's most frequent self-designation. It echoes Daniel 7:13-14, where a human figure receives universal authority.
- v.22g: A shockingly provocative statement. "Let the dead bury their dead" likely means "Let the spiritually dead attend to such matters." Burial duties were considered among the highest obligations in Jewish culture.
- v.29h: The demons recognize Jesus's identity and assume a future judgment. "Before the time" implies they know their destruction is inevitable but expected it later.
