# Matthew 6

**1** "Be careful not to practice your righteousnessᵃ in front of others just to be noticed by them. If you do, you've got no reward coming from your Father in heaven.

**2** "So when you give to the poor, don't blast a trumpet announcing it — the way the hypocritesᵇ do in the synagogues and on the streets so people will admire them. I'm telling you the truth, they've already received their full reward. **3** But when you give, don't even let your left hand know what your right hand is doing. **4** Let your giving be in secret. And your Father, who sees what's done in secret, will reward you.

**5** "And when you pray, don't be like the hypocrites. They love to pray standing in the synagogues and on street corners where everybody can see them. I'm telling you the truth, they've already received their full reward. **6** But when you pray, go into your room, close the door, and pray to your Father who is in that hidden place. And your Father, who sees what's done in secret, will reward you.

**7** "When you pray, don't pile up empty words like the pagans do. They think they'll be heard because of their many words. **8** Don't be like them. Your Father knows what you need before you even ask.

**9** "So here's how you should pray:

'Our Father in heaven, may your name be treated as holyᶜ —
**10** may your kingdom come, may what you want be done on earth just as it is in heaven.
**11** Give us today our bread for the day.ᵈ
**12** And forgive us our debts, just as we've forgiven those who owe us.ᵉ
**13** And don't lead us into a time of testing, but rescue us from the evil one.'

**14** "Because if you forgive other people when they wrong you, your heavenly Father will forgive you too. **15** But if you don't forgive others, your Father won't forgive your wrongs either.

**16** "When you fast, don't put on a gloomy face like the hypocrites do. They make themselves look miserable so everyone can see they're fasting. I'm telling you the truth, they've already received their full reward. **17** But when you fast, put oil on your head and wash your face **18** so it won't be obvious to people that you're fasting — only to your Father, who is unseen. And your Father, who sees what's done in secret, will reward you.

**19** "Don't stockpile treasures for yourselves on earth, where moths and rust destroy and where thieves break in and steal. **20** Instead, stockpile treasures in heaven, where moths and rust can't destroy and thieves can't break in and steal. **21** Because wherever your treasure is, that's where your heart will be too.

**22** "The eye is the lamp of the body.ᶠ So if your eye is healthy, your whole body will be full of light. **23** But if your eye is diseased, your whole body will be full of darkness. If the light that's in you is actually darkness — how deep that darkness must be!

**24** "Nobody can serve two masters. You'll either hate the one and love the other, or you'll be devoted to the one and look down on the other. You can't serve both God and Money.ᵍ

**25** "That's why I'm telling you: stop worrying about your life — what you'll eat or what you'll drink — or about your body — what you'll wear. Isn't life more than food? Isn't the body more than clothing? **26** Look at the birds in the sky. They don't plant, they don't harvest, they don't store up in barns — and your heavenly Father feeds them. Aren't you worth far more than they are? **27** And which of you can add a single hour to your life by worrying?ʰ

**28** "And why do you worry about clothing? Study how the wildflowers grow. They don't work hard or spin thread. **29** But I'm telling you, not even Solomon in all his royal splendor was dressed like one of these. **30** If that's how God clothes the grass of the field — which is here today and tossed into the oven tomorrow — won't he do so much more for you? You have so little faith!

**31** "So stop worrying and saying, 'What are we going to eat?' or 'What are we going to drink?' or 'What are we going to wear?' **32** The pagans chase after all these things. Your heavenly Father knows you need every one of them. **33** Instead, make God's kingdomⁱ and his way of making things right your first pursuit — and all these things will be given to you as well.

**34** "So don't worry about tomorrow. Tomorrow will worry about itself. Each day has enough trouble of its own.

## Notes

- v.1a: *dikaiosynēn* (δικαιοσύνην) — "righteousness" in the sense of religious practices/acts of devotion.
- v.2b: *hypokritai* (ὑποκριταί) — originally a Greek theater term for actors wearing masks. It implies playing a role rather than being genuine.
- v.9c: *hagiasthētō to onoma sou* (ἁγιασθήτω τὸ ὄνομά σου) — "may your name be sanctified/hallowed." The passive voice implies God is the one who will bring this about.
- v.11d: *arton epiousian* (ἄρτον ἐπιούσιον) — the meaning of *epiousios* is debated; it may mean "for the coming day," "necessary for existence," or "daily." It appears almost nowhere else in Greek literature.
- v.12e: *opheilēmata* (ὀφειλήματα) — literally "debts." Matthew uses the financial metaphor where Luke uses "sins." In Aramaic, *hoba* meant both debt and sin.
- v.22f: The "eye" saying connects to the concept of generosity in Jewish thought. A "good eye" meant generosity; an "evil eye" meant stinginess.
- v.24g: *mamōna* (μαμωνᾷ) — *mammon*, an Aramaic word for wealth/possessions, here almost personified as a rival deity.
- v.27h: *hēlikian* (ἡλικίαν) — could mean "lifespan" (adding an hour) or "stature" (adding a cubit). Either way, the point is the same: worry accomplishes nothing.
- v.33i: Some manuscripts read "the kingdom of God" rather than "his kingdom."
