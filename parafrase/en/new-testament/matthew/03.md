# Matthew 3

**1** Around that time, John the Baptizerᵃ showed up in the wilderness of Judea, **2** proclaiming: "Turn your lives around!ᵇ The kingdom of heaven has arrived!"

**3** This is the one the prophet Isaiah was talking about when he said: "A voice shouting in the wilderness: 'Clear the way for the Lord! Straighten out the paths for him!'"

**4** John wore clothes made from camel hair with a leather belt around his waist. His diet was locusts and wild honey. **5** People were streaming out to him from Jerusalem, from all over Judea, and from the whole region around the Jordan. **6** They were being immersed by him in the Jordan River as they openly confessedᶜ their sins.

**7** But when he saw a crowd of Pharisees and Sadducees coming to where he was baptizing, he said to them, "You brood of vipers! Who tipped you off to run from the coming judgment? **8** Produce fruit that shows you've actually turned your lives around. **9** And don't even think about telling yourselves, 'We have Abraham as our ancestor.' I'm telling you, God can raise up children for Abraham from these very stones. **10** The axe is already positioned at the root of the trees. Every tree that doesn't produce good fruit gets chopped down and thrown into the fire.

**11** "I'm immersing you in water as a sign of your changed direction,ᵈ but the one coming after me is more powerful than I am — I'm not even worthy to carry his sandals. He'll immerse you in the Holy Spirit and fire. **12** He's got his winnowing fork in his hand, and he'll thoroughly clean out his threshing floor. He'll gather his wheat into the barn, but the chaff he'll burn up with fire that can't be put out."

**13** Then Jesus arrived at the Jordan from Galilee, coming to John to be immersed by him. **14** But John tried to stop him. "I'm the one who needs to be immersed by you," he said, "and you're coming to me?"

**15** Jesus answered, "Let it happen for now. This is how we should fulfill everything that's right."ᵉ So John agreed.

**16** As soon as Jesus was immersed, he came up out of the water — and suddenly, the heavens were torn openᶠ and he saw the Spirit of God coming down like a dove and settling on him. **17** And a voice from heaven said, "This is my Son, the one I love — I'm completely pleased with him."ᵍ

---

## Notes

- v.1a: *baptistēs* (βαπτιστής) — "the one who immerses." The Greek *baptizō* means to dip, plunge, or immerse.
- v.2b: *metanoeite* (μετανοεῖτε) — literally "change your mind/thinking." It implies a complete reorientation of life, not just feeling sorry.
- v.6c: *exomologoumenoi* (ἐξομολογούμενοι) — a public, open acknowledgment — stronger than a private admission.
- v.11d: *eis metanoian* (εἰς μετάνοιαν) — "into/toward repentance." The immersion is connected to the life-change, not the cause of it.
- v.15e: *pasan dikaiosynēn* (πᾶσαν δικαιοσύνην) — "all righteousness." A key Matthean term — here it means doing what God's purposes require.
- v.16f: *ēneōchthēsan* (ἠνεῴχθησαν) — "were opened." Some manuscripts have the stronger *eschizō* ("torn apart"), echoing Isaiah 64:1.
- v.17g: *eudokēsa* (εὐδόκησα) — "I am well-pleased, I delight in." The voice echoes Psalm 2:7 and Isaiah 42:1, combining royal and servant imagery.
