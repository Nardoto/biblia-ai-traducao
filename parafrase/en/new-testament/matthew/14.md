# Matthew 14

**1** At that time, Herod the tetrarchᵃ heard reports about Jesus **2** and said to his servants, "This is John the Baptizer — he's been raised from the dead! That's why these powers are at work in him."

**3** Here's what had happened: Herod had arrested John, bound him, and thrown him in prison because of Herodias, his brother Philip's wife. **4** John had been telling him, "It's not lawful for you to have her."ᵇ **5** Herod wanted to kill him but was afraid of the people, because they regarded John as a prophet.

**6** On Herod's birthday, Herodias's daughter danced before the guests, and Herod was so delighted **7** that he promised with an oath to give her whatever she asked for. **8** Prompted by her mother, she said, "Give me the head of John the Baptizer on a platter."

**9** The king was grieved, but because of his oaths and his dinner guests, he ordered it done. **10** He sent and had John beheaded in the prison. **11** His head was brought on a platter and given to the girl, who carried it to her mother. **12** John's disciples came, took his body, and buried it. Then they went and told Jesus.

**13** When Jesus heard this, he withdrew by boat to a remote place to be alone. But the crowds found out and followed him on foot from the towns. **14** When he stepped ashore, he saw the huge crowd, and his heart broke for them,ᶜ and he healed their sick.

**15** As evening approached, the disciples came to him and said, "This is a remote place, and it's getting late. Send the crowds away so they can go to the villages and buy food for themselves."

**16** But Jesus said, "They don't need to leave. You give them something to eat."

**17** "We've only got five loaves and two fish here," they said.

**18** "Bring them to me," he said. **19** He told the crowds to sit down on the grass. Taking the five loaves and two fish, he looked up to heaven, spoke a blessing,ᵈ broke the loaves, and gave them to the disciples. The disciples gave them to the crowds. **20** Everyone ate and was satisfied. They picked up twelve baskets full of leftover pieces. **21** The number of those who ate was about five thousand men, not counting women and children.

**22** Immediately, Jesus made the disciples get into the boat and go ahead of him to the other side, while he dismissed the crowds. **23** After sending them away, he went up on the mountain by himself to pray. When evening came, he was there alone.

**24** By this time, the boat was a considerable distance from shore, being battered by the waves because the wind was against them.ᵉ

**25** During the fourth watch of the nightᶠ Jesus came toward them, walking on the sea. **26** When the disciples saw him walking on the sea, they were terrified. "It's a ghost!"ᵍ they said, and cried out in fear.

**27** But Jesus immediately spoke to them: "Take courage! It's me.ʰ Don't be afraid."

**28** Peter answered him, "Lord, if it's really you, command me to come to you on the water."

**29** "Come," he said.

Peter got out of the boat and walked on the water toward Jesus. **30** But when he noticed the strong wind, he got scared and began to sink. "Lord, save me!" he cried.

**31** Immediately Jesus reached out his hand and caught him. "You have so little faith," he said. "Why did you doubt?"ⁱ

**32** When they climbed into the boat, the wind died down. **33** Those in the boat worshiped him and said, "You really are God's Son!"

**34** They crossed over and landed at Gennesaret. **35** When the men of that place recognized him, they sent word throughout the surrounding region. People brought him all who were sick **36** and begged him to let them just touch the edge of his cloak. And everyone who touched it was completely healed.

## Notes

- v.1a: *tetraarchēs* (τετραάρχης) — "ruler of a quarter." Herod Antipas governed Galilee and Perea but lacked the title "king" that his father Herod the Great held.
- v.4b: Herodias had been married to Herod's half-brother Philip. Leviticus 18:16 and 20:21 forbid marrying a brother's wife (while the brother lives). John's boldness cost him his life.
- v.14c: *esplagchnisthē* (ἐσπλαγχνίσθη) — that same gut-level compassion (see 9:36). Even in his own grief over John's death, Jesus is moved by the crowd's need.
- v.19d: *eulogēsen* (εὐλόγησεν) — "blessed/spoke a blessing." The standard Jewish blessing over bread: *Baruch atah Adonai...* This miracle echoes God feeding Israel with manna and Elisha's multiplication of food (2 Kings 4:42-44).
- v.24e: Some manuscripts read "many stadia from the land" (a stadion was about 600 feet).
- v.25f: The fourth watch was 3:00-6:00 AM, using the Roman system of four night watches.
- v.26g: *phantasma* (φάντασμα) — "apparition, phantom." The disciples' first instinct was supernatural terror.
- v.27h: *ego eimi* (ἐγώ εἰμι) — "It is I" or "I am." This phrase echoes God's self-identification in Exodus 3:14 and Isaiah 43:10-11.
- v.31i: *distazō* (διστάζω) — "to be in two minds, to waver." Only used in Matthew (here and 28:17). Peter's faith was real but divided.
