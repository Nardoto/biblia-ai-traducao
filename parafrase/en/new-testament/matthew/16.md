# Matthew 16

**1** The Pharisees and Sadducees came and tested Jesus by asking him to show them a sign from heaven.

**2** He answered, "When evening comes, you say, 'Good weather ahead — the sky is red.' **3** And in the morning, 'Storms today — the sky is red and threatening.' You know how to read the sky but you can't read the signs of the times!ᵃ **4** A wicked and unfaithful generation looks for a sign, but no sign will be given to it except the sign of Jonah." Then he left them and went away.

**5** When the disciples crossed to the other side, they'd forgotten to bring bread. **6** Jesus said to them, "Watch out — beware of the yeast of the Pharisees and Sadducees."

**7** They discussed this among themselves: "It's because we didn't bring bread."

**8** Aware of this, Jesus said, "You have so little faith! Why are you talking among yourselves about not having bread? **9** Don't you understand yet? Don't you remember the five loaves for the five thousand, and how many baskets you collected? **10** Or the seven loaves for the four thousand, and how many baskets you collected? **11** How can you not understand that I wasn't talking to you about bread? Beware of the yeast of the Pharisees and Sadducees!" **12** Then they understood — he wasn't warning them about yeast in bread but about the teaching of the Pharisees and Sadducees.

**13** When Jesus arrived in the region of Caesarea Philippi,ᵇ he asked his disciples, "Who do people say the Son of Man is?"

**14** They said, "Some say John the Baptizer, others say Elijah, others say Jeremiah or one of the prophets."

**15** "But you," he said, "who do you say I am?"

**16** Simon Peter answered, "You're the Messiah, the Son of the living God."ᶜ

**17** Jesus replied, "You're fortunate, Simon son of Jonah, because flesh and blood didn't reveal this to you — my Father in heaven did. **18** And I tell you, you are Peter,ᵈ and on this rock I'll build my community,ᵉ and the gates of Hadesᶠ won't overpower it. **19** I'll give you the keys of the kingdom of heaven. Whatever you bind on earth will have been bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will have been loosed in heaven."ᵍ

**20** Then he strictly ordered the disciples not to tell anyone that he was the Messiah.

**21** From that point on, Jesus began to explain to his disciples that he had to go to Jerusalem and suffer many things at the hands of the elders, chief priests, and legal scholars — be killed, and on the third day be raised.

**22** Peter pulled him aside and began to rebuke him. "God forbid, Lord!ʰ This will never happen to you!"

**23** But Jesus turned and said to Peter, "Get behind me, Satan! You're a stumbling block to me. You're not thinking God's thoughts — you're thinking human thoughts."

**24** Then Jesus told his disciples, "If anyone wants to come after me, they must deny themselves, take up their cross, and follow me. **25** Whoever wants to save their life will lose it, but whoever loses their life for my sake will find it.ⁱ **26** What good is it if someone gains the whole world but forfeits their soul? Or what can anyone give in exchange for their soul?

**27** "The Son of Man is going to come in his Father's glory with his angels, and then he'll repay each person according to what they've done. **28** I'm telling you the truth, some standing here won't taste death before they see the Son of Man coming in his kingdom."

## Notes

- v.3a: *sēmeia tōn kairōn* (σημεῖα τῶν καιρῶν) — "signs of the times." *Kairos* means a decisive, opportune moment — not just chronological time (*chronos*).
- v.13b: Caesarea Philippi was a pagan city at the foot of Mount Hermon, near a grotto dedicated to Pan. The setting — deep in Gentile territory near a pagan shrine — makes Peter's confession all the more dramatic.
- v.16c: *ho Christos ho huios tou theou tou zōntos* (ὁ Χριστὸς ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ θεοῦ τοῦ ζῶντος) — the fullest confession in Matthew, combining two titles: Messiah and Son of the living God.
- v.18d: *Petros* (Πέτρος) — "Rock/Stone." A wordplay: Peter (*Petros*) and this rock (*petra*). The meaning of "on this rock" has been debated for centuries — Peter himself, Peter's confession, or Christ.
- v.18e: *ekklēsia* (ἐκκλησία) — usually translated "church," but it literally means "assembly, gathering, called-out community." It's the word the Septuagint uses for Israel's assembly. Matthew is the only Gospel to use this term.
- v.18f: *pylai hadou* (πύλαι ᾅδου) — "gates of Hades." Gates are defensive structures. The image suggests Jesus's community will be on the offensive, and death itself won't be able to resist.
- v.19g: The verb tenses are significant — *estai dedemenon/lelymenon* are future perfect passives: "will have been bound/loosed." Heaven's decision precedes and authorizes the earthly action.
- v.22h: *hileōs soi* (ἵλεώς σοι) — literally "mercy to you!" or "God be gracious to you!" — an expression of horror at what Jesus is describing.
- v.25i: *psychē* (ψυχή) — translated both "life" and "soul" in this passage. It means the whole self, one's essential being.
