# Matthew 13

**1** That same day, Jesus left the house and sat down beside the sea. **2** Such huge crowds gathered around him that he got into a boat and sat down, while the whole crowd stood on the shore. **3** Then he told them many things using parables:ᵃ

"A farmer went out to sow his seed. **4** As he was scattering it, some fell along the path, and the birds came and ate it up. **5** Some fell on rocky ground, where it didn't have much soil. It sprouted quickly because the soil was shallow. **6** But when the sun came up, it was scorched, and since it had no roots, it withered away. **7** Some fell among thorns, and the thorns grew up and choked it out. **8** But some fell on good soil and produced a crop — some a hundred times what was sown, some sixty, some thirty. **9** Whoever has ears, listen!"

**10** The disciples came to him and asked, "Why do you speak to them in parables?"

**11** He answered, "You've been given the ability to understand the mysteriesᵇ of the kingdom of heaven, but they haven't. **12** Whoever has will be given more, and they'll have plenty. But whoever doesn't have — even what they have will be taken from them. **13** That's why I speak to them in parables: because they look but don't see, they listen but don't hear or understand.

**14** "Isaiah's prophecy is being fulfilled in them: 'You'll listen and listen but never understand. You'll look and look but never see. **15** This people's heart has grown dull. Their ears barely hear. They've shut their eyes — so they won't see with their eyes, hear with their ears, understand with their hearts, and turn around so I could heal them.'ᶜ

**16** "But how fortunate are your eyes, because they see, and your ears, because they hear! **17** I'm telling you the truth, many prophets and righteous people longed to see what you're seeing but didn't see it, and to hear what you're hearing but didn't hear it.

**18** "So listen to what the parable of the farmer means: **19** When anyone hears the message about the kingdom and doesn't understand it, the evil one comes and snatches away what was planted in their heart. This is the seed sown along the path. **20** The seed on the rocky ground — that's the person who hears the message and immediately receives it with joy. **21** But they have no root in themselves; they're short-lived. When trouble or persecution comes because of the message, they immediately fall away. **22** The seed among the thorns — that's the person who hears the message, but the worries of this life and the seduction of wealthᵈ choke the message out, and it becomes unfruitful. **23** But the seed on good soil — that's the person who hears the message and understands it. They produce fruit: some a hundred, some sixty, some thirty times what was sown."

**24** He put another parable before them: "The kingdom of heaven is like a man who sowed good seed in his field. **25** But while people were sleeping, his enemy came, sowed weedsᵉ among the wheat, and left. **26** When the plants sprouted and produced grain, the weeds appeared too.

**27** "The owner's servants came to him and said, 'Master, didn't you sow good seed in your field? Where did the weeds come from?'

**28** "'An enemy did this,' he said.

"The servants asked him, 'Do you want us to go pull them up?'

**29** "'No,' he said, 'because while you're pulling up the weeds, you might uproot the wheat along with them. **30** Let both grow together until the harvest. At harvest time I'll tell the reapers: First collect the weeds and tie them in bundles to be burned, then gather the wheat into my barn.'"

**31** He put another parable before them: "The kingdom of heaven is like a mustard seed that someone took and planted in their field. **32** It's the smallest of all seeds,ᶠ but when it's grown, it's the largest of garden plants and becomes a tree, so that the birds come and nest in its branches."

**33** He told them another parable: "The kingdom of heaven is like yeast that a woman took and mixed into a large amount of flour until it worked all the way through."ᵍ

**34** Jesus told the crowds all these things in parables. He didn't tell them anything without using a parable — **35** so that what was spoken through the prophet would come true: "I'll open my mouth in parables; I'll declare things hidden since the foundation of the world."ʰ

**36** Then he left the crowds and went into the house. His disciples came to him and said, "Explain the parable of the weeds in the field to us."

**37** He answered: "The one who sows the good seed is the Son of Man. **38** The field is the world. The good seed — those are the children of the kingdom. The weeds are the children of the evil one. **39** The enemy who sowed them is the devil. The harvest is the end of the age,ⁱ and the reapers are angels.

**40** "Just as the weeds are collected and burned in the fire, that's how it'll be at the end of the age. **41** The Son of Man will send his angels, and they'll weed out of his kingdom everything that causes sin and all who practice lawlessness. **42** They'll throw them into the blazing furnace, where there'll be weeping and grinding of teeth. **43** Then the righteous will shine like the sun in their Father's kingdom. Whoever has ears, listen!

**44** "The kingdom of heaven is like a treasure hidden in a field. When a man found it, he hid it again, and in his joy went and sold everything he had and bought that field.

**45** "Also, the kingdom of heaven is like a merchant searching for fine pearls. **46** When he found one pearl of extraordinary value, he went and sold everything he had and bought it.

**47** "Also, the kingdom of heaven is like a net thrown into the sea that caught every kind of fish. **48** When it was full, the fishermen hauled it up on shore. They sat down and sorted the good fish into containers but threw out the bad ones. **49** That's how it'll be at the end of the age. The angels will come and separate the wicked from the righteous **50** and throw them into the blazing furnace, where there'll be weeping and grinding of teeth.

**51** "Have you understood all this?" Jesus asked.

"Yes," they said.

**52** He said to them, "That's why every legal scholar who's been trained for the kingdom of heaven is like the owner of a house who brings out of his storeroom both new treasures and old."

**53** When Jesus had finished these parables, he moved on from there. **54** He came to his hometown and began teaching in their synagogue. They were stunned. "Where did this man get this wisdom and these powerful works?" they asked. **55** "Isn't this the carpenter's son? Isn't his mother called Mary? Aren't James, Joseph, Simon, and Judas his brothers? **56** And aren't all his sisters here with us? So where did he get all this?" **57** And they were offended by him.ʲ

But Jesus told them, "A prophet isn't without honor except in his hometown and in his own household." **58** And he didn't do many powerful works there because of their lack of faith.

## Notes

- v.3a: *parabolē* (παραβολή) — from *para* ("alongside") + *ballō* ("throw"). A parable places two things side by side for comparison. Matthew 13 is the great "Parable Discourse," the third of Jesus's five major teaching blocks in this Gospel.
- v.11b: *mystēria* (μυστήρια) — not "mysteries" in the modern sense of puzzles, but truths previously hidden that are now being revealed to insiders.
- v.15c: Quoting Isaiah 6:9-10. The point is not that parables prevent understanding but that they reveal the condition of the heart.
- v.22d: *apatē tou ploutou* (ἀπάτη τοῦ πλούτου) — "the deceitfulness/seduction of wealth." Wealth actively misleads.
- v.25e: *zizania* (ζιζάνια) — darnel, a toxic weed that looks almost identical to wheat until the grain forms. Separating them early would damage the wheat.
- v.32f: The mustard seed was proverbially the smallest seed in Palestinian agriculture. The point is contrast: from the tiniest beginning, the kingdom grows enormous.
- v.33g: *zymē* (ζύμη) — "leaven/yeast." Three measures of flour is about 40 pounds — enough bread for over 100 people. The kingdom's transformative power is hidden but pervasive.
- v.35h: Quoting Psalm 78:2.
- v.39i: *synteleia tou aiōnos* (συντέλεια τοῦ αἰῶνος) — "the completion/consummation of the age." A phrase unique to Matthew.
- v.57j: *eskandalizonto* (ἐσκανδαλίζοντο) — "were scandalized, tripped up, caused to stumble." His ordinariness was an obstacle to recognizing who he was.
