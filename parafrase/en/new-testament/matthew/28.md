# Matthew 28

**1** After the Sabbath, at dawn on the first day of the week, Mary Magdalene and the other Mary went to look at the tomb.

**2** Suddenly there was a violent earthquake. A messenger of the Lordᵃ came down from heaven, went to the stone, rolled it back, and sat on it. **3** His appearance was like lightning, and his clothes were white as snow. **4** The guards shook with fear and became like dead men.

**5** The messenger said to the women, "Don't be afraid. I know you're looking for Jesus, who was crucified. **6** He's not here — he's been raised, just as he said! Come see the place where he was lying. **7** Then go quickly and tell his disciples: 'He's been raised from the dead and is going ahead of you to Galilee. You'll see him there.' That's my message to you."

**8** The women left the tomb quickly — with fear and overwhelming joy — and ran to tell his disciples. **9** Suddenly Jesus met them. "Greetings!"ᵇ he said. They came to him, took hold of his feet, and worshiped him.

**10** Then Jesus said to them, "Don't be afraid. Go tell my brothers to go to Galilee. They'll see me there."

**11** While the women were on their way, some of the guards went into the city and reported everything that had happened to the chief priests. **12** After the priests met with the elders and came up with a plan, they gave the soldiers a large sum of money **13** and told them, "Say, 'His disciples came during the night and stole him while we were sleeping.' **14** If the governor hears about this, we'll take care of it and keep you out of trouble." **15** The soldiers took the money and did as they were instructed. And this story has been widely circulated among Jewish people to this day.ᶜ

**16** The eleven disciples went to Galilee, to the mountain where Jesus had told them to go. **17** When they saw him, they worshiped him — though some doubted.ᵈ

**18** Jesus came to them and said, "All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me.ᵉ **19** So go and make disciplesᶠ of all nations, immersingᵍ them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, **20** teaching them to observe everything I've commanded you. And remember — I am with you every single day, until the end of the age."ʰ

## Notes

- v.2a: *angelos kyriou* (ἄγγελος κυρίου) — "angel/messenger of the Lord." The same phrase from 1:20 that opened the Gospel now appears near its close.
- v.9b: *chairete* (χαίρετε) — "Rejoice!" or simply "Hello!" An ordinary greeting that's extraordinary in context. The risen Jesus's first word to his followers is joy.
- v.15c: Matthew includes this counter-narrative to address a circulating objection within his community. The detail that guards were bribed actually serves as evidence — it confirms the tomb was empty, since even opponents didn't claim the body was still there.
- v.17d: *hoi de edistasan* (οἱ δὲ ἐδίστασαν) — "but some doubted/hesitated." Remarkably, Matthew doesn't hide the fact that even in the presence of the risen Jesus, some wavered. *Distazō* is the same word used of Peter sinking in the water (14:31).
- v.18e: *exousia* (ἐξουσία) — "authority, power." This echoes Daniel 7:14, where the Son of Man receives universal dominion. The claim is cosmic in scope.
- v.19f: *mathēteusate* (μαθητεύσατε) — "make disciples." This is the main verb of the Great Commission — not "go" (which is a participle). The core command is to create learners/followers.
- v.19g: *baptizontes* (βαπτίζοντες) — "immersing." The trinitarian formula ("in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit") is unique to this passage and represents the fullest articulation of God's triune identity in the Gospels.
- v.20h: *egō meth' hymōn eimi pasas tas hēmeras* (ἐγὼ μεθ᾽ ὑμῶν εἰμι πάσας τὰς ἡμέρας) — "I am with you all the days." The Gospel that began with "Immanuel — God with us" (1:23) ends with the same promise. *Egō eimi* ("I am") echoes the divine name. The story that started with God's presence in a baby ends with God's presence until the end of the world.
