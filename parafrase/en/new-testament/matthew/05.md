# Matthew 5

**1** When Jesus saw the crowds, he went up on the mountainside and sat down. His disciples came to him, **2** and he began to teach them:

**3** "How fortunateᵃ are those who know they're spiritually bankrupt — the kingdom of heaven belongs to them.
**4** How fortunate are those who mourn — they'll be comforted.
**5** How fortunate are the gentle — they'll inherit the earth.ᵇ
**6** How fortunate are those who hunger and thirst for things to be made right — they'll be completely satisfied.
**7** How fortunate are those who show mercy — they'll receive mercy.
**8** How fortunate are those whose hearts are pure — they'll see God.
**9** How fortunate are the peacemakers — they'll be called God's children.
**10** How fortunate are those who are persecuted for doing what's right — the kingdom of heaven belongs to them.

**11** "You're fortunate when people insult you, persecute you, and falsely say every kind of evil thing about you because of me. **12** Celebrate! Jump for joy! Your reward in heaven is enormous. They treated the prophets who came before you the same way.

**13** "You're the salt of the earth. But if salt loses its flavor, how can it be made salty again? It's no good for anything except to be thrown out and walked on.

**14** "You're the light of the world. A city built on a hilltop can't be hidden. **15** Nobody lights a lamp and then puts it under a basket. They set it on a stand so it lights up the whole house. **16** In the same way, let your light shine in front of people so they can see the good things you do and give credit to your Father in heaven.

**17** "Don't think I've come to tear down the Torah or the Prophets.ᶜ I haven't come to tear them down — I've come to bring them to their full meaning. **18** I tell you the truth: until heaven and earth pass away, not a single letter, not a single pen stroke will disappear from the Torah until everything it points to has happened. **19** So anyone who sets aside even the smallest of these commands and teaches others to do the same will be called least in the kingdom of heaven. But whoever practices them and teaches them — that person will be called great in the kingdom of heaven. **20** I'm telling you, unless your way of doing what's rightᵈ goes way beyond what the legal scholars and Pharisees do, you won't enter the kingdom of heaven at all.

**21** "You've heard it was said to people long ago, 'Don't murder,' and anyone who murders will face judgment. **22** But I'm telling you, anyone who stays angry with a brother or sister will face judgment. Anyone who calls someone 'Worthless!'ᵉ will answer to the council. And anyone who says 'You fool!' is in danger of the fire of Gehenna.ᶠ

**23** "So if you're bringing your offering to the altar and there you remember that your brother or sister has something against you, **24** leave your offering right there in front of the altar. Go make things right with them first, and then come back and present your offering.

**25** "Settle matters quickly with someone who's taking you to court. Do it while you're still on the way there, or they might hand you over to the judge, and the judge to the officer, and you'll be thrown into prison. **26** I'm telling you the truth — you won't get out until you've paid every last cent.

**27** "You've heard it was said, 'Don't commit adultery.' **28** But I'm telling you, anyone who looks at a woman with the intention of lusting after herᵍ has already committed adultery with her in his heart. **29** If your right eye is causing you to stumble, tear it out and throw it away. It's better for you to lose one part of your body than for your whole body to be thrown into Gehenna. **30** And if your right hand is causing you to stumble, cut it off and throw it away. Better to lose one part than for your whole body to end up in Gehenna.

**31** "It was said, 'Anyone who divorces his wife must give her a certificate of divorce.'ʰ **32** But I'm telling you, anyone who divorces his wife — except in a case of sexual unfaithfulness — makes her a victim of adultery. And anyone who marries a divorced woman commits adultery.

**33** "Again, you've heard it was said to people long ago, 'Don't break your oaths, but keep the promises you've made to the Lord.' **34** But I'm telling you, don't swear oaths at all — not by heaven, because it's God's throne; **35** not by earth, because it's his footstool; not by Jerusalem, because it's the city of the great King. **36** Don't even swear by your own head, because you can't make a single hair white or black. **37** Just let your 'yes' be yes and your 'no' be no. Anything more than that comes from the evil one.

**38** "You've heard it was said, 'An eye for an eye and a tooth for a tooth.' **39** But I'm telling you, don't resist an evil person with force.ⁱ If someone slaps you on the right cheek, turn the other one to them too. **40** If someone wants to sue you and take your shirt, let them have your coat as well. **41** If someone forces you to carry their load for one mile, carry it two. **42** Give to anyone who asks you, and don't turn away from someone who wants to borrow from you.

**43** "You've heard it was said, 'Love your neighbor and hate your enemy.' **44** But I'm telling you, love your enemies and pray for those who persecute you, **45** so that you'll be children of your Father in heaven. He makes his sun rise on evil people and good people alike, and sends rain on those who do right and those who don't. **46** If you only love those who love you, what reward is that? Even the tax collectors do that. **47** And if you only greet your own people, what are you doing that's extraordinary? Even the pagans do that. **48** So be complete,ʲ just as your heavenly Father is complete.

---

## Notes

- v.3a: *makarioi* (μακάριοι) — often translated "blessed." It describes a state of deep well-being and divine favor, not mere happiness. "Fortunate" captures the sense without the religious baggage.
- v.5b: *praeis* (πραεῖς) — not "weak" but controlled strength, gentleness from a position of power. Echoes Psalm 37:11.
- v.17c: *katalysai* (καταλῦσαι) — "to demolish, destroy, tear down." Jesus is not abolishing the Torah but *plēroō* (πληρόω) — bringing it to fullness.
- v.20d: *dikaiosynē* (δικαιοσύνη) — "righteousness, justice." A central Matthean concept meaning living in right relationship with God and others.
- v.22e: *raka* (ῥακά) — an Aramaic insult meaning "empty-headed, worthless."
- v.22f: *geenna* (γέεννα) — Gehenna, the Valley of Hinnom south of Jerusalem, a burning rubbish dump that became a metaphor for ultimate destruction.
- v.28g: *pros to epithymēsai* (πρὸς τὸ ἐπιθυμῆσαι) — the grammar indicates deliberate intent, not involuntary attraction. It's about choosing to cultivate desire.
- v.31h: Referencing Deuteronomy 24:1.
- v.39i: *mē antistēnai tō ponērō* (μὴ ἀντιστῆναι τῷ πονηρῷ) — "don't resist the evil one/evil" by retaliating in kind. Not passivity, but a different kind of power.
- v.48j: *teleioi* (τέλειοι) — "complete, mature, whole" — not moral perfection but wholeness of character, being undivided in love.
