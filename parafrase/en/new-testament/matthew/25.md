# Matthew 25

**1** "Then the kingdom of heaven will be like ten bridesmaidsᵃ who took their lamps and went out to meet the groom. **2** Five of them were foolish and five were wise. **3** The foolish ones took their lamps but didn't bring extra oil. **4** But the wise ones brought flasks of oil along with their lamps.

**5** "When the groom was delayed, they all got drowsy and fell asleep. **6** At midnight, a shout rang out: 'Here's the groom! Come out to meet him!'

**7** "Then all the bridesmaids woke up and trimmed their lamps. **8** The foolish ones said to the wise, 'Give us some of your oil — our lamps are going out.'

**9** "'No,' the wise ones answered, 'there might not be enough for both us and you. Go to the dealers and buy some for yourselves.'

**10** "But while they were gone to buy oil, the groom arrived. Those who were ready went in with him to the wedding feast, and the door was shut.

**11** "Later, the other bridesmaids arrived. 'Lord, lord, open up for us!' they said.

**12** "But he answered, 'I'm telling you the truth — I don't know you.'

**13** "So stay alert, because you don't know the day or the hour.

**14** "It's also like a man going on a journey. He called his servants and entrusted his propertyᵇ to them. **15** To one he gave five talents, to another two, and to another one — each according to their ability.ᶜ Then he left.

**16** "The one who'd received five talents went right away and put them to work and earned five more. **17** The one with two talents did the same and earned two more. **18** But the one who'd received one talent went off, dug a hole in the ground, and hid his master's money.

**19** "After a long time, the master of those servants returned and settled accounts with them.

**20** "The one who'd received five talents came and presented the other five. 'Master,' he said, 'you entrusted me with five talents. Look — I've earned five more.'

**21** "His master told him, 'Well done, good and faithful servant! You were faithful with a little — I'll put you in charge of a lot. Come share your master's joy!'

**22** "The one with two talents also came. 'Master,' he said, 'you entrusted me with two talents. Look — I've earned two more.'

**23** "His master told him, 'Well done, good and faithful servant! You were faithful with a little — I'll put you in charge of a lot. Come share your master's joy!'

**24** "Then the one who'd received one talent came. 'Master,' he said, 'I knew you were a hard man — harvesting where you didn't plant, gathering where you didn't scatter seed. **25** I was afraid, so I went and hid your talent in the ground. Here — you have what's yours.'

**26** "His master answered, 'You wicked, lazy servant! You knew I harvest where I didn't plant and gather where I didn't scatter? **27** Then you should've at least deposited my money with the bankers, and when I returned I would've gotten it back with interest.

**28** "'Take the talent from him and give it to the one who has ten. **29** Because everyone who has will be given more, and they'll have an abundance. But the one who doesn't have — even what they have will be taken from them. **30** And throw this worthless servant outside into the darkness, where there'll be weeping and grinding of teeth.'

**31** "When the Son of Man comes in his glory, with all the angels with him, he'll sit on his glorious throne. **32** All the nations will be gathered before him, and he'll separate them from each other the way a shepherd separates the sheep from the goats.ᵈ **33** He'll place the sheep on his right and the goats on his left.

**34** "Then the King will say to those on his right, 'Come, you who are blessed by my Father — inherit the kingdom prepared for you since the creation of the world. **35** Because I was hungry and you gave me food. I was thirsty and you gave me a drink. I was a stranger and you took me in. **36** I was naked and you clothed me. I was sick and you looked after me. I was in prison and you visited me.'

**37** "Then the righteous will answer, 'Lord, when did we see you hungry and feed you, or thirsty and give you a drink? **38** When did we see you a stranger and take you in, or naked and clothe you? **39** When did we see you sick or in prison and visit you?'

**40** "The King will answer, 'I'm telling you the truth, whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers and sisters of mine, you did for me.'ᵉ

**41** "Then he'll say to those on his left, 'Get away from me, you who are cursed, into the eternal fire prepared for the devil and his angels.ᶠ **42** Because I was hungry and you didn't give me food. I was thirsty and you didn't give me a drink. **43** I was a stranger and you didn't take me in. I was naked and you didn't clothe me. I was sick and in prison and you didn't look after me.'

**44** "Then they'll also answer, 'Lord, when did we see you hungry or thirsty or a stranger or naked or sick or in prison and didn't help you?'

**45** "He'll answer, 'I'm telling you the truth, whatever you didn't do for one of the least of these, you didn't do for me.'

**46** "And they'll go away into eternal punishment,ᵍ but the righteous into eternal life."

## Notes

- v.1a: *parthenoi* (παρθένοι) — literally "virgins." These were young women who served as attendants at a wedding, part of the processional escort for the bridegroom.
- v.14b: *hyparchonta* (ὑπάρχοντα) — "possessions, property, assets." Everything belongs to the master; it's entrusted, not given.
- v.15c: *talanton* (τάλαντον) — a talent was an enormous sum: about 6,000 denarii, or roughly 20 years' wages for a laborer. Even one talent was a staggering amount of trust. Our English word "talent" (meaning ability/gift) comes from this parable.
- v.32d: In Palestinian flocks, sheep and goats often grazed together during the day but were separated at night. Sheep were generally more valuable.
- v.40e: *tōn elachistōn* (τῶν ἐλαχίστων) — "the least, the smallest." Whether "brothers and sisters of mine" means all suffering people or specifically Jesus's followers who are suffering is debated. Either way, Jesus identifies himself completely with those in need.
- v.41f: The eternal fire was "prepared for the devil and his angels" — not originally intended for human beings. The judgment is a consequence of choices, not predetermined for people.
- v.46g: *kolasin aiōnion* (κόλασιν αἰώνιον) — "eternal punishment." *Kolasis* originally meant "pruning" or "corrective punishment," though by New Testament times it had broadened in meaning. *Aiōnios* can mean "eternal" or "of the age to come" — its exact scope is a matter of significant theological discussion.
