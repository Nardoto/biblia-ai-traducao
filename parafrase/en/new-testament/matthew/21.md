# Matthew 21

**1** As they approached Jerusalem and came to Bethphage on the Mount of Olives, Jesus sent two disciples ahead, **2** telling them, "Go into the village ahead of you, and right away you'll find a donkey tied there, with her colt beside her. Untie them and bring them to me. **3** If anyone says anything to you, tell them, 'The Lord needs them,' and they'll send them right away."

**4** This happened so that what was spoken through the prophet would come true: **5** "Tell the daughter of Zion: 'Look, your king is coming to you, gentle and riding on a donkey — on a colt, the foal of a donkey.'"ᵃ

**6** The disciples went and did what Jesus told them. **7** They brought the donkey and the colt, placed their cloaks on them, and he sat on them. **8** A massive crowd spread their cloaks on the road, while others cut branches from the trees and spread them on the road. **9** The crowds going ahead of him and those following shouted:

"Hosanna to the Son of David!"ᵇ
"Blessed is the one who comes in the name of the Lord!"
"Hosanna in the highest heaven!"

**10** When he entered Jerusalem, the whole city was stirred up.ᶜ "Who is this?" they asked.

**11** The crowds said, "This is Jesus, the prophet from Nazareth of Galilee."

**12** Jesus entered the temple area and drove out everyone who was buying and selling there. He overturned the tables of the money changers and the seats of those selling doves.ᵈ **13** "It's written," he told them, "'My house will be called a house of prayer' — but you're making it 'a den of robbers!'"ᵉ

**14** Blind and lame people came to him in the temple, and he healed them. **15** But when the chief priests and legal scholars saw the amazing things he was doing and the children shouting in the temple, "Hosanna to the Son of David!" — they were indignant.

**16** "Do you hear what these children are saying?" they asked him.

"Yes," Jesus replied. "Haven't you ever read, 'From the mouths of children and nursing infants you've drawn out perfect praise'?"ᶠ

**17** Then he left them, went out of the city to Bethany, and spent the night there.

**18** Early in the morning, as he was heading back to the city, he was hungry. **19** Seeing a fig tree by the road, he went up to it but found nothing on it except leaves. He said to it, "May you never bear fruit again!" And immediately the fig tree withered.ᵍ

**20** When the disciples saw this, they were astonished. "How did the fig tree wither so quickly?" they asked.

**21** Jesus answered, "I'm telling you the truth, if you have faith and don't doubt, you'll not only do what was done to the fig tree — you could even say to this mountain, 'Be lifted up and thrown into the sea,' and it would happen. **22** Whatever you ask for in prayer with faith, you'll receive."

**23** When he entered the temple area, the chief priests and elders of the people came to him while he was teaching. "By what authority are you doing these things?" they asked. "And who gave you this authority?"

**24** Jesus answered, "I'll ask you one question too. If you answer me, I'll tell you by what authority I do these things. **25** Where did John's baptism come from — heaven or human beings?"

They debated among themselves: "If we say 'from heaven,' he'll ask, 'Then why didn't you believe him?' **26** But if we say 'from human beings' — we're afraid of the crowd, because they all regard John as a prophet."

**27** So they answered Jesus, "We don't know."

He said to them, "Then I'm not going to tell you by what authority I do these things either.

**28** "But what do you think? A man had two sons. He went to the first and said, 'Son, go work in the vineyard today.'

**29** "'I don't want to,' the son answered. But later he changed his mind and went.

**30** "He went to the other son and said the same thing. 'I will, sir,' he answered — but he didn't go.

**31** "Which of the two did what the father wanted?"

"The first," they said.

Jesus told them, "I'm telling you the truth, tax collectors and prostitutes are entering the kingdom of God ahead of you. **32** Because John came to you showing the way of righteousness, and you didn't believe him. But the tax collectors and prostitutes did. And even after you saw that, you still didn't change your minds and believe him.

**33** "Listen to another parable: There was a landowner who planted a vineyard. He put a wall around it, dug a winepress, built a watchtower,ʰ rented it out to tenant farmers, and went on a journey. **34** When harvest time came, he sent his servants to the tenants to collect his share of the fruit.

**35** "But the tenants seized his servants — beat one, killed another, stoned another. **36** He sent more servants than the first time, and they treated them the same way. **37** Finally, he sent his son to them. 'They'll respect my son,' he said.

**38** "But when the tenants saw the son, they said to each other, 'This is the heir! Come on, let's kill him and take his inheritance!' **39** They grabbed him, threw him out of the vineyard, and killed him.

**40** "So when the vineyard owner comes, what will he do to those tenants?"

**41** "He'll bring those wretches to a wretched end," they answered, "and rent the vineyard to other tenants who'll give him his share of the fruit at harvest time."

**42** Jesus said to them, "Haven't you ever read in the Scriptures: 'The stone the builders rejected has become the cornerstone.ⁱ This is the Lord's doing, and it's wonderful in our eyes'?

**43** "That's why I'm telling you, the kingdom of God will be taken from you and given to a people who produce its fruit. **44** Anyone who falls on this stone will be shattered, and anyone it falls on will be crushed."ʲ

**45** When the chief priests and Pharisees heard his parables, they knew he was talking about them. **46** They wanted to arrest him, but they were afraid of the crowds, who considered him a prophet.

## Notes

- v.5a: Quoting Zechariah 9:9 (with Isaiah 62:11). The king comes on a donkey, not a war horse — humble, not conquering by force.
- v.9b: *hōsanna* (ὡσαννά) — from Hebrew *hoshi'a na* (הושיעה נא), "Save, please!" Originally a prayer for deliverance (Psalm 118:25-26), it had become a shout of acclamation.
- v.10c: *eseisthē* (ἐσείσθη) — "was shaken, trembled." The same word used for earthquakes. Jerusalem quakes at Jesus's arrival, as it did at his birth (2:3).
- v.12d: Money changers converted foreign currency into the Tyrian shekel required for temple payments. Dove sellers provided sacrificial animals for the poor. The issue was exploitation, not commerce itself.
- v.13e: Combining Isaiah 56:7 and Jeremiah 7:11.
- v.16f: Quoting Psalm 8:2 (LXX).
- v.19g: The fig tree is widely understood as a prophetic sign-act representing Israel's failure to produce spiritual fruit despite outward religious appearance (leaves but no figs).
- v.33h: The vineyard imagery echoes Isaiah 5:1-7, where the vineyard is Israel. Jesus's audience would have recognized it immediately.
- v.42i: Quoting Psalm 118:22-23. *Kephalēn gōnias* (κεφαλὴν γωνίας) — "head of the corner," either a cornerstone (foundation) or capstone (crown of an arch).
- v.44j: This verse is absent in some important manuscripts and may be a later addition from Luke 20:18.
