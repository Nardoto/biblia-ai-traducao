# Matthew 12

**1** Around that time, Jesus was walking through the grain fields on the Sabbath. His disciples were hungry and began picking heads of grain and eating them. **2** When the Pharisees saw this, they said to him, "Look! Your disciples are doing what's not permitted on the Sabbath."ᵃ

**3** He answered, "Haven't you read what David did when he and his companions were hungry? **4** He entered the house of God and ate the sacred breadᵇ — which wasn't permitted for him or his companions, only for the priests. **5** Or haven't you read in the Torah that on the Sabbath the priests in the temple violate the Sabbath and yet are innocent? **6** I'm telling you, something greater than the temple is here.

**7** "If you'd understood what this means — 'I want mercy, not sacrifice'ᶜ — you wouldn't have condemned the innocent. **8** Because the Son of Man is Lord of the Sabbath."

**9** Moving on from there, he entered their synagogue. **10** A man with a withered hand was there. Looking for a way to accuse Jesus, they asked him, "Is it permitted to heal on the Sabbath?"

**11** He said to them, "If any of you has a sheep and it falls into a pit on the Sabbath, won't you grab it and pull it out? **12** How much more valuable is a person than a sheep! So yes — it is permitted to do good on the Sabbath."

**13** Then he said to the man, "Stretch out your hand." He stretched it out, and it was restored — as healthy as the other one. **14** But the Pharisees went out and plotted how to destroy him.

**15** Aware of this, Jesus withdrew from that place. Large crowds followed him, and he healed all of them. **16** He warned them not to make him publicly known — **17** so that what was spoken through the prophet Isaiah would come true:

**18** "Here is my servant, whom I've chosen, my beloved, in whom my soul delights. I'll put my Spirit on him, and he'll announce justice to the nations. **19** He won't argue or shout — no one will hear his voice in the streets. **20** He won't snap a bent reed or snuff out a smoldering wick, until he leads justice to victory. **21** And in his name, the nations will put their hope."ᵈ

**22** Then they brought him a man who was demonized, blind, and mute. Jesus healed him so that the man could both speak and see. **23** All the crowds were astounded. "Could this be the Son of David?" they asked.

**24** But when the Pharisees heard this, they said, "This man only drives out demons by Beelzebul, the ruler of demons."

**25** Jesus knew their thoughts and said to them, "Every kingdom divided against itself is destroyed. Every city or household divided against itself won't stand. **26** If Satan drives out Satan, he's divided against himself. So how will his kingdom stand? **27** And if I drive out demons by Beelzebul, by whom do your own people drive them out? That's why they'll be your judges. **28** But if I drive out demons by the Spirit of God, then the kingdom of God has caught up with you.ᵉ

**29** "Or how can anyone break into a strong man's house and steal his possessions unless they first tie up the strong man? Then they can ransack his house.

**30** "Whoever isn't with me is against me, and whoever doesn't gather with me scatters. **31** That's why I'm telling you: every sin and slander can be forgiven, but slander against the Spiritᶠ won't be forgiven. **32** Anyone who speaks against the Son of Man can be forgiven. But anyone who speaks against the Holy Spirit won't be forgiven — not in this age or the one to come.

**33** "Either make the tree good and its fruit good, or make the tree rotten and its fruit rotten — because a tree is known by its fruit. **34** You brood of vipers! How can you say anything good when you're evil? The mouth speaks from whatever fills the heart. **35** A good person brings good things out of a good storehouse, and an evil person brings evil things out of an evil storehouse. **36** I'm telling you, on the day of judgment people will give an account for every careless word they've spoken. **37** Because by your words you'll be declared righteous, and by your words you'll be condemned."

**38** Then some of the legal scholars and Pharisees said to him, "Teacher, we want to see a sign from you."

**39** He answered, "A wicked and unfaithful generation demands a sign, but no sign will be given to it except the sign of the prophet Jonah. **40** Just as Jonah was in the belly of the great fish for three days and three nights, the Son of Man will be in the heart of the earth for three days and three nights.ᵍ **41** The people of Nineveh will stand up at the judgment alongside this generation and condemn it, because they turned their lives around at Jonah's preaching — and look, something greater than Jonah is here. **42** The Queen of the South will rise up at the judgment alongside this generation and condemn it, because she came from the ends of the earth to hear Solomon's wisdom — and look, something greater than Solomon is here.

**43** "When an unclean spirit leaves a person, it wanders through dry places looking for rest and doesn't find it. **44** Then it says, 'I'll go back to my house that I left.' When it arrives, it finds the place unoccupied, swept clean, and put in order. **45** Then it goes and brings along seven other spirits more evil than itself, and they move in and settle there. And the final condition of that person is worse than the first. That's how it'll be with this evil generation."

**46** While he was still speaking to the crowds, his mother and brothers were standing outside, wanting to talk to him. **47** Someone told him, "Your mother and brothers are standing outside, wanting to speak with you."

**48** But he replied to the one who told him, "Who is my mother? And who are my brothers?" **49** Pointing to his disciples, he said, "Here are my mother and my brothers. **50** Whoever does the will of my Father in heaven — that person is my brother and sister and mother."

## Notes

- v.2a: Picking grain was classified as "harvesting" under Pharisaic Sabbath rules — one of 39 categories of prohibited work.
- v.4b: *artous tēs protheseōs* (ἄρτους τῆς προθέσεως) — "bread of the Presence/showbread," the twelve loaves placed before God in the tabernacle (1 Samuel 21:1-6).
- v.7c: Hosea 6:6 again — a favorite text of Matthew's Jesus, quoted here for the second time (cf. 9:13).
- v.18-21d: The longest Old Testament quotation in Matthew, from Isaiah 42:1-4 (the first Servant Song). It emphasizes gentleness and hope for the nations.
- v.28e: *ephthasen eph' hymas* (ἔφθασεν ἐφ᾽ ὑμᾶς) — "has arrived upon you, has caught up with you." The kingdom isn't just near — it's already here in Jesus's actions.
- v.31f: *blasphēmia tou pneumatos* (βλασφημία τοῦ πνεύματος) — "blasphemy against the Spirit." In context, this means deliberately attributing the Spirit's work to Satan — a total, willful rejection of God's evident activity, not an accidental sin.
- v.40g: Jesus draws a parallel between Jonah's time in the fish and his own coming death and resurrection.
