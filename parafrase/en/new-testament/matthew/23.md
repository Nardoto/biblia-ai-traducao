# Matthew 23

**1** Then Jesus spoke to the crowds and to his disciples: **2** "The legal scholars and the Pharisees sit in Moses's seat.ᵃ **3** So do and observe everything they tell you — but don't do what they do, because they don't practice what they preach. **4** They tie up heavy, crushing loads and put them on people's shoulders, but they won't lift a finger to move them.

**5** "Everything they do is for show. They make their phylacteriesᵇ wide and the tassels on their garments long. **6** They love the place of honor at banquets, the best seats in the synagogues, **7** greetings in the marketplaces, and being called 'Rabbi'ᶜ by people.

**8** "But you — don't be called 'Rabbi,' because you have one Teacher, and you're all brothers and sisters. **9** And don't call anyone on earth 'father,' because you have one Father — the one in heaven. **10** And don't be called 'instructors,' because you have one Instructor — the Messiah. **11** The greatest among you will be your servant. **12** Whoever exalts themselves will be humbled, and whoever humbles themselves will be exalted.

**13** "How terrible for you, legal scholars and Pharisees, you hypocrites!ᵈ You slam the door of the kingdom of heaven in people's faces. You don't go in yourselves, and you won't let those trying to enter go in.

**15** "How terrible for you, legal scholars and Pharisees, you hypocrites! You cross sea and land to make a single convert,ᵉ and when you succeed, you make them twice the child of Gehenna you are.

**16** "How terrible for you, blind guides! You say, 'If anyone swears by the temple, it doesn't count. But if anyone swears by the gold of the temple, they're bound by their oath.' **17** You blind fools! Which is greater — the gold, or the temple that makes the gold sacred? **18** You also say, 'If anyone swears by the altar, it doesn't count. But if anyone swears by the offering on the altar, they're bound.' **19** You blind ones! Which is greater — the offering, or the altar that makes the offering sacred? **20** So whoever swears by the altar swears by it and by everything on it. **21** Whoever swears by the temple swears by it and by the one who dwells in it. **22** And whoever swears by heaven swears by God's throne and by the one who sits on it.

**23** "How terrible for you, legal scholars and Pharisees, you hypocrites! You tithe your mint, dill, and cuminᶠ — but you've neglected the weightier matters of the Torah: justice, mercy, and faithfulness. You should've practiced these without neglecting those. **24** Blind guides! You strain out a gnat but swallow a camel!ᵍ

**25** "How terrible for you, legal scholars and Pharisees, you hypocrites! You clean the outside of the cup and dish, but inside they're full of greed and self-indulgence. **26** Blind Pharisee! First clean the inside of the cup, and then the outside will be clean too.

**27** "How terrible for you, legal scholars and Pharisees, you hypocrites! You're like whitewashed tombsʰ — beautiful on the outside but full of dead bones and every kind of uncleanness inside. **28** In the same way, you appear righteous to people on the outside, but inside you're full of hypocrisy and lawlessness.

**29** "How terrible for you, legal scholars and Pharisees, you hypocrites! You build tombs for the prophets and decorate the monuments of the righteous. **30** You say, 'If we'd lived in our ancestors' day, we wouldn't have joined them in shedding the prophets' blood.' **31** So you testify against yourselves that you're the descendants of those who murdered the prophets. **32** Go ahead, then — finish what your ancestors started!

**33** "You snakes! You brood of vipers! How will you escape the judgment of Gehenna?

**34** "That's why — look — I'm sending you prophets, wise people, and scholars.ⁱ Some of them you'll kill and crucify. Others you'll flog in your synagogues and chase from town to town. **35** So all the righteous blood shed on earth will fall on you — from the blood of righteous Abel to the blood of Zechariah son of Berechiah, whom you murdered between the temple and the altar.ʲ **36** I'm telling you the truth, all of this will fall on this generation.

**37** "Jerusalem, Jerusalem — the city that kills the prophets and stones those sent to her! How often I wanted to gather your children together the way a hen gathers her chicks under her wings — but you weren't willing!ᵏ **38** Look — your house is left to you abandoned.ˡ **39** I'm telling you, you won't see me again until you say, 'Blessed is the one who comes in the name of the Lord.'"

## Notes

- v.2a: *kathedras Mōyseōs* (καθέδρας Μωϋσέως) — "Moses's seat/chair." A physical seat in the synagogue from which Torah was read and taught. They occupy a legitimate teaching position.
- v.5b: *phylaktēria* (φυλακτήρια) — small leather boxes containing Torah verses, worn on the forehead and left arm during prayer (based on Deuteronomy 6:8). The criticism is about showing off, not the practice itself.
- v.7c: *rhabbi* (ῥαββί) — "my great one," an Aramaic title of honor for Torah teachers.
- v.13d: *ouai* (οὐαί) — "woe, how terrible." Not just anger but grief — a prophetic lament. The seven "woes" parallel and invert the eight "blesseds" of chapter 5.
- v.15e: *prosēlytos* (προσήλυτος) — a Gentile convert to Judaism who underwent circumcision and full Torah observance.
- v.23f: These are tiny garden herbs. Tithing them represents extreme legal precision about minor matters while ignoring the Torah's core concerns.
- v.24g: In Aramaic, "gnat" (*galma*) and "camel" (*gamla*) sound almost identical — this is likely a deliberate wordplay.
- v.27h: Tombs were whitewashed before Passover so people wouldn't accidentally touch them and become ritually unclean. The whitewash made them look clean while marking them as sources of defilement.
- v.34i: Where Luke 11:49 attributes this to "the Wisdom of God," Matthew has Jesus himself sending the prophets — a remarkable self-identification with divine Wisdom.
- v.35j: Abel is the first murder in Genesis; Zechariah's murder (2 Chronicles 24:20-21) is the last in the Hebrew Bible's arrangement (Chronicles is the final book). Together they span the entire Old Testament.
- v.37k: The image of a mother hen protecting her chicks is a powerful feminine metaphor for God's care — and the tragedy of its rejection.
- v.38l: *oikos* (οἶκος) — "house," meaning the temple. This echoes Jeremiah 12:7 and 22:5.
