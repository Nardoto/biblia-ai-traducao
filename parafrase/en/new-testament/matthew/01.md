# Matthew 1

**1** This is the family record of Jesus the Messiah,ᵃ son of David, son of Abraham:

**2** Abraham was the father of Isaac, Isaac the father of Jacob, Jacob the father of Judah and his brothers. **3** Judah was the father of Perez and Zerah (their mother was Tamar), Perez the father of Hezron, Hezron the father of Ram. **4** Ram was the father of Amminadab, Amminadab the father of Nahshon, Nahshon the father of Salmon. **5** Salmon was the father of Boaz (his mother was Rahab), Boaz the father of Obed (his mother was Ruth), Obed the father of Jesse. **6** Jesse was the father of King David.

David was the father of Solomon (his mother had been Uriah's wife),ᵇ **7** Solomon the father of Rehoboam, Rehoboam the father of Abijah, Abijah the father of Asa. **8** Asa was the father of Jehoshaphat, Jehoshaphat the father of Joram, Joram the father of Uzziah. **9** Uzziah was the father of Jotham, Jotham the father of Ahaz, Ahaz the father of Hezekiah. **10** Hezekiah was the father of Manasseh, Manasseh the father of Amon, Amon the father of Josiah. **11** Josiah was the father of Jeconiah and his brothers, around the time of the exile to Babylon.

**12** After the Babylonian exile: Jeconiah was the father of Shealtiel, Shealtiel the father of Zerubbabel. **13** Zerubbabel was the father of Abiud, Abiud the father of Eliakim, Eliakim the father of Azor. **14** Azor was the father of Zadok, Zadok the father of Achim, Achim the father of Eliud. **15** Eliud was the father of Eleazar, Eleazar the father of Matthan, Matthan the father of Jacob. **16** Jacob was the father of Joseph, the husband of Mary — and from her, Jesus was born, the one called the Messiah.

**17** So all together: fourteen generations from Abraham to David, fourteen from David to the Babylonian exile, and fourteen from the exile to the Messiah.

**18** Here's how the birth of Jesus the Messiah came about: His mother Mary was engaged to Joseph, but before they came together as husband and wife, she was found to be carrying a child through the Holy Spirit.ᶜ **19** Now Joseph, her husband-to-be, was a man who did what was right. He didn't want to make a public spectacle of her, so he decided to break the engagement quietly.

**20** But while he was wrestling with this decision, a messenger of the Lordᵈ appeared to him in a dream and said, "Joseph, son of David, don't be afraid to take Mary as your wife. The child she's carrying comes from the Holy Spirit. **21** She's going to have a son, and you'll name him Jesus,ᵉ because he'll rescue his people from their sins."

**22** All of this happened so that what the Lord had spoken through the prophet would come true: **23** "Look — the virgin will conceive and give birth to a son, and they'll call him Immanuel"ᶠ (which means "God with us").

**24** When Joseph woke up, he did exactly what the Lord's messenger had told him. He took Mary as his wife, **25** but he didn't sleep with her until she gave birth to a son. And he named him Jesus.

## Notes

- v.1a: *Christos* (Χριστός) — the Greek equivalent of the Hebrew *Mashiach* (Messiah/Anointed One). Matthew uses this title deliberately to connect Jesus to Israel's messianic expectations.
- v.6b: Matthew pointedly avoids naming Bathsheba, referring to her only as "Uriah's wife" — highlighting the scandal and David's moral failure.
- v.18c: *pneumatos hagiou* (πνεύματος ἁγίου) — "Holy Spirit." The genitive construction here indicates source/origin.
- v.20d: *angelos kyriou* (ἄγγελος κυρίου) — literally "angel/messenger of the Lord," a phrase with deep Old Testament resonance.
- v.21e: *Iēsous* (Ἰησοῦς) — the Greek form of the Hebrew *Yehoshua* (Joshua), meaning "the LORD saves/rescues."
- v.23f: Quoted from Isaiah 7:14 (LXX). Matthew uses *parthenos* (παρθένος, "virgin"), following the Septuagint's rendering of the Hebrew *almah*.
