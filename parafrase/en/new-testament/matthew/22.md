# Matthew 22

**1** Jesus spoke to them again in parables: **2** "The kingdom of heaven is like a king who prepared a wedding feast for his son. **3** He sent his servants to call those who'd been invited to the feast, but they refused to come.

**4** "He sent other servants, saying, 'Tell those who've been invited: Look, I've prepared my dinner. My bulls and fattened cattle have been slaughtered, and everything is ready. Come to the wedding feast!'

**5** "But they paid no attention and went off — one to his field, another to his business. **6** The rest grabbed his servants, mistreated them, and killed them.

**7** "The king was enraged. He sent his armies, destroyed those murderers, and burned their city.ᵃ

**8** "Then he told his servants, 'The wedding feast is ready, but those who were invited didn't deserve it. **9** So go to the street corners and invite everyone you find to the feast.' **10** Those servants went out into the streets and gathered everyone they could find — both bad and good — and the wedding hall was filled with guests.

**11** "But when the king came in to see the guests, he noticed a man who wasn't wearing wedding clothes.ᵇ **12** 'Friend,' he said to him, 'how did you get in here without wedding clothes?' The man was speechless.

**13** "Then the king told the attendants, 'Tie him hand and foot and throw him outside into the darkness, where there'll be weeping and grinding of teeth.'

**14** "Because many are invited, but few are chosen."

**15** Then the Pharisees went and plotted how to trap him with his own words. **16** They sent their disciples to him along with the Herodiansᶜ. "Teacher," they said, "we know you're honest and teach God's way truthfully. You don't care about anyone's opinion, because you don't show favoritismᵈ. **17** So tell us what you think: Is it lawful to pay taxes to Caesar or not?"

**18** But Jesus saw through their malice. "Why are you testing me, hypocrites?" he said. **19** "Show me the coin used for the tax." They brought him a denarius.

**20** "Whose image and inscription is this?" he asked.

**21** "Caesar's," they said.

Then he said to them, "Give to Caesar what belongs to Caesar, and give to God what belongs to God."ᵉ

**22** When they heard this, they were astonished. They left him and went away.

**23** That same day, Sadducees — who say there is no resurrection — came to him with a question. **24** "Teacher," they said, "Moses told us that if a man dies without having children, his brother must marry the widow and have children for him.ᶠ **25** Now there were seven brothers among us. The first married and died, and since he had no children, he left his wife to his brother. **26** The same thing happened with the second, the third, and on through all seven. **27** Last of all, the woman died. **28** So in the resurrection, whose wife will she be? All seven were married to her."

**29** Jesus answered, "You're mistaken, because you don't know the Scriptures or God's power. **30** In the resurrection, people won't marry or be given in marriage. They'll be like the angels in heaven.ᵍ **31** But regarding the resurrection of the dead — haven't you read what God said to you: **32** 'I am the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob'?ʰ He's not the God of the dead but of the living."

**33** When the crowds heard this, they were amazed by his teaching.

**34** When the Pharisees heard that he'd silenced the Sadducees, they gathered together. **35** One of them, a legal expert,ⁱ tested him with a question: **36** "Teacher, which command in the Torah is the greatest?"

**37** He said to him, "'Love the Lord your God with all your heart, with all your soul, and with all your mind.'ʲ **38** This is the greatest and most important command. **39** And the second is like it: 'Love your neighbor as yourself.'ᵏ **40** The entire Torah and the Prophets hang on these two commands."

**41** While the Pharisees were gathered together, Jesus asked them, **42** "What do you think about the Messiah? Whose son is he?"

"David's," they said.

**43** He said to them, "Then how does David, speaking by the Spirit, call him 'Lord'? He says: **44** 'The Lord said to my Lord: Sit at my right hand until I put your enemies under your feet.'ˡ **45** If David calls him 'Lord,' how can he be his son?"

**46** Nobody could say a word in reply, and from that day on, no one dared to ask him any more questions.

## Notes

- v.7a: Many scholars see this as a reference to the destruction of Jerusalem in 70 AD, either as prophecy or post-event writing.
- v.11b: Wedding garments may have been provided by the host (a common practice). The man's lack of proper clothing represents showing up without genuine transformation — accepting the invitation but refusing its implications.
- v.16c: *Hērōdianoi* (Ἡρωδιανοί) — supporters of Herod's dynasty, who cooperated with Rome. An unusual alliance with the Pharisees, who generally opposed Herod. United only by their opposition to Jesus.
- v.16d: *ou blepeis eis prosōpon anthrōpōn* — literally "you don't look at the face of people." They meant it as flattery; it was actually true.
- v.21e: *apodote... ta Kaisaros Kaisari kai ta tou theou tō theō* — The question intended to trap Jesus between Roman collaboration and Jewish rebellion. His answer transcends the dilemma: since humans bear God's image (Genesis 1:27), what belongs to God is everything.
- v.24f: Referencing *levirate marriage* (Deuteronomy 25:5-6), designed to continue a deceased brother's family line.
- v.30g: Jesus isn't saying resurrected people become angels, but that the categories of earthly life (including marriage) don't apply in the same way.
- v.32h: Quoting Exodus 3:6. The argument is subtle: God says "I am" (present tense), not "I was." Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob must therefore still be alive to God.
- v.35i: *nomikos* (νομικός) — an expert in Torah law, overlapping with but distinct from *grammateus* ("scribe").
- v.37j: Quoting Deuteronomy 6:5, the *Shema*, the most fundamental Jewish confession.
- v.39k: Quoting Leviticus 19:18. Jesus links vertical love (toward God) and horizontal love (toward people) as inseparable.
- v.44l: Quoting Psalm 110:1. Jesus's point: the Messiah is more than merely David's descendant — David himself acknowledges the Messiah as his Lord, implying a status greater than ancestry can explain.
