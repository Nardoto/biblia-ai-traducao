# Matthew 27

**1** When morning came, all the chief priests and elders of the people reached their decision against Jesus — to put him to death. **2** They bound him, led him away, and handed him over to Pilate the governor.ᵃ

**3** When Judas — the one who betrayed him — saw that Jesus had been condemned, he was seized with regret.ᵇ He returned the thirty pieces of silver to the chief priests and elders. **4** "I've sinned," he said. "I've betrayed innocent blood."

"What's that to us?" they said. "That's your problem."

**5** He threw the silver coins into the temple, left, and went and hanged himself.

**6** The chief priests picked up the coins and said, "It's not lawful to put this into the temple treasury, since it's blood money." **7** After deliberating, they used it to buy the potter's field as a burial place for foreigners. **8** That's why it's been called the Field of Blood to this day. **9** Then what was spoken through the prophet Jeremiah was fulfilled: "They took the thirty pieces of silver — the price set on him by the people of Israel — **10** and they used them to buy the potter's field, as the Lord directed me."ᶜ

**11** Jesus stood before the governor. The governor asked him, "Are you the king of the Jews?"

"You say so,"ᵈ Jesus replied.

**12** When he was accused by the chief priests and elders, he gave no answer. **13** Then Pilate asked him, "Don't you hear all the charges they're bringing against you?" **14** But he didn't answer him on a single charge — which greatly astonished the governor.

**15** Now at the festival, the governor had a custom of releasing one prisoner to the crowd — whoever they wanted. **16** At that time they had a notorious prisoner called Barabbas.ᵉ **17** So when the crowd had gathered, Pilate asked them, "Which one do you want me to release to you — Barabbas, or Jesus who is called the Messiah?" **18** He knew it was out of envy that they'd handed Jesus over.

**19** While Pilate was sitting on the judge's seat, his wife sent him a message: "Have nothing to do with that innocent man. I suffered terribly in a dream today because of him."

**20** But the chief priests and elders persuaded the crowds to ask for Barabbas and to have Jesus executed. **21** The governor asked them, "Which of the two do you want me to release to you?"

"Barabbas!" they said.

**22** "Then what should I do with Jesus who is called the Messiah?" Pilate asked.

They all said, "Crucify him!"

**23** "Why? What crime has he committed?" he asked.

But they shouted even louder, "Crucify him!"

**24** When Pilate saw that he was getting nowhere — in fact, a riot was starting — he took water and washed his hands in front of the crowd.ᶠ "I'm innocent of this man's blood," he said. "This is your responsibility."

**25** All the people answered, "His blood be on us and on our children!"ᵍ

**26** Then he released Barabbas to them. He had Jesus flogged and handed him over to be crucified.

**27** Then the governor's soldiers took Jesus into the headquarters and gathered the whole cohortʰ around him. **28** They stripped him and put a scarlet robe on him. **29** They twisted together a crown of thorns and set it on his head, and put a reed in his right hand. They knelt before him and mocked him: "Hail, King of the Jews!" **30** They spat on him, took the reed, and kept hitting him on the head. **31** After they'd mocked him, they stripped the robe off him, put his own clothes back on him, and led him away to crucify him.

**32** On the way out, they found a man from Cyrene named Simon, and they forced him to carry the cross.ⁱ

**33** They came to a place called Golgotha (which means "Place of the Skull"). **34** They offered him wine mixed with gallʲ to drink. He tasted it but refused to drink it.

**35** After they crucified him, they divided his clothes by casting lots.ᵏ **36** Then they sat down and kept watch over him there. **37** Above his head they placed the written charge against him: THIS IS JESUS, THE KING OF THE JEWS.

**38** Two rebels were crucified with him — one on his right and one on his left.

**39** People passing by hurled insults at him, shaking their heads **40** and saying, "You who would destroy the temple and rebuild it in three days — save yourself! If you're God's Son, come down from the cross!"

**41** The chief priests, along with the legal scholars and elders, also mocked him. **42** "He saved others," they said, "but he can't save himself! He's the King of Israel — let him come down from the cross right now and we'll believe in him. **43** He trusts in God — let God rescue him now, if God wants him. After all, he said, 'I'm God's Son.'"ˡ **44** Even the rebels crucified with him heaped the same insults on him.

**45** From noon until three in the afternoon, darkness fell over the whole land.ᵐ **46** Around three, Jesus cried out in a loud voice, "Eli, Eli, lema sabachthani?" — which means, "My God, my God, why have you abandoned me?"ⁿ

**47** Some of those standing there heard this and said, "He's calling for Elijah." **48** Right away one of them ran, got a sponge, filled it with sour wine, put it on a reed, and offered it to him to drink. **49** But the rest said, "Leave him alone. Let's see if Elijah comes to save him."

**50** Then Jesus cried out again in a loud voice — and released his spirit.ᵒ

**51** At that moment, the curtain of the temple was torn in two, from top to bottom.ᵖ The earth shook, rocks split apart, **52** tombs broke open, and the bodies of many holy people who had died were raised. **53** After his resurrection, they came out of the tombs, went into the holy city, and appeared to many people.

**54** When the centurion and those guarding Jesus with him saw the earthquake and everything that had happened, they were terrified. "This man really was God's Son!" they said.

**55** Many women were there, watching from a distance. They had followed Jesus from Galilee and taken care of him. **56** Among them were Mary Magdalene, Mary the mother of James and Joseph, and the mother of Zebedee's sons.

**57** When evening came, a wealthy man from Arimathea arrived — named Joseph — who had himself become a disciple of Jesus. **58** He went to Pilate and asked for Jesus's body. Pilate ordered it to be given to him. **59** Joseph took the body, wrapped it in clean linen, **60** and placed it in his own new tomb that he'd cut out of the rock. He rolled a large stone in front of the entrance and went away. **61** Mary Magdalene and the other Mary were sitting there, facing the tomb.

**62** The next day — the day after Preparation Day — the chief priests and Pharisees went together to Pilate. **63** "Sir," they said, "we remember that while he was still alive, that deceiver said, 'After three days I'll be raised.' **64** So give orders for the tomb to be secured until the third day. Otherwise his disciples might come, steal the body, and tell the people, 'He's been raised from the dead.' Then the last deception would be worse than the first."

**65** "You have a guard,"ᵠ Pilate told them. "Go secure it the best you know how." **66** So they went and secured the tomb by sealing the stone and posting the guard.

## Notes

- v.2a: *Pilatos* (Πιλᾶτος) — Pontius Pilate, Roman prefect of Judea from approximately 26-36 AD.
- v.3b: *metamelētheis* (μεταμεληθείς) — "felt regret, was remorseful." A different word from *metanoeō* ("repent"). Judas felt terrible but didn't experience true transformation.
- v.9-10c: The quotation blends elements from Zechariah 11:12-13 and Jeremiah 19:1-13; 32:6-9. Matthew attributes it to Jeremiah, likely because Jeremiah was considered the primary prophet in the scroll collection.
- v.11d: *sy legeis* (σὺ λέγεις) — "you say it." Like *sy eipas* in 26:25, 64 — an affirmation that avoids adopting Pilate's political framing of "king."
- v.16e: *Barabbas* (Βαραββᾶς) — from Aramaic *bar abba*, "son of the father." Some manuscripts give his full name as "Jesus Barabbas," creating a stark choice between two men named Jesus — one called "son of the father," the other actually being the Son of the Father.
- v.24f: Pilate's hand-washing is a Jewish ritual gesture (Deuteronomy 21:6-7), ironic coming from a Roman governor. It doesn't actually remove responsibility.
- v.25g: This verse has been tragically misused throughout history to justify antisemitism. In context, the crowd at that moment is accepting responsibility — it cannot be applied to an entire people across time.
- v.27h: *speira* (σπεῖρα) — a Roman cohort, up to 600 soldiers.
- v.32i: The condemned typically carried the horizontal crossbeam (*patibulum*). Simon was conscripted (*angareuo* — the same word as 5:41) because Jesus was too weakened.
- v.34j: *cholē* (χολή) — "gall/bitterness." Mark says wine mixed with myrrh (a painkiller). Matthew uses "gall" to echo Psalm 69:21.
- v.35k: Fulfilling Psalm 22:18.
- v.43l: Echoing Psalm 22:8 and Wisdom 2:18. The mockers unknowingly quote Scripture.
- v.45m: *skotos* (σκότος) — darkness over the land recalls the plague of darkness in Egypt (Exodus 10:22) and Amos 8:9.
- v.46n: Quoting Psalm 22:1 in Aramaic. This is the only saying from the cross in Matthew. Whether it expresses genuine abandonment, prophetic identification with the psalm (which ends in triumph), or both, is a profound question.
- v.50o: *aphēken to pneuma* (ἀφῆκεν τὸ πνεῦμα) — "released/let go of his spirit." An active verb — Jesus doesn't merely die; he gives up his life deliberately.
- v.51p: *katapetasma* (καταπέτασμα) — the temple curtain, either the outer curtain or the inner one separating the Holy of Holies. Torn "from top to bottom" — the action comes from God's side.
- v.65q: *echete koustōdian* (ἔχετε κουστωδίαν) — either "you have a guard [already]" or "take a guard [from me]." The word *koustōdia* is a Latin loanword for a Roman military guard unit.
