# Matthew 2

**1** After Jesus was born in Bethlehem of Judea during the reign of King Herod, star-gazersᵃ from the East arrived in Jerusalem **2** asking, "Where is the one who's been born king of the Jews? We saw his star when it rose, and we've come to pay him honor."ᵇ

**3** When King Herod heard this, it shook him — and all of Jerusalem along with him. **4** He gathered all the chief priests and legal scholarsᶜ of the people and demanded to know where the Messiah was supposed to be born. **5** "In Bethlehem of Judea," they told him. "Here's what the prophet wrote:

**6** 'And you, Bethlehem, in the land of Judah — you're by no means the least among Judah's leaders. Because from you will come a ruler who'll shepherd my people Israel.'"

**7** Then Herod secretly called the star-gazers in and found out from them the exact time the star had appeared. **8** He sent them to Bethlehem and said, "Go search carefully for the child. When you find him, report back to me so I can go and honor him too." (He was lying.)

**9** After listening to the king, they went on their way — and there was the star they'd seen rising, going ahead of them until it came to rest over the place where the child was. **10** When they saw the star, their joy was absolutely overwhelming.ᵈ

**11** They went into the house and saw the child with Mary his mother. They fell to the ground and worshiped him. Then they opened their treasure boxes and presented him with gifts: gold, frankincense, and myrrh.ᵉ **12** And having been warned in a dream not to go back to Herod, they returned to their own country by a different route.

**13** After they'd left, a messenger of the Lord appeared to Joseph in a dream. "Get up," he said, "take the child and his mother and escape to Egypt. Stay there until I tell you, because Herod is about to launch a search to destroy the child." **14** So Joseph got up, took the child and his mother during the night, and left for Egypt. **15** He stayed there until Herod died — fulfilling what the Lord had spoken through the prophet: "Out of Egypt I called my son."ᶠ

**16** When Herod realized the star-gazers had outsmarted him, he flew into a rage. He gave orders to kill every boy in Bethlehem and the surrounding area who was two years old or younger — based on the time he'd calculated from the star-gazers. **17** Then what was spoken through the prophet Jeremiah came true:

**18** "A voice was heard in Ramah — weeping and deep mourning. Rachel crying for her children, refusing to be comforted, because they're gone."

**19** After Herod died, a messenger of the Lord appeared in a dream to Joseph in Egypt. **20** "Get up," he said, "take the child and his mother and go back to the land of Israel, because those who were trying to kill the child are dead." **21** So he got up, took the child and his mother, and entered the land of Israel.

**22** But when he heard that Archelaus was reigning over Judea in place of his father Herod, he was afraid to go there. Warned again in a dream, he withdrew to the region of Galilee **23** and settled in a town called Nazareth — so that what was spoken through the prophets would come true: "He'll be called a Nazarene."ᵍ

## Notes

- v.1a: *magoi* (μάγοι) — often translated "wise men" or "Magi." These were likely Zoroastrian astrologer-priests from Persia or Babylon. The number isn't specified — "three" is a later tradition based on the three gifts.
- v.2b: *proskyneō* (προσκυνέω) — to bow down, worship, pay homage. The term carries both political and religious weight.
- v.4c: *grammateis* (γραμματεῖς) — scribes or scholars of the Torah. Rendered "legal scholars" to convey their function.
- v.10d: *echarēsan charan megalēn sphodra* (ἐχάρησαν χαρὰν μεγάλην σφόδρα) — a stacked expression of extreme joy, literally "they rejoiced with great joy exceedingly."
- v.11e: These three gifts have been seen as symbolic: gold for royalty, frankincense for divinity, myrrh for suffering/burial. The text doesn't state this explicitly.
- v.15f: Quoted from Hosea 11:1, originally referring to Israel's exodus from Egypt. Matthew sees a typological pattern.
- v.23g: *Nazōraios* (Ναζωραῖος) — the exact prophetic source is debated. It may be a wordplay on *netzer* (נֵצֶר, "branch") from Isaiah 11:1.
