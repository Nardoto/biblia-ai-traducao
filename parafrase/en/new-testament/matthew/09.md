# Matthew 9

**1** Jesus got into a boat, crossed over, and came to his own town. **2** Some people brought him a paralyzed man lying on a mat. When Jesus saw their faith, he said to the paralyzed man, "Take heart, son — your sins are forgiven."ᵃ

**3** At that, some of the legal scholars said to themselves, "This man is blaspheming!"

**4** But Jesus, knowing what they were thinking, said, "Why are you entertaining evil thoughts in your hearts? **5** Which is easier to say — 'Your sins are forgiven' or 'Get up and walk'? **6** But so you'll know that the Son of Man has authority on earth to forgive sins —" then he said to the paralyzed man, "Get up, pick up your mat, and go home." **7** And the man got up and went home.

**8** When the crowds saw this, they were awestruck and praised God for giving this kind of authority to human beings.

**9** As Jesus moved on from there, he saw a man named Matthew sitting at the tax collection booth. "Follow me," he said. And Matthew got up and followed him.

**10** While Jesus was eating at Matthew's house, a whole crowd of tax collectors and "sinners"ᵇ came and joined him and his disciples at the table. **11** When the Pharisees saw this, they asked his disciples, "Why does your teacher eat with tax collectors and sinners?"

**12** Jesus overheard them and said, "Healthy people don't need a doctor — sick people do. **13** Go learn what this means: 'I want mercy, not sacrifice.'ᶜ I didn't come to call the righteous but sinners."

**14** Then John's disciples came to him and asked, "How come we and the Pharisees fast regularly, but your disciples don't fast?"

**15** Jesus answered, "Can wedding guests mourn while the bridegroom is still with them? The time will come when the bridegroom is taken away from them. Then they'll fast.

**16** "Nobody patches an old garment with unshrunk cloth — the patch will pull away from the garment and make the tear worse. **17** And nobody puts new wine into old wineskins. If they do, the skins will burst, the wine will spill, and the skins will be ruined. No — new wine goes into fresh wineskins, and both are preserved."

**18** While he was telling them these things, a synagogue leaderᵈ came and knelt before him. "My daughter just died," he said. "But come and place your hand on her, and she'll live." **19** Jesus got up and followed him, along with his disciples.

**20** Just then, a woman who'd been suffering from bleeding for twelve yearsᵉ came up behind him and touched the edge of his cloak. **21** She'd been telling herself, "If I can just touch his cloak, I'll be healed."

**22** Jesus turned around and saw her. "Take heart, daughter," he said. "Your faith has healed you."ᶠ And the woman was healed from that moment.

**23** When Jesus arrived at the synagogue leader's house and saw the flute players and the noisy crowd, **24** he said, "Go away. The girl isn't dead — she's sleeping." They laughed at him. **25** But after the crowd had been sent outside, he went in, took the girl by the hand, and she got up. **26** News of this spread throughout that whole region.

**27** As Jesus went on from there, two blind men followed him, shouting, "Have mercy on us, Son of David!"ᵍ

**28** When he went indoors, the blind men came to him. Jesus asked them, "Do you believe I can do this?"

"Yes, Lord," they said.

**29** Then he touched their eyes and said, "Let it happen according to your faith." **30** And their eyes were opened. Jesus sternly warned them, "Make sure nobody finds out about this." **31** But they went out and spread the news about him throughout that whole region.

**32** As they were leaving, some people brought him a man who was mute and demonized. **33** After the demon was driven out, the mute man spoke. The crowds were amazed. "Nothing like this has ever been seen in Israel!" they said.

**34** But the Pharisees said, "He drives out demons by the ruler of demons."ʰ

**35** Jesus went through all the towns and villages, teaching in their synagogues, proclaiming the good news of the kingdom, and healing every disease and every sickness. **36** When he saw the crowds, his heart broke for them,ⁱ because they were worn out and scattered — like sheep without a shepherd. **37** Then he said to his disciples, "The harvest is enormous, but the workers are few. **38** So plead with the Lord of the harvest to send out workers into his harvest."

## Notes

- v.2a: Jesus connects physical healing with forgiveness — a link that would have been natural in Jewish thought but his claim to forgive sins was explosive.
- v.10b: *hamartōloi* (ἁμαρτωλοί) — "sinners" was a social label applied to those considered outside respectable religious society, not just moral failures.
- v.13c: Quoting Hosea 6:6. *Eleos* (ἔλεος) — "mercy/compassion" — is what God actually desires, not ritual performance.
- v.18d: *archōn* (ἄρχων) — a ruler/leader. Mark and Luke identify him as Jairus.
- v.20e: This condition made the woman perpetually ritually unclean under Levitical law, isolating her socially and religiously for twelve years.
- v.22f: *sesōken* (σέσωκέν) — from *sōzō*, meaning both "save" and "heal." The double meaning is intentional.
- v.27g: "Son of David" is a messianic title. The blind men are confessing faith in Jesus as the promised descendant of David.
- v.34h: This accusation sets the stage for the major conflict in chapter 12. They attribute Jesus's power to Beelzebul.
- v.36i: *esplagchnisthē* (ἐσπλαγχνίσθη) — from *splagchna*, "intestines/guts." It describes a visceral, gut-level compassion.
