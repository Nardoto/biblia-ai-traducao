# Matthew 7

**1** "Don't judge, so that you won't be judged.ᵃ **2** Because the standard you use to judge others is the standard that'll be used on you, and the measure you use will be measured back to you.

**3** "Why do you stare at the speck in your brother's eye but don't even notice the plank in your own? **4** How can you say to your brother, 'Let me take that speck out of your eye' — when there's a plank sticking out of yours? **5** Hypocrite! First take the plank out of your own eye, and then you'll see clearly enough to remove the speck from your brother's eye.

**6** "Don't give what's sacred to dogs, and don't throw your pearls in front of pigs. They'll just trample them underfoot — and then turn and tear you apart.ᵇ

**7** "Ask, and it'll be given to you. Seek, and you'll find. Knock, and the door will be opened for you.ᶜ **8** Because everyone who asks receives, everyone who seeks finds, and to everyone who knocks, the door gets opened.

**9** "Which of you, if your child asks for bread, would hand them a stone? **10** Or if they ask for a fish, would give them a snake? **11** If you, then — flawed as you are — know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more will your Father in heaven give good things to those who ask him!

**12** "So in everything, treat people the way you'd want them to treat you.ᵈ This sums up the Torah and the Prophets.

**13** "Enter through the narrow gate. The gate is wide and the road is spacious that leads to destruction, and plenty of people take that route. **14** But the gate is narrow and the road is difficult that leads to life, and only a few find it.

**15** "Watch out for false prophets. They come to you dressed up like sheep, but inside they're ravenous wolves. **16** You'll recognize them by their fruit.ᵉ Do people pick grapes from thornbushes, or figs from thistles? **17** Every healthy tree produces good fruit, but a rotten tree produces bad fruit. **18** A healthy tree can't produce bad fruit, and a rotten tree can't produce good fruit. **19** Every tree that doesn't produce good fruit gets cut down and thrown into the fire. **20** So you'll recognize them by what they produce.

**21** "Not everyone who says to me 'Lord, Lord' will enter the kingdom of heaven — only those who actually do what my Father in heaven wants. **22** On that day, many will say to me, 'Lord, Lord, didn't we prophesy in your name? Didn't we cast out demons in your name? Didn't we do all kinds of powerful things in your name?' **23** And I'll tell them plainly, 'I never knew you. Get away from me, you who practice lawlessness.'ᶠ

**24** "So everyone who hears these words of mine and puts them into practice is like a wise person who built their house on bedrock. **25** The rain poured down, the floods rose, the winds blew and slammed against that house — but it didn't collapse, because its foundation was bedrock. **26** But everyone who hears these words of mine and doesn't put them into practice is like a foolish person who built their house on sand. **27** The rain poured down, the floods rose, the winds blew and crashed against that house — and it collapsed. And the collapse was total."

**28** When Jesus finished saying all this, the crowds were stunned by his teaching,ᵍ **29** because he was teaching them like someone with real authority — not like their legal scholars.

## Notes

- v.1a: *krinete* (κρίνετε) — "judge" in the sense of harsh, condemning judgment — not the discernment Jesus calls for in v.15-20.
- v.6b: A difficult saying. "Dogs" and "pigs" were used in Jewish culture for those who were hostile to sacred things. The point is discernment about when and with whom to share.
- v.7c: The Greek uses present imperatives — *aiteite, zēteite, krouete* — implying continuous action: "keep asking, keep seeking, keep knocking."
- v.12d: Known as the "Golden Rule." While similar sayings exist in other traditions (usually in negative form — "don't do to others..."), Jesus states it positively as active goodness.
- v.16e: *karpos* (καρπός) — "fruit." A recurring metaphor in Matthew for the visible results of a person's inner character.
- v.23f: *anomian* (ἀνομίαν) — "lawlessness." Ironic — these people performed spectacular religious acts but lived outside God's actual will.
- v.28g: *exeplēssonto* (ἐξεπλήσσοντο) — literally "struck out of their senses." They were knocked flat by what they'd just heard.
