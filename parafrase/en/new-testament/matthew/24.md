# Matthew 24

**1** Jesus left the temple and was walking away when his disciples came to him to point out the temple buildings. **2** "You see all of this?" he said. "I'm telling you the truth, not one stone will be left on another here. Every one will be torn down."ᵃ

**3** While he was sitting on the Mount of Olives, the disciples came to him privately. "Tell us," they said, "when will these things happen? And what will be the sign of your coming and of the end of the age?"ᵇ

**4** Jesus answered them: "Watch out that nobody deceives you. **5** Many will come using my name, saying 'I'm the Messiah,' and will deceive many people. **6** You'll hear about wars and rumors of wars. Make sure you're not alarmed — these things have to happen, but it's not the end yet. **7** Nation will rise against nation, and kingdom against kingdom. There'll be famines and earthquakes in various places. **8** But all of these are just the beginning of the birth pains.ᶜ

**9** "Then they'll hand you over to be persecuted and will kill you. You'll be hated by all nations because of my name. **10** At that point, many will fall away, betray each other, and hate each other. **11** Many false prophets will appear and deceive many people. **12** Because lawlessness will increase, the love of most will grow cold.ᵈ **13** But whoever endures to the end will be saved. **14** And this good news of the kingdom will be proclaimed throughout the whole world as a witness to all nations — and then the end will come.

**15** "So when you see 'the abomination that causes desolation'ᵉ spoken of through the prophet Daniel, standing in the holy place (let the reader understand), **16** then those in Judea should flee to the mountains. **17** Whoever is on the rooftop shouldn't go down to get things out of the house. **18** Whoever is in the field shouldn't turn back to get their cloak. **19** How terrible it'll be for pregnant women and nursing mothers in those days! **20** Pray that your escape won't be in winter or on a Sabbath.ᶠ **21** Because there'll be great suffering then — the kind that hasn't happened since the beginning of the world until now, and never will again. **22** If those days weren't cut short, no one would survive. But for the sake of the chosen, those days will be cut short.

**23** "If anyone tells you then, 'Look, here's the Messiah!' or 'There he is!' — don't believe it. **24** False messiahs and false prophets will appear and perform great signs and wonders to deceive, if possible, even the chosen. **25** Listen — I've told you ahead of time.

**26** "So if they say to you, 'Look, he's in the wilderness!' — don't go out. 'Look, he's in the inner rooms!' — don't believe it. **27** Because just as lightning comes from the east and flashes all the way to the west, that's how the coming of the Son of Man will be. **28** Wherever the carcass is, the vultures will gather.ᵍ

**29** "Immediately after the suffering of those days, 'the sun will be darkened, the moon won't give its light, the stars will fall from the sky, and the powers of the heavens will be shaken.'ʰ **30** Then the sign of the Son of Man will appear in heaven, and all the tribes of the earth will mourn. They'll see the Son of Man coming on the clouds of heaven with power and great glory.ⁱ **31** He'll send his angels with a loud trumpet blast, and they'll gather his chosen ones from the four winds — from one end of the heavens to the other.

**32** "Now learn this lesson from the fig tree: As soon as its branches become tender and its leaves sprout, you know summer is near. **33** In the same way, when you see all these things, you know itʲ is near — right at the door. **34** I'm telling you the truth, this generation won't pass away until all these things have happened. **35** Heaven and earth will pass away, but my words will never pass away.

**36** "But about that day and hour, nobody knows — not the angels in heaven, not even the Son — only the Father.ᵏ

**37** "As it was in the days of Noah, that's how the coming of the Son of Man will be. **38** In those days before the flood, people were eating and drinking, marrying and giving in marriage, right up until the day Noah entered the ark. **39** They didn't realize what was happening until the flood came and swept them all away. That's how the coming of the Son of Man will be.

**40** "Two men will be in the field — one will be taken and the other left. **41** Two women will be grinding at the mill — one will be taken and the other left.

**42** "So stay alert, because you don't know what day your Lord is coming.ˡ **43** But understand this: if the homeowner had known what time of night the thief was coming, he would've stayed awake and not let his house be broken into. **44** That's why you also need to be ready, because the Son of Man is coming at a time you don't expect.

**45** "Who then is the faithful and wise servant whose master put him in charge of the household to give them their food at the right time? **46** How fortunate is that servant whose master finds him doing his job when he arrives. **47** I'm telling you the truth, he'll put him in charge of everything he owns.

**48** "But if that servant is wicked and says in his heart, 'My master's taking a long time,' **49** and starts beating the other servants and eating and drinking with drunkards — **50** that servant's master will come on a day he doesn't expect, at a time he doesn't know. **51** He'll cut him to pieces and assign him a place with the hypocrites, where there'll be weeping and grinding of teeth."

## Notes

- v.2a: Fulfilled dramatically in 70 AD when Roman forces under Titus destroyed the temple completely.
- v.3b: The disciples ask what may be two or three separate questions, and Jesus's answer weaves between the temple's destruction and the final coming. Scholars disagree on where one subject ends and the other begins.
- v.8c: *ōdinōn* (ὠδίνων) — "birth pains." In Jewish thought, the sufferings preceding the messianic age were called "the birth pains of the Messiah" — not meaningless suffering but labor leading to new life.
- v.12d: *agapē* (ἀγάπη) — "love." The self-giving love that defines the kingdom community will be threatened by the surrounding lawlessness.
- v.15e: *bdelygma tēs erēmōseōs* (βδέλυγμα τῆς ἐρημώσεως) — from Daniel 9:27, 11:31, 12:11. Originally referring to the desecration of the temple by Antiochus IV Epiphanes (167 BC), here applied to a future event.
- v.20f: Sabbath travel restrictions would hinder escape — a detail uniquely relevant to Matthew's Jewish-Christian audience.
- v.28g: *aetoi* (ἀετοί) — "eagles" or "vultures." A proverb meaning the event will be unmistakable and self-evident. Some see an allusion to Roman eagles (military standards).
- v.29h: Drawing on Isaiah 13:10, 34:4, and Joel 2:31. Whether literal cosmic events or prophetic/apocalyptic imagery for political upheaval is debated.
- v.30i: Echoing Daniel 7:13-14. The "coming on clouds" in Daniel describes coming to God to receive authority, not necessarily coming down to earth.
- v.34j: *genea* (γενεά) — "generation." This has been understood as the contemporary generation (pointing to 70 AD events), the Jewish people, or the generation alive at the final signs.
- v.36k: Jesus's admission that even the Son doesn't know the timing is remarkably honest and guards against all date-setting.
- v.42l: *grēgoreite* (γρηγορεῖτε) — "keep watch, stay alert." The dominant command of the entire discourse.
