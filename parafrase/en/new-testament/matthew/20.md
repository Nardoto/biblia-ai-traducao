# Matthew 20

**1** "The kingdom of heaven is like a landowner who went out early in the morning to hire workers for his vineyard. **2** He agreed with the workers on a denariusᵃ for the day and sent them into his vineyard.

**3** "Around nine in the morning, he went out and saw others standing around in the marketplace with nothing to do. **4** 'You go into the vineyard too,' he told them, 'and I'll pay you whatever is fair.' **5** So they went.

"He went out again around noon and around three in the afternoon and did the same thing. **6** Around five in the afternoon he went out and found still others standing around. 'Why have you been standing here all day doing nothing?' he asked.

**7** "'Because nobody hired us,' they said.

"'You go into the vineyard too,' he told them.

**8** "When evening came, the vineyard owner told his foreman, 'Call the workers and pay them their wages, starting with the last ones and ending with the first.'

**9** "The workers who'd been hired around five in the afternoon each received a denarius. **10** When the first ones came, they expected to get more. But each of them also received a denarius. **11** When they got it, they started grumbling at the landowner. **12** 'These last ones only worked one hour,' they said, 'and you've made them equal to us — and we're the ones who did the heavy lifting all day in the scorching heat!'

**13** "But he answered one of them, 'Friend, I'm not being unfair to you. Didn't you agree with me on a denarius? **14** Take what's yours and go. I want to give this last worker the same as I gave you. **15** Don't I have the right to do what I want with what belongs to me? Or are you envious because I'm generous?'ᵇ

**16** "So the last will be first, and the first will be last."

**17** As Jesus was going up to Jerusalem, he took the twelve disciples aside privately and said to them on the road, **18** "Listen — we're going up to Jerusalem, and the Son of Man will be handed over to the chief priests and legal scholars. They'll condemn him to death **19** and hand him over to the Gentiles to be mocked, flogged, and crucified. And on the third day he'll be raised."ᶜ

**20** Then the mother of Zebedee's sons came to him with her sons. She knelt before him to make a request.

**21** "What do you want?" he asked.

She said, "Promise that these two sons of mine will sit at your right and your left in your kingdom."

**22** Jesus answered, "You don't know what you're asking. Can you drink the cup I'm about to drink?"ᵈ

"We can," they said.

**23** He told them, "You will drink my cup. But sitting at my right and left isn't mine to grant — those places belong to the ones my Father has prepared them for."

**24** When the other ten heard about this, they were furious with the two brothers. **25** But Jesus called them together and said, "You know how the rulers of the nationsᵉ lord it over their people, and their high officials throw their weight around. **26** It must not be that way among you. Instead, whoever wants to be great among you must be your servant. **27** And whoever wants to be first must be your slave. **28** Just as the Son of Man didn't come to be served but to serve, and to give his lifeᶠ as a ransom for many."ᵍ

**29** As they were leaving Jericho, a large crowd followed him. **30** Two blind men sitting by the road heard that Jesus was passing by and shouted, "Lord, Son of David, have mercy on us!"

**31** The crowd told them to be quiet, but they shouted even louder, "Lord, Son of David, have mercy on us!"

**32** Jesus stopped and called them over. "What do you want me to do for you?" he asked.

**33** "Lord," they said, "we want our eyes opened."

**34** Moved with compassion,ʰ Jesus touched their eyes. Immediately they could see, and they followed him.

## Notes

- v.2a: *dēnarion* (δηνάριον) — a denarius was a standard day's wage for a laborer. The parable isn't about fair wages but about God's scandalous generosity.
- v.15b: *ho ophthalmos sou ponēros estin hoti egō agathos eimi* — literally "is your eye evil because I am good?" The "evil eye" means envy/stinginess in Jewish idiom (cf. 6:23).
- v.19c: This is the third and most detailed passion prediction. For the first time, Jesus explicitly mentions crucifixion and Gentile involvement.
- v.22d: *potērion* (ποτήριον) — "cup." In the Old Testament, the cup is often a metaphor for God's wrath or a person's assigned destiny (Psalm 75:8; Isaiah 51:17).
- v.25e: *archontes tōn ethnōn* (ἄρχοντες τῶν ἐθνῶν) — "rulers of the Gentiles/nations." Jesus names the dominant power model and explicitly rejects it for his community.
- v.28f: *psychē* (ψυχή) — "life, self, soul." Jesus gives his whole self.
- v.28g: *lytron anti pollōn* (λύτρον ἀντὶ πολλῶν) — "ransom in place of many." *Lytron* is the price paid to free a slave or prisoner. *Anti* ("in place of") indicates substitution. "Many" in Semitic usage often means "all" (cf. Isaiah 53:11-12).
- v.34h: *splagchnistheis* (σπλαγχνισθείς) — that deep, visceral compassion again, driving Jesus to act.
