# Matthew 26

**1** When Jesus had finished saying all these things, he told his disciples, **2** "You know that Passover is two days away, and the Son of Man will be handed over to be crucified."

**3** Meanwhile, the chief priests and the elders of the people gathered in the palace of the high priest, whose name was Caiaphas. **4** They plotted to arrest Jesus by deception and kill him. **5** "Not during the festival," they said, "or there'll be a riot among the people."

**6** While Jesus was in Bethany at the home of Simon the leper, **7** a woman came to him with an alabaster jar of very expensive perfume.ᵃ She poured it on his head as he reclined at the table.

**8** When the disciples saw this, they were indignant. "What a waste!" they said. **9** "This could've been sold for a large amount and the money given to the poor."

**10** Aware of this, Jesus said to them, "Why are you giving this woman a hard time? She's done a beautiful thing for me. **11** You'll always have the poor with you, but you won't always have me.ᵇ **12** By pouring this perfume on my body, she's prepared me for burial. **13** I'm telling you the truth, wherever this good news is proclaimed in the whole world, what she's done will also be told — in memory of her."

**14** Then one of the Twelve — the one called Judas Iscariot — went to the chief priests **15** and asked, "What will you give me if I hand him over to you?" They paid him thirty pieces of silver.ᶜ **16** From then on, he watched for an opportunity to betray him.

**17** On the first day of Unleavened Bread, the disciples came to Jesus. "Where do you want us to prepare the Passover meal for you?" they asked.

**18** He said, "Go into the city to a certain man and tell him, 'The Teacher says: My timeᵈ is near. I'm celebrating the Passover at your house with my disciples.'" **19** The disciples did as Jesus directed, and they prepared the Passover.

**20** When evening came, he was reclining at the table with the Twelve. **21** While they were eating, he said, "I'm telling you the truth, one of you is going to betray me."

**22** Deeply grieved, they began asking him one after another, "It isn't me, is it, Lord?"

**23** He answered, "The one who dipped his hand into the bowl with me — he's the one who'll betray me. **24** The Son of Man is going away just as it's been written about him. But how terrible for that man through whom the Son of Man is betrayed! It would've been better for him if he'd never been born."

**25** Then Judas — the one betraying him — said, "It isn't me, is it, Rabbi?"

Jesus told him, "You've said it yourself."ᵉ

**26** While they were eating, Jesus took bread, spoke a blessing, broke it, and gave it to the disciples. "Take and eat," he said. "This is my body." **27** Then he took a cup, gave thanks, and gave it to them. "All of you drink from this," he said. **28** "This is my blood of the covenant,ᶠ which is poured out for many for the forgiveness of sins. **29** I'm telling you, I won't drink this fruit of the vine again from now on until that day when I drink it new with you in my Father's kingdom."

**30** After singing a hymn,ᵍ they went out to the Mount of Olives.

**31** Then Jesus told them, "All of you will fall away because of me tonight. It's written: 'I'll strike the shepherd, and the sheep of the flock will be scattered.'ʰ **32** But after I've been raised, I'll go ahead of you to Galilee."

**33** Peter answered, "Even if everyone else falls away because of you, I never will."

**34** Jesus said to him, "I'm telling you the truth, this very night — before the rooster crows — you'll deny me three times."

**35** Peter said to him, "Even if I have to die with you, I'll never deny you." And all the other disciples said the same.

**36** Then Jesus went with them to a place called Gethsemane.ⁱ He told his disciples, "Sit here while I go over there and pray." **37** He took Peter and the two sons of Zebedee along with him. He began to be overwhelmed with grief and distress. **38** Then he said to them, "My soul is crushed with sorrow — to the point of death.ʲ Stay here and keep watch with me."

**39** He went a little farther, fell face down, and prayed: "My Father, if it's possible, let this cup pass from me. Yet not what I want — but what you want."ᵏ

**40** He came back to the disciples and found them sleeping. He said to Peter, "So you couldn't keep watch with me for one hour? **41** Stay awake and pray so you won't fall into temptation. The spirit is willing, but the flesh is weak."

**42** He went away a second time and prayed: "My Father, if this cup can't pass unless I drink it, let your will be done." **43** He came back and found them sleeping again — their eyes were heavy. **44** So he left them, went away again, and prayed a third time, saying the same thing.

**45** Then he came to the disciples and said, "Are you still sleeping and resting? Look — the hour has arrived, and the Son of Man is being betrayed into the hands of sinners. **46** Get up, let's go. Here comes my betrayer!"

**47** While he was still speaking, Judas — one of the Twelve — arrived, and with him a large crowd with swords and clubs, sent from the chief priests and elders of the people. **48** The betrayer had arranged a signal: "The one I kiss is the man — arrest him." **49** He went straight to Jesus. "Greetings, Rabbi!" he said, and kissed him.

**50** Jesus said to him, "Friend,ˡ do what you came to do."

Then they came forward, grabbed Jesus, and arrested him. **51** Suddenly, one of those with Jesus reached for his sword, drew it, and struck the high priest's servant, cutting off his ear.

**52** Jesus told him, "Put your sword back in its place. Everyone who lives by the sword will die by the sword. **53** Do you think I can't call on my Father, and he'd immediately send me more than twelve legions of angels? **54** But then how would the Scriptures be fulfilled that say it has to happen this way?"

**55** At that moment, Jesus said to the crowd, "Have you come out with swords and clubs to arrest me, like I'm some kind of rebel? Day after day I sat teaching in the temple, and you didn't arrest me. **56** But all this has happened so that the writings of the prophets would be fulfilled." Then all the disciples abandoned him and ran.

**57** Those who arrested Jesus took him to the home of Caiaphas the high priest, where the legal scholars and elders had gathered. **58** Peter followed at a distance, all the way to the high priest's courtyard. He went in and sat down with the guards to see how it would end.

**59** The chief priests and the whole council were looking for false testimony against Jesus so they could put him to death. **60** But they couldn't find any, even though many false witnesses came forward. Finally, two came forward **61** and said, "This man said, 'I can destroy God's temple and rebuild it in three days.'"

**62** The high priest stood up and said to him, "Have you no answer? What is this these men are testifying against you?" **63** But Jesus remained silent.

The high priest said to him, "I put you under oath before the living God: Tell us if you're the Messiah, the Son of God."

**64** Jesus said to him, "You've said it yourself.ᵐ But I tell you, from now on you'll see the Son of Man sitting at the right hand of Power and coming on the clouds of heaven."ⁿ

**65** Then the high priest tore his robes and said, "He's blasphemed! Why do we need any more witnesses? Look — you've just heard the blasphemy! **66** What's your verdict?"

"He deserves death!" they answered.

**67** Then they spat in his face and struck him with their fists. Others slapped him **68** and said, "Prophesy to us, Messiah! Who hit you?"

**69** Now Peter was sitting outside in the courtyard. A servant girl came up to him and said, "You were with Jesus the Galilean too."

**70** But he denied it in front of everyone. "I don't know what you're talking about," he said.

**71** When he went out to the gateway, another servant girl saw him and said to the people there, "This man was with Jesus of Nazareth."

**72** Again he denied it, with an oath: "I don't know the man!"

**73** After a little while, the bystanders came up to Peter and said, "You're definitely one of them — your accent gives you away!"

**74** Then he started cursing and swearing: "I don't know the man!" And immediately a rooster crowed.

**75** Peter remembered what Jesus had said: "Before the rooster crows, you'll deny me three times." And he went outside and wept bitterly.

## Notes

- v.7a: *myrou barytimou* (μύρου βαρυτίμου) — "very costly ointment/perfume." John's Gospel identifies it as nard and values it at 300 denarii — nearly a year's wages.
- v.11b: Not indifference to the poor (cf. Deuteronomy 15:11) but recognition that this is a unique, unrepeatable moment.
- v.15c: *triakonta argyria* (τριάκοντα ἀργύρια) — thirty silver coins, echoing Zechariah 11:12-13 and the price of a slave in Exodus 21:32.
- v.18d: *kairos* (καιρός) — the decisive, appointed time, not just any moment.
- v.25e: *sy eipas* (σὺ εἶπας) — "you have said it." An affirmation that places responsibility back on the speaker.
- v.28f: *haima mou tēs diathēkēs* (αἷμά μου τῆς διαθήκης) — "my blood of the covenant." Echoes both the Sinai covenant (Exodus 24:8) and the new covenant of Jeremiah 31:31-34. Some manuscripts add "new" before "covenant."
- v.30g: The Hallel psalms (Psalms 113-118) were traditionally sung at the Passover meal.
- v.31h: Quoting Zechariah 13:7.
- v.36i: *Gethsēmani* (Γεθσημανί) — from Aramaic/Hebrew *gat shemanim*, "oil press." An olive grove on the Mount of Olives.
- v.38j: *perilypos... heōs thanatou* (περίλυπος... ἕως θανάτου) — echoing Psalm 42:5-6, 11; 43:5. The grief is life-threatening in its intensity.
- v.39k: *potērion* (ποτήριον) — "cup." The same cup metaphor from 20:22, now terrifyingly real.
- v.50l: *hetaire* (ἑταῖρε) — "friend, companion." Not the warm *philos* but a formal, almost cold address. Matthew uses it only in contexts of disappointing behavior (cf. 20:13; 22:12).
- v.64m: Again *sy eipas* — an affirmation that doesn't use the exact words the high priest wants.
- v.64n: Combining Psalm 110:1 and Daniel 7:13 — a claim to share in divine authority that the high priest correctly understands as either truth or blasphemy.
