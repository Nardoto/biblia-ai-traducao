# Matthew 15

**1** Then some Pharisees and legal scholars from Jerusalem came to Jesus and asked, **2** "Why do your disciples break the tradition of the elders?ᵃ They don't wash their hands before they eat."

**3** He answered them, "And why do you break God's commandment for the sake of your tradition? **4** God said, 'Honor your father and mother,' and 'Anyone who speaks evil of father or mother must be put to death.' **5** But you say that if anyone tells their father or mother, 'Whatever support you might have received from me has been dedicated as an offering to God,'ᵇ **6** they don't have to honor their father. You've canceled God's word for the sake of your tradition.

**7** "Hypocrites! Isaiah was right when he prophesied about you: **8** 'This people honors me with their lips, but their heart is far from me. **9** Their worship of me is pointless, because they teach human rules as if they were divine commands.'"ᶜ

**10** He called the crowd over and said, "Listen and understand: **11** It's not what goes into the mouth that makes a person unclean — it's what comes out of the mouth that makes them unclean."ᵈ

**12** Then the disciples came and said, "Did you know the Pharisees were offended when they heard that?"

**13** He replied, "Every plant that my heavenly Father didn't plant will be pulled up by the roots. **14** Leave them alone. They're blind guides of the blind. And if one blind person leads another, they'll both fall into a ditch."

**15** Peter said, "Explain this parable to us."

**16** "Are you still so slow to understand?" Jesus said. **17** "Don't you realize that whatever enters the mouth goes into the stomach and then passes out of the body? **18** But the things that come out of the mouth come from the heart — and those are what make a person unclean. **19** Out of the heart come evil thoughts, murder, adultery, sexual immorality, theft, false testimony, slander. **20** These are what make a person unclean. But eating with unwashed hands doesn't make anyone unclean."

**21** Jesus left that area and withdrew to the region of Tyre and Sidon. **22** A Canaanite womanᵉ from that region came out and kept crying, "Have mercy on me, Lord, Son of David! My daughter is being terribly tormented by a demon."

**23** But he didn't answer her a word. His disciples came and urged him, "Send her away — she keeps shouting after us."

**24** He answered, "I was sent only to the lost sheep of the house of Israel."

**25** But she came and knelt before him. "Lord, help me!" she said.

**26** He answered, "It isn't right to take the children's bread and throw it to the dogs."ᶠ

**27** "That's true, Lord," she said. "But even the dogs eat the crumbs that fall from their masters' table."

**28** Then Jesus answered her, "Woman, your faith is extraordinary!ᵍ Let it be done for you as you want." And her daughter was healed from that very moment.

**29** Jesus moved on from there and went along the Sea of Galilee. He went up on a mountainside and sat down. **30** Huge crowds came to him, bringing with them the lame, the blind, the crippled, the mute, and many others. They laid them at his feet, and he healed them. **31** The crowd was amazed when they saw the mute speaking, the crippled made whole, the lame walking, and the blind seeing. And they praised the God of Israel.

**32** Jesus called his disciples to him and said, "My heart goes out to this crowd.ʰ They've already been with me three days and have nothing to eat. I don't want to send them away hungry — they might collapse on the way."

**33** The disciples said to him, "Where would we get enough bread in this remote place to feed such a huge crowd?"

**34** "How many loaves do you have?" Jesus asked.

"Seven," they said, "and a few small fish."

**35** He told the crowd to sit down on the ground. **36** He took the seven loaves and the fish, gave thanks, broke them, and gave them to the disciples, and the disciples gave them to the crowds. **37** Everyone ate and was satisfied. They picked up seven large baskets full of leftover pieces. **38** Those who ate were four thousand men, besides women and children.

**39** After sending the crowds away, he got into the boat and went to the region of Magadan.

## Notes

- v.2a: *paradosin tōn presbyterōn* (παράδοσιν τῶν πρεσβυτέρων) — "tradition of the elders." This refers to the oral Torah, later codified in the Mishnah — interpretive traditions layered on top of the written Torah.
- v.5b: *korban* (κορβᾶν) — a gift/offering dedicated to God. This legal loophole allowed people to declare assets as "dedicated to God" to avoid using them to support parents — technically keeping a vow while violating the commandment to honor parents.
- v.9c: Quoting Isaiah 29:13 (LXX).
- v.11d: A revolutionary principle that cuts through the entire system of ritual purity laws. The source of defilement is internal, not external.
- v.22e: Matthew deliberately uses "Canaanite" — an archaic term evoking Israel's ancient enemies. Mark calls her "Syrophoenician." Matthew's choice heightens the boundary she crosses.
- v.26f: *kynaria* (κυνάρια) — "little dogs, puppies." Not the contemptuous word for wild street dogs (*kynes*) but household pets. The diminutive softens the image, though it remains a hard saying. Jesus may be testing and drawing out her faith.
- v.28g: *megalē sou hē pistis* (μεγάλη σου ἡ πίστις) — "great is your faith!" One of only two people in Matthew whose faith Jesus calls "great" — both are Gentiles (cf. the centurion in 8:10).
- v.32h: *splagchnizomai* (σπλαγχνίζομαι) — again that deep gut-level compassion. This second feeding miracle (paralleling chapter 14) may be directed primarily at Gentiles, given the setting.
