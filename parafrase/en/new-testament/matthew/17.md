# Matthew 17

**1** Six days later, Jesus took Peter, James, and John his brother, and led them up a high mountain by themselves.ᵃ **2** He was transformed right before their eyes. His face shone like the sun, and his clothes became as white as light.ᵇ **3** Suddenly, Moses and Elijah appeared before them, talking with Jesus.

**4** Peter spoke up and said to Jesus, "Lord, it's wonderful that we're here! If you want, I'll set up three sheltersᶜ — one for you, one for Moses, and one for Elijah."

**5** While he was still speaking, a bright cloud enveloped them, and a voice from the cloud said, "This is my Son, the one I love — I'm completely pleased with him. Listen to him!"ᵈ

**6** When the disciples heard this, they fell face down, utterly terrified. **7** But Jesus came and touched them. "Get up," he said. "Don't be afraid." **8** When they looked up, they saw no one except Jesus alone.

**9** As they were coming down the mountain, Jesus commanded them, "Don't tell anyone about this vision until the Son of Man has been raised from the dead."

**10** The disciples asked him, "Then why do the legal scholars say that Elijah has to come first?"

**11** He answered, "Elijah does come and will restore everything.ᵉ **12** But I'm telling you, Elijah has already come, and they didn't recognize him — they did whatever they wanted to him. In the same way, the Son of Man is going to suffer at their hands." **13** Then the disciples understood that he was talking about John the Baptizer.

**14** When they came to the crowd, a man approached him, knelt before him, **15** and said, "Lord, have mercy on my son. He has seizuresᶠ and suffers terribly. He often falls into the fire and often into the water. **16** I brought him to your disciples, but they couldn't heal him."

**17** Jesus answered, "You faithless and twisted generation! How long do I have to be with you? How long do I have to put up with you? Bring him here to me." **18** Jesus rebuked the demon, and it came out of the boy, and he was healed from that moment.

**19** Then the disciples came to Jesus privately and asked, "Why couldn't we drive it out?"

**20** He told them, "Because of your little faith. I'm telling you the truth, if you have faith the size of a mustard seed, you can say to this mountain, 'Move from here to there,' and it'll move. Nothing will be impossible for you."ᵍ

**22** While they were gathering in Galilee, Jesus told them, "The Son of Man is about to be handed over to men. **23** They'll kill him, and on the third day he'll be raised." And they were deeply grieved.

**24** When they arrived in Capernaum, the collectors of the two-drachma temple taxʰ came to Peter and asked, "Doesn't your teacher pay the temple tax?"

**25** "Yes," he said.

When Peter came into the house, Jesus spoke first. "What do you think, Simon? From whom do the kings of the earth collect tolls or taxes — from their own sons or from others?"

**26** "From others," Peter said.

"Then the sons are exempt," Jesus replied. **27** "But so we don't offend them — go to the sea, cast a hook, and take the first fish you catch. When you open its mouth, you'll find a four-drachma coin.ⁱ Take that and give it to them for me and for you."

## Notes

- v.1a: Traditionally identified as Mount Tabor, though Mount Hermon (near Caesarea Philippi) is geographically more likely.
- v.2b: *metemorphōthē* (μετεμορφώθη) — "was transfigured/transformed." The English "metamorphosis" comes from this word. His inner divine reality became outwardly visible.
- v.4c: *skēnas* (σκηνάς) — "tents, tabernacles, shelters." Peter may be thinking of the Feast of Tabernacles or trying to prolong the mountaintop experience.
- v.5d: The voice from heaven repeats the baptism declaration (3:17) but adds "Listen to him!" — echoing Deuteronomy 18:15, where Moses promises a prophet like himself whom the people must hear.
- v.11e: *apokathistēsi* (ἀποκαθιστάνει) — "restores." Referencing Malachi 4:5-6, which predicts Elijah's return before the great day of the Lord.
- v.15f: *selēniazetai* (σεληνιάζεται) — literally "is moon-struck," describing symptoms that correspond to epileptic seizures. Matthew attributes the condition to a demon while using medical language.
- v.20g: Verse 21 ("This kind doesn't come out except by prayer and fasting") appears in some manuscripts but is likely a later addition from Mark 9:29.
- v.24h: *didrachma* (δίδραχμα) — the annual half-shekel temple tax required of every Jewish male over twenty (Exodus 30:13-16). It supported temple operations.
- v.27i: *statēra* (στατῆρα) — a stater, worth four drachmas — exactly enough for two people's temple tax. A playful miracle that makes a theological point about Jesus's relationship to the temple.
