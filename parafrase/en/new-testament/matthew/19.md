# Matthew 19

**1** When Jesus finished saying these things, he left Galilee and went to the region of Judea beyond the Jordan. **2** Large crowds followed him, and he healed them there.

**3** Some Pharisees came to him to test him. They asked, "Is it lawful for a man to divorce his wife for any reason at all?"ᵃ

**4** He answered, "Haven't you read that the Creator made them male and female from the beginning **5** and said, 'For this reason a man will leave his father and mother and be united with his wife, and the two will become one flesh'?ᵇ **6** So they're no longer two but one flesh. What God has joined together, no human being should separate."

**7** They said to him, "Then why did Moses command giving a certificate of divorce and sending her away?"

**8** He told them, "Moses permitted you to divorce your wives because of your hard-heartedness.ᶜ But from the beginning it wasn't that way. **9** I'm telling you, whoever divorces his wife — except for sexual unfaithfulnessᵈ — and marries another woman commits adultery."

**10** His disciples said to him, "If that's how it is between husband and wife, it's better not to marry!"

**11** He said to them, "Not everyone can accept this teaching — only those it's been given to. **12** Some people are born unable to marry. Some have been made that way by others. And some have chosen not to marry for the sake of the kingdom of heaven.ᵉ Whoever can accept this, accept it."

**13** Then people brought little children to him so he could place his hands on them and pray. But the disciples scolded them. **14** Jesus said, "Let the little children come to me — don't stop them! The kingdom of heaven belongs to people like these." **15** He placed his hands on them, then moved on from there.

**16** Someone came up to him and asked, "Teacher, what good thing must I do to get eternal life?"ᶠ

**17** Jesus said, "Why do you ask me about what's good? There's only one who is good. But if you want to enter life, keep the commandments."

**18** "Which ones?" he asked.

Jesus said, "'Don't murder, don't commit adultery, don't steal, don't give false testimony, **19** honor your father and mother,' and 'love your neighbor as yourself.'"

**20** The young man said to him, "I've kept all of these. What am I still missing?"

**21** Jesus told him, "If you want to be complete,ᵍ go sell what you own and give to the poor, and you'll have treasure in heaven. Then come follow me."

**22** When the young man heard this, he went away grieving — because he had a great deal of wealth.

**23** Then Jesus said to his disciples, "I'm telling you the truth, it's hard for a rich person to enter the kingdom of heaven. **24** Again I'm telling you, it's easier for a camel to go through the eye of a needle than for a rich person to enter the kingdom of God."ʰ

**25** When the disciples heard this, they were completely shocked. "Then who can be saved?" they asked.

**26** Jesus looked at them and said, "For human beings this is impossible. But for God, everything is possible."

**27** Peter spoke up: "Look, we've left everything and followed you. So what will there be for us?"

**28** Jesus said to them, "I'm telling you the truth, in the renewal of all things,ⁱ when the Son of Man sits on his glorious throne, you who've followed me will also sit on twelve thrones, judging the twelve tribes of Israel. **29** And everyone who's left houses, brothers, sisters, father, mother, children, or fields for my name's sake will receive a hundred times as much and will inherit eternal life. **30** But many who are first will be last, and many who are last will be first."

## Notes

- v.3a: The Pharisees' question reflects a famous debate between the schools of Shammai (divorce only for sexual immorality) and Hillel (divorce for virtually any reason). They're trying to force Jesus to pick a side.
- v.5b: Quoting Genesis 1:27 and 2:24. Jesus goes behind Moses to creation itself, establishing the original design.
- v.8c: *sklērokardian* (σκληροκαρδίαν) — "hardness of heart." Divorce law was a concession to human brokenness, not God's ideal.
- v.9d: *porneia* (πορνεία) — a broad term covering various forms of sexual immorality. The exact scope is debated. This "exception clause" is unique to Matthew.
- v.12e: *eunouchoi* (εὐνοῦχοι) — "eunuchs." Jesus uses a socially stigmatized term to describe voluntary celibacy for the kingdom — a radical concept in a culture where marriage and procreation were expected of everyone.
- v.16f: *zōēn aiōnion* (ζωὴν αἰώνιον) — "life of the age to come," the quality of life belonging to God's future age.
- v.21g: *teleios* (τέλειος) — "complete, mature, whole" — the same word from 5:48.
- v.24h: This was a proverb for impossibility. Various attempts to soften it (a small gate called "the needle's eye," or reading *kamilon* "rope" for *kamēlon* "camel") are unsupported. Jesus means exactly what he says — it's impossible without God.
- v.28i: *palingenesia* (παλιγγενεσία) — "rebirth, regeneration, renewal." Used only here and in Titus 3:5 in the New Testament. It describes the cosmic renewal of all creation.
