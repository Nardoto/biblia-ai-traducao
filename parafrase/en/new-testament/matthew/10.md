# Matthew 10

**1** Jesus called his twelve disciples to him and gave them authority over unclean spirits — to drive them out — and to heal every disease and every sickness.

**2** Here are the names of the twelve apostles:ᵃ first, Simon (called Peter) and his brother Andrew; James son of Zebedee and his brother John; **3** Philip and Bartholomew; Thomas and Matthew the tax collector; James son of Alphaeus and Thaddaeus; **4** Simon the Zealotᵇ and Judas Iscariot — the one who would betray him.

**5** Jesus sent these twelve out with these instructions: "Don't go to Gentile territory, and don't enter any Samaritan town. **6** Instead, go to the lost sheep of the house of Israel.ᶜ **7** As you go, proclaim: 'The kingdom of heaven has arrived!' **8** Heal the sick, raise the dead, cleanse those with skin diseases, drive out demons. You received freely — give freely.

**9** "Don't take gold, silver, or copper in your belts. **10** No travel bag, no extra shirt, no sandals, no walking stick. The worker deserves to be fed.

**11** "Whatever town or village you enter, find out who in it is worthy, and stay with them until you leave. **12** When you enter the house, offer it your peace.ᵈ **13** If the house is worthy, let your peace rest on it. If it isn't, let your peace return to you. **14** And if anyone won't welcome you or listen to what you have to say, shake the dust off your feet as you leave that house or town. **15** I'm telling you the truth, it'll be more bearable for Sodom and Gomorrah on the day of judgment than for that town.

**16** "Listen — I'm sending you out like sheep among wolves. So be as shrewd as snakes and as innocent as doves.ᵉ

**17** "Be on your guard against people. They'll hand you over to local councils and flog you in their synagogues. **18** You'll be dragged before governors and kings because of me — as a witness to them and to the nations. **19** But when they hand you over, don't worry about what to say or how to say it. What you need to say will be given to you at that moment. **20** It won't just be you speaking — the Spirit of your Father will be speaking through you.

**21** "Brother will betray brother to death, and a father his child. Children will turn against their parents and have them put to death. **22** You'll be hated by everyone because of my name. But whoever endures to the end will be saved.ᶠ **23** When they persecute you in one town, flee to the next. I'm telling you the truth, you won't finish going through the towns of Israel before the Son of Man comes.

**24** "A student isn't above the teacher, and a servant isn't above the master. **25** It's enough for the student to be like the teacher, and the servant like the master. If they've called the head of the house Beelzebul,ᵍ how much more will they slander the members of his household!

**26** "So don't be afraid of them. There's nothing concealed that won't be revealed, nothing hidden that won't be made known. **27** What I tell you in the dark, speak in the daylight. What's whispered in your ear, shout from the rooftops.

**28** "Don't be afraid of those who kill the body but can't kill the soul. Instead, fear the one who can destroy both soul and body in Gehenna. **29** Aren't two sparrows sold for a penny? Yet not one of them falls to the ground without your Father's knowledge.ʰ **30** Even the hairs on your head are all counted. **31** So don't be afraid — you're worth more than many sparrows.

**32** "Everyone who acknowledges me in front of people, I'll acknowledge in front of my Father in heaven. **33** But anyone who denies me in front of people, I'll deny in front of my Father in heaven.

**34** "Don't think I came to bring peace to the earth. I didn't come to bring peace but a sword.ⁱ **35** I came to turn 'a man against his father, a daughter against her mother, a daughter-in-law against her mother-in-law. **36** A person's enemies will be the members of their own household.'

**37** "Anyone who loves father or mother more than me isn't worthy of me. Anyone who loves son or daughter more than me isn't worthy of me. **38** And anyone who doesn't take up their cross and follow me isn't worthy of me.ʲ **39** Whoever finds their life will lose it, and whoever loses their life for my sake will find it.

**40** "Anyone who welcomes you welcomes me, and anyone who welcomes me welcomes the one who sent me. **41** Anyone who welcomes a prophet because they're a prophet will receive a prophet's reward. Anyone who welcomes a righteous person because they're righteous will receive a righteous person's reward. **42** And anyone who gives even a cup of cold water to one of these little ones because they're a disciple — I'm telling you the truth, they won't lose their reward."

## Notes

- v.2a: *apostoloi* (ἀπόστολοι) — "sent ones." This is the only time Matthew uses this word; it marks a shift from *mathētai* ("learners/disciples") to *apostoloi* ("commissioned agents").
- v.4b: *Kananaios* (Καναναῖος) — from Aramaic *qan'an*, meaning "zealot/zealous one." Not from Canaan.
- v.6c: The mission is initially limited to Israel — the universal mission comes in 28:19.
- v.12d: *eirēnē* (εἰρήνη) — the Greek equivalent of the Hebrew *shalom*, meaning wholeness, completeness, well-being — not just absence of conflict.
- v.16e: *phronimoi* (φρόνιμοι) — "shrewd, prudent, wise." Combined with *akeraioi* (ἀκέραιοι) — "unmixed, pure, innocent." Wisdom without naivety, integrity without stupidity.
- v.22f: *sōthēsetai* (σωθήσεται) — "will be saved/delivered." The scope of this "salvation" encompasses both spiritual and physical rescue.
- v.25g: *Beelzeboul* (Βεελζεβούλ) — likely from Hebrew *ba'al zevuv* ("lord of the flies") or *ba'al zevul* ("lord of the house/dwelling"), a mocking name for the prince of demons.
- v.29h: *aneu tou patros hymōn* (ἄνευ τοῦ πατρὸς ὑμῶν) — literally "without your Father." God is present even in the death of a sparrow.
- v.34i: *machairan* (μάχαιραν) — a short sword or large knife. Jesus isn't promoting violence but warning that his message will inevitably divide households.
- v.38j: The first mention of the cross in Matthew. For the original audience, this was a reference to Roman execution — a shocking image of total self-surrender.
