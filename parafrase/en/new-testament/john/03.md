# John 3

**1** There was a man among the Pharisees named Nicodemus — a member of the Jewish ruling council. **2** He came to Jesus at night and said, "Rabbi, we know you're a teacher who has come from God. Nobody could do the signs you're doing unless God were with him."

**3** Jesus replied, "I'm telling you the straight truth: unless a person is born from above,ᵃ they can't even see God's kingdom."

**4** Nicodemus said, "How can someone be born when they're already old? You can't crawl back into your mother's womb and be born a second time."

**5** Jesus answered, "Here's the truth: unless someone is born of water and the Spirit, they can't enter God's kingdom. **6** What's born from the flesh is flesh. What's born from the Spirit is spirit. **7** Don't be so shocked that I said you need to be born from above. **8** The wind blows wherever it wants — you hear it, but you can't tell where it's coming from or where it's headed. That's how it is with everyone born of the Spirit."ᵇ

**9** "How is any of this possible?" Nicodemus asked.

**10** Jesus said, "You're Israel's teacher and you don't understand this? **11** I'm telling you, we're speaking about what we know and testifying to what we've seen — and still you people won't accept our testimony. **12** If I talk to you about earthly things and you don't believe, how are you going to believe when I talk about heavenly things? **13** No one has gone up into heaven except the one who came down from heaven — the Son of Man.

**14** "Just like Moses lifted up the snake in the wilderness, the Son of Man has to be lifted up — **15** so that everyone who trusts in him can have life that never ends.

**16** "Because here's how much God loved the world: he gave his one and only Son, so that everyone who puts their trust in him won't be destroyed but will have life that never ends. **17** God didn't send his Son into the world to condemn it — he sent him to rescue it. **18** Whoever trusts in him isn't condemned. But whoever refuses to trust has already been condemned, because they haven't put their faith in God's one and only Son.

**19** "And here's how the judgment works: the light has come into the world, but people chose the darkness over the light because what they were doing was ugly. **20** Everyone who does evil hates the light and avoids it — they don't want their deeds exposed. **21** But the person who lives by the truth comes toward the light, so it becomes clear that what they've done has been done through God."

**22** After this, Jesus and his followers headed into the Judean countryside, where he spent time with them and was baptizing.ᶜ **23** John was also baptizing, at Aenon near Salim, because there was plenty of water there, and people kept coming to be baptized. **24** This was before John had been thrown in prison.

**25** A disagreement broke out between some of John's followers and a Jewish man about ceremonial purification. **26** They came to John and said, "Rabbi, that man who was with you on the other side of the Jordan — the one you testified about — he's baptizing now, and everybody's going to him."

**27** John answered, "A person can only receive what's given to them from heaven. **28** You yourselves can back me up — I said clearly, 'I am not the Messiah. I was sent ahead of him.' **29** The groom is the one who gets the bride. The best man stands nearby, listens, and is overjoyed just to hear the groom's voice. That's my joy right now — and it's complete. **30** He must increase. I must decrease."

**31** The one who comes from above is above everyone. The one who comes from the earth belongs to the earth and speaks in earthly terms. The one who comes from heaven is above all. **32** He testifies to what he's seen and heard, and yet nobody accepts his testimony. **33** But whoever does accept it has certified that God is true. **34** The one God sent speaks God's words, because God gives the Spirit without holding back.ᵈ **35** The Father loves the Son and has placed everything in his hands. **36** Whoever puts their trust in the Son has life that never ends. But whoever rejects the Son won't see life — instead, God's wrath remains on them.

## Notes

- v.3a: The Greek ἄνωθεν (anōthen) is deliberately ambiguous — it means both "from above" and "again." Nicodemus hears "again"; Jesus means "from above." The double meaning drives the whole conversation.
- v.8b: The Greek πνεῦμα (pneuma) means both "wind" and "spirit." Jesus is making a wordplay that's impossible to fully reproduce in English — the Spirit moves like wind, unpredictable and free.
- v.13: Some manuscripts add "who is in heaven" after "Son of Man." The paraphrase follows the shorter, earlier reading.
- v.16: μονογενής (monogenēs) — "one and only" or "unique," not strictly "only begotten." The emphasis is on the Son's uniqueness, not the mechanism of his origin.
- v.22c: John 4:2 later clarifies that Jesus himself wasn't doing the baptizing — his followers were.
- v.34d: ἐκ μέτρου (ek metrou) — "by measure." God doesn't ration the Spirit. Some manuscripts are ambiguous about whether the subject is God giving or the Son receiving, but the meaning converges: the Spirit is given in limitless fullness.
