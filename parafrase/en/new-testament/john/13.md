# John 13

**1** It was just before the Passover festival. Jesus knew his hour had come — the hour to leave this world and go to the Father. He had loved his own who were in the world, and now he loved them to the very end.ᵃ

**2** They were eating dinner. The devil had already planted the idea of betrayal in the heart of Judas, son of Simon Iscariot. **3** Jesus, knowing that the Father had put everything in his hands, and that he'd come from God and was going back to God — **4** he got up from the table, took off his outer robe, and wrapped a towel around his waist. **5** Then he poured water into a basin and began washing his followers' feet, drying them with the towel he'd wrapped around himself.

**6** When he came to Simon Peter, Peter said, "Lord — you're going to wash my feet?"

**7** Jesus answered, "You don't understand what I'm doing right now. But later, you will."

**8** Peter said, "You'll never wash my feet — ever!"

Jesus replied, "If I don't wash you, you have no part with me."

**9** Simon Peter said, "Then Lord, not just my feet — wash my hands and my head too!"

**10** Jesus told him, "Someone who's already had a bath only needs to wash their feet — the rest of them is clean. And you are clean — though not all of you."ᵇ **11** He knew who was going to betray him. That's why he said, "Not all of you are clean."

**12** After he finished washing their feet, he put his robe back on, sat down again, and said, "Do you understand what I just did? **13** You call me Teacher and Lord, and you're right — that's what I am. **14** So if I, your Lord and Teacher, have washed your feet, you should wash each other's feet. **15** I've given you an example — so you'll do the same kind of thing I did for you. **16** I'm telling you the truth: a servant isn't greater than their master, and a messenger isn't greater than the one who sent them. **17** Now that you know these things, you'll be blessed if you actually do them.

**18** "I'm not talking about all of you — I know who I've chosen. But the Scripture has to be fulfilled: 'The one who shares my bread has turned against me.'ᶜ **19** I'm telling you this now, before it happens, so that when it does, you'll believe that I am who I am. **20** I'm telling you the truth: whoever receives someone I send receives me, and whoever receives me receives the one who sent me."

**21** After saying this, Jesus was deeply troubled in his spirit. He said plainly, "I'm telling you the truth: one of you is going to betray me."

**22** His followers stared at each other, completely at a loss about who he could mean. **23** One of them — the one Jesus was closest toᵈ — was leaning back against Jesus' chest. **24** Simon Peter motioned to him and said, "Ask him who he's talking about."

**25** So that follower leaned in closer to Jesus and asked, "Lord, who is it?"

**26** Jesus answered, "It's the one I give this piece of bread to after I've dipped it." He dipped the bread and handed it to Judas, son of Simon Iscariot. **27** The moment Judas took the bread, Satan entered him.

Jesus said to him, "What you're going to do — do it quickly."

**28** Nobody at the table understood why he said this. **29** Since Judas was in charge of the money, some thought Jesus was telling him to buy what they needed for the festival, or to give something to the poor. **30** As soon as Judas took the bread, he left. And it was night.ᵉ

**31** After Judas was gone, Jesus said, "Now the Son of Man is glorified, and God is glorified in him. **32** If God is glorified in him, God will also glorify him in himself — and will do it soon.

**33** "Little children, I'm only going to be with you a little while longer. You'll look for me, and just as I told the Jewish leaders, 'Where I'm going, you can't come' — I'm telling you the same thing now.

**34** "I'm giving you a new commandment: love one another.ᶠ Just as I have loved you, you should love one another. **35** This is how everyone will know you're my followers — by the way you love each other."

**36** Simon Peter asked, "Lord, where are you going?"

Jesus answered, "Where I'm going, you can't follow me right now. But you will follow later."

**37** Peter said, "Lord, why can't I follow you now? I'll lay down my life for you!"

**38** Jesus replied, "You'll lay down your life for me? I'm telling you the truth: before the rooster crows, you'll have denied knowing me three times."

## Notes

- v.1a: εἰς τέλος (eis telos) carries a powerful double meaning — "to the end" (until the last moment) and "to the uttermost" (completely, fully). Jesus' love is both enduring and total.
- v.10b: Some manuscripts omit "except for their feet" (εἰ μὴ τοὺς πόδας). The shorter reading ("the one who has bathed has no need to wash") is well attested but changes the symbolism. The paraphrase follows the longer reading, which fits the narrative logic.
- v.18c: Quoting Psalm 41:9 (LXX 40:10). The Hebrew reads "lifted his heel against me" — the image of a trusted companion kicking, like an animal turning on its keeper.
- v.23d: ὃν ἠγάπα ὁ Ἰησοῦς — "the one Jesus loved (agapaō)." Traditionally called "the beloved disciple." The paraphrase uses "the one Jesus was closest to" to convey the relational intimacy without the formal title.
- v.30e: καὶ ἦν νύξ — "And it was night." One of the most evocative sentences in the Gospel. The darkness is simultaneously literal and symbolic — Judas walks out of the light of Jesus' presence into moral and spiritual darkness.
- v.34f: ἐντολὴν καινήν (entolēn kainēn) — "new commandment." Love itself wasn't new (Leviticus 19:18). What's new is the standard: "as I have loved you" — a self-sacrificing love about to be demonstrated on the cross.
