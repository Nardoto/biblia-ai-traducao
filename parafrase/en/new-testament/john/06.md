# John 6

**1** After this, Jesus crossed to the far side of the Sea of Galilee — also called the Sea of Tiberias. **2** A huge crowd kept following him because they'd seen the signs he was performing on the sick. **3** Jesus went up on a hillside and sat down there with his followers. **4** The Jewish Passover festival was near.

**5** When Jesus looked up and saw the massive crowd heading toward him, he said to Philip, "Where are we going to buy enough bread to feed all these people?" **6** He already knew what he was going to do — he was testing Philip.

**7** Philip answered, "Eight months' wages wouldn't be enough to give each person even a small piece of bread!"ᵃ

**8** One of his followers — Andrew, Simon Peter's brother — spoke up: **9** "There's a boy here who has five barley loaves and two fish. But what good is that for a crowd this size?"

**10** Jesus said, "Have everybody sit down." There was plenty of grass in that area, so the people sat — about five thousand men. **11** Jesus took the loaves, gave thanks, and distributed them to the people sitting there. He did the same with the fish — as much as anyone wanted. **12** When everyone had eaten their fill, he told his followers, "Gather the leftovers. Don't waste anything." **13** They collected twelve baskets full of pieces left over from the five barley loaves.

**14** When the people saw the sign he'd performed, they started saying, "This really is the Prophet who's supposed to come into the world." **15** Jesus, knowing they were about to come grab him and make him king by force, withdrew again to the mountain by himself.

**16** When evening came, his followers went down to the lake, **17** got into a boat, and started crossing toward Capernaum. It was already dark, and Jesus hadn't come to them yet. **18** A strong wind was blowing and the water was getting rough. **19** After they'd rowed about three or four miles, they saw Jesus walking on the water, coming toward the boat — and they were terrified. **20** But he said to them, "It's me. Don't be afraid."ᵇ **21** They were willing to take him into the boat, and immediately the boat reached the shore they were heading for.

**22** The next day, the crowd that had stayed on the other side of the lake noticed there had been only one boat, and that Jesus hadn't gotten into it with his followers — they'd left without him. **23** Other boats from Tiberias landed near the place where they'd eaten the bread after the Lord had given thanks. **24** So when the crowd realized that neither Jesus nor his followers were there, they got into the boats and headed for Capernaum looking for him.

**25** When they found him on the other side, they asked, "Rabbi, when did you get here?"

**26** Jesus answered, "I'm telling you the truth: you're not looking for me because you saw signs — you're looking for me because you ate the bread and had your fill. **27** Don't work for food that spoils. Work for the food that lasts into life that never ends — the kind the Son of Man will give you. God the Father has put his seal of approval on him."

**28** They asked, "What do we have to do to perform God's works?"

**29** Jesus answered, "This is God's work: that you trust in the one he sent."

**30** They said, "So what sign are you going to show us so we can see it and believe you? What are you going to do? **31** Our ancestors ate manna in the desert — as it's written, 'He gave them bread from heaven to eat.'"

**32** Jesus said, "I'm telling you the truth: it wasn't Moses who gave you the bread from heaven. It's my Father who gives you the true bread from heaven. **33** God's bread is the one who comes down from heaven and gives life to the world."

**34** "Sir," they said, "give us this bread from now on!"

**35** Jesus told them, "I am the bread of life. Whoever comes to me will never go hungry, and whoever trusts in me will never be thirsty. **36** But like I told you — you've seen me and still you don't believe.

**37** "Everyone the Father gives me will come to me, and whoever comes to me I'll never turn away — never. **38** Because I didn't come down from heaven to do my own thing. I came to do the will of the one who sent me. **39** And here's the will of the one who sent me: that I won't lose a single person he's given me, but raise every one of them up on the last day. **40** This is my Father's will: that everyone who sees the Son and trusts in him will have life that never ends — and I'll raise them up on the last day."

**41** The Jewish leaders started grumbling about him because he said, "I am the bread that came down from heaven." **42** They said, "Isn't this Jesus, Joseph's son? We know his father and mother. How can he say, 'I came down from heaven'?"

**43** Jesus answered, "Stop grumbling among yourselves. **44** No one can come to me unless the Father who sent me draws them in, and I'll raise them up on the last day. **45** It's written in the Prophets: 'They'll all be taught by God.' Everyone who listens to the Father and learns from him comes to me. **46** Not that anyone has seen the Father — only the one who is from God has seen the Father.

**47** "I'm telling you the truth: whoever believes has life that never ends. **48** I am the bread of life. **49** Your ancestors ate manna in the wilderness, and they died. **50** But here's the bread that comes down from heaven — anyone can eat it and not die. **51** I am the living bread that came down from heaven. If anyone eats this bread, they'll live forever. And the bread I'll give is my flesh, given for the life of the world."ᶜ

**52** The Jewish leaders started arguing intensely with each other: "How can this man give us his flesh to eat?"

**53** Jesus said, "I'm telling you the absolute truth: unless you eat the flesh of the Son of Man and drink his blood, you have no life in you. **54** Whoever eats my flesh and drinks my blood has life that never ends, and I'll raise them up on the last day. **55** My flesh is real food and my blood is real drink. **56** Whoever eats my flesh and drinks my blood stays in me, and I stay in them.

**57** "Just as the living Father sent me and I live because of the Father, the person who feeds on me will live because of me. **58** This is the bread that came down from heaven — not like the bread your ancestors ate, and then they died. Whoever eats this bread will live forever."

**59** He said all this while teaching in the synagogue in Capernaum.

**60** When they heard it, many of his followers said, "This is a hard teaching. Who can accept it?"

**61** Jesus, aware that his followers were grumbling about this, said, "Does this offend you? **62** What if you saw the Son of Man going back up to where he was before? **63** The Spirit is what gives life — the flesh doesn't accomplish anything on its own. The words I've spoken to you are spirit, and they're life. **64** But some of you don't believe." Jesus knew from the beginning who would not believe and who would betray him. **65** He said, "That's why I told you: no one can come to me unless the Father makes it possible."

**66** From that point on, many of his followers turned back and stopped walking with him.

**67** So Jesus asked the Twelve, "Are you going to leave too?"

**68** Simon Peter answered, "Lord, who else would we go to? You have the words of life that never ends. **69** We've come to believe and know that you're the Holy One of God."

**70** Jesus said, "Didn't I choose all twelve of you? And yet one of you is a devil."ᵈ **71** He was talking about Judas, son of Simon Iscariot — because Judas, one of the Twelve, was going to betray him.

## Notes

- v.7a: Literally "two hundred denarii" (δηνάρια διακόσια). A denarius was roughly a day's wage for a laborer, so this represents about eight months of income. Philip's point: this is a completely impossible situation.
- v.20b: ἐγώ εἰμι (egō eimi) — "I am" — could simply mean "It's me," but in John's Gospel this phrase consistently echoes the divine name. The double meaning is likely intentional.
- v.51c: The shift from ἄρτος (artos, bread) to σάρξ (sarx, flesh) is jarring and deliberate. Jesus isn't softening his metaphor — he's intensifying it. The Eucharistic overtones are unmistakable, but the primary referent is Jesus giving his entire self for the world's life.
- v.54: The Greek τρώγων (trōgōn) — "eats/chews" — is more visceral than the standard verb for eating (ἐσθίω). Jesus uses increasingly graphic language, not less.
- v.70d: διάβολος (diabolos) — literally "slanderer" or "accuser." Jesus is not calling Judas Satan but identifying a satanic quality within him — betrayal is already taking root.
