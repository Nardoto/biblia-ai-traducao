# John 8

**1** But Jesus went to the Mount of Olives. **2** At dawn, he showed up at the temple again. The whole crowd gathered around him, and he sat down and started teaching.ᵃ

**3** The scribes and Pharisees dragged in a woman who'd been caught in the act of adultery. They stood her right in the middle of everyone **4** and said to him, "Teacher, this woman was caught in the very act of adultery. **5** Moses commanded us in the law to stone women like this. What do you say?"

**6** They were using this to trap him, looking for something to accuse him of. But Jesus bent down and started writing on the ground with his finger.ᵇ

**7** When they kept pressing him, he straightened up and said, "Whichever one of you has never sinned — go ahead, throw the first stone." **8** Then he bent down and wrote on the ground again.

**9** When they heard this, they started leaving one at a time — the oldest ones first — until Jesus was left alone with the woman still standing there. **10** Jesus straightened up and said, "Woman, where did they all go? Didn't anyone condemn you?"

**11** "No one, sir," she said.

Jesus said, "I don't condemn you either. Go — and from now on, don't sin anymore."

---

**12** Jesus spoke to them again: "I am the light of the world.ᶜ Whoever follows me won't walk in darkness — they'll have the light of life."

**13** The Pharisees challenged him: "You're testifying about yourself. Your testimony doesn't count."

**14** Jesus answered, "Even if I testify about myself, my testimony is valid, because I know where I came from and where I'm going. You don't know where I come from or where I'm going. **15** You judge by human standards. I'm not judging anyone — **16** but if I do judge, my judgment is true, because I'm not in this alone. It's me and the Father who sent me. **17** Even in your own law it says that the testimony of two witnesses is valid. **18** I testify about myself, and the Father who sent me testifies about me."

**19** They asked, "Where is this father of yours?"

Jesus answered, "You don't know me and you don't know my Father. If you knew me, you'd know my Father too."

**20** He said all this while teaching in the temple treasury area. Nobody arrested him — his time hadn't come.

**21** Then he said to them again, "I'm going away. You'll look for me, and you'll die in your sin. Where I'm going, you can't come."

**22** The Jewish leaders said, "Is he going to kill himself? Is that what he means by 'Where I'm going, you can't come'?"

**23** He told them, "You're from below; I'm from above. You belong to this world; I don't belong to this world. **24** That's why I told you you'd die in your sins. Unless you believe that I am,ᵈ you'll die in your sins."

**25** "Who are you?" they demanded.

Jesus said, "What I've been telling you from the very start. **26** I have plenty to say about you — and to judge. But the one who sent me is true, and what I heard from him is what I tell the world."

**27** They didn't understand he was talking about the Father. **28** So Jesus said, "When you've lifted up the Son of Man, then you'll know that I am, and that I do nothing on my own — I only say what the Father taught me. **29** The one who sent me is with me. He hasn't left me alone, because I always do what pleases him." **30** As he said these things, many put their trust in him.

**31** Jesus said to the Jews who had believed him, "If you hold to what I say, you're really my followers. **32** And you'll know the truth, and the truth will set you free."

**33** They answered, "We're Abraham's descendants. We've never been slaves to anyone. What do you mean, 'You'll be set free'?"

**34** Jesus replied, "I'm telling you the truth: everyone who sins is a slave to sin. **35** A slave doesn't have a permanent place in the household, but a son belongs there forever. **36** So if the Son sets you free, you're genuinely free.

**37** "I know you're Abraham's descendants. But you're trying to kill me because my word has no room in your hearts. **38** I'm telling you what I've seen in my Father's presence. And you're doing what you've heard from your father."

**39** "Abraham is our father," they insisted.

Jesus said, "If you were really Abraham's children, you'd be doing what Abraham did. **40** But right now you're trying to kill me — a man who told you the truth that I heard from God. Abraham never did anything like that. **41** You're doing what your father does."

They said, "We're not illegitimate children! We have one Father — God himself."

**42** Jesus told them, "If God were your Father, you'd love me, because I came from God and I'm here. I didn't come on my own — he sent me. **43** Why can't you understand what I'm saying? Because you can't stand to hear my word.

**44** "You belong to your father — the devil — and you want to carry out his desires. He was a murderer from the start. He has no relationship with the truth because there's no truth in him. When he lies, he's speaking his native language, because he's a liar and the father of lies. **45** But because I tell the truth, you don't believe me. **46** Which one of you can point to a sin I've committed? If I'm telling the truth, why don't you believe me? **47** Whoever belongs to God hears God's words. The reason you don't hear them is that you don't belong to God."

**48** The Jewish leaders shot back, "Aren't we right that you're a Samaritan and you've got a demon?"

**49** Jesus answered, "I don't have a demon. I honor my Father, and you dishonor me. **50** I'm not chasing my own glory. There is one who seeks it, and he's the judge. **51** I'm telling you the truth: if anyone keeps my word, they'll never see death."

**52** They said, "Now we know you've got a demon! Abraham died, the prophets died — and you're saying, 'If anyone keeps my word, they'll never taste death'? **53** Are you greater than our father Abraham? He died! The prophets died! Who do you think you are?"

**54** Jesus answered, "If I glorify myself, my glory means nothing. The one who glorifies me is my Father — the one you claim as your God. **55** You don't know him, but I know him. If I said I didn't, I'd be a liar like you. But I do know him and I keep his word. **56** Your father Abraham was overjoyed at the thought of seeing my day — he saw it and was glad."

**57** The Jewish leaders said, "You're not even fifty years old and you've seen Abraham?"

**58** Jesus said, "I'm telling you the truth: before Abraham existed, I am."ᵉ

**59** They picked up stones to throw at him, but Jesus slipped away and left the temple.

## Notes

- v.1-11a: The passage known as the Pericope Adulterae (7:53–8:11) is absent from the earliest and best Greek manuscripts and differs in style from the rest of John. Most scholars believe it's an authentic early tradition that was inserted into the Gospel later. It's included here because of its deep canonical significance, but set apart with a divider.
- v.6b: What Jesus wrote on the ground (κατέγραφεν) is one of the great mysteries of the Gospels. Some speculate he was writing the accusers' sins; others see an allusion to Jeremiah 17:13 ("those who turn away from you will be written in the dust"). The text deliberately does not say.
- v.12c: "I am the light of the world" — spoken during or just after the Festival of Tabernacles, when the temple courts were lit by massive candelabras. Jesus claims to be the reality those lights pointed to.
- v.24d: ἐγώ εἰμι (egō eimi) — "I am" — used here without a predicate, echoing the divine self-identification in Exodus 3:14. The absolute "I am" appears again in v.28 and v.58.
- v.58e: πρὶν Ἀβραὰμ γενέσθαι, ἐγὼ εἰμί — "before Abraham came into being, I am." The contrast between γενέσθαι (came into being, aorist) and εἰμί (am, present tense) is deliberate. Abraham had a beginning; Jesus claims timeless existence. The crowd understood exactly what he meant — they picked up stones for blasphemy.
