# John 1

**1** Before anything else existed — before the universe, before time itself — there was the Word. The Word was right there with God, and the Word was God. **2** He was there with God from the very start. **3** Everything that exists was made through him. Not a single thing came into being without him. **4** Life itself was in him, and that life was the light that shines for every person. **5** That light keeps shining in the darkness, and the darkness has never been able to put it out.

**6** There was a man named John, sent by God with a mission. **7** He came to tell people about the light so that everyone could believe through what he said. **8** He wasn't the light himself — he was there to point to it. **9** The real light, the one that illuminates every human being, was coming into the world.

**10** He was in the world — a world he had made — and the world didn't even recognize him. **11** He came home, to his own people, and they shut the door in his face. **12** But to everyone who did welcome him, everyone who put their trust in him, he gave the right to become God's children — **13** not children born through bloodlines or human desire or anyone's decision, but born from God himself.

**14** And the Word became a human being and moved into our neighborhood. We saw his glory with our own eyes — the kind of glory that belongs to a father's one and only son — overflowing with grace and truth.

**15** John kept pointing to him, shouting, "This is the one I was talking about! The one coming after me has always been ahead of me, because he existed long before I did."

**16** Out of his fullness, we've all received one gift after another — grace piled on top of grace. **17** The law came through Moses, but grace and truth? Those came through Jesus the Messiah. **18** No one has ever seen God. But the one and only Son, who is himself God and who is closer to the Father than anyone — he's the one who has made God known.

**19** Now here's what happened when the religious leaders in Jerusalem sent priests and Temple assistants to ask John, "So who are you, exactly?" **20** He didn't dodge the question. He came right out and said, "I'm not the Messiah." **21** "Then who? Are you Elijah?" "No." "Are you the Prophet we've been waiting for?" "No." **22** "Well, you've got to give us something to take back to the people who sent us. What do you say about yourself?" **23** John said, "I'm the voice out in the wilderness shouting, 'Clear the road for the Lord!' — just like the prophet Isaiah said."

**24** Some Pharisees in the group pressed him: **25** "If you're not the Messiah, and you're not Elijah, and you're not the Prophet — then what gives you the right to baptize people?" **26** John answered, "I'm just baptizing with water. But standing right here among you is someone you don't even know yet — **27** the one coming after me. I'm not even worthy to untie his sandals."

**28** All of this took place at Bethany on the east side of the Jordan River, where John had been baptizing.

**29** The very next day, John saw Jesus walking toward him and said, "Look! There he is — God's Lamb, the one who takes away the sin of the whole world! **30** This is who I meant when I said, 'Someone is coming after me who ranks way above me, because he was already here before me.' **31** I didn't know who he was either — but the whole reason I came baptizing with water was so that Israel could see him for who he really is."

**32** Then John gave this testimony: "I watched the Spirit come down from heaven like a dove and rest on him. **33** I wouldn't have known him, but the one who sent me to baptize with water told me, 'When you see the Spirit come down and stay on someone — that's the one who will baptize with the Holy Spirit.' **34** I saw it happen with my own eyes, and I'm telling you: this is the Son of God."

**35** The next day, John was standing there again with two of his followers. **36** When he saw Jesus walk by, he said, "There — that's God's Lamb." **37** The two disciples heard him say it and immediately went after Jesus. **38** Jesus turned around, saw them following, and asked, "What are you looking for?" They said, "Rabbi" — which means Teacher — "where are you staying?" **39** "Come and see for yourselves," he said. So they went and saw where he was staying, and they spent the rest of the day with him. It was about four in the afternoon.

**40** One of the two who heard John and followed Jesus was Andrew, Simon Peter's brother. **41** The first thing Andrew did was find his brother Simon and tell him, "We've found the Messiah!" — which means the Anointed One. **42** He brought Simon to Jesus. Jesus looked at him and said, "You're Simon, John's son. But from now on, you'll be called Cephas" — which is Peter, meaning Rock.

**43** The next day, Jesus decided to head for Galilee. He found Philip and said, "Come, follow me." **44** Philip was from Bethsaida, the same town as Andrew and Peter. **45** Philip went and found Nathanael and told him, "We've found the one Moses wrote about in the Law, the one the prophets talked about — Jesus, Joseph's son, from Nazareth." **46** Nathanael shot back, "Nazareth? Can anything good come from there?" Philip said, "Just come and see."

**47** When Jesus saw Nathanael approaching, he said, "Now here's a genuine Israelite — not a deceptive bone in his body." **48** Nathanael was taken aback. "How do you know me?" Jesus answered, "I saw you under that fig tree before Philip even called you." **49** Nathanael blurted out, "Rabbi, you're the Son of God! You're the King of Israel!" **50** Jesus said, "You believe all that just because I told you I saw you under a fig tree? You're going to see things far greater than that." **51** Then he said, "I'm telling you the truth — you're going to see heaven wide open and God's angels going up and coming down on the Son of Man."

## Notes

- v.1: The Greek *logos* (λόγος) carries layers of meaning — word, reason, creative expression, divine order. "The Word" is kept as the most recognized rendering, but the concept is far richer than any single English term captures.
- v.5: The Greek *katelaben* (κατέλαβεν) can mean "overcome," "grasp," or "comprehend." The darkness both failed to understand the light and failed to extinguish it. "Put it out" captures the sense of defeat while echoing the light metaphor.
- v.14: *Eskēnōsen* (ἐσκήνωσεν) literally means "pitched his tent" or "tabernacled" — evoking God's tabernacle presence with Israel in the wilderness. "Moved into our neighborhood" conveys the radical intimacy of the incarnation.
- v.18: Some early manuscripts read "the only begotten God" (μονογενὴς θεός) rather than "the only begotten Son." The paraphrase follows the reading attested in the earliest manuscripts while keeping the language accessible.
- v.21: "The Prophet" refers to the figure promised in Deuteronomy 18:15, whom many Jews expected as a distinct figure from the Messiah.
- v.42: *Kēphas* (Κηφᾶς) is Aramaic for "rock," translated into Greek as *Petros* (Πέτρος). Jesus is giving Simon a new identity, not just a nickname.
- v.51: Jesus alludes to Jacob's dream in Genesis 28:12, reframing himself as the new point of connection between heaven and earth.
