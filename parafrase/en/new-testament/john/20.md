# John 20

**1** Early on the first day of the week, while it was still dark, Mary Magdalene went to the tomb and saw that the stone had been removed from the entrance. **2** She ran to Simon Peter and the other follower — the one Jesus was closest to — and said, "They've taken the Lord out of the tomb, and we don't know where they've put him!"

**3** Peter and the other follower headed straight for the tomb. **4** They were both running, but the other follower outran Peter and got there first. **5** He bent down, looked in, and saw the linen strips lying there — but he didn't go in. **6** Then Simon Peter arrived right behind him and went straight into the tomb. He saw the linen strips lying there, **7** and the cloth that had been wrapped around Jesus' head. It wasn't with the other strips — it was folded up neatly by itself in a separate spot.ᵃ **8** Then the other follower, the one who'd arrived first, also went inside. He saw — and he believed. **9** They still didn't understand the Scripture that said he had to rise from the dead. **10** The two followers went back home.

**11** But Mary stood outside the tomb, weeping. As she cried, she bent down to look into the tomb **12** and saw two angels in white sitting where Jesus' body had been — one at the head and one at the feet.

**13** They asked her, "Woman, why are you crying?"

She said, "They've taken my Lord, and I don't know where they've put him."

**14** As she said this, she turned around and saw Jesus standing there — but she didn't recognize him.

**15** Jesus said to her, "Woman, why are you crying? Who are you looking for?"

Thinking he was the gardener, she said, "Sir, if you've carried him away, tell me where you put him and I'll take him."

**16** Jesus said, "Mary."

She turned to him and cried out in Aramaic, "Rabboni!" — which means Teacher.ᵇ

**17** Jesus said, "Don't cling to me — I haven't gone up to the Father yet. But go to my brothers and tell them: 'I'm going up to my Father and your Father, to my God and your God.'"ᶜ

**18** Mary Magdalene went and announced to the followers, "I've seen the Lord!" And she told them what he'd said to her.

**19** That evening — the first day of the week — the followers were behind locked doors because they were afraid of the Jewish leaders. Jesus came and stood right there among them and said, "Peace be with you." **20** Then he showed them his hands and his side. The followers were overjoyed when they saw the Lord.

**21** Jesus said to them again, "Peace be with you. Just as the Father sent me, I'm sending you." **22** Then he breathed on them and said, "Receive the Holy Spirit.ᵈ **23** If you forgive anyone's sins, they're forgiven. If you hold them, they're held."

**24** Now Thomas — one of the Twelve, called the Twin — wasn't with them when Jesus came. **25** The other followers told him, "We've seen the Lord!"

But Thomas said, "Unless I see the nail marks in his hands, put my finger where the nails were, and put my hand into his side — I will not believe."

**26** A week later, his followers were in the house again, and this time Thomas was with them. The doors were locked, but Jesus came and stood among them and said, "Peace be with you." **27** Then he said to Thomas, "Put your finger here — look at my hands. Reach out your hand and put it into my side. Stop doubting and believe."

**28** Thomas said, "My Lord and my God!"ᵉ

**29** Jesus told him, "You believe because you've seen me. Blessed are the ones who haven't seen and still believe."

**30** Jesus performed many other signs in the presence of his followers that aren't written down in this book. **31** But these are written so that you'll believe that Jesus is the Messiah, the Son of God — and that by believing, you'll have life in his name.

## Notes

- v.7a: The detail of the neatly folded head cloth (σουδάριον, soudarion) is unique to John. It suggests an unhurried departure — not a frantic grave robbery. Jesus left death the way he entered it: with deliberate calm.
- v.16b: Ραββουνί (Rabbouni) is an Aramaic term of heightened respect and intimacy, more personal than Rabbi. Mary's recognition comes not through sight but through Jesus speaking her name — echoing 10:3, where the shepherd calls his sheep by name.
- v.17c: μή μου ἅπτου (mē mou haptou) — present imperative with μή, meaning "stop clinging to me" rather than "don't touch me." Mary's relationship with the risen Jesus is being redefined — his physical presence is transitioning to a new mode. Note also: "my Father and your Father" — not "our Father." The relationship is shared but not identical.
- v.22d: ἐνεφύσησεν (enephysēsen) — "breathed on." The same rare verb used in Genesis 2:7 (LXX) when God breathed life into Adam. John presents this as a new creation moment — the risen Jesus breathes new life into his community.
- v.28e: ὁ κύριός μου καὶ ὁ θεός μου — "My Lord and my God." The highest christological declaration in the Gospel, forming an inclusio with 1:1 ("the Word was God"). Thomas' doubt leads to the fullest confession. The Gospel moves from "the Word was God" to a human being saying "my God" to the risen Jesus.
- v.31: Some manuscripts read πιστεύσητε (aorist — "come to believe," aimed at new faith) while others read πιστεύητε (present — "continue believing," aimed at sustaining faith). The textual evidence is evenly split, and both readings fit the Gospel's purpose.
