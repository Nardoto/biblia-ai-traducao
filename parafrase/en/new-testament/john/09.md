# John 9

**1** As Jesus was walking along, he saw a man who'd been blind since birth. **2** His followers asked him, "Rabbi, who sinned — this man or his parents — that he was born blind?"

**3** Jesus answered, "Neither this man nor his parents sinned. This happened so that God's work could be displayed in his life. **4** We need to do the work of the one who sent me while it's still daytime. Night is coming, when no one can work. **5** As long as I'm in the world, I am the light of the world."

**6** After saying this, he spit on the ground, made some mud with the saliva, and spread it on the man's eyes. **7** Then he told him, "Go wash in the Pool of Siloam" — the name means "Sent."ᵃ So the man went, washed, and came back seeing.

**8** His neighbors and the people who'd seen him before as a beggar started saying, "Isn't this the guy who used to sit and beg?" **9** Some said, "That's him." Others said, "No, he just looks like him." But the man himself kept saying, "It's me."

**10** They asked, "How did your eyes get opened?"

**11** He answered, "The man they call Jesus made some mud, put it on my eyes, and told me, 'Go to Siloam and wash.' So I went, washed, and now I can see."

**12** "Where is he?" they asked.

"I don't know," he said.

**13** They brought the man who'd been blind to the Pharisees. **14** Now the day Jesus made the mud and opened his eyes was a Sabbath.ᵇ **15** So the Pharisees also asked him how he'd received his sight. He told them, "He put mud on my eyes, I washed, and now I see."

**16** Some of the Pharisees said, "This man can't be from God — he doesn't keep the Sabbath." But others said, "How can a sinner do signs like these?" They were divided.

**17** So they asked the blind man again, "What do you have to say about him? It was your eyes he opened."

The man said, "He's a prophet."

**18** The Jewish leaders refused to believe he'd actually been blind and received his sight until they sent for his parents. **19** They asked them, "Is this your son — the one you say was born blind? How can he see now?"

**20** His parents answered, "We know he's our son, and we know he was born blind. **21** But how he can see now — we have no idea. And who opened his eyes — we don't know either. Ask him. He's an adult. He can speak for himself." **22** His parents said this because they were afraid of the Jewish leaders, who had already agreed that anyone who acknowledged Jesus as the Messiah would be thrown out of the synagogue. **23** That's why they said, "He's an adult. Ask him."

**24** So for the second time, they called in the man who'd been blind and said, "Give glory to God.ᶜ We know this man is a sinner."

**25** He answered, "Whether he's a sinner, I don't know. One thing I do know: I was blind, and now I see."

**26** They asked again, "What did he do to you? How did he open your eyes?"

**27** He said, "I already told you and you didn't listen. Why do you want to hear it again? Are you looking to become his followers too?"

**28** They hurled insults at him: "You're a follower of that man! We're followers of Moses! **29** We know God spoke to Moses. As for this fellow — we don't even know where he comes from."

**30** The man replied, "Now that's remarkable. You don't know where he comes from, and yet he opened my eyes. **31** We know God doesn't listen to sinners, but he does listen to someone who worships him and does his will. **32** Nobody has ever heard of someone opening the eyes of a person born blind — not since the beginning of the world. **33** If this man weren't from God, he couldn't do anything."

**34** They shot back, "You were steeped in sin from birth, and you're going to lecture us?" And they threw him out.

**35** Jesus heard they'd thrown him out. When he found him, he said, "Do you trust in the Son of Man?"

**36** "Who is he, sir?" the man asked. "Tell me, so I can trust in him."

**37** Jesus said, "You've seen him. In fact, he's the one talking to you right now."

**38** The man said, "Lord, I trust you." And he worshiped him.

**39** Jesus said, "I came into this world for judgment — so that the blind would see and those who think they see would become blind."

**40** Some Pharisees who were nearby heard this and asked, "Wait — are you calling us blind?"

**41** Jesus said, "If you were blind, you wouldn't be guilty. But since you keep claiming, 'We can see' — your guilt remains."

## Notes

- v.7a: Σιλωάμ (Siloam), interpreted as "Sent" (ἀπεσταλμένος). John highlights the name because "sent" is a key theme — Jesus is the one "sent" by the Father, and the healing comes through an act of being sent (to the pool). The Pool of Siloam has been archaeologically excavated and confirmed.
- v.14b: Making mud was considered a form of "work" (kneading) prohibited on the Sabbath under rabbinic interpretation. This is the second Sabbath healing controversy in John (after ch. 5).
- v.24c: "Give glory to God" (δὸς δόξαν τῷ θεῷ) was a solemn oath formula, essentially meaning "Tell the truth before God" — like putting someone under oath. Compare Joshua 7:19.
- v.34: The Pharisees' response — "you were steeped in sin from birth" — ironically echoes the exact theological assumption Jesus rejected in v.3.
- v.39: The irony of the chapter: the man born physically blind gains both physical and spiritual sight, while the religious leaders who can physically see become progressively spiritually blind.
