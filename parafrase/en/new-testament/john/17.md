# John 17

**1** After saying all this, Jesus looked up toward heaven and said, "Father, the time has come. Show the world who your Son really is, so that your Son can show the world who you really are. **2** You've given me authority over every person alive — so that everyone you've entrusted to me, I can give them real life, life that never ends. **3** And here's what that life is: it's knowing you — the only true God — and knowing Jesus, the Messiah you sent.

**4** I've honored you here on earth. I've finished everything you gave me to do. **5** So now, Father, bring me back into the glory we shared before the world even existed — that glory I had with you before anything was made.

**6** I've revealed who you are to the people you pulled out of the world and gave to me. They were yours first, and you gave them to me, and they've held on to your word. **7** Now they understand that everything you've given me actually comes from you. **8** Because the words you gave me, I passed on to them. They received them and know — they really know — that I came from you. They believe that you're the one who sent me.

**9** I'm praying for them right now. I'm not praying for the world at large — I'm praying for the ones you gave me, because they belong to you. **10** Everything that's mine is yours, and everything that's yours is mine — and my glory shines through them.

**11** I'm not going to be in the world much longer, but they will be. I'm coming back to you. Holy Father, protect them by the power of your name — the name you gave me — so they can be one, unified, just like we are. **12** While I was with them, I kept them safe. I guarded them by the power of your name — the name you gave me. Not a single one was lost, except the one who was destined for destruction, and that was to fulfill what Scripture said would happen.

**13** But now I'm coming to you, and I'm saying all this while I'm still in the world so that they can experience my joy — completely full, overflowing joy — right here inside them. **14** I've given them your word, and the world hates them for it, because they don't fit in with the world anymore — just like I don't fit in with the world. **15** I'm not asking you to pull them out of the world. I'm asking you to protect them from the evil one. **16** They don't belong to the world, just like I don't belong to the world.

**17** Set them apart through the truth. Your word is the truth. **18** In the same way you sent me into the world, I'm sending them into the world. **19** And for their sake, I'm setting myself apart — so that they too can be genuinely set apart through the truth.

**20** But I'm not just praying for these guys. I'm also praying for everyone who will ever come to trust in me because of their message — **21** that all of them will be one. Just as you, Father, are in me and I'm in you, I want them to be in us too. Then the world will believe that you really did send me. **22** The glory you gave me, I've passed on to them — so they can be one the way we are one. **23** Me in them, you in me — completely woven together into one. Then the world will know that you sent me and that you love them just as much as you love me.

**24** Father, I want the ones you gave me to be with me where I am — so they can see my glory, the glory you gave me because you loved me before the foundation of the world.

**25** Righteous Father, the world doesn't know you, but I know you, and these people know that you sent me. **26** I've made your name known to them, and I'll keep making it known — so that the love you have for me will be in them, and I myself will be in them.

## Notes

- v.2: The Greek ἐξουσία (exousia) here refers to divinely granted authority rather than raw power — Jesus' authority is relational, given by the Father for a redemptive purpose.
- v.3: "Real life, life that never ends" — the Greek ζωὴ αἰώνιος (zōē aiōnios) is not just about duration ("eternal") but about quality of life that participates in God's own reality. The paraphrase tries to capture both dimensions.
- v.5: πρὸ τοῦ τὸν κόσμον εἶναι — "before the world existed" — a striking claim of pre-existence that the paraphrase retains without theological elaboration.
- v.9: Jesus' statement that he is not praying "for the world" (οὐ περὶ τοῦ κόσμου ἐρωτῶ) is limited in scope to this specific prayer, not a universal rejection of the world — the context of the whole Gospel makes this clear.
- v.11: ἐν τῷ ὀνόματί σου ᾧ δέδωκάς μοι — "the name you gave me" — a textually difficult phrase. Some manuscripts read "protect them in your name, those whom you have given me." The paraphrase follows the reading that the Father's name was given to the Son.
- v.12: "The one destined for destruction" (ὁ υἱὸς τῆς ἀπωλείας) — literally "the son of destruction," a Semitic idiom meaning someone characterized by or headed toward ruin. The paraphrase avoids the loaded word "perdition."
- v.17: ἁγίασον — "set apart" rather than the traditional "sanctify," which carries heavy theological baggage. The core meaning is consecration for a specific purpose.
- v.23: τετελειωμένοι εἰς ἕν — "perfected into one" — the paraphrase uses "completely woven together" to capture the sense of a unity that has been brought to its intended completion.
