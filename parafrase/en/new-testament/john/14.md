# John 14

**1** "Don't let your hearts be torn apart by worry. You trust God — trust me too. **2** My Father's house has plenty of room. If it didn't, would I have told you I'm going to get a place ready for you? **3** And after I go and set everything up, I'm coming back to get you so you can be right there with me. **4** You already know where I'm headed — and you know the way to get there."

**5** Thomas spoke up: "Lord, we don't even know where you're going. How can we possibly know the way?"

**6** Jesus told him, "I am the way, the truth, and the life. Nobody gets to the Father except through me. **7** If you really knew me, you'd know my Father too. And from this point on, you do know him — you've been looking right at him."

**8** Philip jumped in: "Lord, just show us the Father and that'll be enough for us."

**9** Jesus said, "Philip, I've been with you all this time and you still don't get it? Anyone who has seen me has seen the Father. So why are you saying, 'Show us the Father'? **10** Don't you believe that I'm in the Father and the Father is in me? The things I say to you aren't just coming from me — the Father, who lives in me, is doing his work through me. **11** Believe me when I tell you I'm in the Father and the Father is in me. Or at the very least, believe because of what you've seen me do.

**12** "I'm telling you the truth: whoever trusts in me will do the same things I've been doing — and even greater things than these, because I'm going to the Father. **13** Whatever you ask for using my name, I'll do it, so the Father's greatness shines through the Son. **14** If you ask me for anything in my name, I'll do it.

**15** "If you love me, you'll follow what I've asked you to do. **16** And I'll ask the Father, and he'll give you another Companion — one who will stay with you forever: **17** the Spirit of truth. The world can't receive this Spirit because it doesn't see or recognize him. But you know him because he's already living with you and will be right inside you.

**18** "I'm not going to leave you stranded like orphans. I'm coming back to you. **19** In just a little while, the world won't see me anymore, but you will. Because I live, you'll live too. **20** When that day comes, you'll understand that I'm in my Father, you're in me, and I'm in you.

**21** "Whoever holds on to what I've asked and actually lives it out — that's the person who loves me. And the one who loves me will be loved by my Father, and I'll love them and reveal myself to them."

**22** Judas — not Iscariot, the other one — asked, "Lord, how come you're going to reveal yourself to us but not to the rest of the world?"

**23** Jesus answered, "Anyone who loves me will keep my word, and my Father will love them, and we'll come make our home with them. **24** Whoever doesn't love me doesn't follow what I say. And the message you're hearing isn't really mine — it comes from the Father who sent me.

**25** "I'm telling you all this while I'm still here with you. **26** But the Companion — the Holy Spirit, whom the Father will send in my name — he'll teach you everything and remind you of all I've said to you.

**27** "I'm leaving you with peace. I'm giving you my peace — not the shallow kind the world offers. Don't let your hearts be troubled, and don't be afraid.

**28** "You heard me say, 'I'm going away, and I'm coming back to you.' If you really loved me, you'd be happy for me, because the Father is greater than I am. **29** I'm telling you this now, before it happens, so that when it does happen, you'll believe.

**30** "I don't have much more time to talk with you. The ruler of this world is on his way, and he has no hold on me — none at all. **31** But the world needs to know that I love the Father and do exactly what he's told me to do. Come on — let's go."

---

## Notes

- v.2: The Greek μοναί (monai) means "dwelling places" or "rooms" — not separate mansions, but spaces within the Father's household. The image is of a large family home with room for everyone.
- v.16: The Greek παράκλητος (paraklētos) is variously rendered "Comforter," "Advocate," "Helper." I used "Companion" to capture the sense of someone who comes alongside and stays — neither purely legal (advocate) nor purely emotional (comforter).
- v.27: "My peace" (εἰρήνην τὴν ἐμήν) — Jesus distinguishes his peace from worldly peace (the absence of conflict). His peace is an inner wholeness that persists even in turmoil.
- v.30: "The ruler of this world" (ὁ τοῦ κόσμου ἄρχων) refers to the devil/Satan. The phrase "has no hold on me" translates ἐν ἐμοὶ οὐκ ἔχει οὐδέν — literally "has nothing in me," meaning no claim, no leverage, no foothold.
