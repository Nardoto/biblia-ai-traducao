# John 5

**1** Some time later, there was a Jewish festival, and Jesus went up to Jerusalem. **2** Now in Jerusalem, near the Sheep Gate, there's a pool — called Bethesda in Hebrew — surrounded by five covered walkways.ᵃ **3** Crowds of sick people lay in them — blind, lame, paralyzed.ᵇ

**5** One man had been sick for thirty-eight years. **6** When Jesus saw him lying there and learned how long he'd been in that condition, he asked him, "Do you want to get well?"

**7** The sick man answered, "Sir, I don't have anyone to help me into the pool when the water gets stirred up. While I'm trying to drag myself there, someone else always gets in ahead of me."

**8** Jesus said to him, "Get up. Pick up your mat. Walk."

**9** Instantly the man was healed. He picked up his mat and started walking.

Now that day happened to be a Sabbath. **10** So the Jewish leaders said to the man who'd been healed, "It's the Sabbath — you're not allowed to carry your mat."

**11** He told them, "The man who healed me told me, 'Pick up your mat and walk.'"

**12** They asked, "Who told you to pick it up and walk?" **13** But the man who'd been healed had no idea who it was — Jesus had slipped away into the crowd.

**14** Later, Jesus found him in the temple and said, "Look — you've been made well. Stop sinning, or something worse might happen to you." **15** The man went off and told the Jewish leaders that it was Jesus who had healed him.

**16** That's why the Jewish leaders started going after Jesus — because he was doing these things on the Sabbath. **17** Jesus responded, "My Father is still working, right now, and so am I."

**18** This made them even more determined to kill him. Not only was he breaking the Sabbath in their eyes — he was calling God his own Father, making himself equal with God.

**19** So Jesus said to them, "I'm telling you the truth: the Son can't do anything on his own. He only does what he sees the Father doing. Whatever the Father does, the Son does the same. **20** Because the Father loves the Son and shows him everything he's doing. And he'll show him even greater things than these — things that'll blow your minds. **21** Just as the Father raises the dead and gives them life, the Son gives life to whoever he chooses. **22** The Father doesn't judge anyone directly — he's handed all judgment over to the Son, **23** so that everyone will honor the Son the same way they honor the Father. Whoever doesn't honor the Son doesn't honor the Father who sent him.

**24** "I'm telling you the truth: whoever hears what I'm saying and trusts the one who sent me has life that never ends. They won't face condemnation — they've already crossed over from death to life.

**25** "I'm telling you the truth: a time is coming — and it's already here — when the dead will hear the voice of God's Son, and those who hear will live. **26** Just as the Father has life in himself, he's also given the Son life in himself. **27** And he's given him authority to judge, because he's the Son of Man.

**28** "Don't be amazed at this. A time is coming when everyone in their graves will hear his voice **29** and come out — those who did good will rise to life, and those who practiced evil will rise to face judgment.

**30** "I can't do anything on my own. I judge based on what I hear, and my judgment is fair — because I'm not chasing my own agenda. I'm pursuing the will of the one who sent me.

**31** "If I were the only one testifying about myself, my testimony wouldn't hold up. **32** But there's someone else who testifies about me, and I know his testimony about me is true.

**33** "You sent people to John, and he told you the truth. **34** Not that I need a human being to vouch for me — but I'm mentioning this so you can be saved. **35** John was a lamp that burned and gave light, and for a while you were happy to enjoy his light.

**36** "But I have testimony that carries more weight than John's. The works the Father has given me to accomplish — the very things I'm doing — these testify that the Father sent me. **37** And the Father who sent me has himself testified about me. You've never heard his voice or seen his form, **38** and his word doesn't live in you — because you won't trust the one he sent.

**39** "You dig through the Scriptures because you think they give you eternal life. But these Scriptures are pointing to me! **40** Yet you refuse to come to me so you can actually have life.

**41** "I don't accept praise from people. **42** But I know you — I know that the love of God isn't in your hearts. **43** I've come in my Father's name and you won't receive me. If someone else shows up in their own name, you'll welcome them with open arms. **44** How can you possibly believe when you're so busy accepting praise from each other and don't care about the praise that comes from the only God?

**45** "Don't think I'm the one who's going to accuse you before the Father. The one accusing you is Moses — the very one you've pinned your hopes on. **46** If you really believed Moses, you'd believe me, because he wrote about me. **47** But if you don't believe what he wrote, how are you going to believe what I say?"

## Notes

- v.2a: The name Βηθεσδά (Bethesda) likely means "house of mercy" or "house of the outpouring." Manuscript traditions vary — some read Bethzatha or Bethsaida. Archaeological excavations near St. Anne's Church in Jerusalem have uncovered a pool matching John's description.
- v.3b: Verse 4 is absent from the earliest manuscripts. It describes an angel stirring the water, likely a later scribal addition to explain the popular belief referenced in v.7. The paraphrase omits it, following the critical text.
- v.17: The present tense ἐργάζεται (ergazetai) — "is working" — is theologically loaded. Jewish theology acknowledged that God continued to sustain the universe on the Sabbath. Jesus places his own work alongside the Father's ongoing creative work.
- v.39: ἐραυνᾶτε (eraunate) could be imperative ("Search the Scriptures!") or indicative ("You search the Scriptures"). The context strongly favors the indicative — Jesus is describing what they're already doing, not commanding them to do more of it.
