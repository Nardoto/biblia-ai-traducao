# John 19

**1** Then Pilate took Jesus and had him flogged. **2** The soldiers twisted together a crown of thorns and jammed it on his head. They threw a purple robe on him **3** and kept coming up to him, saying, "Hail, King of the Jews!" And they slapped him across the face.

**4** Pilate went outside again and said to them, "Look — I'm bringing him out to you so you'll know I find no basis for a charge against him." **5** Jesus came out wearing the crown of thorns and the purple robe. Pilate said to them, "Here's the man."ᵃ

**6** The moment the chief priests and their officers saw him, they screamed, "Crucify! Crucify him!"

Pilate said, "Take him and crucify him yourselves. I find no charge against him."

**7** The Jewish leaders answered, "We have a law, and according to that law he ought to die — because he made himself out to be the Son of God."

**8** When Pilate heard this, he became even more afraid. **9** He went back inside and asked Jesus, "Where are you from?"

Jesus gave him no answer.

**10** Pilate said, "You won't talk to me? Don't you realize I have the authority to release you and the authority to crucify you?"

**11** Jesus answered, "You'd have no authority over me at all if it hadn't been given to you from above. That's why the one who handed me over to you carries the greater sin."

**12** From then on, Pilate tried to release him. But the Jewish leaders kept shouting, "If you release this man, you're no friend of Caesar! Anyone who claims to be a king is opposing Caesar!"ᵇ

**13** When Pilate heard this, he brought Jesus outside and sat down on the judgment seat at a place called the Stone Pavement — Gabbatha in Hebrew. **14** It was the day of preparation for the Passover, about noon.ᶜ

Pilate said to the Jewish leaders, "Here is your king."

**15** They shouted, "Take him away! Take him away! Crucify him!"

Pilate said, "Should I crucify your king?"

The chief priests answered, "We have no king but Caesar."

**16** So he handed Jesus over to be crucified.

They took Jesus away. **17** Carrying his own cross, he went out to what's called the Place of the Skull — Golgotha in Hebrew. **18** There they crucified him, and two others with him, one on each side, with Jesus in the middle.

**19** Pilate had a notice written and fastened to the cross. It read: JESUS OF NAZARETH, THE KING OF THE JEWS. **20** Many of the Jewish people read this sign because the place where Jesus was crucified was near the city, and it was written in Hebrew, Latin, and Greek. **21** The Jewish chief priests said to Pilate, "Don't write 'The King of the Jews.' Write that he said, 'I am the King of the Jews.'"

**22** Pilate answered, "What I've written, I've written."

**23** When the soldiers crucified Jesus, they took his clothes and divided them into four shares — one for each soldier. The inner garment was left over. It was seamless, woven in one piece from top to bottom.ᵈ **24** They said to each other, "Let's not tear it. Let's cast lots and see who gets it." This fulfilled the Scripture that says, "They divided my clothes among them and cast lots for my garment." So that's what the soldiers did.

**25** Standing near the cross were Jesus' mother, his mother's sister, Mary the wife of Clopas, and Mary Magdalene. **26** When Jesus saw his mother and the follower he was closest to standing nearby, he said to his mother, "Woman, here is your son." **27** Then he said to the follower, "Here is your mother." From that moment on, that follower took her into his own home.

**28** After this, knowing that everything was now completed, Jesus said — so that the Scripture would be fulfilled — "I'm thirsty."ᵉ **29** A jar of sour wine was sitting there. They soaked a sponge in it, put it on a hyssop branch, and held it up to his mouth.ᶠ **30** When Jesus had received the wine, he said, "It is finished."ᵍ

Then he bowed his head and gave up his spirit.

**31** Since it was the day of preparation, the Jewish leaders didn't want the bodies left on the crosses during the Sabbath — especially since that Sabbath was a particularly important one. So they asked Pilate to have the legs broken and the bodies taken down. **32** The soldiers came and broke the legs of the first man who'd been crucified with Jesus, and then the other. **33** But when they came to Jesus and saw he was already dead, they didn't break his legs. **34** Instead, one of the soldiers pierced his side with a spear, and immediately blood and water flowed out.ʰ

**35** The one who saw it has testified to it — and his testimony is true. He knows he's telling the truth — so that you also may believe. **36** These things happened to fulfill the Scripture: "Not one of his bones will be broken." **37** And another Scripture says, "They will look on the one they pierced."

**38** After this, Joseph of Arimathea — who was a follower of Jesus, but secretly, because he was afraid of the Jewish leaders — asked Pilate for permission to take Jesus' body. Pilate gave him permission, so he came and took the body. **39** Nicodemus, the man who had first come to Jesus at night, also came, bringing about seventy-five pounds of a mixture of myrrh and aloes. **40** They took Jesus' body and wrapped it in linen strips with the spices, following Jewish burial customs. **41** There was a garden at the place where he'd been crucified, and in the garden a new tomb where no one had ever been laid. **42** Since it was the Jewish day of preparation and the tomb was close by, they laid Jesus there.

## Notes

- v.5a: Ἴδε ὁ ἄνθρωπος (Ide ho anthrōpos) — "Behold the man." Pilate likely meant it dismissively: "Look at this pathetic figure." But John's readers hear deeper resonance — here is the human being, the incarnate Word, bearing the full weight of human suffering.
- v.12b: φίλος τοῦ Καίσαρος (philos tou Kaisaros) — "friend of Caesar" was a semi-official political title. Losing it could mean the end of Pilate's career. The Jewish leaders weaponize Roman politics to force Pilate's hand.
- v.14c: παρασκευὴ τοῦ πάσχα (paraskeuē tou pascha) — "preparation of the Passover." John's timing places Jesus' death at the very hour the Passover lambs were being slaughtered in the temple. This is theologically deliberate — Jesus is the true Passover Lamb.
- v.23d: The seamless garment (χιτὼν ... ἄραφος, chitōn ... araphos) may echo the high priest's robe, which was also woven in one piece. If intentional, John is portraying Jesus as the true High Priest even in death.
- v.28e: διψῶ (dipsō) — "I thirst." Fulfilling Psalm 69:21. The one who offered living water (ch. 4) now thirsts. The incarnation reaches its lowest point.
- v.29f: The hyssop branch (ὑσσώπῳ, hyssōpō) recalls the original Passover in Exodus 12:22, where hyssop was used to apply the lamb's blood to the doorframes.
- v.30g: τετέλεσται (tetelestai) — "It is finished" or "It is accomplished." A perfect tense verb: the action is complete and its effects endure. The same root appears in 13:1 (εἰς τέλος) — Jesus has loved his own "to the end" and accomplished the work "to completion."
- v.34h: The flow of blood and water has been interpreted medically (pericardial fluid), sacramentally (baptism and eucharist), and symbolically (fulfillment of the living water promise). John emphasizes the eyewitness testimony in v.35, suggesting the event itself carries theological weight he wants readers to contemplate.
