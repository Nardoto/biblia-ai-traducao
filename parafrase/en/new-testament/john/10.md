# John 10

**1** "I'm telling you the truth: anyone who doesn't enter the sheep pen through the gate but climbs in some other way is a thief and a robber. **2** The one who enters through the gate is the shepherd of the sheep. **3** The gatekeeper opens up for him, and the sheep recognize his voice. He calls his own sheep by name and leads them out. **4** When he's brought all his own out, he walks ahead of them, and the sheep follow him because they know his voice. **5** They won't follow a stranger — they'll actually run from him, because they don't recognize a stranger's voice."

**6** Jesus used this figure of speech with them, but they didn't understand what he was getting at.

**7** So Jesus said again, "I'm telling you the truth: I am the gate for the sheep. **8** Everyone who came before me was a thief and a robber, and the sheep didn't listen to them. **9** I am the gate. Whoever enters through me will be saved — they'll come in and go out and find good pasture. **10** The thief only comes to steal, slaughter, and destroy. I came so they could have life — life in all its fullness.ᵃ

**11** "I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep. **12** The hired hand — the one who isn't really the shepherd and doesn't own the sheep — sees the wolf coming and abandons them. He runs off, and the wolf snatches and scatters the flock. **13** The hired hand runs because he's just in it for the paycheck — he doesn't actually care about the sheep.

**14** "I am the good shepherd. I know my sheep and my sheep know me — **15** the same way the Father knows me and I know the Father. And I lay down my life for the sheep. **16** I have other sheep too — sheep that aren't from this pen. I need to bring them in as well. They'll hear my voice, and there will be one flock with one shepherd.ᵇ

**17** "The Father loves me for this reason: I lay down my life — so that I can take it back up again. **18** Nobody takes it from me. I lay it down on my own. I have the authority to lay it down and the authority to take it back up. This is the assignment I received from my Father."

**19** These words caused another split among the Jewish leaders. **20** Many of them said, "He's got a demon — he's lost his mind. Why are you even listening to him?" **21** Others said, "These aren't the words of someone with a demon. Can a demon open the eyes of the blind?"

**22** Then came the Festival of Dedication in Jerusalem. It was winter.ᶜ **23** Jesus was walking in the temple, in Solomon's Colonnade. **24** The Jewish leaders surrounded him and said, "How long are you going to keep us in suspense? If you're the Messiah, just tell us straight."

**25** Jesus answered, "I already told you and you don't believe. The things I do in my Father's name — they testify about me. **26** But you don't believe because you're not my sheep. **27** My sheep hear my voice. I know them, and they follow me. **28** I give them life that never ends, and they'll never be destroyed. Nobody can snatch them out of my hand. **29** My Father, who gave them to me, is greater than all — and nobody can snatch them out of the Father's hand. **30** The Father and I are one."ᵈ

**31** The Jewish leaders picked up stones again to stone him.

**32** Jesus said, "I've shown you many good works from the Father. Which one are you stoning me for?"

**33** They answered, "We're not stoning you for a good work. We're stoning you for blasphemy — because you, a mere man, are making yourself God."

**34** Jesus replied, "Isn't it written in your law, 'I said, you are gods'?ᵉ **35** If he called them 'gods' — the people who received God's word — and Scripture can't be set aside, **36** then how can you say that the one the Father set apart and sent into the world is blaspheming because I said, 'I am God's Son'?

**37** "If I'm not doing my Father's works, don't believe me. **38** But if I am doing them, even if you don't believe me, believe the works — so you can understand and know that the Father is in me and I'm in the Father."

**39** They tried to seize him again, but he slipped through their hands.

**40** He went back across the Jordan to the place where John had first been baptizing, and he stayed there. **41** Many people came to him and said, "John never performed a sign, but everything John said about this man was true." **42** And many believed in him there.

## Notes

- v.10a: ζωὴν ... περισσὸν (zōēn ... perisson) — life "abundantly" or "to the full." Not just survival, but a life overflowing with quality and meaning. The contrast with the thief's agenda (steal, kill, destroy) is stark.
- v.16b: ἄλλα πρόβατα (alla probata) — "other sheep" — likely refers to Gentile believers, those outside the Jewish fold. The vision is of one unified community under one shepherd, transcending ethnic boundaries.
- v.22c: τὰ ἐγκαίνια (ta enkainia) — the Festival of Dedication, known today as Hanukkah. It celebrated the rededication of the temple by the Maccabees in 164 BC. John notes it was winter, setting the physical and perhaps emotional atmosphere.
- v.30d: ἐγὼ καὶ ὁ πατὴρ ἕν ἐσμεν — "I and the Father are one." The neuter ἕν (hen, "one thing") rather than masculine εἷς (heis, "one person") suggests unity of nature and purpose rather than identity of person. The Jewish leaders understood it as a claim to deity (v.33).
- v.34e: Psalm 82:6. Jesus uses a lesser-to-greater argument (qal va-chomer): if Scripture can apply the word "gods" to human judges, how much more appropriate is "Son of God" for the one uniquely sent and consecrated by the Father?
