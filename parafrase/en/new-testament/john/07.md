# John 7

**1** After this, Jesus traveled around Galilee. He deliberately stayed away from Judea because the Jewish leaders there were trying to kill him. **2** But the Jewish Festival of Sheltersᵃ was coming up, **3** and his brothers said to him, "Leave here and go to Judea so your followers there can see what you're doing. **4** Nobody who wants to be well-known does things in secret. If you're really doing all this, show yourself to the world." **5** Even his own brothers didn't believe in him.

**6** Jesus told them, "My time hasn't come yet. But for you, any time works. **7** The world can't hate you, but it hates me because I keep telling it that what it does is wrong. **8** You go on up to the festival. I'm not going up yet — my time hasn't fully come." **9** After saying this, he stayed behind in Galilee.

**10** But after his brothers left for the festival, he went too — just not publicly. He went quietly. **11** The Jewish leaders were looking for him at the festival, asking, "Where is that man?" **12** There was a lot of whispering about him among the crowds. Some said, "He's a good man." Others said, "No — he's leading people astray." **13** But nobody spoke about him openly, because they were afraid of the Jewish leaders.

**14** Halfway through the festival, Jesus went up to the temple and started teaching. **15** The Jewish leaders were astonished: "How does this man know so much? He never formally studied!"

**16** Jesus answered, "What I teach doesn't come from me — it comes from the one who sent me. **17** Anyone who genuinely wants to do God's will is going to recognize whether my teaching is from God or whether I'm just making things up. **18** A person who speaks on their own behalf is chasing their own glory. But someone who seeks the glory of the one who sent him — that person is truthful, and there's nothing false in them.

**19** "Didn't Moses give you the law? Yet not one of you actually keeps it. So why are you trying to kill me?"

**20** The crowd answered, "You've got a demon! Who's trying to kill you?"

**21** Jesus said, "I did one thing and you're all shocked by it. **22** Moses gave you circumcision — not that it actually originated with Moses; it came from the patriarchs — and you circumcise a boy on the Sabbath. **23** If a boy can be circumcised on the Sabbath without breaking Moses' law, why are you furious with me for making a whole person well on the Sabbath? **24** Stop judging by appearances. Judge by what's right."

**25** Some of the people from Jerusalem started saying, "Isn't this the man they're trying to kill? **26** And look — here he is, speaking out in the open, and nobody's saying a word to him. Could the leaders have actually concluded that he's the Messiah? **27** But we know where this man is from. When the Messiah comes, nobody's supposed to know where he's from."

**28** So Jesus, while teaching in the temple, raised his voice and said, "You think you know me and you think you know where I'm from. But I didn't come on my own. The one who sent me is real — and you don't know him. **29** I know him because I come from him, and he's the one who sent me."

**30** They tried to arrest him, but nobody laid a hand on him — his time hadn't come yet. **31** Still, many in the crowd put their trust in him. They said, "When the Messiah comes, is he going to do more signs than this man has done?"

**32** The Pharisees heard the crowd buzzing about him, so the chief priests and Pharisees sent temple guards to arrest him.

**33** Jesus said, "I'm only going to be with you a little while longer, and then I'm going back to the one who sent me. **34** You'll look for me and you won't find me, and where I'm going, you can't come."

**35** The Jewish leaders said to each other, "Where does he think he's going that we can't find him? Is he going to go to the Jews scattered among the Greeks and teach the Greeks? **36** What did he mean, 'You'll look for me and won't find me, and where I'm going you can't come'?"

**37** On the last and greatest day of the festival, Jesus stood up and called out, "If anyone is thirsty, come to me and drink! **38** Whoever trusts in me — as Scripture has said — rivers of living water will flow from deep inside them."ᵇ **39** He was talking about the Spirit, whom those who believed in him would later receive. The Spirit hadn't been given yet because Jesus hadn't yet been glorified.

**40** When they heard these words, some in the crowd said, "This really is the Prophet." **41** Others said, "This is the Messiah." But some objected, "The Messiah doesn't come from Galilee, does he? **42** Doesn't Scripture say the Messiah comes from David's line and from Bethlehem, the village David was from?" **43** So the crowd was divided over him. **44** Some wanted to arrest him, but nobody actually touched him.

**45** The temple guards went back to the chief priests and Pharisees, who demanded, "Why didn't you bring him in?"

**46** The guards answered, "Nobody has ever spoken the way this man speaks."

**47** The Pharisees shot back, "Have you been fooled too? **48** Has a single one of the leaders or Pharisees believed in him? No! **49** This crowd that doesn't know the law — they're cursed."

**50** Nicodemus — the one who had come to Jesus earlier — spoke up: **51** "Our law doesn't condemn someone without hearing them first and finding out what they've done, does it?"

**52** They snapped at him, "Are you from Galilee too? Look it up — no prophet comes from Galilee."ᶜ

**53** Then everyone went home.

## Notes

- v.2a: ἡ σκηνοπηγία (hē skēnopēgia) — the Festival of Tabernacles or Booths (Sukkot), a week-long autumn festival celebrating the harvest and commemorating Israel's wilderness wandering. It involved water-pouring and light ceremonies, which frame Jesus' declarations in chapters 7-8.
- v.37-38b: The punctuation and referent of this passage are debated. Some read it as: "If anyone is thirsty, let him come to me, and let the one who believes in me drink. As Scripture says, 'Rivers of living water will flow from within him'" — where "him" could refer to either Jesus or the believer. The paraphrase follows the reading where the rivers flow from the believer, which fits the Spirit-giving context of v.39.
- v.52c: The claim "no prophet comes from Galilee" is historically inaccurate — Jonah was from Gath-hepher in Galilee (2 Kings 14:25). Some manuscripts read "the prophet" (ὁ προφήτης), referring specifically to the expected Mosaic prophet. The leaders' dismissal is driven by prejudice, not scholarship.
