# John 4

**1** When Jesus found out that the Pharisees had heard he was gaining and baptizing more followers than John — **2** though actually it was his followers doing the baptizing, not Jesus himself — **3** he left Judea and headed back toward Galilee.

**4** He had to go through Samaria on the way. **5** He came to a Samaritan town called Sychar, near the piece of land Jacob had given to his son Joseph. **6** Jacob's well was there, and Jesus, worn out from the journey, sat down by it. It was around noon.

**7** A Samaritan woman came to draw water. Jesus said to her, "Give me a drink." **8** His followers had gone into town to buy food.

**9** The woman said, "You're a Jewish man, and I'm a Samaritan woman — how can you ask me for a drink?" (Jews and Samaritans wanted nothing to do with each other.)ᵃ

**10** Jesus answered, "If you had any idea what God wants to give you — and who it is asking you for water — you'd be the one asking him, and he would've given you living water."

**11** "Sir," she said, "you don't even have a bucket, and this well is deep. Where exactly are you going to get this 'living water'? **12** You're not greater than our ancestor Jacob, are you? He gave us this well — he drank from it himself, along with his sons and his livestock."

**13** Jesus said, "Everyone who drinks this water will get thirsty again. **14** But whoever drinks the water I give them will never be thirsty again. The water I give becomes a spring inside them, bubbling up into life that never ends."

**15** The woman said, "Sir, give me this water so I won't get thirsty and won't have to keep coming out here to draw."

**16** He told her, "Go get your husband and come back."

**17** "I don't have a husband," she said.

Jesus replied, "You're right — you don't have a husband. **18** The truth is, you've had five husbands, and the man you're with now isn't your husband. What you just said is completely honest."

**19** "Sir," the woman said, "I can tell you're a prophet. **20** Our ancestors worshiped on this mountain, but you Jews claim that Jerusalem is the only place where people should worship."

**21** Jesus told her, "Believe me, a time is coming when you won't worship the Father on this mountain or in Jerusalem. **22** You Samaritans worship what you don't fully know. We worship what we do know, because salvation comes through the Jewish people.ᵇ **23** But a time is coming — and it's already here — when the real worshipers will worship the Father in spirit and in truth. Those are the kind of worshipers the Father is looking for. **24** God is spirit, and the people who worship him have to worship in spirit and truth."

**25** The woman said, "I know the Messiah is coming — the one they call the Anointed One. When he gets here, he'll explain everything to us."

**26** Jesus said, "I am he — the one talking to you right now."ᶜ

**27** Just then his followers came back. They were stunned to find him talking with a woman, but none of them asked, "What do you want?" or "Why are you talking to her?"

**28** The woman left her water jar behind, went back into town, and said to the people, **29** "Come see this man who told me everything I've ever done. Could this be the Messiah?" **30** They streamed out of town and headed toward him.

**31** Meanwhile, his followers were urging him, "Rabbi, eat something."

**32** But he said, "I have food you don't know about."

**33** The followers whispered to each other, "Did somebody already bring him something to eat?"

**34** Jesus said, "My food is to do the will of the one who sent me and to finish his work. **35** Don't you have a saying — 'Four more months, then comes the harvest'? Open your eyes and look at the fields — they're already ripe for harvesting. **36** The reaper is already getting paid and gathering a crop for eternal life, so the one who plants and the one who harvests can celebrate together. **37** Here's where that old saying rings true: 'One person plants, another person harvests.' **38** I'm sending you to harvest what you didn't work for. Other people did the hard work, and you're stepping into what they built."

**39** A lot of Samaritans from that town put their trust in him because of the woman's testimony: "He told me everything I've ever done." **40** So when the Samaritans came to him, they begged him to stay with them, and he stayed two days. **41** Many more believed because of what they heard directly from him. **42** They told the woman, "We don't just believe anymore because of what you said. We've heard him ourselves now, and we know — this really is the Savior of the world."

**43** After the two days, he left for Galilee. **44** Jesus himself had pointed out that a prophet gets no respect in his own hometown. **45** When he arrived in Galilee, the Galileans welcomed him warmly because they'd seen everything he'd done in Jerusalem during the festival — they'd been there too.

**46** So he came back to Cana of Galilee, where he'd turned the water into wine. There was a royal official whose son was sick in Capernaum. **47** When this man heard that Jesus had come from Judea to Galilee, he went to him and begged him to come down and heal his son, who was close to death.

**48** Jesus said to him, "Unless you people see signs and wonders, you just won't believe."

**49** The official said, "Sir, please come down before my little boy dies."

**50** Jesus told him, "Go home. Your son is going to live." The man took Jesus at his word and left.

**51** While he was still on his way home, his servants met him with the news: "Your boy is alive!" **52** He asked them what time the boy had gotten better. They said, "The fever broke yesterday at one in the afternoon." **53** The father realized that was the exact moment Jesus had told him, "Your son is going to live." And he and his whole household believed.

**54** This was the second sign Jesus performed after coming from Judea into Galilee.

## Notes

- v.9a: The Greek συγχράομαι (synchraomai) can mean "use together with" or "have dealings with." The hostility between Jews and Samaritans was centuries old, rooted in ethnic, religious, and political division. Jesus crosses every social boundary in this conversation — ethnic, religious, and gender.
- v.22b: "Salvation comes through the Jewish people" — ἡ σωτηρία ἐκ τῶν Ἰουδαίων ἐστίν. Jesus affirms the historical role of the Jewish people while simultaneously transcending the temple-versus-mountain debate.
- v.26c: ἐγώ εἰμι (egō eimi) — "I am." This is one of the great self-revelations of Jesus in John's Gospel. The phrase echoes the divine name from Exodus 3:14. Here Jesus makes his identity plain to a Samaritan woman — an outsider — before he reveals it publicly to the Jewish establishment.
- v.35: The harvest imagery may refer to the Samaritans already making their way toward Jesus across the fields.
- v.42: σωτὴρ τοῦ κόσμου (sōtēr tou kosmou) — "Savior of the world" — notably, it's the Samaritans, not the Jewish establishment, who arrive at this title.
