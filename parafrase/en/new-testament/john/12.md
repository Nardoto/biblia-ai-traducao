# John 12

**1** Six days before the Passover, Jesus came to Bethany, where Lazarus lived — the man he'd raised from the dead. **2** They prepared a dinner for him there. Martha was serving, and Lazarus was one of the people reclining at the table with him. **3** Mary took about a pint of pure nard — extremely expensive perfume — poured it on Jesus' feet, and wiped his feet with her hair. The whole house filled with the fragrance.ᵃ

**4** But Judas Iscariot — one of his followers, the one who would later betray him — objected: **5** "Why wasn't this perfume sold for a year's wages and the money given to the poor?" **6** He didn't say this because he cared about the poor. He was a thief — he was in charge of the money bag and helped himself to what was in it.

**7** Jesus said, "Leave her alone. She kept this for the day of my burial. **8** You'll always have the poor among you, but you won't always have me."

**9** A large crowd of Jewish people found out Jesus was there. They came not just because of him but also to see Lazarus, the man he'd raised from the dead. **10** So the chief priests made plans to kill Lazarus too, **11** because on account of him many of the Jewish people were deserting them and putting their trust in Jesus.

**12** The next day, the huge crowd that had come for the festival heard that Jesus was on his way to Jerusalem. **13** They grabbed palm branches and went out to meet him, shouting, "Hosanna! Blessed is the one who comes in the name of the Lord — the King of Israel!"

**14** Jesus found a young donkey and sat on it — just as it's written: **15** "Don't be afraid, daughter of Zion. Look — your king is coming, sitting on a donkey's colt."ᵇ

**16** His followers didn't understand any of this at the time. But after Jesus was glorified, they remembered that these things had been written about him and that they'd done these things for him.

**17** The crowd that had been with him when he called Lazarus out of the tomb and raised him from the dead kept spreading the word. **18** That's also why the crowd went out to meet him — they'd heard he'd done this sign. **19** The Pharisees said to each other, "See? We're getting nowhere. The whole world has gone after him."

**20** Now among those who went up to worship at the festival were some Greeks.ᶜ **21** They approached Philip — who was from Bethsaida in Galilee — and said, "Sir, we'd like to see Jesus." **22** Philip went and told Andrew, and then Andrew and Philip together went and told Jesus.

**23** Jesus responded, "The hour has come for the Son of Man to be glorified. **24** I'm telling you the truth: unless a grain of wheat falls into the ground and dies, it stays just a single seed. But if it dies, it produces a great harvest. **25** Anyone who loves their own life will lose it. But anyone who hates their life in this worldᵈ will keep it for life that never ends. **26** If someone wants to serve me, they need to follow me. And where I am, my servant will be too. The Father will honor anyone who serves me.

**27** "Right now my soul is in turmoil. What should I say — 'Father, save me from this hour'? No. This is the very reason I came to this hour. **28** Father, glorify your name!"

Then a voice came from heaven: "I have glorified it, and I'll glorify it again."

**29** The crowd standing there heard it and said it was thunder. Others said, "An angel spoke to him."

**30** Jesus said, "That voice wasn't for my benefit — it was for yours. **31** Now is the judgment of this world. Now the ruler of this world will be thrown out. **32** And when I'm lifted up from the earth, I'll draw everyone to myself." **33** He said this to indicate the kind of death he was about to die.

**34** The crowd answered, "We've heard from the law that the Messiah remains forever. So how can you say the Son of Man must be 'lifted up'? Who is this Son of Man?"

**35** Jesus told them, "The light is with you for just a little while longer. Walk while you have the light, before the darkness overtakes you. Whoever walks in the dark doesn't know where they're going. **36** While you have the light, trust in the light, so you can become children of light."

After saying this, Jesus left and hid himself from them.

**37** Even though he'd performed so many signs right in front of them, they still didn't believe in him — **38** fulfilling what the prophet Isaiah said: "Lord, who has believed our message? And to whom has the Lord's arm been revealed?"ᵉ **39** The reason they couldn't believe was that Isaiah also said: **40** "He has blinded their eyes and hardened their hearts, so they can't see with their eyes or understand with their hearts or turn — and be healed by me."ᶠ **41** Isaiah said this because he saw his glory and spoke about him.

**42** Even so, many of the leaders did believe in him. But because of the Pharisees, they wouldn't say so openly — they were afraid of being thrown out of the synagogue. **43** They loved human approval more than God's approval.

**44** Jesus called out, "Whoever trusts in me isn't just trusting in me — they're trusting in the one who sent me. **45** And whoever sees me sees the one who sent me. **46** I've come as light into the world, so that no one who trusts in me has to stay in the dark.

**47** "If anyone hears my words and doesn't keep them, I'm not the one who judges them. I didn't come to judge the world — I came to save it. **48** Whoever rejects me and doesn't accept my words already has a judge: the very words I've spoken will judge them on the last day. **49** Because I haven't spoken on my own. The Father who sent me — he's the one who told me what to say and how to say it. **50** And I know that his command leads to life that never ends. So whatever I say, I say exactly the way the Father told me."

## Notes

- v.3a: νάρδου πιστικῆς (nardou pistikēs) — "pure nard" or "genuine nard." Nard was imported from the Himalayas, making it extraordinarily expensive. The quantity (a Roman pound, about 340ml) represents extravagant, uncalculating devotion. The detail about the fragrance filling the house is unique to John.
- v.15b: Quoting Zechariah 9:9. The original prophecy emphasizes the king's humility and peace — arriving on a donkey, not a war horse. The crowd shouting royal titles while Jesus rides a donkey creates a deliberate tension between their expectations and his method.
- v.20c: Ἕλληνές τινες (Hellēnes tines) — "certain Greeks" — likely Gentile God-fearers who came to worship at the festival. Their appearance triggers Jesus' declaration that "the hour has come," as if the arrival of the nations signals the climax of his mission.
- v.25d: μισεῖ (misei) — "hates" — a Semitic idiom expressing radical priority, not emotional hatred. It means to place something in decisively lower priority.
- v.38e: Isaiah 53:1 — the Suffering Servant passage. John applies it to explain Israel's unbelief in the face of Jesus' signs.
- v.40f: Isaiah 6:10, from Isaiah's vision in the temple. John attributes the hardening to God's sovereign action while holding it in tension with genuine human responsibility (v.42-43). This is not fatalism — the leaders chose approval over faith.
