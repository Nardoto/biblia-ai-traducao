# John 11

**1** A man named Lazarus was sick. He was from Bethany, the village where Mary and her sister Martha lived. **2** This Mary was the one who later poured perfume on the Lord and wiped his feet with her hair. It was her brother Lazarus who was ill. **3** So the sisters sent word to Jesus: "Lord, the one you love dearly is sick."ᵃ

**4** When Jesus heard this, he said, "This sickness isn't going to end in death. It's for God's glory — so that God's Son can be glorified through it."

**5** Now Jesus loved Martha, her sister, and Lazarus deeply.ᵇ **6** Yet when he heard Lazarus was sick, he stayed where he was for two more days. **7** Then he said to his followers, "Let's go back to Judea."

**8** They said, "Rabbi, the Jewish leaders were just trying to stone you there — and you want to go back?"

**9** Jesus answered, "Aren't there twelve hours of daylight? Anyone who walks during the day doesn't stumble, because they see by this world's light. **10** But if someone walks at night, they stumble because the light isn't in them." **11** Then he added, "Our friend Lazarus has fallen asleep, but I'm going there to wake him up."

**12** His followers said, "Lord, if he's sleeping, he'll get better."

**13** Jesus was talking about death, but they thought he meant actual sleep. **14** So Jesus told them plainly, "Lazarus is dead. **15** And for your sake, I'm glad I wasn't there — so that you'll believe. But let's go to him."

**16** Thomas — called the Twin — said to the other followers, "Let's go too, so we can die with him."

**17** When Jesus arrived, he found that Lazarus had already been in the tomb for four days.ᶜ **18** Bethany was less than two miles from Jerusalem, **19** and many of the Jewish people had come to comfort Martha and Mary about their brother.

**20** When Martha heard that Jesus was coming, she went out to meet him. Mary stayed at the house. **21** Martha said to Jesus, "Lord, if you'd been here, my brother wouldn't have died. **22** But even now I know that whatever you ask God, God will give you."

**23** Jesus said, "Your brother will rise again."

**24** Martha answered, "I know he'll rise in the resurrection on the last day."

**25** Jesus told her, "I am the resurrection and the life. Whoever trusts in me will live, even if they die. **26** And everyone who lives and trusts in me will never die — ever. Do you believe this?"

**27** "Yes, Lord," she said. "I believe that you're the Messiah, the Son of God — the one coming into the world."

**28** After saying this, she went back and quietly told her sister Mary, "The Teacher is here and he's asking for you." **29** When Mary heard this, she got up quickly and went to him. **30** Jesus hadn't entered the village yet — he was still at the place where Martha had met him. **31** The Jewish people who'd been in the house comforting Mary saw her get up and hurry out, so they followed her, assuming she was going to the tomb to weep there.

**32** When Mary reached the spot where Jesus was and saw him, she fell at his feet and said, "Lord, if you'd been here, my brother wouldn't have died."

**33** When Jesus saw her crying, and the Jewish people who'd come with her crying too, he was deeply moved in his spirit and visibly shaken.ᵈ **34** He asked, "Where have you laid him?"

"Come and see, Lord," they said.

**35** Jesus wept.ᵉ

**36** The Jewish people said, "Look how much he loved him."

**37** But some of them said, "He opened the eyes of the blind man — couldn't he have kept this man from dying?"

**38** Jesus, deeply moved again, came to the tomb. It was a cave with a stone laid across the entrance. **39** "Take away the stone," Jesus said.

Martha, the dead man's sister, said, "Lord, by now there'll be a terrible smell. He's been dead four days."

**40** Jesus said, "Didn't I tell you that if you believe, you'll see God's glory?"

**41** So they removed the stone. Jesus looked up and said, "Father, thank you for hearing me. **42** I know you always hear me, but I'm saying this out loud for the people standing here — so they'll believe you sent me."

**43** After saying this, he shouted in a loud voice, "Lazarus, come out!"

**44** The dead man came out, his hands and feet wrapped in burial cloths, his face covered with a cloth. Jesus told them, "Unwrap him and let him go."

**45** Many of the Jewish people who had come to visit Mary and saw what Jesus did put their trust in him. **46** But some went to the Pharisees and told them what had happened.

**47** So the chief priests and Pharisees called an emergency meeting of the council. "What are we going to do?" they said. "This man is performing a lot of signs. **48** If we let him keep going, everyone's going to believe in him, and then the Romans will come and take away our temple and our nation."

**49** One of them — Caiaphas, who was high priest that year — said, "You people don't understand anything. **50** Don't you see that it's better for you if one man dies for the people than for the whole nation to be destroyed?"

**51** He didn't come up with this on his own. As high priest that year, he was actually prophesying that Jesus was going to die for the nation — **52** and not just for that nation, but to gather together all of God's scattered children into one.ᶠ

**53** From that day on, they plotted to kill him.

**54** So Jesus stopped moving about publicly among the Jewish leaders. He withdrew to a region near the wilderness, to a town called Ephraim, and stayed there with his followers.

**55** The Jewish Passover was near, and many people went up to Jerusalem from the countryside before the festival to purify themselves. **56** They kept looking for Jesus, and as they stood around in the temple they asked each other, "What do you think? Is he going to come to the festival or not?" **57** The chief priests and Pharisees had given orders that if anyone knew where Jesus was, they should report it so they could arrest him.

## Notes

- v.3a: The Greek φιλεῖς (phileis) — the sisters describe Lazarus as the one Jesus "has affection for," using the warm, personal love word.
- v.5b: Here John uses ἠγάπα (ēgapa) — the self-giving love (agapaō) — to describe Jesus' love for all three siblings. The shift from the sisters' phileis to John's agapaō is characteristic of John's interplay between these love words.
- v.17c: Four days was significant — Jewish tradition held that the soul lingered near the body for three days. By the fourth day, there was no ambiguity: Lazarus was irreversibly dead.
- v.33d: ἐνεβριμήσατο τῷ πνεύματι καὶ ἐτάραξεν ἑαυτόν — the Greek is remarkably strong. ἐμβριμάομαι (embrimaomai) carries the sense of indignation or deep groaning, not just sorrow. Jesus may be angry at death itself, or profoundly moved by the grief around him. "Deeply moved and visibly shaken" tries to hold both dimensions.
- v.35e: ἐδάκρυσεν ὁ Ἰησοῦς — the shortest verse in the Bible. The verb δακρύω (dakryō) indicates quiet tears, distinct from the loud wailing (κλαίω, klaiō) of the crowd in v.33.
- v.52f: John sees Caiaphas' political calculation as unwitting prophecy — Jesus' death would indeed gather dispersed people, but not the way Caiaphas imagined.
