# John 16

**1** "I'm telling you all of this so you won't fall apart when things get hard. **2** They're going to kick you out of the synagogues. In fact, a time is coming when people who kill you will actually think they're doing God a favor. **3** They'll do these things because they never really knew the Father — and they don't know me either. **4** But I'm warning you now so that when it happens, you'll remember I told you. I didn't bring this up at the start because I was still right here with you."

**5** "But now I'm heading back to the one who sent me, and not one of you is asking, 'Where are you going?' **6** Instead, your hearts are drowning in grief because of what I've said. **7** But I'm telling you the truth — it's actually better for you that I leave. Because if I don't go, the Helper won't come to you. But when I go, I'll send him your way."

**8** "And when he comes, he's going to expose the world for what it really is — he'll show people the truth about sin, about what's right, and about judgment. **9** About sin, because they refuse to trust me. **10** About what's right, because I'm going to the Father and you won't see me anymore. **11** And about judgment, because the ruler of this world has already been condemned."

**12** "There's so much more I want to tell you, but you can't handle it right now. **13** When the Spirit of truth comes, he'll walk you into the full picture. He won't be making things up on his own — he'll speak what he hears, and he'll show you what's coming down the road. **14** He'll shine a spotlight on me, because everything he shares with you will come from what is mine. **15** Everything the Father has belongs to me — that's why I said the Spirit will take what's mine and unpack it for you."

**16** "In a little while, you won't see me anymore. Then a little while after that, you'll see me again."

**17** Some of his disciples started whispering to each other, "What's he talking about? 'A little while and you won't see me, then a little while and you will'? And this business about 'going to the Father'?" **18** They kept saying, "'A little while' — what does that even mean? We have no idea what he's getting at."

**19** Jesus could tell they wanted to ask him about it, so he said, "You're trying to figure out what I meant when I said, 'In a little while you won't see me, and then a little while later you will.' **20** Here's the honest truth: you're going to cry and grieve while the world throws a party. You'll be devastated — but your devastation will turn into joy. **21** Think about a woman in labor. While she's in the middle of it, the pain is unbearable. But the moment her baby is born, she forgets all the agony because of the sheer joy that a new person has come into the world. **22** That's exactly what's happening with you. Right now you're hurting, but I'm going to see you again, and when I do, your hearts will explode with a joy that nobody — nobody — can take from you."

**23** "When that day comes, you won't need to ask me for anything. I'm telling you straight: whatever you ask the Father using my name, he'll give it to you. **24** Up until now, you haven't asked for anything in my name. Ask — and you'll receive — and your joy will be completely off the charts."

**25** "I've been speaking in metaphors and roundabout language this whole time. But a time is coming when I'll drop the figures of speech and tell you about the Father in plain, direct words. **26** When that day comes, you'll ask in my name. I'm not saying I'll have to go petition the Father on your behalf — **27** the Father himself loves you deeply, because you've loved me and believed that I came from God. **28** I came from the Father into this world. Now I'm leaving this world and going back to the Father."

**29** His disciples said, "Now you're talking! That's clear — no riddles, no metaphors. **30** Now we can see that you know everything — you don't even need anyone to ask you questions. This is why we believe you came from God."

**31** Jesus answered them, "So now you believe? **32** Listen — a time is coming, and it's already here, when you'll all scatter. Every one of you will run off to your own place and leave me completely alone. But I'm not really alone, because the Father is with me."

**33** "I've told you all of this so that in me you can have peace. In this world, you're going to have trouble — count on it. But take heart. I have overcome the world."

## Notes

- v.2: The Greek προσφέρειν λατρείαν (*prospherein latreian*) literally means "to offer service/worship" — they genuinely believe killing believers is an act of devotion to God.
- v.7: παράκλητος (*paraklētos*) — rendered as "Helper." The word carries meanings of advocate, comforter, counselor, and one called alongside. No single English word captures the full range.
- v.8: ἐλέγξει (*elenxei*) means to convict, expose, or bring to light — a legal and moral term, like a prosecutor laying out the evidence.
- v.13: ὁδηγήσει ὑμᾶς εἰς τὴν ἀλήθειαν πᾶσαν — "he will guide you into all the truth." Rendered as "walk you into the full picture" to convey the progressive, relational nature of the Spirit's guidance.
- v.21: The labor metaphor (ἡ γυνὴ ὅταν τίκτῃ) was a well-known prophetic image in Jewish thought for the messianic age's birth pangs (cf. Isaiah 26:17-18).
- v.33: θαρσεῖτε (*tharseite*) — a command meaning "take courage." νενίκηκα (*nenikēka*) is perfect tense — "I have conquered and the victory stands." The world is already defeated.
