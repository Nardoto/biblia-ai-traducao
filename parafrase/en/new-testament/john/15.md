# John 15

**1** "I am the real grapevine, and my Father is the gardener. **2** Every branch connected to me that doesn't produce anything, he cuts off. And every branch that does bear fruit, he prunes back so it'll produce even more. **3** You've already been pruned by the message I've spoken to you. **4** Stay connected to me, and I'll stay connected to you. A branch can't produce fruit on its own if it's cut off from the vine — and neither can you if you're disconnected from me.

**5** "I'm the vine; you're the branches. Whoever stays connected to me — and I to them — will produce an abundance of fruit. But apart from me? You can't do a thing. **6** Anyone who doesn't stay connected to me gets tossed aside like a dead branch. Those branches dry up, get gathered into a pile, and thrown into the fire.

**7** "If you stay connected to me and my words take root in you, ask for whatever you want and it will happen for you. **8** This is how my Father's greatness is displayed — when you produce an abundance of fruit. That's what makes you my disciples.

**9** "I've loved you the same way the Father has loved me. Make your home in that love. **10** If you follow what I've asked of you, you'll stay right in the middle of my love — the same way I've followed what my Father asked and stay in his love.

**11** "I've told you all this so that my joy — my actual joy — will be inside you, and your joy will be completely full.

**12** "Here's what I'm asking of you: love each other the way I've loved you. **13** There's no greater love than this — laying down your life for the people you love. **14** You are my friends if you do what I'm asking. **15** I'm not calling you servants anymore, because a servant has no idea what their master is up to. I've called you friends, because everything I've heard from my Father, I've shared with you.

**16** "You didn't choose me — I chose you. And I appointed you to go out and produce fruit that lasts, so that whatever you ask the Father in my name, he'll give it to you. **17** This is my command: love each other.

**18** "When the world hates you, remember — it hated me first. **19** If you belonged to the world, the world would love you as its own. But I pulled you out of the world, and because you don't belong to it anymore, the world hates you. **20** Remember what I told you: a servant isn't above their master. If they came after me, they'll come after you too. If they took my message to heart, they'll take yours to heart as well. **21** They'll do all this to you because of me — because they don't know the one who sent me.

**22** "If I hadn't come and spoken to them, they wouldn't be guilty. But now they have no excuse for their sin. **23** Whoever hates me hates my Father too. **24** If I hadn't done things among them that nobody else has ever done, they wouldn't be guilty. But they've seen it all — and still they've hated both me and my Father. **25** This fulfills what's written in their own Law: 'They hated me for no reason.'

**26** "When the Companion arrives — the one I'll send to you from the Father, the Spirit of truth who comes from the Father — he will testify about me. **27** And you will testify too, because you've been with me from the very beginning."

---

## Notes

- v.1: The Greek ἄμπελος ἡ ἀληθινή is "the true/real vine." Jesus contrasts himself with Israel, often symbolized as a vine in the Hebrew scriptures (cf. Isaiah 5, Psalm 80). "Real grapevine" captures the concrete agricultural image.
- v.2: The Greek καθαίρει (kathairei, "prunes/cleanses") and καθαροί (katharoi, "clean") in v.3 share the same root — a deliberate wordplay. Pruning and being made clean are linked ideas.
- v.13: The Greek ψυχή (psychē) here means "life/self" rather than just "soul," so "laying down your life" captures the full weight.
- v.15: The shift from δοῦλος (doulos, "slave/servant") to φίλος (philos, "friend") is radical in the ancient context, where the master-slave relationship defined the household. Jesus is overturning social hierarchy.
- v.25: The quote combines Psalm 35:19 and Psalm 69:4. Jesus says "their Law" (τῷ νόμῳ αὐτῶν) — not distancing himself from Scripture, but pointing out the irony that their own sacred texts predicted this rejection.
- v.26: "Companion" continues the translation of παράκλητος (paraklētos) from chapter 14, maintaining consistency. "Who comes from the Father" translates ὃ παρὰ τοῦ πατρὸς ἐκπορεύεται — a theologically significant phrase in the history of the church regarding the procession of the Spirit.
