# John 18

**1** After saying all this, Jesus went out with his followers across the Kidron Valley to a place where there was a garden. He and his followers went into it.ᵃ **2** Judas, who was betraying him, also knew the place because Jesus had often met there with his followers.

**3** So Judas arrived, bringing a detachment of soldiers along with officers sent by the chief priests and Pharisees. They came carrying lanterns, torches, and weapons.

**4** Jesus, knowing everything that was about to happen to him, stepped forward and asked them, "Who are you looking for?"

**5** "Jesus of Nazareth," they said.

"I am he," Jesus answered.ᵇ

Judas, the one betraying him, was standing right there with them. **6** The moment Jesus said "I am he," they stumbled backward and fell to the ground.

**7** He asked them again, "Who are you looking for?"

"Jesus of Nazareth," they said.

**8** Jesus answered, "I told you — I am he. If it's me you're looking for, let these others go." **9** This fulfilled what he'd said earlier: "I haven't lost a single one of those you gave me."

**10** Simon Peter, who had a sword, drew it and struck the high priest's servant, cutting off his right ear. The servant's name was Malchus. **11** Jesus said to Peter, "Put that sword away! Shouldn't I drink the cup the Father has given me?"

**12** The soldiers, their commander, and the Jewish officers arrested Jesus and tied him up. **13** They took him first to Annas, who was the father-in-law of Caiaphas, the high priest that year.ᶜ **14** Caiaphas was the one who had advised the Jewish leaders that it would be better for one man to die for the people.

**15** Simon Peter and another follower trailed behind Jesus. That other follower was known to the high priest and went right into the high priest's courtyard with Jesus. **16** But Peter was left standing outside at the gate. The other follower — the one the high priest knew — went back out, spoke to the woman on duty at the gate, and brought Peter in.

**17** The servant girl at the gate said to Peter, "Aren't you one of this man's followers?"

"I am not," he said.

**18** It was cold, so the servants and officers had made a charcoal fire and were standing around it, warming themselves. Peter was standing with them, warming himself too.

**19** The high priest questioned Jesus about his followers and his teaching. **20** Jesus answered, "I've spoken openly to the world. I always taught in synagogues and in the temple, where all the Jewish people gather. I said nothing in secret. **21** Why are you questioning me? Ask the people who heard me. They know what I said."

**22** When he said this, one of the officers standing nearby slapped Jesus across the face. "Is that how you answer the high priest?" he demanded.

**23** Jesus replied, "If I said something wrong, point out what was wrong. But if I spoke the truth, why did you hit me?"

**24** Annas then sent him, still bound, to Caiaphas the high priest.

**25** Meanwhile, Simon Peter was still standing there warming himself. They asked him, "Aren't you one of his followers?"

He denied it. "I am not," he said.

**26** One of the high priest's servants — a relative of the man whose ear Peter had cut off — challenged him: "Didn't I see you in the garden with him?"

**27** Peter denied it again. And immediately a rooster crowed.

**28** They took Jesus from Caiaphas to the governor's headquarters.ᵈ It was early morning. The Jewish leaders didn't go inside the headquarters themselves — they didn't want to become ritually unclean, since they wanted to eat the Passover meal.

**29** So Pilate came outside to them and asked, "What charge are you bringing against this man?"

**30** They answered, "If he weren't a criminal, we wouldn't have handed him over to you."

**31** Pilate said, "Take him and judge him by your own law."

The Jewish leaders replied, "We're not allowed to execute anyone." **32** This happened to fulfill what Jesus had said about the kind of death he was going to die.

**33** Pilate went back inside, summoned Jesus, and asked him, "Are you the King of the Jews?"

**34** Jesus answered, "Are you asking this on your own, or did others tell you about me?"

**35** Pilate said, "Am I a Jew? Your own nation and chief priests handed you over to me. What did you do?"

**36** Jesus said, "My kingdomᵉ isn't from this world. If my kingdom were from this world, my servants would be fighting to keep me from being handed over to the Jewish leaders. But my kingdom isn't from here."

**37** Pilate said, "So you are a king, then?"

Jesus answered, "You're the one using the word 'king.' The reason I was born, the reason I came into the world, is to testify to the truth. Everyone who belongs to the truth hears my voice."

**38** Pilate said, "What is truth?"

After he said this, he went out to the Jewish leaders again and told them, "I find no basis for any charge against him. **39** But you have a custom that I release one prisoner for you at Passover. Do you want me to release the King of the Jews?"

**40** They shouted back, "Not him! Barabbas!" Now Barabbas was a rebel.ᶠ

## Notes

- v.1a: John doesn't name Gethsemane (as the Synoptics do) but simply calls it a "garden" (κῆπος, kēpos). The crossing of the Kidron Valley echoes David's flight from Absalom (2 Samuel 15:23), adding a layer of royal suffering.
- v.5b: ἐγώ εἰμι (egō eimi) — again the phrase that functions on two levels: the practical "I am he" and the divine "I AM." The soldiers falling back in v.6 suggests they encountered something more than a simple identification.
- v.13c: Annas served as high priest from 6-15 AD and retained enormous influence even after being deposed by Rome. He was the power behind the throne; Caiaphas was the official high priest but Annas was the patriarch of the priestly dynasty.
- v.28d: τὸ πραιτώριον (to praitōrion) — the Roman governor's official residence in Jerusalem, likely either the Antonia Fortress or Herod's palace. The irony is thick: the Jewish leaders avoid ritual defilement from entering a Gentile building while orchestrating the execution of an innocent man.
- v.36e: ἡ βασιλεία ἡ ἐμή (hē basileia hē emē) — Jesus says his kingdom is not "of" (ἐκ, ek) this world — meaning not originating from or deriving its power from worldly systems. He doesn't say it has no relevance to this world.
- v.40f: λῃστής (lēstēs) — often rendered "robber," but in this period the term frequently meant an insurrectionist or violent rebel. The crowd chooses a man of violence over the Prince of Peace.
