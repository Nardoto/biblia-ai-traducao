# 2 Corinthians 12

**1** I have to keep boasting — even though it doesn't do any good. I'll move on to visions and revelations from the Lord. **2** I know a man in Christ who fourteen years ago — whether in the body or out of the body, I don't know, God knows — was caught up to the third heaven. **3** And I know that this man — whether in the body or out of the body, I don't know, God knows — **4** was caught up into paradise and heard inexpressible things, things no human being is allowed to repeat. **5** About such a person I'll boast, but about myself I won't boast — except about my weaknesses. **6** Even if I wanted to boast, I wouldn't be a fool, because I'd be telling the truth. But I hold back, so no one thinks more of me than what they see in me or hear from me.

**7** And to keep me from becoming arrogant because of the extraordinary nature of these revelations, I was given a thorn in the flesh, a messenger of Satan, to torment me and keep me from exalting myself. **8** Three times I pleaded with the Lord to take it away from me. **9** But he told me, "My grace is sufficient for you, because my power is made perfect in weakness." So I'll gladly boast all the more about my weaknesses, so that Christ's power may rest on me. **10** That's why I take pleasure in weaknesses, in insults, in hardships, in persecutions, in difficulties, for Christ's sake. For when I'm weak, that's when I'm strong.

**11** I've become a fool — you forced me into it. You should've been the ones commending me, since I'm not one bit inferior to the "super-apostles," even though I'm nothing. **12** The signs of an apostle were performed among you with all perseverance — signs, wonders, and miracles. **13** How were you treated as inferior to the other churches, except that I wasn't a financial burden to you? Forgive me this "injustice"!

**14** Look, I'm ready to visit you a third time, and I won't be a burden. I'm not after what you have — I'm after you. After all, children shouldn't have to save up for their parents; parents save up for their children. **15** I'll gladly spend everything I have and be spent for your souls. If I love you more, will I be loved less?

**16** Be that as it may, I wasn't a burden to you. But "crafty" as I am, I caught you by trickery! **17** Did I exploit you through anyone I sent? **18** I urged Titus to go and sent the brother along with him. Did Titus exploit you? Didn't we walk in the same spirit? Didn't we follow the same steps?

**19** Have you been thinking all along that we're defending ourselves to you? We're speaking before God in Christ — and everything, dear friends, is for building you up. **20** I'm afraid that when I come, I may not find you the way I want, and you may not find me the way you want. I'm afraid there may be quarreling, jealousy, outbursts of anger, selfish ambition, slander, gossip, arrogance, and disorder. **21** I'm afraid that when I come again, my God may humble me before you, and I may have to grieve over many who sinned earlier and haven't repented of the impurity, sexual immorality, and excess they've indulged in.

---

## Notes

- v.2: "Third heaven" — in Jewish cosmology, the highest level of God's presence; Paul speaks of himself in the third person out of humility.
- v.4: "Inexpressible things" (ἄρρητα ῥήματα) — literally "unspeakable words"; an intentional paradox.
- v.7: The "thorn in the flesh" (σκόλοψ τῇ σαρκί) is one of Paul's great mysteries — suggestions include eye disease, malaria, epilepsy, depression, or constant opposition. The text deliberately doesn't specify.
- v.9: "My power is made perfect in weakness" (ἡ δύναμίς μου ἐν ἀσθενείᾳ τελεῖται) — this is the central theology of 2 Corinthians: God's strength operates precisely where human strength fails.
