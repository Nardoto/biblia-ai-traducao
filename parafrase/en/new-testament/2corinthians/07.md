# 2 Corinthians 7

**1** Since we have these promises, dear friends, let's purify ourselves from everything that contaminates body and spirit, perfecting holiness in the fear of God.

**2** Make room for us in your hearts! We haven't wronged anyone, we haven't corrupted anyone, we haven't exploited anyone. **3** I'm not saying this to condemn you. I've already said that you're in our hearts — to die together and to live together. **4** I have great confidence in you and great pride in you. I'm filled with encouragement; my joy overflows in the midst of all our trouble.

**5** When we arrived in Macedonia, our bodies had no rest at all. We were pressured from every side — conflicts on the outside, fears on the inside. **6** But God, who comforts the downcast, comforted us with the arrival of Titus, **7** and not just with his arrival, but with the comfort he received from you. He told us about your longing, your mourning, your zeal for me — and it made me even happier.

**8** Because even though my letter caused you grief, I don't regret it. I did regret it at first — I could see that letter grieved you, even if only for a short time. **9** Now I'm glad, not because you were grieved, but because your grief led to repentance. You were grieved in a godly way, so you weren't harmed by us in any way. **10** For godly grief produces repentance that leads to salvation and leaves no regret. But worldly grief produces death. **11** Look at what this godly grief produced in you: what earnestness, what eagerness to clear yourselves, what indignation, what alarm, what longing, what zeal, what readiness to see justice done! At every point you've proved yourselves innocent in this matter.

**12** So even though I wrote that letter, it wasn't because of the one who did the wrong, or because of the one who was wronged, but so that your devotion to us would be made clear before God. **13** That's why we were comforted.

Beyond our own comfort, we were even more delighted by Titus's joy, because his spirit was refreshed by all of you. **14** If I boasted about you to him, I wasn't put to shame. But just as everything we said to you was true, our boasting to Titus also proved to be true. **15** And his affection for you grows even stronger as he remembers the obedience of all of you and how you received him with fear and trembling. **16** I'm glad that I can have complete confidence in you.

---

## Notes

- v.5: "Conflicts outside, fears inside" — Paul reveals a rare vulnerability; even the apostle experienced fear.
- v.8: The "letter" mentioned is likely the so-called "severe letter" (not preserved), distinct from 1 Corinthians.
- v.10: "Godly grief" (κατὰ θεὸν λύπη) vs. "worldly grief" (τοῦ κόσμου λύπη) — a fundamental distinction between remorse that transforms and remorse that only destroys.
- v.15: "Fear and trembling" (φόβου καὶ τρόμου) — an idiomatic expression indicating deep respect, not terror; Paul uses it in other letters (Phil 2:12).
