# 2 Corinthians 4

**1** That's why, since we've received this ministry through God's mercy, we don't give up. **2** Instead, we've renounced everything shameful and underhanded. We don't use clever tricks or distort God's word. On the contrary, by openly declaring the truth, we commend ourselves to everyone's conscience before God. **3** And if our gospel is still veiled, it's veiled to those who are perishing. **4** The god of this age has blinded the minds of unbelievers so they can't see the light of the gospel of Christ's glory — Christ who is the image of God. **5** We don't preach ourselves but Christ Jesus as Lord — and ourselves as your servants for Jesus' sake. **6** For the same God who said "Let light shine out of darkness" has shone his light in our hearts, so we could know God's glory in the face of Christ.

**7** But we carry this treasure in jars of clay, to make it clear that this extraordinary power comes from God and not from us. **8** We're pressed on every side but not crushed. We're at a loss but never without a way out. **9** We're persecuted but not abandoned. We're knocked down but not destroyed. **10** We always carry around in our bodies the death of Jesus, so that the life of Jesus may also be revealed in our bodies. **11** For we who are alive are constantly being handed over to death for Jesus' sake, so that Jesus' life may be revealed in our mortal flesh. **12** So death is at work in us, but life is at work in you.

**13** Having the same spirit of faith as what's written — "I believed, and so I spoke" — we also believe and so we speak. **14** We know that the one who raised the Lord Jesus will also raise us with Jesus and present us together with you. **15** All of this is for your sake, so that as grace extends to more and more people, it overflows in thanksgiving to God's glory.

**16** That's why we don't lose heart. Even though our outer self is wasting away, our inner self is being renewed day by day. **17** For our light and momentary affliction is producing for us an eternal weight of glory that's beyond all comparison. **18** We don't focus on what we can see but on what we can't see. Because what's visible is temporary, but what's invisible is eternal.

---

## Notes

- v.4: "God of this age" (ὁ θεὸς τοῦ αἰῶνος τούτου) — a reference to Satan as ruler of the present age, not as an actual deity.
- v.7: "Jars of clay" (ὀστρακίνοις σκεύεσιν) — cheap, fragile everyday containers, contrasting with the priceless treasure they carry.
- v.13: Quotation from Psalm 116:10 (LXX 115:1).
- v.17: "Light and momentary" (τὸ παραυτίκα ἐλαφρὸν) — the irony is intentional; Paul describes brutal suffering as "light" in view of eternal glory.
