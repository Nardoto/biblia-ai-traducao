# 2 Corinthians 2

**1** So I made up my mind not to make another painful visit to you. **2** Because if I make you sad, who's going to cheer me up? Only the ones I've made sad! **3** That's exactly why I wrote that letter — so I wouldn't show up and be saddened by the very people who should bring me joy. I'm confident about all of you that my joy is your joy too. **4** I wrote to you out of deep anguish, with a heavy heart and many tears — not to make you sad, but so you'd know just how much I love you.

**5** If someone caused grief, he didn't grieve me so much as — not to put it too strongly — all of you, at least to some extent. **6** The punishment the majority of you imposed on this person is enough. **7** Now instead, you should forgive and comfort him, so he isn't swallowed up by overwhelming sorrow. **8** So I urge you to reaffirm your love for him. **9** This was also why I wrote: to test you and see if you're obedient in everything. **10** Whoever you forgive, I forgive too. And whatever I've forgiven — if there was anything to forgive — I did it for your sake in Christ's presence, **11** so that Satan won't get the upper hand over us. After all, we know his schemes all too well.

**12** When I came to Troas to preach Christ's gospel, even though the Lord had opened a door for me, **13** my spirit couldn't rest because I didn't find my brother Titus there. So I said goodbye and headed to Macedonia.

**14** But thanks be to God, who always leads us in triumphal procession in Christ and through us spreads the fragrance of knowing him everywhere! **15** We're the aroma of Christ to God among those being saved and those perishing. **16** To some, we're the smell of death leading to death; to others, the fragrance of life leading to life. And who's up for a task like that? **17** Unlike so many, we don't peddle God's word for profit. On the contrary, we speak in Christ with sincerity, as people sent from God, standing in God's very presence.

---

## Notes

- v.1: The "painful visit" (ἐν λύπῃ, en lypē) refers to an unrecorded visit between 1 and 2 Corinthians, probably after a confrontation.
- v.5-8: The identity of the offender is debated — possibly the man from 1 Cor 5 or a different person.
- v.14: "Triumphal procession" (θριαμβεύω, thriambeuō) evokes the Roman military triumph where the victorious general paraded — Paul sees himself as part of Christ's retinue.
- v.17: "Peddle" (καπηλεύω, kapēleuō) refers to street vendors who watered down wine for profit — Paul contrasts his integrity with dishonest preachers.
