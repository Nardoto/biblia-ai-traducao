# 2 Corinthians 5

**1** We know that if our earthly tent — this body — is taken down, we have a building from God, an eternal house in heaven, not made by human hands. **2** In this body we groan, longing deeply to be clothed with our heavenly dwelling, **3** since once we're clothed, we won't be found naked. **4** While we're in this tent, we groan under the burden — not because we want to be unclothed, but because we want to be clothed over the top, so that what's mortal gets swallowed up by life. **5** And the one who prepared us for this very thing is God, who gave us the Spirit as a down payment.

**6** So we're always confident. We know that while we're at home in this body, we're away from the Lord. **7** For we walk by faith, not by sight. **8** Yes, we're confident, and we'd actually prefer to leave the body and be at home with the Lord. **9** So our goal — whether we're in the body or away from it — is to please him. **10** For all of us must appear before Christ's judgment seat, so that each of us receives what's due for what we did through the body, whether good or bad.

**11** So, knowing what it means to fear the Lord, we try to persuade people. Before God, we're an open book, and I hope we're an open book to your conscience too. **12** We're not recommending ourselves to you again but giving you a reason to be proud of us, so you can answer those who boast about appearances rather than what's in the heart. **13** If we're out of our minds, it's for God; if we're in our right minds, it's for you. **14** For Christ's love compels us, because we've reached this conclusion: one died for all, therefore all died. **15** And he died for all so that those who live would no longer live for themselves, but for the one who died and was raised for them.

**16** So from now on, we don't evaluate anyone by human standards anymore. Even though we once knew Christ by human standards, we don't know him that way anymore. **17** Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, they're a new creation. The old things have passed away; look, everything has become new! **18** And all of this comes from God, who reconciled us to himself through Christ and gave us the ministry of reconciliation. **19** In other words, God was in Christ reconciling the world to himself, not counting people's sins against them, and he entrusted us with the message of reconciliation.

**20** So we're ambassadors for Christ, as though God were making his appeal through us. On Christ's behalf, we plead with you: be reconciled to God! **21** The one who knew no sin, God made him sin for us, so that in him we might become the righteousness of God.

---

## Notes

- v.1: "Tent" (σκῆνος, skēnos) and "building" (οἰκοδομή, oikodomē) — Paul uses temporary vs. permanent dwellings as images for the earthly body vs. the resurrection body.
- v.5: "Down payment" (ἀρραβών, arrabōn) — same commercial term as 1:22, reinforcing the Spirit as the first installment of future life.
- v.14: "Compels" (συνέχει, synechei) — can mean "constrains," "presses," or "envelops"; Christ's love leaves Paul no other option.
- v.17: "New creation" (καινὴ κτίσις, kainē ktisis) — language echoing Isaiah 43:18-19 and 65:17, the prophetic hope of total renewal.
- v.21: "Made him sin" (ἁμαρτίαν ἐποίησεν) — one of Paul's densest theological statements; not that Christ became a sinner, but that he took on the condition of sin in solidarity with us.
