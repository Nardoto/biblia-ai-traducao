# 2 Corinthians 8

**1** Now, brothers and sisters, we want you to know about the grace God has given to the churches in Macedonia. **2** In the midst of a severe trial of affliction, their overflowing joy and their extreme poverty resulted in a wealth of generosity. **3** I testify that they gave according to their ability, and even beyond their ability, entirely on their own, **4** begging us earnestly for the privilege of sharing in this service to the saints. **5** And they did more than we expected: they gave themselves first to the Lord and then to us, by God's will.

**6** So we urged Titus that, since he'd already started, he should also complete this act of grace among you. **7** You excel in everything — in faith, in speech, in knowledge, in all earnestness, and in the love you have for us. So excel in this act of grace too.

**8** I'm not saying this as a command but using the example of others' eagerness to test the genuineness of your love. **9** For you know the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ: though he was rich, he became poor for your sake, so that through his poverty you might become rich.

**10** Here's my advice on this: last year you were the first not only to give but to want to give. **11** Now finish the job, so that your eagerness to start is matched by your completion of it, according to what you have. **12** For if the willingness is there, the gift is acceptable according to what you have, not according to what you don't have. **13** This isn't about burdening you to relieve others, but about equality. **14** Right now your abundance meets their need, so that when their abundance comes, it can meet your need. That way there's equality, **15** as it's written: "The one who gathered much didn't have too much, and the one who gathered little didn't have too little."

**16** Thanks be to God, who put the same earnest care for you into Titus's heart. **17** He not only accepted our request but went to you on his own initiative, with great enthusiasm. **18** Along with him, we're sending the brother who's praised by all the churches for his work in the gospel. **19** What's more, he was chosen by the churches to travel with us as we carry out this act of grace, administered for the Lord's own glory and to show our eagerness to help. **20** We want to avoid any criticism of how we handle this generous gift. **21** For we're concerned about doing what's right, not only before the Lord but also before people.

**22** With them we're also sending our brother whose dedication we've proven many times in many matters, and who's now even more dedicated because of his great confidence in you. **23** As for Titus, he's my partner and fellow worker among you. As for our brothers, they're representatives of the churches, an honor to Christ. **24** So show them the proof of your love and of our reason for boasting about you, in the sight of the churches.

---

## Notes

- v.1-5: The Macedonian churches (Philippi, Thessalonica, Berea) were notably poor, making their generosity even more striking.
- v.9: This verse is one of Paul's most compressed christological statements — pre-existence, incarnation, and salvific purpose in a single sentence.
- v.15: Quotation from Exodus 16:18, about manna in the wilderness — God's provision equalizes.
- v.18: The identity of this "brother" is unknown — suggestions include Luke, Barnabas, or others; the text doesn't name him.
