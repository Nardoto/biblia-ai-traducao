# Romans 16

**1** I commend to you our sister Phoebe, who is a deaconᵃ of the church in Cenchreae. **2** Welcome her in the Lord in a way that's worthy of God's people, and give her whatever help she needs from you — because she herself has been a patronᵇ of many, including me.

**3** Greet Prisca and Aquila, my coworkers in the Messiah Jesus, **4** who risked their own necks for my life. Not only I but all the Gentile churches are grateful to them. **5** Also greet the church that meets in their house.ᶜ Greet my dear friend Epaenetus, who was the first convert to the Messiah in Asia. **6** Greet Mary, who has worked very hard for you. **7** Greet Andronicus and Junia,ᵈ my fellow Jews who were in prison with me. They're outstanding among the apostles, and they were in the Messiah before I was.

**8** Greet Ampliatus, my dear friend in the Lord. **9** Greet Urbanus, our coworker in the Messiah, and my dear friend Stachys. **10** Greet Apelles, who is approved in the Messiah. Greet those who belong to the household of Aristobulus. **11** Greet Herodion, my fellow Jew. Greet those in the household of Narcissus who are in the Lord. **12** Greet Tryphaena and Tryphosa, who work hard in the Lord. Greet my dear friend Persis, who has worked very hard in the Lord. **13** Greet Rufus, chosenᵉ in the Lord, and his mother — who's been a mother to me too. **14** Greet Asyncritus, Phlegon, Hermes, Patrobas, Hermas, and the brothers and sisters with them. **15** Greet Philologus and Julia, Nereus and his sister, and Olympas, and all God's people with them.

**16** Greet one another with a holy kiss.ᶠ All the churches of the Messiah send their greetings.

**17** I urge you, brothers and sisters, to watch out for those who create divisionsᵍ and put obstacles in your way that go against the teaching you've learned. Stay away from them. **18** Because such people don't serve our Lord the Messiah but their own appetites,ʰ and by smooth talk and flattery they deceive the hearts of the unsuspecting.

**19** Everyone has heard about your obedience, so I'm full of joy over you. But I want you to be wise about what is good and innocent about what is evil. **20** The God of peace will crush Satanⁱ under your feet soon. The grace of our Lord Jesus be with you.

**21** Timothy, my coworker, sends greetings to you, as do Lucius, Jason, and Sosipater, my fellow Jews. **22** I, Tertius,ʲ who wrote down this letter, greet you in the Lord. **23** Gaius, who is host to me and to the whole church, greets you. Erastus, the city treasurer,ᵏ greets you, and our brother Quartus.

**25** Now to the one who has the power to strengthen you according to my good news and the proclamation of Jesus the Messiah — according to the revelation of the mysteryˡ that was kept secret for long ages **26** but has now been disclosed and made known through the prophetic Scriptures, by the command of the eternal God, to bring about the obedience of faith among all the nations — **27** to the only wise God, through Jesus the Messiah — to him be the glory forever! Amen.

---

## Notes

- v.1a: *diakonon* (διάκονον) — "deacon/minister." The same word Paul uses for his own ministry. Phoebe likely carried this letter to Rome, which means she would have been the first to read and explain it publicly — a significant role.
- v.2b: *prostatis* (προστάτις) — "patron/benefactor." This was a term of high social standing. Phoebe wasn't just helpful — she was a woman of means and influence who used her resources to support the church and Paul personally.
- v.5c: *kat' oikon autōn ekklēsian* (κατ᾽ οἶκον αὐτῶν ἐκκλησίαν) — "the church in their house." Early Christians met in homes, not dedicated buildings. Prisca (Priscilla) is named before Aquila — unusually, the wife before the husband — suggesting her prominence in the community.
- v.7d: *Iounian* (Ἰουνίαν) — "Junia." This is almost certainly a woman's name. The accentuation was debated for centuries, with some suggesting the masculine "Junias" — but no ancient evidence supports a male name *Junias*. Church fathers like Chrysostom recognized Junia as a woman and as an apostle. "Outstanding among the apostles" means they were recognized as apostles, not merely well-known to the apostles.
- v.13e: *eklekton* (ἐκλεκτόν) — "chosen/elect." This may be the same Rufus mentioned in Mark 15:21 as the son of Simon of Cyrene who carried Jesus' cross.
- v.16f: *philēmati hagiō* (φιλήματι ἁγίῳ) — "holy kiss." A standard early Christian greeting practice that expressed the familial bond among believers.
- v.17g: *dichostasias kai ta skandala* (διχοστασίας καὶ τὰ σκάνδαλα) — "divisions and obstacles/traps." After sixteen chapters of nuanced theological argument, Paul suddenly warns against troublemakers — suggesting a real and present danger to the Roman community.
- v.18h: *tē heautōn koilia* (τῇ ἑαυτῶν κοιλίᾳ) — literally "their own belly/stomach." This probably means their own appetites and self-interest in general, not just gluttony.
- v.20i: *syntrispei ton Satanan* (συντρίψει τὸν Σατανᾶν) — "will crush Satan." This echoes Genesis 3:15, where the seed of the woman would crush the serpent's head. Paul sees this promise being fulfilled through the community of believers.
- v.22j: Tertius is the scribe — the only letter secretary in the NT who inserts his own greeting. Paul dictated; Tertius wrote. This gives us a glimpse into the physical production of the letter.
- v.23k: *oikonomos tēs poleōs* (οἰκονόμος τῆς πόλεως) — "city treasurer." An inscription found in Corinth mentions an Erastus who was a city official — possibly the same person. This indicates that the early church included people of significant civic standing.
- v.25l: *mystērion* (μυστήριον) — the "mystery" revealed is the inclusion of Gentiles alongside Jews in God's people through the Messiah — the grand theme of the entire letter, now stated one final time. The doxology wraps the letter back to where it began (1:1–5): the good news about Jesus the Messiah, promised in Scripture, for all nations.
