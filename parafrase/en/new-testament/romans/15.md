# Romans 15

**1** We who are strong have an obligation to bear with the weaknesses of those who aren't strong — not to please ourselves. **2** Each of us should please our neighbor for their good, to build them up.ᵃ **3** Because even the Messiah didn't please himself. Instead, as it's written: "The insults of those who insult you fell on me."ᵇ **4** Everything that was written in the past was written for our instruction, so that through endurance and through the encouragement of the Scriptures we might have hope.

**5** May the God of endurance and encouragement give you the same mindset toward one another that the Messiah Jesus had, **6** so that with one heart and one voiceᶜ you may glorify the God and Father of our Lord Jesus the Messiah.

**7** So welcome one another, just as the Messiah welcomed you — for the glory of God. **8** What I'm saying is this: the Messiah became a servantᵈ of the circumcised on behalf of God's truthfulness, to confirm the promises made to the patriarchs, **9** and so that the Gentiles might glorify God for his mercy. As it's written: "Therefore I will praise you among the Gentiles and sing to your name." **10** And again: "Rejoice, you Gentiles, with his people." **11** And again: "Praise the Lord, all you Gentiles, and let all the peoples praise him." **12** And Isaiah says: "The root of Jesse will come — the one who rises to rule the nations. The Gentiles will put their hope in him."ᵉ

**13** May the God of hope fill you with all joy and peace as you believe, so that you overflow with hope by the power of the Holy Spirit.

**14** I myself am convinced about you, my brothers and sisters — convinced that you yourselves are full of goodness, filled with all knowledge, and perfectly capable of instructing one another. **15** But I've written to you rather boldly on some points, as a reminder, because of the grace God gave me **16** to be a minister of the Messiah Jesus to the Gentiles, serving as a priestᶠ of God's good news, so that the offering of the Gentiles might be acceptable, sanctified by the Holy Spirit.

**17** So in the Messiah Jesus I have reason to boast about my work for God. **18** I wouldn't dare to speak of anything except what the Messiah has accomplished through me to bring the Gentiles to obedience — by word and deed, **19** by the power of signs and wonders, by the power of the Spirit of God. So from Jerusalem all the way around to Illyricumᵍ I've fully proclaimed the good news of the Messiah. **20** And I've made it my ambition to proclaim the good news where the Messiah hasn't been named, so I wouldn't be building on someone else's foundation — **21** as it's written: "Those who were never told about him will see, and those who have never heard will understand."ʰ

**22** This is why I've so often been hindered from coming to you. **23** But now, since I no longer have any room left to work in these regions, and since for many years I've been longing to come visit you, **24** I plan to do so when I travel to Spain.ⁱ I hope to see you as I pass through, and to be helped on my way there by you, once I've enjoyed your company for a while.

**25** But right now I'm heading to Jerusalem to serve God's people there.ʲ **26** Because Macedonia and Achaia were pleased to make a contribution for the poor among God's people in Jerusalem. **27** They were pleased to do it — and indeed they owe it to them. If the Gentiles have shared in their spiritual blessings, they ought to serve them with material blessings in return.

**28** So when I've completed this task and delivered this fruit to them safely, I'll leave for Spain by way of you. **29** And I know that when I come to you, I'll come in the fullness of the Messiah's blessing.

**30** I urge you, brothers and sisters, by our Lord Jesus the Messiah and by the love of the Spirit, to join me in my struggle by praying to God for me — **31** that I may be rescued from the unbelievers in Judea, and that my service to Jerusalem may be acceptable to God's people there, **32** so that by God's will I may come to you with joy and be refreshed together with you. **33** The God of peace be with all of you. Amen.

## Notes

- v.2a: *eis oikodomēn* (εἰς οἰκοδομήν) — "for building up." The architectural metaphor continues from chapter 14: the community is a structure under construction, and every member's actions either strengthen or weaken it.
- v.3b: Quoted from Psalm 69:9 (LXX 68:10). Christ bore insults directed at God himself — he absorbed the cost rather than shielding himself.
- v.6c: *homothymadon en heni stomati* (ὁμοθυμαδὸν ἐν ἑνὶ στόματι) — "with one mind and one mouth." Unity in the community isn't uniformity on secondary matters but shared worship and shared purpose.
- v.8d: *diakonon* (διάκονον) — "servant/minister." The Messiah came as a servant to the Jewish people to fulfill God's covenantal promises. Paul then strings together four Old Testament quotations (from the Torah, Writings, and Prophets) to show that Gentile inclusion was always part of the plan.
- v.12e: Paul cites Psalm 18:49, Deuteronomy 32:43, Psalm 117:1, and Isaiah 11:10 in rapid succession — a comprehensive scriptural case from every section of the Hebrew Bible that the Gentiles were always meant to be included.
- v.16f: *leitourgon... hierourgounta* (λειτουργὸν... ἱερουργοῦντα) — priestly language. Paul describes his missionary work as a liturgical act: he's a priest presenting the Gentile converts as a holy offering to God.
- v.19g: *Illyrikon* (Ἰλλυρικόν) — the Roman province on the eastern coast of the Adriatic Sea (modern Albania/Croatia). This represents the western boundary of Paul's eastern Mediterranean mission arc.
- v.21h: Quoted from Isaiah 52:15 (LXX). Paul's pioneer missionary strategy is driven by this prophetic vision of previously unreached peoples encountering God.
- v.24i: *Spanian* (Σπανίαν) — Spain represents the western edge of the known world. Paul's ambition is global in scope. Whether he ever reached Spain remains historically uncertain.
- v.25j: This is the collection Paul mentions extensively in 1 and 2 Corinthians — a financial gift from Gentile churches to the impoverished Jerusalem believers. For Paul, it wasn't just charity but a theological statement about the unity of Jew and Gentile in the Messiah.
