# Romans 10

**1** Brothers and sisters, my heart's desire and my prayer to God for them is for their salvation. **2** I can testify that they have a zeal for God — but it's not based on real knowledge.ᵃ **3** Because they didn't understand God's righteousness and tried to establish their own, they didn't submit to God's righteousness. **4** Because the Messiah is the goalᵇ of the Law, bringing righteousness to everyone who believes.

**5** Moses writes about the righteousness that comes from the Law: "The person who does these things will live by them."ᶜ **6** But the righteousness that comes from faith says this: "Don't say in your heart, 'Who will go up to heaven?'" — that is, to bring the Messiah down — **7** "or 'Who will go down into the depths?'"ᵈ — that is, to bring the Messiah up from the dead. **8** Instead, what does it say? "The word is near you, in your mouth and in your heart" — that is, the word of faith that we're proclaiming.

**9** Because if you confessᵉ with your mouth that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved. **10** It's with the heart that a person believes, leading to righteousness, and it's with the mouth that a person confesses, leading to salvation. **11** The Scripture says, "Everyone who believes in him will never be put to shame."ᶠ **12** There's no distinction between Jew and Greek — the same Lord is Lord of all, lavishly generous to everyone who calls on him. **13** Because "everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved."ᵍ

**14** But how can they call on someone they haven't believed in? And how can they believe in someone they haven't heard? And how can they hear without someone proclaiming? **15** And how can anyone proclaim unless they're sent? As it's written: "How beautiful are the feet of those who bring good news!"ʰ

**16** But not everyone obeyed the good news. Isaiah says, "Lord, who has believed our message?" **17** So faith comes from hearing, and hearing comes through the word of the Messiah.ⁱ

**18** But I ask: didn't they hear? Of course they did — "Their voice has gone out to all the earth, their words to the ends of the world."ʲ **19** But I ask again: didn't Israel understand? First, Moses says, "I will make you jealous through a nation that isn't really a nation; I will provoke you to anger through a nation without understanding." **20** Then Isaiah boldly says, "I was found by those who weren't looking for me; I revealed myself to those who weren't asking for me." **21** But about Israel he says, "All day long I held out my hands to a disobedient and defiant people."

## Notes

- v.2a: *epignōsin* (ἐπίγνωσιν) — not just knowledge (*gnōsis*) but full, accurate knowledge. Israel's zeal was intense but misdirected — passion without proper understanding of what God was actually doing in the Messiah.
- v.4b: *telos nomou* (τέλος νόμου) — *telos* can mean "end" (termination), "goal" (purpose), or "fulfillment" (completion). Paul likely means all three: Christ is what the Law was always pointing toward, he fulfills its deepest purpose, and he brings its era to a close as a system of righteousness.
- v.5c: Quoted from Leviticus 18:5. The Law offered life through perfect obedience — a path that turned out to be impossible for fallen humanity.
- v.6-8d: Paul creatively reinterprets Deuteronomy 30:12–14. In its original context, Moses was saying the Law wasn't inaccessible. Paul applies this to the Messiah: you don't need to perform impossible feats to find him — the word of faith is already close to you.
- v.9e: *homologēsēs* (ὁμολογήσῃς) — "confess/declare publicly." In the Roman world, declaring "Jesus is Lord" (*Kyrios Iēsous*) was a politically loaded statement, since *Kyrios* was a title claimed by Caesar. This confession had potential consequences.
- v.11f: From Isaiah 28:16 (LXX). Paul adds *pas* ("everyone") — emphasizing the universality of the offer.
- v.13g: Quoted from Joel 2:32 (LXX 3:5). In Joel, "the Lord" is YHWH. Paul applies this to Jesus, implying a remarkable identification.
- v.15h: From Isaiah 52:7 — originally about messengers bringing news of Israel's return from exile. Paul applies it to gospel proclaimers bringing news of a greater liberation.
- v.17i: *rhēmatos Christou* (ῥήματος Χριστοῦ) — "word of Christ." Some manuscripts read "word of God." The message about Christ, spoken by Christ, or both — is what generates faith.
- v.18j: Quoted from Psalm 19:4 (LXX 18:5). Paul may be using the psalm's language about creation's universal testimony to suggest that the gospel has similarly reached everywhere — or he may be drawing a typological parallel between natural and special revelation.
