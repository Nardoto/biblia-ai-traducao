# Romans 2

**1** So you have no excuse either — you, the one who sits in judgment on others. Because when you judge someone else, you're condemning yourself, since you who pass judgment do the exact same things. **2** Now we know that God's judgment falls truthfully on those who practice such things. **3** But do you really think — you who judge others for doing these things while doing them yourself — that you'll somehow escape God's judgment? **4** Or are you treating his incredible kindness, patience, and generosityᵃ with contempt? Don't you realize that God's kindness is meant to lead you to repentanceᵇ?

**5** But because of your stubbornness and your unrepentant heart, you're storing up wrath for yourself on the day of wrathᶜ — the day when God's righteous judgment will be unveiled. **6** He'll pay back each person according to what they've done. **7** To those who pursue glory, honor, and immortality through persistence in doing good — eternal life. **8** But to those who are driven by selfish ambitionᵈ and who reject the truth while embracing wickedness — furious wrath. **9** There will be trouble and anguish for every human being who does evil — for the Jew first, and also for the Greek — **10** but glory, honor, and peace for everyone who does good — for the Jew first, and also for the Greek. **11** Because God doesn't play favorites.ᵉ

**12** Everyone who sinned without the Law will also perish without the Law, and everyone who sinned under the Law will be judged by the Law. **13** Because it's not those who merely hear the Law who are righteous before God — it's those who do it who'll be declared righteous. **14** When Gentiles, who don't have the Law, instinctively do what the Law requires, they are a law for themselves, even though they don't have the Law. **15** They show that the work of the Law is written on their heartsᶠ — their conscience bearing witness alongside it, and their conflicting thoughts accusing or even defending them — **16** on the day when God judges people's secrets through Jesus the Messiah, according to my good news.

**17** Now you — you call yourself a Jew, you lean on the Law, you boast in God, **18** you know his will, you can discern what really matters because you've been instructed in the Law, **19** you're confident that you're a guide for the blind, a light for those in darkness, **20** an educator of the foolish, a teacher of infants, because you have in the Law the embodiment of knowledge and truth — **21** so then, you who teach others, don't you teach yourself? You who preach "Don't steal" — do you steal? **22** You who say "Don't commit adultery" — do you commit adultery? You who are disgusted by idols — do you rob temples?ᵍ **23** You who boast in the Law — do you dishonor God by breaking it? **24** As it's written: "God's name is blasphemed among the nations because of you."

**25** Circumcision has value — if you practice the Law. But if you break the Law, your circumcision has become uncircumcision. **26** So if an uncircumcised person keeps the righteous requirements of the Law, won't their uncircumcision be counted as circumcision? **27** And the person who is physically uncircumcised but keeps the Law will judge you — you who have the written code and circumcision yet break the Law.

**28** Because a person isn't a Jew just on the outside, and real circumcision isn't something external, done to the body. **29** No — a real Jew is one on the inside, and real circumcision is circumcision of the heartʰ, by the Spirit, not by the written code. That person's praise doesn't come from people but from God.

## Notes

- v.4a: *chrēstotētos... anochēs... makrothymias* (χρηστότητος... ἀνοχῆς... μακροθυμίας) — three distinct terms: kindness (gracious character), patience (holding back from punishment), generosity/long-suffering (enduring with composure). Paul piles them up to amplify God's restraint.
- v.4b: *metanoian* (μετάνοιαν) — a complete change of mind and direction, not merely regret. The verb *meta-noeō* literally means to think differently afterward.
- v.5c: *hēmeran orgēs* (ἡμέραν ὀργῆς) — echoes prophetic "Day of the Lord" language. Paul sees final judgment as a future event, not just a present process.
- v.8d: *eritheias* (ἐριθείας) — originally meant working for hire, then came to mean selfish partisanship or factious ambition. It's not mere selfishness but a calculating pursuit of one's own advantage.
- v.11e: *prosōpolēmpsia* (προσωποληψία) — literally "face-receiving," meaning to judge by external appearance or status. God doesn't do this.
- v.15f: *to ergon tou nomou grapton en tais kardiais* (τὸ ἔργον τοῦ νόμου γραπτὸν ἐν ταῖς καρδίαις) — "the work of the Law written on their hearts." Not the same as Jeremiah 31:33's new covenant promise — Paul is making a different, natural-law argument about moral awareness.
- v.22g: *hierosyleis* (ἱεροσυλεῖς) — "rob temples." This may refer to plundering pagan temples for their precious metals while publicly despising the idols within — profiting from the very system you condemn.
- v.29h: *peritomē kardias en pneumati* (περιτομὴ καρδίας ἐν πνεύματι) — echoes Deuteronomy 30:6. The wordplay here is deliberate: *Ioudaios* (Jew) comes from *Ioudas* (Judah), which means "praise." The true Jew receives praise (*epainos*) not from humans but from God.
