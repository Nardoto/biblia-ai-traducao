# Romans 12

**1** So here's what I urge you to do, brothers and sisters, in light of God's mercies: offer your bodies as a living sacrifice — holy, pleasing to God. This is your rational worship.ᵃ **2** Don't let the pattern of this age shape you. Instead, be transformed by the renewing of your mind,ᵇ so that you can discern what God's will is — what is good, pleasing, and complete.

**3** By the grace given to me, I'm telling every one of you: don't think of yourself more highly than you should. Instead, think with sober judgment, each according to the measure of faith God has given you.ᶜ **4** Just as we have many parts in one body, and not all the parts have the same function, **5** so we who are many are one body in the Messiah, and individually we belong to each other.

**6** We have different giftsᵈ according to the grace given to us. If your gift is prophecy, use it in proportion to your faith. **7** If it's serving, then serve. If it's teaching, then teach. **8** If it's encouraging, then encourage. If it's giving, give generously. If it's leading, lead with diligence. If it's showing mercy, do it cheerfully.

**9** Let your love be genuine — no pretending.ᵉ Hate what is evil; hold tight to what is good. **10** Be devoted to one another with brotherly affection.ᶠ Outdo each other in showing honor. **11** Don't lag in zeal. Be on fire in the Spirit. Serve the Lord. **12** Rejoice in hope. Stand firm in suffering. Be persistent in prayer. **13** Contribute to the needs of God's people. Pursue hospitality.ᵍ

**14** Bless those who persecute you — bless and don't curse. **15** Rejoice with those who rejoice; weep with those who weep. **16** Live in harmony with one another. Don't be drawn to high and mighty things — instead, associate with the humble.ʰ Don't be wise in your own eyes.

**17** Don't repay anyone evil for evil. Give thought to what is honorable in everyone's eyes. **18** If it's possible, as far as it depends on you, live at peace with everyone. **19** Don't take revenge yourselves, dear friends — leave room for God's wrath. Because it's written: "Vengeance is mine; I will repay," says the Lord.ⁱ **20** Instead, "If your enemy is hungry, feed them; if they're thirsty, give them something to drink. By doing this, you'll heap burning coals on their head."ʲ **21** Don't be conquered by evil — conquer evil with good.

---

## Notes

- v.1a: *logikēn latreian* (λογικὴν λατρείαν) — "rational/spiritual worship." *Logikos* (from *logos*) means "pertaining to reason" — this worship is both reasonable (it makes sense given what God has done) and spiritual (transcending mere ritual). *Latreia* is temple service language — Paul is saying your everyday life is your worship service.
- v.2b: *metamorphousthe tē anakainōsei tou noos* (μεταμορφοῦσθε τῇ ἀνακαινώσει τοῦ νοός) — *metamorphoō* is where we get "metamorphosis." This isn't cosmetic change but fundamental transformation from the inside out. The present tense indicates an ongoing process.
- v.3c: *metron pisteōs* (μέτρον πίστεως) — "measure of faith." Not everyone has the same measure, and that's by design. The point isn't comparison but accurate self-assessment.
- v.6d: *charismata* (χαρίσματα) — "grace-gifts," from *charis* (grace). These aren't earned abilities but given capacities for serving the community.
- v.9e: *anypokritos* (ἀνυπόκριτος) — "without hypocrisy," literally "without a mask." In Greek theater, *hypokritēs* was an actor who wore a mask. Love must be unmasked, authentic.
- v.10f: *philadelphia* (φιλαδελφία) — "brotherly love," the affection shared among family members. *Philostorgoi* adds the nuance of natural family tenderness — the kind of warmth you can't fake.
- v.13g: *philoxenian diōkontes* (φιλοξενίαν διώκοντες) — literally "pursuing love of strangers." *Diōkō* means to chase, hunt, pursue eagerly. Hospitality isn't passive availability — it's active pursuit of opportunities to welcome others.
- v.16h: *tois tapeinois synapagomenoi* (τοῖς ταπεινοῖς συναπαγόμενοι) — can mean "associate with humble people" or "give yourselves to humble tasks." The ambiguity may be intentional — both are needed.
- v.19i: Quoted from Deuteronomy 32:35. Paul isn't saying God will eventually get your enemies — he's saying vengeance belongs to a different jurisdiction. Let go of the gavel.
- v.20j: Quoted from Proverbs 25:21–22. "Burning coals" is debated: it may refer to (1) causing shame and repentance, (2) an Egyptian repentance ritual where coals were carried on the head, or (3) intensifying the enemy's judgment if they don't repent. The context of overcoming evil with good (v.21) favors the first reading — kindness that melts hostility.
