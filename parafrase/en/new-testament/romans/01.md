# Romans 1

**1** From Paul — a slave of Jesus the Messiah, called to be an apostle, set apart for God's good news. **2** This good news God promised long ago through his prophets in the sacred Scriptures — **3** it's about his Son, who in his human nature was born from David's line, **4** and who was powerfully declared to be the Son of God by the Spirit of holinessᵃ through his resurrection from the dead: Jesus the Messiah, our Lord. **5** Through him we received graceᵇ and apostleship — to bring about the obedience that comes from faithᶜ among all the nations, for the honor of his name. **6** And you're included in that call — you who belong to Jesus the Messiah.

**7** To everyone in Rome who is loved by God and called to be holy: graceᵈ and peace to you from God our Father and the Lord Jesus the Messiah.

**8** First things first — I thank my God through Jesus the Messiah for all of you, because your faith is being talked about all over the world. **9** God is my witness — the God I serve with my whole heart in proclaiming the good news about his Son — that I constantly mention you in my prayers, **10** always asking that somehow, by God's will, the way might finally open for me to come visit you. **11** I'm longing to see you so I can share some spiritual gift with you to strengthen you — **12** or better yet, so we can encourage each other through the faith we share, yours and mine alike.

**13** I want you to know, brothers and sisters, that I've planned many times to come to you — but I've been blocked every time until now — so I could see some resultsᵉ among you, just as I have among the other nations. **14** I owe a debt to Greeks and to non-Greeks, to the educated and the uneducated alike. **15** That's why I'm so eager to proclaim the good news to you in Rome as well.

**16** Because I'm not ashamed of the good news — it's God's power unleashed for salvation to everyone who believes, to the Jew first and also to the Greek. **17** In it God's righteousnessᶠ is being revealed, from faith to faith — just as it's written: "The righteous one will live by faith."

**18** But God's wrathᵍ is also being revealed from heaven against every form of godlessness and injustice committed by people who suppress the truth through their wickedness. **19** Because what can be known about God is plain to them — God himself made it plain. **20** Ever since the creation of the world, his invisible qualities — his eternal power and divine nature — have been clearly seen, understood through what he's made. So they have no excuse.

**21** Because even though they knew God, they didn't honor him as God or give him thanks. Instead, their thinking became futileʰ and their senseless hearts were plunged into darkness. **22** Claiming to be wise, they became fools. **23** They traded the glory of the immortal God for images shaped like mortal humans, birds, four-legged animals, and crawling things.

**24** So God handed them overⁱ to the cravings of their own hearts, to the degradation of their own bodies among themselves — **25** because they exchanged the truth about God for a lie and worshiped and served created things rather than the Creator, who is blessed forever. Amen.

**26** Because of this, God handed them over to degrading passions. Their women exchanged natural relationsʲ for unnatural ones, **27** and likewise the men abandoned natural relations with women and burned with desire for one another — men committing shameless acts with men and receiving in themselves the due consequence of their error.

**28** And since they didn't see fit to hold on to the knowledge of God, God handed them over to a worthless mindᵏ, to do things that should never be done — **29** stuffed full of every kind of injustice, wickedness, greed, and malice. They're eaten up with envy, murder, conflict, deceit, and viciousness. They're gossips, **30** slanderers, God-haters, arrogant, boastful, inventors of new ways to do evil, disobedient to parents, **31** senseless, faithless, heartless, merciless. **32** They know perfectly well God's just decree — that those who practice such things deserve death — yet they not only keep doing them but actually applaud others who do the same.

## Notes

- v.4a: *pneuma hagiōsynēs* (πνεῦμα ἁγιωσύνης) — "Spirit of holiness." This could refer to the Holy Spirit or to Christ's own holy spirit/nature. The phrase is unusual in Paul and may echo a pre-Pauline creed.
- v.5b: *charis* (χάρις) — grace here is not merely unmerited favor but the empowering gift that makes Paul's mission possible.
- v.5c: *hypakoēn pisteōs* (ὑπακοὴν πίστεως) — "obedience of faith." This phrase can mean "the obedience that is faith," "obedience that springs from faith," or "obedience to the faith." The genitive is deliberately rich — faith and obedience aren't separate for Paul.
- v.7d: *charis kai eirēnē* (χάρις καὶ εἰρήνη) — Paul fuses the Greek greeting (*chairein*) with the Hebrew *shalom*, creating a distinctly Christian salutation.
- v.13e: *karpon* (καρπόν) — literally "fruit," meaning tangible results or spiritual harvest from his ministry.
- v.17f: *dikaiosynē theou* (δικαιοσύνη θεοῦ) — "God's righteousness." This is the thematic engine of the entire letter. It can mean God's own righteous character, the right standing God gives to people, or the saving activity by which God puts things right. Paul likely intends all three simultaneously.
- v.18g: *orgē theou* (ὀργὴ θεοῦ) — God's wrath is not an emotional outburst but his settled, righteous opposition to everything that corrupts and destroys his creation.
- v.21h: *emataīōthēsan* (ἐματαιώθησαν) — from *mataios*, meaning empty, pointless, futile. Their reasoning became a dead-end road.
- v.24i: *paredōken* (παρέδωκεν) — "handed over." This verb appears three times (vv. 24, 26, 28), structuring the downward spiral. God's judgment here is to let people have what they insist on wanting.
- v.26j: *physikēn chrēsin* (φυσικὴν χρῆσιν) — "natural use/function." Paul's language here draws on Stoic categories of what accords with nature (*physis*).
- v.28k: *adokimon noun* (ἀδόκιμον νοῦν) — a wordplay: since they didn't *dokimazō* (test and approve) God, God gave them over to an *adokimos* (untested, worthless, failing the test) mind.
