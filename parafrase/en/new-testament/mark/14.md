# Mark 14

**1** It was two days before the Passover and the Festival of Unleavened Bread.ᵃ The chief priests and scripture scholars were looking for a sneaky way to arrest Jesus and kill him. **2** "Not during the festival," they said, "or there'll be a riot."

**3** While Jesus was in Bethany, reclining at the table in the home of Simon the leper,ᵇ a woman came with an alabaster jar of very expensive perfume — pure nard.ᶜ She broke the jar open and poured it over his head. **4** Some of those present were indignant and said to each other, "Why was this perfume wasted? **5** It could've been sold for more than three hundred denarii and the money given to the poor!" And they scolded her harshly.

**6** But Jesus said, "Leave her alone. Why are you giving her a hard time? She's done something beautiful for me. **7** You'll always have the poor with you, and you can help them whenever you want. But you won't always have me. **8** She did what she could — she anointed my body ahead of time for burial. **9** I'm telling you the truth: wherever the good news is proclaimed in the whole world, what she's done will also be told — in memory of her."

**10** Then Judas Iscariot — one of the Twelve — went to the chief priests to hand Jesus over to them. **11** They were delighted when they heard this and promised to give him money. So he started looking for the right opportunity to betray him.

**12** On the first day of the Festival of Unleavened Bread, when the Passover lamb was slaughtered, Jesus's disciples asked him, "Where do you want us to go and prepare the Passover meal for you?" **13** He sent two of his disciples and told them, "Go into the city, and a man carrying a water jarᵈ will meet you. Follow him. **14** Wherever he enters, tell the owner of the house, 'The Teacher asks: Where is my guest room where I can eat the Passover with my disciples?' **15** He'll show you a large upstairs room, furnished and ready. Prepare it for us there." **16** The disciples left, went into the city, found everything exactly as Jesus had told them, and prepared the Passover.

**17** When evening came, Jesus arrived with the Twelve. **18** While they were reclining and eating, Jesus said, "I'm telling you the truth: one of you is going to betray me — one who's eating with me right now." **19** They were devastated and began saying to him one by one, "Surely not me?" **20** He told them, "It's one of the Twelve — the one dipping bread into the bowl with me. **21** The Son of Man goes just as it's written about him. But how terrible for the man who betrays the Son of Man! It would've been better for that man if he'd never been born."

**22** While they were eating, Jesus took bread, gave thanks,ᵉ broke it, gave it to them, and said, "Take this — this is my body." **23** Then he took a cup, gave thanks, and gave it to them, and they all drank from it. **24** He said, "This is my blood of the covenant,ᶠ which is poured out for many. **25** I'm telling you the truth: I won't drink the fruit of the vine again until that day when I drink it new in God's kingdom."

**26** After singing a hymn,ᵍ they went out to the Mount of Olives.

**27** Jesus told them, "You're all going to fall away, because it's written: 'I will strike the shepherd, and the sheep will be scattered.'ʰ **28** But after I've been raised, I'll go ahead of you into Galilee." **29** Peter declared, "Even if everyone else falls away, I won't." **30** Jesus said, "I'm telling you the truth: today — this very night — before the rooster crows twice, you'll deny me three times." **31** But Peter insisted even more forcefully, "Even if I have to die with you, I will never deny you!" And they all said the same thing.

**32** They came to a place called Gethsemane,ⁱ and Jesus said to his disciples, "Sit here while I pray." **33** He took Peter, James, and John with him, and he began to feel deeply distressed and anguished.ʲ **34** He told them, "My soul is crushed with grief — to the point of death. Stay here and keep watch."

**35** He went a little further, fell to the ground, and prayed that if possible, the hourᵏ might pass him by. **36** "Abba,ˡ Father," he said, "everything is possible for you. Take this cup away from me. Yet not what I want, but what you want."

**37** He came back and found them sleeping. He said to Peter, "Simon, are you asleep? Couldn't you keep watch for one hour? **38** Stay awake and pray that you won't fall into temptation.ᵐ The spirit is willing, but the flesh is weak."

**39** He went away again and prayed the same thing. **40** When he came back, he found them sleeping again — their eyes were so heavy. They didn't know what to say to him.

**41** He came a third time and said, "Are you still sleeping and resting? Enough!ⁿ The hour has come. Look — the Son of Man is being handed over into the hands of sinners. **42** Get up! Let's go! Here comes the one who's betraying me!"

**43** Immediately, while Jesus was still speaking, Judas — one of the Twelve — arrived with a crowd carrying swords and clubs, sent by the chief priests, scripture scholars, and elders. **44** The betrayer had arranged a signal with them: "The one I kiss — that's him. Arrest him and take him away under guard." **45** He went straight to Jesus and said, "Rabbi!" and kissed him. **46** They seized Jesus and arrested him.

**47** One of those standing nearby drew his sword, struck the high priest's servant, and cut off his ear. **48** Jesus said to them, "Am I some kind of rebel that you've come with swords and clubs to capture me? **49** Day after day I was right there with you, teaching in the temple courts, and you didn't arrest me. But the Scriptures must be fulfilled." **50** Then all the disciples abandoned him and ran.

**51** A certain young man was following Jesus, wearing nothing but a linen cloth.ᵒ They grabbed him, **52** but he slipped out of the cloth and ran away naked.

**53** They led Jesus away to the high priest, and all the chief priests, elders, and scripture scholars assembled. **54** Peter followed at a distance, all the way into the high priest's courtyard, where he sat with the guards and warmed himself by the fire.

**55** The chief priests and the entire council were looking for testimony against Jesus to put him to death, but couldn't find any. **56** Many gave false testimony against him, but their statements didn't agree. **57** Some stood up and testified falsely: **58** "We heard him say, 'I'll destroy this temple made by human hands, and in three days I'll build another one — not made by hands.'" **59** But even on this point, their testimony didn't match.

**60** The high priest stood up before them and asked Jesus, "Aren't you going to answer? What about all this testimony against you?" **61** But Jesus stayed silent and gave no answer. The high priest asked him again, "Are you the Messiah, the Son of the Blessed One?"ᵖ

**62** Jesus said, "I am.ᵍ And you will see the Son of Man sitting at the right hand of the Powerʳ and coming on the clouds of heaven."

**63** The high priest tore his robes and said, "Why do we need any more witnesses? **64** You've heard the blasphemy! What's your verdict?" They all condemned him as deserving death.

**65** Some began spitting on him. They blindfolded him, struck him with their fists, and said, "Prophesy!" The guards took him and beat him.

**66** While Peter was below in the courtyard, one of the high priest's servant girls came by. **67** When she saw Peter warming himself, she looked closely at him and said, "You were with that Nazarene — Jesus." **68** But Peter denied it: "I don't know or understand what you're talking about." He went out to the entrance, and a rooster crowed.ˢ

**69** The servant girl saw him there and started telling the bystanders again, "This man is one of them." **70** He denied it again. A little later, the bystanders said to Peter, "You're definitely one of them — you're a Galilean!" **71** He began to call down curses on himself and swore, "I don't know this man you're talking about!" **72** Immediately a rooster crowed a second time. Then Peter remembered what Jesus had said: "Before the rooster crows twice, you'll deny me three times." And he broke down and wept.

## Notes

- v.1a: *Pascha kai ta azyma* (Πάσχα καὶ τὰ ἄζυμα) — Passover (Nisan 14) and the week-long Festival of Unleavened Bread (Nisan 15-21); by this period they were often treated as a single festival
- v.3b: "Simon the leper" — presumably someone Jesus had healed; the title likely stuck as an identifier
- v.3c: *nardou pistikēs* (νάρδου πιστικῆς) — "pure/genuine nard"; an aromatic oil imported from the Himalayas, extremely costly
- v.13d: A man carrying water would be unusual and easy to spot — this was normally women's work
- v.22e: *eulogēsas* (εὐλογήσας) — "having blessed/given thanks"; the standard Jewish blessing over bread
- v.24f: *to haima mou tēs diathēkēs* (τὸ αἷμά μου τῆς διαθήκης) — "my blood of the covenant"; echoing Exodus 24:8. Some manuscripts add "new" (*kainēs*) before "covenant"
- v.26g: Likely the Hallel psalms (Psalms 115-118), traditionally sung at the conclusion of the Passover meal
- v.27h: Quoting Zechariah 13:7
- v.32i: *Gethsēmani* (Γεθσημανί) — Aramaic *gat shemanim*, "oil press"; an olive grove on the Mount of Olives
- v.33j: *ekthambeisthai kai adēmonein* (ἐκθαμβεῖσθαι καὶ ἀδημονεῖν) — extremely strong terms: *ekthambeō* suggests horrified amazement; *adēmoneō* suggests anguished restlessness, being troubled to the point of not knowing what to do
- v.35k: *hē hōra* (ἡ ὥρα) — "the hour"; a theologically loaded term for the appointed time of his suffering
- v.36l: *Abba* (Ἀββᾶ) — Aramaic for "Father"; an intimate family term, preserved here in Jesus's original language alongside the Greek translation
- v.38m: *peirasmon* (πειρασμόν) — testing, trial, temptation; the same root as Satan's "testing" of Jesus in 1:13
- v.41n: *apechei* (ἀπέχει) — an ambiguous word; can mean "enough!" or "the account is settled" or "he [Judas] has received [his payment]"
- v.51-52o: This unnamed young man appears only in Mark; some early traditions identify him as Mark himself
- v.61p: "The Blessed One" — a reverential circumlocution for God, avoiding the divine name
- v.62q: *egō eimi* (ἐγώ εἰμι) — "I am"; a direct affirmation. Matthew's parallel has the more ambiguous "You have said so"
- v.62r: "The Power" — another reverential substitute for God's name
- v.68s: Some manuscripts omit "and a rooster crowed" here
