# Mark 16

**1** When the Sabbath was over, Mary Magdalene, Mary the mother of James, and Salome bought spices so they could go and anoint Jesus's body. **2** Very early on the first day of the week, just after sunrise, they went to the tomb. **3** They were asking each other, "Who's going to roll the stone away from the entrance for us?"

**4** But when they looked up, they saw that the stone — which was very large — had already been rolled away. **5** They entered the tomb and saw a young man dressed in a white robe sitting on the right side. They were alarmed.

**6** He said to them, "Don't be alarmed. You're looking for Jesus the Nazarene, who was crucified. He has been raised!ᵃ He's not here. Look — here's the place where they laid him. **7** Now go, tell his disciples and Peter:ᵇ 'He's going ahead of you into Galilee. You'll see him there, just as he told you.'"

**8** They went out and fled from the tomb, trembling and bewildered. And they said nothing to anyone, because they were afraid.ᶜ

---

[The following verses (9-20) are not found in the earliest and most reliable manuscripts. They appear to be a later addition to provide a more complete ending.]ᵈ

**9** After Jesus rose early on the first day of the week, he appeared first to Mary Magdalene, from whom he had driven out seven demons. **10** She went and told those who had been with him, who were grieving and weeping. **11** When they heard that he was alive and that she had seen him, they didn't believe it.

**12** After that, he appeared in a different form to two of them as they were walking in the countryside. **13** They went back and told the rest, but they didn't believe them either.

**14** Later he appeared to the Eleven themselves while they were eating. He rebuked them for their unbelief and hardness of heart, because they hadn't believed those who'd seen him after he was raised.

**15** He told them, "Go into all the world and proclaim the good news to all creation. **16** Whoever believes and is baptized will be saved, but whoever doesn't believe will be condemned. **17** And these signs will accompany those who believe: in my name they'll drive out demons, they'll speak in new languages, **18** they'll pick up snakes with their hands, and if they drink anything deadly, it won't harm them. They'll lay their hands on the sick, and they'll recover."

**19** After the Lord Jesus had spoken to them, he was taken up into heaven and sat down at the right hand of God. **20** They went out and preached everywhere, and the Lord worked with them, confirming the message through the signs that accompanied it.

## Notes

- v.6a: *ēgerthē* (ἠγέρθη) — "he has been raised"; divine passive — God is the implied agent who raised Jesus from the dead
- v.7b: "And Peter" — a poignant addition after Peter's three denials (14:66-72); he is specifically singled out for restoration
- v.8c: *ephobounto gar* (ἐφοβοῦντο γάρ) — "for they were afraid." The earliest manuscripts of Mark end here, with this jarring, abrupt sentence ending on the Greek word *gar* ("for"). This is widely considered the original ending of Mark's gospel. The abruptness is deliberate — leaving the reader in the same position as the women: terrified, overwhelmed, and confronted with the question of what to do with the empty tomb
- v.9-20d: These verses (known as the "Longer Ending of Mark") are absent from the two oldest Greek manuscripts (Codex Sinaiticus and Codex Vaticanus), from important early translations, and are noted as inauthentic by early church fathers including Eusebius and Jerome. The vocabulary, style, and theology differ from the rest of Mark. They appear to be a second-century summary drawn from the other Gospels and Acts, added because the abrupt ending at v.8 was felt to be incomplete. They are included here because of their long history in the church tradition, but the original gospel almost certainly ended at verse 8
