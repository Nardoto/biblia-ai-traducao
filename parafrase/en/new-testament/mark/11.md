# Mark 11

**1** As they approached Jerusalem — near Bethphage and Bethany, by the Mount of Olives — Jesus sent two of his disciples ahead. **2** He told them, "Go into the village just ahead of you. As soon as you enter it, you'll find a young donkeyᵃ tied up that no one has ever ridden. Untie it and bring it here. **3** If anyone asks you, 'Why are you doing that?' say, 'The Lord needs it and will send it back shortly.'"

**4** They went and found a young donkey tied up at a door, out on the street, and they untied it. **5** Some people standing there asked, "What are you doing, untying that donkey?" **6** They told them what Jesus had said, and the people let them go.

**7** They brought the donkey to Jesus, threw their cloaks over it, and he sat on it. **8** Many people spread their cloaks on the road, and others cut leafy branches from the fields and spread them out. **9** Those walking ahead and those following behind were shouting:

"Hosanna!ᵇ
Blessed is the one who comes in the name of the Lord!ᶜ
**10** Blessed is the coming kingdom of our father David!
Hosanna in the highest!"

**11** Jesus entered Jerusalem and went into the temple courts.ᵈ He looked around at everything, but since it was already late, he went out to Bethany with the Twelve.

**12** The next day, as they were leaving Bethany, Jesus was hungry. **13** Seeing a fig tree in the distance with leaves on it, he went over to see if it had any fruit. When he reached it, he found nothing but leaves — because it wasn't the season for figs.ᵉ **14** He said to the tree, "May no one ever eat fruit from you again." And his disciples heard him say it.

**15** They arrived in Jerusalem, and Jesus entered the temple courts and began driving out those who were buying and selling there. He overturned the tables of the money changers and the seats of those selling doves. **16** He wouldn't let anyone carry merchandiseᶠ through the temple courts. **17** And he taught them: "Isn't it written, 'My house will be called a house of prayer for all nations'?ᵍ But you've turned it into a den of robbers!"ʰ

**18** The chief priests and the scripture scholars heard about this and started looking for a way to kill him — because they were afraid of him, since the whole crowd was amazed by his teaching. **19** When evening came, Jesus and his disciples left the city.

**20** In the morning, as they passed by, they saw the fig tree withered from the roots up. **21** Peter remembered and said, "Rabbi, look! The fig tree you cursed — it's completely dead!" **22** Jesus replied, "Have faith in God. **23** I'm telling you the truth: if anyone says to this mountain, 'Be lifted up and thrown into the sea,' and doesn't doubt in their heart but believes that what they say will happen — it will be done for them. **24** So I tell you: whatever you pray for and ask for, believe that you've received it, and it will be yours. **25** And when you stand praying, if you hold anything against anyone, forgive them — so that your Father in heaven will forgive your failures too."ⁱ

**27** They came back to Jerusalem. While Jesus was walking in the temple courts, the chief priests, the scripture scholars, and the elders came up to him **28** and demanded, "By what authority are you doing these things? Who gave you this authority?"

**29** Jesus said, "I'll ask you one question. Answer me, and I'll tell you by what authority I'm doing these things. **30** John's baptism — was it from heaven or from human beings? Answer me."

**31** They discussed it among themselves: "If we say 'from heaven,' he'll say, 'Then why didn't you believe him?' **32** But if we say 'from human beings' —" They were afraid of the crowd, because everyone believed John really was a prophet. **33** So they answered Jesus, "We don't know." Jesus said, "Then I'm not going to tell you by what authority I'm doing these things either."

## Notes

- v.2a: *pōlon* (πῶλον) — a young animal, here specifically a young donkey; echoes the prophecy in Zechariah 9:9 about a humble king riding a donkey's colt
- v.9b: *Hōsanna* (Ὡσαννά) — from Hebrew *hoshia-na*, "Save, please!"; by this time it had become an exclamation of praise
- v.9c: Quoting Psalm 118:25-26, a psalm associated with pilgrimage festivals and messianic expectation
- v.11d: *hieron* (ἱερόν) — the entire temple complex, including the outer courts; not the inner sanctuary (*naos*)
- v.13e: Mark's note that "it wasn't the season for figs" makes the episode clearly symbolic — likely representing judgment on Israel's failure to bear fruit despite outward religious appearance; this "sandwiches" the temple action (a literary technique Mark uses frequently)
- v.16f: *skeuos* (σκεῦος) — vessel, container, merchandise; people were using the temple courts as a shortcut for commercial traffic
- v.17g: Quoting Isaiah 56:7 — notably, "for all nations" (*pasin tois ethnesin*); the commerce was happening in the Court of the Gentiles, the only area where non-Jews could worship
- v.17h: Quoting Jeremiah 7:11
- v.25i: Verse 26 ("But if you do not forgive, neither will your Father who is in heaven forgive your transgressions") appears in some manuscripts but is absent from the earliest ones
