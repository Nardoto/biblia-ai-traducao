# Mark 10

**1** Jesus left there and went into the region of Judea and across the Jordan. Again crowds gathered around him, and as usual, he taught them.

**2** Some Pharisees came up and tested him by asking, "Is it lawful for a man to divorce his wife?" **3** He replied, "What did Moses command you?" **4** They said, "Moses allowed a man to write a divorce certificate and send her away."ᵃ **5** Jesus said, "Moses wrote you that command because of the hardness of your hearts. **6** But from the beginning of creation, 'God made them male and female.'ᵇ **7** 'For this reason a man will leave his father and mother and be joined to his wife,ᶜ **8** and the two will become one flesh.' So they are no longer two — they're one. **9** Therefore, what God has joined together, no person should separate."

**10** Back in the house, the disciples asked him about this again. **11** He told them, "Whoever divorces his wife and marries another woman commits adultery against her. **12** And if she divorces her husband and marries another man, she commits adultery."ᵈ

**13** People were bringing little children to Jesus so he could touch them, but the disciples scolded them. **14** When Jesus saw this, he was indignant.ᵉ He said, "Let the little children come to me — don't stop them! God's kingdom belongs to people like these. **15** I'm telling you the truth: whoever doesn't receive God's kingdom like a child will never enter it." **16** Then he took the children in his arms, laid his hands on them, and blessed them.

**17** As Jesus was setting out on the road, a man ran up, knelt before him, and asked, "Good Teacher, what must I do to inherit eternal life?"ᶠ **18** Jesus said, "Why do you call me good? No one is good except God alone. **19** You know the commandments: 'Don't murder. Don't commit adultery. Don't steal. Don't give false testimony. Don't cheat anyone. Honor your father and mother.'" **20** The man said, "Teacher, I've kept all of these since I was young."

**21** Jesus looked at him and loved him.ᵍ Then he said, "There's one thing you're missing. Go, sell everything you own, give the money to the poor, and you'll have treasure in heaven. Then come — follow me." **22** The man's face fell at these words, and he went away grieving, because he had a lot of wealth.

**23** Jesus looked around and told his disciples, "How hard it is for wealthy people to enter God's kingdom!" **24** The disciples were stunned by his words. But Jesus continued, "Children, how hard it isʰ to enter God's kingdom! **25** It's easier for a camel to go through the eye of a needle than for a rich person to enter God's kingdom." **26** They were even more shocked and said to each other, "Then who can be saved?" **27** Jesus looked at them and said, "With humans it's impossible, but not with God. Everything is possible with God."

**28** Peter started saying, "Look, we've left everything and followed you." **29** Jesus said, "I'm telling you the truth: there's no one who has left home or brothers or sisters or mother or father or children or fields for my sake and for the sake of the good news **30** who won't receive a hundred times as much in this present ageⁱ — homes, brothers, sisters, mothers, children, and fields, along with persecutions — and in the age to come, eternal life. **31** But many who are first will be last, and the last first."

**32** They were on the road going up to Jerusalem, with Jesus walking ahead of them. The disciples were astonished, and those who followed were afraid. Again he took the Twelve aside and began telling them what was going to happen to him: **33** "Look, we're going up to Jerusalem. The Son of Man will be handed over to the chief priests and the scripture scholars. They'll condemn him to death and hand him over to the Gentiles. **34** They'll mock him, spit on him, flog him, and kill him. And after three days, he'll rise."

**35** James and John, Zebedee's sons, came to him and said, "Teacher, we want you to do whatever we ask." **36** "What do you want me to do for you?" he asked. **37** They said, "Let one of us sit at your right and the other at your left in your glory."

**38** Jesus said, "You don't know what you're asking. Can you drink the cup I'm going to drink? Can you be baptized with the baptism I'm going to endure?"ʲ **39** "We can," they said. Jesus told them, "You will drink the cup I drink and be baptized with the baptism I'm baptized with. **40** But sitting at my right or left — that's not mine to grant. Those places belong to those for whom they've been prepared."

**41** When the other ten heard about this, they became furious with James and John. **42** Jesus called them together and said, "You know that those considered rulers over the Gentilesᵏ lord it over them, and their great ones exercise power over them. **43** But it's not going to be like that among you. Whoever wants to become great among you must be your servant, **44** and whoever wants to be first must be slaveˡ of all. **45** Because even the Son of Man didn't come to be served — he came to serve, and to give his life as a ransomᵐ for many."

**46** They came to Jericho. As Jesus was leaving the city with his disciples and a large crowd, a blind beggar named Bartimaeusⁿ — Timaeus's son — was sitting by the road. **47** When he heard that it was Jesus of Nazareth, he began shouting, "Jesus, Son of David, have mercy on me!" **48** Many people scolded him and told him to be quiet. But he shouted even louder, "Son of David, have mercy on me!"

**49** Jesus stopped and said, "Call him over." They called the blind man: "Cheer up! Get up — he's calling you!" **50** He threw off his cloak, jumped up, and came to Jesus. **51** Jesus asked him, "What do you want me to do for you?" The blind man said, "Rabbouni,ᵒ I want to see." **52** Jesus said, "Go — your faith has healed you." Immediately he could see, and he followed Jesus along the road.

## Notes

- v.4a: Referring to Deuteronomy 24:1-4
- v.6b: Quoting Genesis 1:27
- v.7c: Quoting Genesis 2:24; some manuscripts omit "and be joined to his wife"
- v.12d: That a wife could initiate divorce reflects Roman law, not Jewish practice; Mark may be contextualizing for his Roman audience
- v.14e: *ēganaktēsen* (ἠγανάκτησεν) — genuinely angry, indignant; one of the strongest emotions attributed to Jesus
- v.17f: *zōēn aiōnion* (ζωὴν αἰώνιον) — "life of the age to come"; not just living forever but the quality of life in God's renewed world
- v.21g: *ēgapēsen* (ἠγάπησεν) — from *agapaō*; Jesus's love for this man is explicitly stated even as he makes the hardest demand
- v.24h: Some manuscripts add "for those who trust in riches"
- v.30i: *en tō kairō toutō* (ἐν τῷ καιρῷ τούτῳ) — "in this present time/season"; the blessings come in this life, mixed with persecution
- v.38j: "Cup" and "baptism" are metaphors for suffering and death; the cup echoes Old Testament imagery of God's judgment
- v.42k: *ta ethnē* (τὰ ἔθνη) — the nations/Gentiles; Jesus contrasts worldly power structures with kingdom values
- v.44l: *doulos* (δοῦλος) — slave, bondservant; even stronger than *diakonos* (servant) in v.43
- v.45m: *lytron* (λύτρον) — ransom, the price paid to free a slave or prisoner; *anti pollōn* ("for/in place of many") — one of the most theologically significant phrases in Mark
- v.46n: *Bartimaios* (Βαρτίμαιος) — Aramaic *bar* ("son of") + Timaeus; Mark translates the name for his readers
- v.51o: *Rabbouni* (Ῥαββουνί) — an intensified form of "Rabbi"; "my great master/teacher"
