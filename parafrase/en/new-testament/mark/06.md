# Mark 6

**1** Jesus left there and went to his hometown, and his disciples followed him. **2** When the Sabbath came, he started teaching in the synagogue. Many who heard him were amazed. "Where did this man get all this?" they asked. "What kind of wisdomᵃ is this that's been given to him? And these miracles happening through his hands! **3** Isn't this the carpenter,ᵇ Mary's son, the brother of James, Joses, Judas, and Simon? Aren't his sisters right here with us?" And they took offenseᶜ at him.

**4** Jesus told them, "A prophet isn't without honor except in his hometown, among his relatives, and in his own household." **5** He couldn't do any miracles there, except lay his hands on a few sick people and heal them. **6** He was amazed by their lack of faith.

Then he went around teaching in the surrounding villages. **7** He called the Twelve to him and began sending them out two by two, giving them authority over unclean spirits. **8** He told them to take nothing for the journey except a walking stick — no bread, no bag, no money in their belts. **9** They could wear sandals but not pack an extra tunic. **10** He said, "Whenever you enter a house, stay there until you leave that town. **11** And if any place won't welcome you or listen to you, shake the dust off your feet as you leave — as a witness against them."ᵈ

**12** They went out and preached that people should turn their lives around. **13** They drove out many demons, anointed many sick people with oil, and healed them.

**14** King Herodᵉ heard about all this, because Jesus's name had become well known. Some people were saying, "John the Baptizer has been raised from the dead — that's why these miraculous powers are at work in him!" **15** Others said, "He's Elijah." Others said, "He's a prophet, like one of the ancient prophets." **16** But when Herod heard about it, he said, "It's John — the one I beheaded. He's been raised!"

**17** Here's what had happened: Herod himself had sent men to arrest John and chain him up in prison because of Herodias, his brother Philip's wife, whom Herod had married. **18** John had been telling Herod, "It's not lawful for you to have your brother's wife." **19** Herodias held a grudge against John and wanted to kill him, but she couldn't, **20** because Herod was afraid of John. He knew John was a righteous and holy man, and he protected him. Whenever he listened to John, he was deeply disturbed — yet he liked listening to him.ᶠ

**21** The perfect opportunity came on Herod's birthday, when he threw a banquet for his officials, military commanders, and the leading men of Galilee. **22** Herodias's daughter came in and danced, and she delighted Herod and his dinner guests. The king told the girl, "Ask me for anything you want, and I'll give it to you." **23** He swore to her with an oath, "Whatever you ask me, I'll give you — up to half my kingdom."

**24** She went out and asked her mother, "What should I ask for?" Her mother said, "The head of John the Baptizer." **25** The girl hurried back immediately to the king and made her request: "I want you to give me — right now, on a platter — the head of John the Baptist." **26** The king was deeply distressed, but because of his oaths and his dinner guests, he didn't want to refuse her. **27** So immediately the king sent an executioner with orders to bring John's head. The man went and beheaded John in the prison, **28** brought his head on a platter, and gave it to the girl, and the girl gave it to her mother. **29** When John's disciples heard about it, they came, took his body, and laid it in a tomb.

**30** The apostlesᵍ gathered around Jesus and reported everything they'd done and taught. **31** He said to them, "Come away with me to a quiet place by yourselves, and get some rest." Because so many people were coming and going that they didn't even have a chance to eat.

**32** So they went off by themselves in a boat to a remote place. **33** But many people saw them leaving and recognized them, and from all the towns they ran there on foot and arrived ahead of them. **34** When Jesus got out of the boat and saw the huge crowd, his heart went out to them,ʰ because they were like sheep without a shepherd. And he began teaching them many things.

**35** By this time it was getting late. His disciples came to him and said, "This is a remote place, and it's already very late. **36** Send the people away so they can go to the surrounding farms and villages and buy themselves something to eat." **37** But Jesus answered, "You give them something to eat." They said to him, "Should we go spend two hundred denariiⁱ on bread and give it to them to eat?" **38** He asked, "How many loaves do you have? Go and see." When they found out, they said, "Five — and two fish."

**39** Jesus told them to have everyone sit down in groups on the green grass. **40** So they sat down in clusters — groups of hundreds and groups of fifties. **41** He took the five loaves and two fish, looked up to heaven, gave thanks,ʲ broke the loaves, and kept giving pieces to his disciples to distribute to the people. He also divided the two fish among everyone. **42** They all ate and were satisfied. **43** And they picked up twelve baskets full of broken pieces of bread and fish. **44** Five thousand men had eaten.ᵏ

**45** Immediately Jesus made his disciples get into the boat and go ahead of him to Bethsaida on the other side while he dismissed the crowd. **46** After saying goodbye to them, he went up on a mountainside to pray.

**47** When evening came, the boat was out in the middle of the lake, and Jesus was alone on land. **48** He could see them straining at the oars because the wind was against them. Sometime between three and six in the morning,ˡ he came toward them, walking on the lake. He was about to pass by them, **49** but when they saw him walking on the lake, they thought he was a ghost and cried out — **50** because they all saw him and were terrified.

But immediately he spoke to them: "Take courage! It's me.ᵐ Don't be afraid." **51** Then he climbed into the boat with them, and the wind died down. They were completely stunned — **52** because they hadn't understood about the loaves. Their hearts were hardened.

**53** When they had crossed over, they landed at Gennesaret and anchored the boat. **54** The moment they got out, people recognized Jesus. **55** They ran throughout that whole region, carrying sick people on mats to wherever they heard he was. **56** Wherever he went — villages, towns, or countryside — they laid the sick in the marketplaces and begged him to let them touch even the edge of his cloak. And everyone who touched it was healed.

## Notes

- v.2a: *sophia* (σοφία) — wisdom; not just cleverness but deep divine insight
- v.3b: *tektōn* (τέκτων) — traditionally "carpenter," but more broadly a craftsman who worked with wood, stone, or metal
- v.3c: *eskandalizonto* (ἐσκανδαλίζοντο) — literally "stumbled"; they found him to be a stumbling block
- v.11d: Shaking off dust was a Jewish symbolic act performed when leaving Gentile territory; doing it to fellow Jews was a jarring statement
- v.14e: Herod Antipas, technically a tetrarch (regional ruler), not a king — Mark uses the popular title
- v.20f: Some manuscripts read "he did many things" (*polla epoiei*) instead of "he was very perplexed" (*polla ēporei*) — a single letter difference in Greek
- v.30g: *apostoloi* (ἀπόστολοι) — "sent ones"; the only time Mark uses this term for the Twelve
- v.34h: *esplanchnisthē* (ἐσπλαγχνίσθη) — visceral compassion, felt deep in the gut
- v.37i: *dēnaria diakosia* (δηνάρια διακόσια) — a denarius was roughly a day's wage for a laborer; 200 denarii would be about 8 months' wages
- v.41j: *eulogēsen* (εὐλόγησεν) — "blessed" or "gave thanks"; the standard Jewish blessing before meals
- v.44k: *andres* (ἄνδρες) — "men" specifically; women and children were likely present in addition
- v.48l: *tetartē phylakē tēs nyktos* (τετάρτῃ φυλακῇ τῆς νυκτός) — "the fourth watch of the night," Roman time-keeping: approximately 3–6 AM
- v.50m: *egō eimi* (ἐγώ εἰμι) — "I am" or "It is I"; possibly echoing the divine name from Exodus 3:14
