# Mark 8

**1** During those days, another large crowd gathered and they had nothing to eat. Jesus called his disciples over and said, **2** "My heart goes out to this crowd — they've been with me three days now and have nothing to eat. **3** If I send them home hungry, they'll collapse on the way. Some of them have come a long distance."

**4** His disciples answered, "Where could anyone get enough bread to feed these people out here in the middle of nowhere?" **5** "How many loaves do you have?" he asked. "Seven," they said. **6** He told the crowd to sit down on the ground. Then he took the seven loaves, gave thanks,ᵃ broke them, and kept handing pieces to his disciples to distribute. And they served the crowd. **7** They also had a few small fish. He gave thanks for those too and told the disciples to distribute them. **8** Everyone ate and was satisfied, and they picked up seven large basketsᵇ full of leftover pieces. **9** About four thousand people were there. Then he sent them away.

**10** He immediately got into the boat with his disciples and went to the region of Dalmanutha.ᶜ **11** The Pharisees came out and started arguing with him, demanding a signᵈ from heaven to test him. **12** Jesus sighed deeply in his spirit and said, "Why does this generation demand a sign? I'm telling you the truth: no sign will be given to this generation." **13** Then he left them, got back in the boat, and crossed to the other side.

**14** The disciples had forgotten to bring bread — they had only one loaf with them in the boat. **15** Jesus warned them, "Watch out — be on guard against the yeast of the Pharisees and the yeast of Herod."ᵉ **16** They started discussing with each other, "He said that because we don't have any bread." **17** Aware of their conversation, Jesus said, "Why are you talking about having no bread? Do you still not get it? Don't you understand? Are your hearts hardened? **18** You have eyes — don't you see? You have ears — don't you hear?ᶠ Don't you remember? **19** When I broke the five loaves for the five thousand, how many baskets full of leftovers did you pick up?" "Twelve," they said. **20** "And when I broke the seven loaves for the four thousand, how many large baskets full of leftovers did you pick up?" "Seven," they said. **21** He said to them, "And you still don't understand?"

**22** They came to Bethsaida. People brought a blind man to Jesus and begged him to touch the man. **23** Jesus took the blind man by the hand and led him outside the village. He spit on the man's eyes, laid his hands on him, and asked, "Can you see anything?" **24** The man looked up and said, "I can see people — they look like trees walking around." **25** Jesus placed his hands on the man's eyes again. This time the man looked intently, his sight was restored, and he could see everything clearly. **26** Jesus sent him home, saying, "Don't even go into the village."ᵍ

**27** Jesus and his disciples headed out to the villages around Caesarea Philippi. On the way, he asked them, "Who do people say I am?" **28** They replied, "Some say John the Baptist. Others say Elijah. And others say one of the prophets." **29** "But what about you?" he pressed. "Who do you say I am?" Peter answered, "You are the Messiah."ʰ **30** And Jesus strictly warned them not to tell anyone about him.

**31** Then he began teaching them that the Son of Man had to suffer a great deal, be rejected by the elders, chief priests, and scripture scholars, be killed, and after three days rise again. **32** He said all this plainly. Peter took him aside and started to rebuke him. **33** But Jesus turned around, looked at his disciples, and rebuked Peter: "Get behind me, Satan! You're not thinking God's thoughts — you're thinking human thoughts."

**34** Then he called the crowd along with his disciples and told them, "If anyone wants to come after me, they have to deny themselves, pick up their cross,ⁱ and follow me. **35** Because whoever wants to save their lifeʲ will lose it, but whoever loses their life for my sake and for the sake of the good news will save it. **36** What good is it for someone to gain the whole world yet forfeit their own soul?ʲ **37** What could anyone give in exchange for their soul? **38** If anyone is ashamed of me and my words in this unfaithful and sinful generation, the Son of Man will be ashamed of them when he comes in his Father's glory with the holy angels."

## Notes

- v.6a: *eucharistēsas* (εὐχαριστήσας) — "having given thanks"; the root of the English word "Eucharist"
- v.8b: *spyridas* (σπυρίδας) — large woven baskets, different from the *kophinous* (smaller baskets) in the feeding of the 5,000; the distinction may be significant
- v.10c: Dalmanutha is mentioned only here; its exact location is unknown. Matthew's parallel reads "Magadan"
- v.11d: *sēmeion* (σημεῖον) — a sign, proof, or miraculous authentication; they wanted spectacular proof from the sky
- v.15e: *zymē* (ζύμη) — yeast/leaven; here metaphorical for corrupting influence that permeates everything
- v.18f: Echoing Jeremiah 5:21 and Ezekiel 12:2 — Jesus applies the prophets' criticism of Israel to his own disciples
- v.26g: This two-stage healing is unique in the Gospels; some see it as symbolic of the disciples' gradual understanding
- v.29h: *ho Christos* (ὁ Χριστός) — "the Anointed One" / "the Messiah"; Peter's confession is the pivot point of Mark's gospel
- v.34i: *stauron* (σταυρόν) — cross; before Easter, this would have evoked the horrifying Roman instrument of execution, not a religious symbol
- v.35-36j: *psychē* (ψυχή) — can mean "life," "soul," or "self"; the wordplay works on multiple levels — physical life and deepest identity
