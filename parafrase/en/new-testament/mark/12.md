# Mark 12

**1** Then Jesus began speaking to them in comparisons: "A man planted a vineyard, put a wall around it, dug a winepress, and built a watchtower.ᵃ Then he leased it to tenant farmers and went away on a journey. **2** When harvest time came, he sent a servant to the tenants to collect his share of the fruit. **3** But they grabbed the servant, beat him, and sent him away empty-handed. **4** He sent another servant, and they struck that one on the head and humiliated him. **5** He sent another, and they killed him. He sent many others — some they beat, others they killed.

**6** "He had one more to send: his beloved son.ᵇ Finally he sent him, thinking, 'They'll respect my son.' **7** But the tenants said to each other, 'This is the heir! Come on, let's kill him, and the inheritance will be ours!' **8** So they seized him, killed him, and threw him out of the vineyard.

**9** "What will the owner of the vineyard do? He'll come and destroy the tenants and give the vineyard to others. **10** Haven't you read this Scripture: 'The stone the builders rejected has become the cornerstone.ᶜ **11** This came from the Lord, and it's an amazing thing to see'?"

**12** They wanted to arrest him because they knew he'd told the comparison against them. But they were afraid of the crowd, so they left him and went away.

**13** Then they sent some Pharisees and Herodians to trap him with his own words. **14** They came and said, "Teacher, we know you're an honest man who isn't swayed by anyone. You don't show favoritism but teach the way of God truthfully. So — is it lawful to pay the tax to Caesar or not? Should we pay it or shouldn't we?"

**15** But Jesus saw through their hypocrisy and said, "Why are you testing me? Bring me a denarius — let me look at it." **16** They brought one. He asked them, "Whose image is this, and whose inscription?" "Caesar's," they answered. **17** Jesus said, "Give to Caesar what belongs to Caesar, and give to God what belongs to God."ᵈ They were completely amazed at him.

**18** Then some Sadducees — they're the ones who say there's no resurrection — came to him with a question. **19** "Teacher," they said, "Moses wrote for us that if a man's brother dies and leaves a wife but no children, the man should marry the widow and have children for his brother.ᵉ **20** Now, there were seven brothers. The first married and died without children. **21** The second married her and died without children, and the same with the third. **22** None of the seven left children. Finally, the woman died too. **23** In the resurrection, whose wife will she be? All seven were married to her."

**24** Jesus said, "Isn't this exactly your mistake — you don't know the Scriptures or the power of God? **25** When people rise from the dead, they neither marry nor are given in marriage — they're like the angels in heaven. **26** And as for the dead being raised — haven't you read in the book of Moses, at the passage about the bush, how God said to him, 'I am the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob'?ᶠ **27** He's not God of the dead but of the living. You're badly mistaken."

**28** One of the scripture scholars who'd been listening to the debate and noticed how well Jesus answered came up and asked, "Which commandment is the most important of all?" **29** Jesus answered, "The most important is: 'Hear, O Israel — the Lord our God, the Lord is one.ᵍ **30** And you shall love the Lord your God with all your heart, with all your soul,ʰ with all your mind, and with all your strength.' **31** The second is this: 'You shall love your neighbor as yourself.'ⁱ No other commandment is greater than these."

**32** The scripture scholar said, "Well said, Teacher. You're right that God is one and there's no other besides him. **33** And to love him with all your heart, all your understanding, and all your strength, and to love your neighbor as yourself — that matters far more than all the burnt offerings and sacrifices."

**34** When Jesus saw that the man had answered wisely, he told him, "You're not far from God's kingdom." After that, no one dared ask him any more questions.

**35** While teaching in the temple courts, Jesus asked, "How can the scripture scholars say that the Messiah is David's son? **36** David himself, speaking by the Holy Spirit, said: 'The Lord said to my Lord: Sit at my right hand until I put your enemies under your feet.'ʲ **37** David himself calls him 'Lord.' So how can he be his son?" The large crowd listened to him with delight.

**38** In his teaching he said, "Watch out for the scripture scholars. They like walking around in long robes, receiving respectful greetings in the marketplaces, **39** sitting in the best seats in the synagogues, and taking the places of honor at banquets. **40** They devour widows' houses and then, for show, say long prayers. They'll receive the harshest judgment."

**41** Jesus sat down across from the offering boxᵏ and watched as people dropped in their money. Many wealthy people threw in large amounts. **42** Then a poor widow came and dropped in two small copper coins — worth almost nothing.ˡ **43** Jesus called his disciples over and said, "I'm telling you the truth: this poor widow has put in more than everyone else. **44** They all gave out of their surplus, but she, out of her poverty, put in everything she had — her whole livelihood."ᵐ

## Notes

- v.1a: Echoing Isaiah 5:1-7, the Song of the Vineyard, which Jesus's audience would have immediately recognized as a parable about Israel
- v.6b: *huion agapēton* (υἱὸν ἀγαπητόν) — "beloved son"; the same phrase used of Jesus at his baptism (1:11) and transfiguration (9:7)
- v.10c: *kephalēn gōnias* (κεφαλὴν γωνίας) — "head of the corner"; quoting Psalm 118:22-23; whether "cornerstone" or "capstone" is debated
- v.17d: *ta Kaisaros apodote Kaisari kai ta tou theou tō theō* — the verb *apodidōmi* means "give back, return"; implying these things already belong to their respective owners. The deeper question: what bears God's image?
- v.19e: Referring to levirate marriage, from Deuteronomy 25:5-6
- v.26f: Quoting Exodus 3:6; God's use of the present tense "I am" (not "I was") their God implies they are still alive
- v.29g: *Shema Yisrael* — quoting Deuteronomy 6:4-5, the foundational Jewish confession of faith, recited twice daily
- v.30h: *psychē* (ψυχή) — soul, life, self; *dianoia* (διάνοια) — mind, understanding; *ischys* (ἰσχύς) — strength, might
- v.31i: Quoting Leviticus 19:18
- v.36j: Quoting Psalm 110:1; Jesus's point: if David calls the Messiah "Lord," the Messiah must be more than simply David's descendant
- v.41k: *gazophylakion* (γαζοφυλάκιον) — the treasury; likely the trumpet-shaped collection vessels in the Court of Women
- v.42l: *lepta dyo, ho estin kodrantēs* (λεπτὰ δύο, ὅ ἐστιν κοδράντης) — two *lepta* (the smallest Jewish coin) equals one *kodrantēs* (Roman quadrans, 1/64 of a denarius); Mark converts to Roman currency for his audience
- v.44m: *holon ton bion autēs* (ὅλον τὸν βίον αὐτῆς) — literally "her whole life/livelihood"; *bios* here means means of living, everything she had to live on
