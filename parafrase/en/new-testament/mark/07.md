# Mark 7

**1** The Pharisees and some scripture scholars who'd come from Jerusalem gathered around Jesus. **2** They noticed that some of his disciples were eating with "defiled"ᵃ hands — meaning they hadn't done the ritual washing. **3** (The Pharisees and all the Jewish people don't eat unless they wash their hands properly,ᵇ following the tradition passed down from the elders. **4** And when they come from the marketplace, they don't eat until they've washed. They also observe many other traditions — washing cups, pitchers, and kettles.)ᶜ

**5** So the Pharisees and scripture scholars asked Jesus, "Why don't your disciples follow the tradition of the elders? They eat with defiled hands!" **6** Jesus replied, "Isaiah nailed it when he prophesied about you hypocrites. As it's written: 'These people honor me with their lips, but their hearts are far from me. **7** They worship me in vain, teaching human rules as if they were divine commands.'

**8** "You've abandoned God's command and are hanging onto human tradition."

**9** Then he told them, "You've got a fine way of setting aside God's command so you can maintain your own tradition! **10** Moses said, 'Honor your father and your mother,' and 'Whoever curses their father or mother must be put to death.' **11** But you say that if anyone tells their father or mother, 'Whatever help you might have gotten from me is Korban'ᵈ — that is, 'dedicated to God' — **12** then you don't let them do anything more for their father or mother. **13** You cancel out God's word through the tradition you've handed down. And you do plenty of other things like that."

**14** Jesus called the crowd to him again and said, "Everyone listen to me and understand this: **15** Nothing that goes into a person from outside can make them 'defiled.' It's what comes out of a person that defiles them."ᵉ

**17** After he left the crowd and went inside, his disciples asked him about this comparison. **18** He said to them, "Are you really this slow to understand? Don't you see that nothing that enters a person from outside can defile them? **19** It doesn't go into their heart — it goes into their stomach and then out of the body." (In saying this, Jesus declared all foods clean.)ᶠ

**20** He went on: "What comes out of a person — that's what defiles them. **21** Because from within, out of the human heart, come evil thoughts, sexual immorality, theft, murder, **22** adultery, greed, malice, deceit, shamelessness, envy, slander, arrogance, and recklessness. **23** All these evils come from inside, and they are what defile a person."

**24** Jesus left that area and went to the region of Tyre.ᵍ He entered a house and didn't want anyone to know he was there, but he couldn't stay hidden. **25** In fact, as soon as a woman heard about him — her little daughter had an unclean spirit — she came and fell at his feet. **26** The woman was Greek, a Syrophoenician by birth.ʰ She kept asking him to drive the demon out of her daughter.

**27** Jesus said to her, "Let the children eat first. It's not right to take the children's bread and throw it to the dogs."ⁱ **28** She replied, "Lord,ʲ even the dogs under the table eat the children's crumbs." **29** Then he told her, "For saying that, you may go — the demon has left your daughter." **30** She went home and found her child lying on the bed, and the demon was gone.

**31** Then Jesus left the region of Tyre, went through Sidon, and came down to the Sea of Galilee through the region of the Decapolis. **32** People brought him a man who was deaf and could barely speak, and they begged Jesus to lay his hand on him. **33** Jesus took the man away from the crowd to be alone with him. He put his fingers in the man's ears, spit, and touched the man's tongue. **34** Then he looked up to heaven, sighed deeply, and said to him, "Ephphatha!"ᵏ — which means, "Be opened!" **35** Immediately the man's ears were opened, his tongue was freed, and he began speaking clearly.

**36** Jesus ordered them not to tell anyone. But the more he told them, the more they kept spreading the news. **37** People were overwhelmed with astonishment, saying, "He has done everything well — he even makes the deaf hear and the mute speak!"

## Notes

- v.2a: *koinais* (κοιναῖς) — literally "common"; in this context, ritually unclean, not physically dirty
- v.3b: *pygmē* (πυγμῇ) — literally "with the fist"; the exact meaning is debated — perhaps "with a handful of water" or "up to the wrist"
- v.4c: Some manuscripts add "and dining couches" to the list of items washed
- v.11d: *Korban* (Κορβᾶν) — Hebrew/Aramaic term for a gift dedicated to God; Mark explains it for his non-Jewish audience
- v.15e: Verse 16 ("If anyone has ears to hear, let them hear") appears in some manuscripts but is absent from the earliest ones
- v.19f: This parenthetical comment — "declaring all foods clean" — is Mark's editorial aside, drawing out the radical implication of Jesus's teaching
- v.24g: Tyre was a Gentile region; Jesus crosses an ethnic and religious boundary
- v.26h: *Hellēnis, Syrophoinikissa tō genei* — Mark specifies both her cultural identity (Greek-speaking) and ethnic origin (Syrophoenician), emphasizing she was a Gentile
- v.27i: *kynariois* (κυναρίοις) — diminutive "little dogs" or "house dogs," not the wild scavenger dogs; still a sharp metaphor, but the diminutive softens it somewhat
- v.28j: *Kyrie* (Κύριε) — "Lord" or "Sir"; the woman's use of this title is noteworthy
- v.34k: *Ephphatha* (Ἐφφαθά) — Aramaic, another instance where Mark preserves Jesus's original language
