# Mark 4

**1** Jesus began teaching again beside the lake. Such an enormous crowd gathered around him that he got into a boat and sat in it out on the water, while the whole crowd stayed on shore along the water's edge. **2** He taught them many things using comparisons, and in his teaching he said:

**3** "Listen! A farmer went out to sow seed. **4** As he scattered it, some fell along the path, and birds came and ate it up. **5** Some fell on rocky ground where it didn't have much soil. It sprouted quickly because the soil was shallow, **6** but when the sun came up, it got scorched, and since it had no root, it withered away. **7** Some fell among thorns, and the thorns grew up and choked it out, and it didn't produce any grain. **8** But some fell on good soil. It came up, grew, and produced a harvest — thirty, sixty, even a hundredfold."

**9** Then he said, "If you have ears to hear, then hear!"ᵃ

**10** When he was alone, those around him along with the Twelve asked him about the comparisons. **11** He told them, "The mysteryᵇ of God's kingdom has been given to you. But to those on the outside, everything comes in comparisons, **12** so that 'they may look and look but not see, and hear and hear but not understand — otherwise they might turn around and be forgiven.'"ᶜ

**13** Then he said to them, "You don't understand this comparison? Then how will you understand any of the others? **14** The farmer sows the message. **15** The ones along the path are where the message is sown, but as soon as they hear it, Satan comes immediately and snatches away the message that was sown in them. **16** The ones sown on rocky ground are those who hear the message and immediately receive it with joy, **17** but they have no root in themselves — they only last for a while. Then when trouble or persecution comes because of the message, they immediately fall away. **18** Others are like seed sown among thorns — they hear the message, **19** but the worries of this age,ᵈ the seduction of wealth, and cravings for everything else crowd in and choke the message, and it produces nothing. **20** But the ones sown on good soil are those who hear the message, welcomeᵉ it, and produce fruit — thirty, sixty, a hundredfold."

**21** He said to them, "Does anyone bring in a lamp to put it under a basket or under a bed? No — you put it on a lampstand! **22** Because nothing is hidden except to be revealed, and nothing is secret except to come into the open. **23** If you have ears to hear, then hear!"

**24** Then he told them, "Pay attention to what you're hearing. The measure you use will be the measure used for you — and then some. **25** Whoever has will be given more, and whoever doesn't have, even what they have will be taken away."

**26** He also said, "God's kingdom is like this: A man scatters seed on the ground. **27** He sleeps at night and gets up during the day, and the seed sprouts and grows — he has no idea how. **28** The earth produces the crop all by itself:ᶠ first the stalk, then the head, then the full grain in the head. **29** And when the grain is ripe, he immediately puts the sickle to it, because the harvest has come."

**30** He said, "What can we compare God's kingdom to? What picture should we use for it? **31** It's like a mustard seed — when it's planted in the ground, it's the smallest of all the seeds on earth. **32** But once it's planted, it grows up and becomes the largest of all the garden plants, putting out branches so big that birds can make nests in its shade."

**33** With many comparisons like these, he spoke the message to them — as much as they could take in. **34** He didn't say anything to the crowds without using comparisons. But when he was alone with his own disciples, he explained everything.

**35** That same day, when evening came, Jesus said to them, "Let's cross over to the other side." **36** They left the crowd behind and took him along in the boat, just as he was. Other boats went with them too. **37** A fierce windstorm blew up, and waves were crashing into the boat so that it was already getting swamped. **38** But Jesus was in the stern, sleeping on a cushion! They woke him up and said, "Teacher! Don't you care that we're going to drown?!"

**39** He got up, commanded the wind, and said to the sea, "Quiet! Be still!"ᵍ The wind died down and everything became perfectly calm. **40** Then he said to them, "Why are you so afraid? Do you still not have faith?"

**41** They were absolutely terrified and said to each other, "Who is this?! Even the wind and the sea do what he says!"

## Notes

- v.9a: *ho echōn ōta akouein akouetō* (ὁ ἔχων ὦτα ἀκούειν ἀκουέτω) — a call to deeper spiritual perception, not just physical hearing
- v.11b: *mystērion* (μυστήριον) — not "mystery" in the modern sense of a puzzle, but a divine secret now being revealed to insiders
- v.12c: Quoting Isaiah 6:9-10; a difficult passage about the purpose of parables — whether "so that" (*hina*) indicates purpose or result is debated
- v.19d: *aiōnos* (αἰῶνος) — "age" or "era"; not just temporal worries but the anxieties of the present world-system
- v.20e: *paradechontai* (παραδέχονται) — stronger than simply "hearing"; implies embracing, welcoming, receiving wholeheartedly
- v.28f: *automatē* (αὐτομάτη) — "of itself, spontaneously"; origin of English "automatic." The earth produces without human effort
- v.39g: *siōpa, pephimōso* (σιώπα, πεφίμωσο) — "Be silent! Be muzzled!" — the second word is a perfect passive imperative, commanding a permanent state of silence; same word used when silencing the demon in 1:25
