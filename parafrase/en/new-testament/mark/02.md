# Mark 2

**1** A few days later, Jesus came back to Capernaum, and word got around that he was home. **2** So many people packed into the house that there wasn't even room left by the door. And he was teaching them the message.

**3** Four men showed up carrying a paralyzed man on a mat. **4** But they couldn't get anywhere near Jesus because of the crowd, so they went up on the roof, dug through it right above where Jesus was, and lowered the mat with the paralyzed man on it. **5** When Jesus saw their faith,ᵃ he said to the paralyzed man, "Son,ᵇ your sins are forgiven."

**6** Some of the scripture scholars sitting there started thinking to themselves, **7** "How can he say that? That's blasphemy! Only God can forgive sins!" **8** Jesus immediately knew in his spirit what they were thinking, and said to them, "Why are you turning this over in your hearts? **9** Which is easier — to tell this paralyzed man 'Your sins are forgiven,' or to say 'Get up, pick up your mat, and walk'? **10** But so you'll know that the Son of Manᶜ has authority on earth to forgive sins —" he turned to the paralyzed man — **11** "I'm telling you: get up, pick up your mat, and go home." **12** The man got up, immediately grabbed his mat, and walked out in front of everyone. They were all completely amazed and praised God, saying, "We've never seen anything like this!"

**13** Jesus went out again along the lakeshore. The whole crowd came to him, and he taught them. **14** As he walked by, he noticed Leviᵈ — Alphaeus's son — sitting at his tax collection booth. Jesus said to him, "Follow me." And Levi got up and followed him.

**15** Later, Jesus was having dinner at Levi's house, and a whole crowd of tax collectors and sinnersᵉ were eating with him and his disciples — because many of them had started following him. **16** When the scripture scholars who were Pharisees saw him eating with sinners and tax collectors, they asked his disciples, "Why does he eat with tax collectors and sinners?" **17** Jesus overheard them and said, "Healthy people don't need a doctor — sick people do. I didn't come to call people who think they've got it all together. I came to call sinners."

**18** John's disciples and the Pharisees were fasting. Some people came to Jesus and asked, "Why do John's disciples and the Pharisees' disciples fast, but yours don't?" **19** Jesus answered, "Can wedding guests fast while the groom is right there with them? As long as the groom is with them, they can't fast! **20** But the time will come when the groom is taken away from them — and then they'll fast.

**21** "Nobody patches an old coat with a piece of unshrunk cloth. If you do, the new patch will pull away from the old fabric and make the tear even worse. **22** And nobody pours new wine into old wineskins. If you do, the wine will burst the skins — you'll lose both the wine and the skins. No — new wine goes into fresh wineskins."

**23** One Sabbath, Jesus was walking through the grain fields, and his disciples started picking heads of grain as they walked along. **24** The Pharisees said to him, "Look! Why are they doing what's not allowed on the Sabbath?" **25** Jesus replied, "Haven't you ever read what David did when he and his men were hungry and in need? **26** He went into the house of God — back when Abiathar was high priestᶠ — and ate the sacred bread that only priests were supposed to eat. And he even gave some to the men with him." **27** Then Jesus told them, "The Sabbath was made for people, not people for the Sabbath. **28** So the Son of Man is Lord even over the Sabbath."

## Notes

- v.5a: *pistin* (πίστιν) — "their faith"; notably it is the faith of the friends, not just the paralyzed man, that Jesus responds to
- v.5b: *teknon* (τέκνον) — "child" or "son"; a warm, tender form of address
- v.10c: *ho huios tou anthrōpou* (ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου) — "the Son of Man"; Jesus's preferred self-designation, drawn from Daniel 7:13
- v.14d: Levi is widely identified with Matthew the tax collector (cf. Matthew 9:9)
- v.15e: *hamartōloi* (ἁμαρτωλοί) — "sinners"; a social-religious label used by the religious establishment for those considered outside the bounds of proper Torah observance
- v.26f: The historical detail about Abiathar is debated; in 1 Samuel 21, it was Ahimelech (Abiathar's father) who was serving. Various explanations have been proposed
