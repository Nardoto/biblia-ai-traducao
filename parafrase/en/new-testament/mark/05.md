# Mark 5

**1** They crossed to the other side of the lake, into the region of the Gerasenes.ᵃ **2** The moment Jesus got out of the boat, a man with an unclean spirit came out from the tombs to meet him. **3** This man lived among the burial caves, and nobody could restrain him anymore — not even with chains. **4** He'd been bound with shackles and chains many times, but he'd rip the chains apart and smash the shackles to pieces. No one was strong enough to subdue him. **5** Night and day, among the tombs and in the hills, he'd scream and cut himself with stones.

**6** When he saw Jesus from a distance, he ran and fell on his knees before him, **7** and screamed at the top of his lungs, "What do you want with me, Jesus, Son of the Most High God?! I beg youᵇ in God's name, don't torment me!" **8** Because Jesus had been saying to him, "Come out of this man, you unclean spirit!"

**9** Then Jesus asked him, "What's your name?" He answered, "My name is Legion,ᶜ because there are many of us." **10** And he begged Jesus repeatedly not to send them out of the region.

**11** A large herd of pigs was feeding on the nearby hillside. **12** The spirits begged him, "Send us into the pigs — let us go into them." **13** Jesus gave them permission. The unclean spirits came out and went into the pigs, and the herd — about two thousand of them — stampeded down the steep bank into the lake and drowned.

**14** The men tending the pigs ran off and reported it in the town and the countryside. People came out to see what had happened. **15** They came to Jesus and saw the man who'd been possessed by the legion — sitting there, dressed, and in his right mind. And they were afraid. **16** Those who'd seen it told them what had happened to the demon-possessed man and about the pigs. **17** So they started begging Jesus to leave their region.

**18** As Jesus was getting into the boat, the man who'd been possessed pleaded to go with him. **19** But Jesus didn't let him. Instead, he said, "Go home to your own people and tell them everything the Lord has done for you — how he had mercy on you." **20** So the man went off and began spreading the news throughout the Decapolisᵈ about what Jesus had done for him. And everyone was amazed.

**21** When Jesus had crossed back over in the boat to the other side, a large crowd gathered around him by the lake. **22** One of the synagogue leaders — a man named Jairus — came, and when he saw Jesus, he fell at his feet **23** and pleaded desperately, "My little daughter is dying. Please come and lay your hands on her so she'll be healed and live!" **24** Jesus went with him, and a large crowd followed, pressing in on him from all sides.

**25** There was a woman who had been suffering from bleeding for twelve years. **26** She'd endured a great deal under many doctors, spent everything she had, and instead of getting better, she'd only gotten worse. **27** She'd heard about Jesus, so she came up behind him in the crowd and touched his cloak. **28** She was thinking, "If I can just touch his clothes, I'll be healed."ᵉ **29** Immediately the bleeding stopped, and she felt in her body that she was freed from her suffering.

**30** At that same instant, Jesus knew that powerᶠ had gone out from him. He turned around in the crowd and asked, "Who touched my clothes?" **31** His disciples said, "You see the crowd pressing in on you from every side, and you're asking 'Who touched me?'" **32** But Jesus kept looking around to see who had done it. **33** The woman, knowing what had happened to her, came forward trembling with fear, fell down before him, and told him the whole truth. **34** He said to her, "Daughter, your faithᵍ has healed you. Go in peace,ʰ and be free from your suffering."

**35** While he was still speaking, people came from Jairus's house and said, "Your daughter has died. Why bother the teacher anymore?" **36** But Jesus, overhearing what they said, told the synagogue leader, "Don't be afraid. Just keep trusting."ⁱ **37** He didn't let anyone follow him except Peter, James, and John — James's brother. **38** When they arrived at the synagogue leader's house, Jesus saw a commotion — people weeping and wailing loudly. **39** He went in and said to them, "Why all this commotion and crying? The child isn't dead — she's sleeping." **40** They laughed at him. But he put them all outside, took the child's father and mother and his three companions, and went in where the child was.

**41** He took the little girl by the hand and said to her, "Talitha koum"ʲ — which means, "Little girl, I'm telling you, get up!" **42** Immediately the girl stood up and started walking around. She was twelve years old. They were completely overwhelmed with astonishment. **43** Jesus gave them strict orders not to let anyone know about this, and told them to give her something to eat.

## Notes

- v.1a: Manuscripts vary between "Gerasenes," "Gadarenes," and "Gergesenes" — different towns in the same general region east of the Sea of Galilee
- v.7b: The demon speaks through the man, creating a jarring blend of worship and terror
- v.9c: *Legiōn* (Λεγιών) — a Roman military unit of roughly 5,000–6,000 soldiers; conveys overwhelming numbers
- v.20d: *Dekapolis* (Δεκάπολις) — "Ten Cities"; a league of Greco-Roman cities mostly east of the Jordan
- v.28e: *sōthēsomai* (σωθήσομαι) — "I will be saved/healed"; the Greek word encompasses both physical healing and spiritual salvation
- v.30f: *dynamin* (δύναμιν) — power, ability, force; the same word from which English gets "dynamite"
- v.34g: *pistis* (πίστις) — faith/trust; Jesus attributes the healing to her active trust, not to magic in his clothing
- v.34h: *eirēnē* (εἰρήνη) — peace; echoing the Hebrew *shalom*, a wholeness of well-being
- v.36i: *pisteue* (πίστευε) — present imperative: "keep on believing/trusting," not a one-time act
- v.41j: *Talitha koum* (Ταλιθα κουμ) — Aramaic, preserved in the original language; one of several places Mark preserves Jesus's actual Aramaic words
