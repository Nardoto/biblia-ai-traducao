# Mark 1

**1** This is where the good news about Jesus the Messiah,ᵃ the Son of God, begins.

**2** The prophet Isaiah wrote long ago: "Look — I'm sending my messenger ahead of you to clear the way. **3** A voice is shouting out in the wilderness: 'Get the road ready for the Lord! Straighten out his paths!'"

**4** And that's exactly what happened. John showed up in the desert, baptizing people and announcing, "Turn your lives around and be baptizedᵇ — and God will forgive your sins." **5** People streamed out to him from all over Judea and Jerusalem. They confessed their sins and he baptized them right there in the Jordan River. **6** John wore rough camel-hair clothing with a leather belt, and he lived on locusts and wild honey. **7** His message was this: "Someone far more powerful than me is coming. I'm not even worthy to bend down and untie his sandals. **8** I've baptized you with water, but he's going to baptize you with the Holy Spirit."

**9** Right around that time, Jesus came from Nazareth in Galilee, and John baptized him in the Jordan. **10** The instantᶜ Jesus came up out of the water, he saw the sky rip open and the Spirit coming down on him like a dove. **11** And a voice came from heaven: "You are my Son — the one I love. You bring me such joy."

**12** Immediatelyᶜ the Spirit drove him out into the wilderness. **13** He was out there forty days, tested by Satan, surrounded by wild animals — and angels took care of him.

**14** After John was arrested, Jesus went into Galilee and started proclaiming God's good news. **15** "The time has come," he said. "God's kingdomᵈ is right here. Turn your lives around and trust this good news!"

**16** Walking along the shore of the Sea of Galilee, Jesus saw Simon and his brother Andrew throwing their nets into the lake — they were fishermen. **17** Jesus told them, "Come follow me, and I'll make you fish for people instead." **18** Right then and there, they dropped their nets and followed him.

**19** A little further along, he spotted James and his brother John — Zebedee's sons — in their boat mending nets. **20** He called them immediately, and they left their father Zebedee behind in the boat with the hired workers and went after Jesus.

**21** They went into Capernaum, and as soon as the Sabbath came, Jesus walked into the synagogue and started teaching. **22** People were blown away by his teaching — he taught like someone with real authority,ᵉ not like the scripture scholars.

**23** Right then, a man in their synagogue who was controlled by an unclean spiritᶠ screamed out, **24** "What do you want with us, Jesus of Nazareth? Have you come to destroy us? I know exactly who you are — the Holy One of God!" **25** Jesus cut him off sharply: "Be quiet! Come out of him!" **26** The unclean spirit threw the man into convulsions, let out a loud shriek, and came out of him. **27** Everyone was so stunned they started asking each other, "What is this? Some kind of new teaching — and it comes with authority! He even gives orders to unclean spirits and they obey him!" **28** And news about Jesus spread like wildfire across the whole region of Galilee.

**29** As soon as they left the synagogue, they went straight to the home of Simon and Andrew, along with James and John. **30** Simon's mother-in-law was in bed with a fever, and they told Jesus about her right away. **31** He went to her, took her hand, and helped her up. The fever left her instantly, and she started serving them.

**32** That evening, after sunset, people brought everyone who was sick or demon-possessed to him. **33** The whole town crowded around the door. **34** He healed many people suffering from all kinds of diseases and drove out many demons. He wouldn't let the demons speak, because they knew who he was.

**35** Very early the next morning, while it was still dark, Jesus got up, slipped out, and went to a deserted place to pray. **36** Simon and the others went searching for him. **37** When they found him, they said, "Everyone's looking for you!" **38** But Jesus told them, "Let's move on to the neighboring towns — I need to preach there too. That's why I came." **39** So he traveled throughout all of Galilee, preaching in their synagogues and driving out demons.

**40** A man with a skin diseaseᵍ came to him, fell on his knees, and begged him, "If you're willing, you can make me clean." **41** Moved with deep compassion,ʰ Jesus reached out his hand, touched him, and said, "I am willing. Be clean!" **42** Instantly the disease left him, and he was clean. **43** Jesus sent him away immediately with a stern warning: **44** "Don't tell anyone about this. Go show yourself to the priest and offer what Moses commanded for your cleansing — that'll serve as proof to everyone." **45** But the man went out and couldn't stop talking about it, spreading the word everywhere. As a result, Jesus couldn't openly enter any town anymore — he had to stay out in isolated areas. And still, people kept coming to him from everywhere.

## Notes

- v.1a: *Christos* (Χριστός) — Greek for "Anointed One," equivalent to Hebrew "Messiah"
- v.4b: *baptizō* (βαπτίζω) — means to immerse or submerge in water
- v.10c: *euthys* (εὐθύς) — Mark's signature word, used over 40 times in his gospel; conveys urgency and immediacy. Rendered variously as "instantly," "immediately," "right then," etc. throughout to preserve Mark's breathless pacing
- v.15d: *basileia tou theou* (βασιλεία τοῦ θεοῦ) — "kingdom of God"; refers to God's active reign and rule, not just a place
- v.22e: *exousia* (ἐξουσία) — authority that comes from an inherent right, not delegated permission
- v.23f: *pneuma akatharton* (πνεῦμα ἀκάθαρτον) — literally "unclean spirit"; Mark uses this term frequently
- v.40g: *lepra* (λέπρα) — traditionally translated "leprosy" but likely referred to various skin conditions, not just Hansen's disease
- v.41h: *splanchnistheis* (σπλαγχνισθείς) — moved in the gut, visceral compassion; some manuscripts read *orgistheis* ("angered"), which some scholars consider original
