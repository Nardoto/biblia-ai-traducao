# Mark 13

**1** As Jesus was leaving the temple, one of his disciples said, "Teacher, look! What incredible stones! What amazing buildings!" **2** Jesus said, "You see all these great buildings? Not one stone will be left on top of another — every one of them will be torn down."

**3** Later, as Jesus was sitting on the Mount of Olives facing the temple, Peter, James, John, and Andrew asked him privately, **4** "Tell us — when will these things happen? And what will be the sign that all of this is about to take place?"

**5** Jesus began telling them, "Watch out that no one deceives you. **6** Many will come using my name, saying 'I am he,' and they'll deceive a lot of people. **7** When you hear about wars and rumors of wars, don't panic. These things have to happen, but the end isn't here yet. **8** Nation will rise against nation and kingdom against kingdom. There will be earthquakes in various places and famines. These are just the beginning of the birth pains.ᵃ

**9** "But watch yourselves. They'll hand you over to courts. You'll be beaten in synagogues. You'll stand before governors and kings because of me — as a testimony to them. **10** And the good news must first be proclaimed to all nations.ᵇ **11** When they arrest you and bring you to trial, don't worry ahead of time about what to say. Just say whatever is given to you at that moment — because it won't be you speaking, but the Holy Spirit.

**12** "Brother will betray brother to death, and a father his child. Children will rise up against their parents and have them put to death. **13** Everyone will hate you because of my name. But whoever endures to the endᶜ will be saved.

**14** "When you see 'the abomination that causes desolation'ᵈ standing where it should not be" — let the reader understand — "then those in Judea should flee to the mountains. **15** Someone on the rooftop shouldn't go down or go inside to get anything from their house. **16** Someone in the field shouldn't turn back for their cloak. **17** How terrible it will be in those days for pregnant women and nursing mothers! **18** Pray that it doesn't happen in winter. **19** Because those days will bring suffering unlike anything since the beginning of God's creation until now — and nothing like it will ever happen again.ᵉ **20** If the Lord hadn't cut those days short, no one would survive. But for the sake of the ones he's chosen, he has shortened them.

**21** "If anyone tells you then, 'Look, here's the Messiah!' or 'Look, there he is!' — don't believe it. **22** False messiahs and false prophets will appear and perform signs and wonders to deceive, if possible, even the chosen ones. **23** So stay alert. I've told you everything ahead of time.

**24** "But in those days, after that suffering, 'the sun will go dark and the moon won't give its light. **25** The stars will fall from the sky, and the powers in the heavens will be shaken.'ᶠ

**26** "And then they'll see the Son of Man coming on clouds with great power and glory.ᵍ **27** He'll send out his angels and gather his chosen ones from the four winds — from the ends of the earth to the ends of the sky.

**28** "Learn this lesson from the fig tree: When its branches get tender and its leaves sprout, you know summer is near. **29** In the same way, when you see these things happening, you know it'sʰ near — right at the door. **30** I'm telling you the truth: this generationⁱ will not pass away until all these things have taken place. **31** Heaven and earth will pass away, but my words will never pass away.

**32** "But as for that day or hour — no one knows. Not the angels in heaven. Not even the Son.ʲ Only the Father. **33** Stay alert! Stay watchful! You don't know when the time will come.

**34** "It's like a man going on a journey. He leaves his home, puts his servants in charge — each with their own task — and tells the doorkeeper to stay awake. **35** So stay awake! Because you don't know when the master of the house is coming — whether in the evening, at midnight, when the rooster crows, or at dawn. **36** Don't let him come suddenly and find you asleep. **37** What I say to you, I say to everyone: Stay awake!"

## Notes

- v.8a: *ōdinōn* (ὠδίνων) — labor pains; a Jewish metaphor for the sufferings that precede the Messianic age, called "the birth pangs of the Messiah"
- v.10b: *pasin tois ethnesin* (πᾶσιν τοῖς ἔθνεσιν) — "to all the nations/peoples"; a universal mission
- v.13c: *ho hypomeinas eis telos* (ὁ ὑπομείνας εἰς τέλος) — *hypomonē* is patient endurance under pressure, not passive waiting
- v.14d: *to bdelygma tēs erēmōseōs* (τὸ βδέλυγμα τῆς ἐρημώσεως) — from Daniel 9:27, 11:31, 12:11; originally referred to the desecration of the temple by Antiochus IV Epiphanes in 167 BC. "Let the reader understand" is likely Mark's editorial note, signaling the phrase should be interpreted symbolically
- v.19e: Echoing Daniel 12:1
- v.24-25f: Drawing on Isaiah 13:10, 34:4; Joel 2:10 — apocalyptic imagery of cosmic upheaval used by the Hebrew prophets
- v.26g: Alluding to Daniel 7:13-14, where "one like a son of man" receives dominion from the Ancient of Days
- v.29h: The subject is ambiguous — "it" (the event) or "he" (the Son of Man) is near
- v.30i: *hē genea hautē* (ἡ γενεὰ αὕτη) — "this generation"; one of the most debated phrases in the chapter. Interpretations include: the generation alive at that time, the Jewish people as a whole, or the generation that sees the signs begin
- v.32j: *oude ho huios* (οὐδὲ ὁ υἱός) — "not even the Son"; a striking admission of limited knowledge, emphasizing genuine humanity; some later manuscripts omit this phrase
