# Mark 9

**1** And he told them, "I'm telling you the truth: some of you standing here won't taste death before you see God's kingdom arrive in power."

**2** Six days later, Jesus took Peter, James, and John and led them up a high mountain where they were completely alone. And he was transformed right before their eyes.ᵃ **3** His clothes became dazzlingly white — whiter than anything any bleacher on earth could make them. **4** Then Elijah appeared to them, along with Moses, and they were talking with Jesus.

**5** Peter spoke up and said to Jesus, "Rabbi,ᵇ it's so good that we're here! Let us set up three shelters — one for you, one for Moses, and one for Elijah." **6** He didn't really know what to say — they were so terrified.

**7** Then a cloud came and covered them, and a voice came from the cloud: "This is my Son — the one I love. Listen to him!" **8** Suddenly, when they looked around, they no longer saw anyone except Jesus alone with them.

**9** As they were coming down the mountain, Jesus ordered them not to tell anyone what they'd seen until the Son of Man had risen from the dead. **10** They kept this to themselves, though they debated what "rising from the dead" meant.

**11** They asked him, "Why do the scripture scholars say that Elijah must come first?" **12** He answered, "Elijah does come first to restore everything. And yet, how is it written about the Son of Man? That he must suffer greatly and be treated with contempt. **13** But I tell you, Elijah has already come, and they did whatever they pleased to him — just as it's written about him."

**14** When they came to the other disciples, they saw a large crowd around them and scripture scholars arguing with them. **15** The whole crowd was thrilled to see Jesus as soon as they spotted him, and they ran to greet him. **16** "What are you arguing about with them?" he asked.

**17** A man in the crowd answered, "Teacher, I brought you my son. He has a spirit that won't let him speak. **18** Whenever it seizes him, it throws him to the ground. He foams at the mouth, grinds his teeth, and goes rigid. I asked your disciples to drive it out, but they couldn't."

**19** Jesus responded, "What a faithless generation! How long must I be with you? How long must I put up with you? Bring him to me." **20** They brought the boy. The moment the spirit saw Jesus, it threw the boy into a convulsion. He fell on the ground and rolled around, foaming at the mouth. **21** Jesus asked the father, "How long has this been happening to him?" "Since he was little," the father said. **22** "It's often thrown him into fire or water to kill him. But if you can do anything — have compassion on us and help us!"

**23** Jesus said to him, "'If you can'?ᶜ Everything is possible for the one who trusts." **24** Immediately the boy's father cried out, "I do trust! Help me with my distrust!"ᵈ

**25** When Jesus saw that a crowd was rushing toward them, he rebuked the unclean spirit: "You spirit that makes him deaf and mute — I command you, come out of him and never enter him again!" **26** The spirit screamed, threw the boy into terrible convulsions, and came out. The boy looked so lifeless that most people said, "He's dead." **27** But Jesus took him by the hand and lifted him up, and he stood on his own.

**28** After Jesus had gone indoors, his disciples asked him privately, "Why couldn't we drive it out?" **29** He told them, "This kind can only come out through prayer."ᵉ

**30** They left that place and traveled through Galilee. Jesus didn't want anyone to know where they were, **31** because he was teaching his disciples. He told them, "The Son of Man is going to be handed over to people. They'll kill him, and three days after being killed, he'll rise." **32** But they didn't understand what he was saying and were afraid to ask him.

**33** They came to Capernaum. Once he was inside the house, he asked them, "What were you arguing about on the road?" **34** They stayed quiet, because on the road they'd been arguing about which of them was the greatest.

**35** Jesus sat down, called the Twelve over, and said, "If anyone wants to be first, they must be last of all and servant of all."ᶠ **36** He took a little child and stood the child among them. Then he wrapped his arms around the child and said, **37** "Whoever welcomes one of these little children in my name welcomes me. And whoever welcomes me isn't just welcoming me — they're welcoming the One who sent me."

**38** John said to him, "Teacher, we saw someone driving out demons in your name, and we told him to stop because he wasn't one of us." **39** But Jesus said, "Don't stop him. No one who does a mighty work in my name can turn around and speak against me anytime soon. **40** Whoever isn't against us is for us. **41** I'm telling you the truth: anyone who gives you a cup of water because you belong to the Messiahᵍ won't lose their reward."

**42** "But whoever causes one of these little ones who trust in me to stumble — it would be better for them to have a massive millstone hung around their neck and be thrown into the sea.

**43** "If your hand causes you to stumble, cut it off. It's better to enter life maimed than to go into Gehenna — the unquenchable fireʰ — with both hands. **45** If your foot causes you to stumble, cut it off. It's better to enter life lame than to be thrown into Gehenna with both feet. **47** And if your eye causes you to stumble, tear it out. It's better to enter God's kingdom with one eye than to be thrown into Gehenna with both eyes — **48** 'where the worm never dies and the fire never goes out.'ⁱ

**49** "Everyone will be salted with fire.ʲ **50** Salt is good, but if it loses its saltiness, how will you make it salty again? Have salt among yourselves, and be at peace with each other."

## Notes

- v.2a: *metemorphōthē* (μετεμορφώθη) — "was transfigured/transformed"; the root gives us English "metamorphosis." A change from within, not just outward appearance
- v.5b: *Rhabbi* (Ῥαββί) — "my great one" or "my teacher"; an Aramaic title of respect. Mark translates this as *didaskalos* (teacher) elsewhere
- v.23c: Jesus echoes the father's own words back to him — turning the question from Jesus's ability to the father's faith
- v.24d: *pisteuō; boēthei mou tē apistia* (πιστεύω· βοήθει μου τῇ ἀπιστίᾳ) — one of the most honest prayers in Scripture; faith and doubt coexisting
- v.29e: Some manuscripts add "and fasting," but the earliest manuscripts have only "prayer"
- v.35f: *diakonos* (διάκονος) — servant, one who serves at table; the root of English "deacon." A radical redefinition of greatness
- v.41g: Literally "because you are Christ's" (*Christou este*) — belonging to the Messiah
- v.43h: *Geenna* (γέεννα) — Gehenna, from Hebrew *Ge-Hinnom* (Valley of Hinnom); a valley south of Jerusalem associated with burning refuse; became a symbol for divine judgment. Verses 44 and 46 are absent from the earliest manuscripts (they repeat v.48)
- v.48i: Quoting Isaiah 66:24
- v.49j: A notoriously difficult saying; fire may refer to purification, persecution, or judgment — "salted" suggesting preservation through testing
