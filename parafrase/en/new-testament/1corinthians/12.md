# 1 Corinthians 12

**1** Now about spiritual giftsᵃ, brothers and sisters, I don't want you to be uninformed. **2** You know that when you were pagans, you were somehow drawn away and led astray to mute idols. **3** So I want you to know that no one speaking by the Spirit of God says, "Jesus is cursed,"ᵇ and no one can say, "Jesus is Lord," except by the Holy Spirit.

**4** There are different kinds of giftsᶜ, but the same Spirit. **5** There are different kinds of service, but the same Lord. **6** There are different kinds of working, but the same God works all of them in everyone.

**7** Now to each person the manifestationᵈ of the Spirit is given for the common good. **8** To one person the Spirit gives a message of wisdom; to another a message of knowledge by the same Spirit; **9** to another faith by the same Spirit; to another gifts of healing by that one Spirit; **10** to another miraculous powers; to another prophecy; to another distinguishing between spirits; to another speaking in different tonguesᵉ; and to still another the interpretation of tongues. **11** All these are the work of one and the same Spirit, who distributes them to each person individually, just as he decides.

**12** Just as a body, though one, has many parts, and all its parts, though many, form one body — so it is with Christ. **13** For we were all baptized by one Spirit into one body — whether Jews or Greeks, slave or free — and we were all given the one Spirit to drink.

**14** Now the body isn't made up of one part but of many. **15** If the foot says, "Because I'm not a hand, I don't belong to the body," that doesn't make it any less a part of the body. **16** And if the ear says, "Because I'm not an eye, I don't belong to the body," that doesn't make it any less a part of the body. **17** If the whole body were an eye, where would the sense of hearing be? If the whole body were an ear, where would the sense of smell be? **18** But in fact, God has placed each part in the body just as he wanted it. **19** If they were all one part, where would the body be? **20** As it is, there are many parts but one body.

**21** The eye can't say to the hand, "I don't need you!" And the head can't say to the feet, "I don't need you!" **22** On the contrary, those parts of the body that seem weaker are actually indispensable. **23** And the parts we think are less honorable, we treat with special honor. And the parts that aren't presentable are treated with special modesty, **24** while our presentable parts need no special treatment. But God has put the body together, giving greater honor to the parts that lacked it, **25** so that there would be no division in the body, but that its parts would have equal concern for each other. **26** If one part suffers, every part suffers with it. If one part is honored, every part rejoices with it.

**27** Now you are the body of Christ, and each one of you is a part of it. **28** And God has placed in the church: first apostles, second prophets, third teachers, then miracles, then gifts of healing, helping, guidingᶠ, and speaking in various tongues. **29** Are all apostles? Are all prophets? Are all teachers? Do all work miracles? **30** Do all have gifts of healing? Do all speak in tongues? Do all interpret? **31** But eagerly desire the greater gifts. And now I'll show you the most excellent wayᵍ.

---

## Notes
- v.1a: πνευματικῶν (pneumatikōn) — could be translated "spiritual gifts" or "spiritual people"; the neuter "gifts" is more likely given the context
- v.3b: ἀνάθεμα Ἰησοῦς (anathema Iēsous) — "Jesus is cursed"; possibly something said under ecstatic pagan influence, or a test case Paul constructs
- v.4c: χαρίσματα (charismata) — grace-gifts; Paul pairs this with διακονιῶν (diakoniōn, "services," v.5) and ἐνεργημάτων (energēmatōn, "workings," v.6), connecting gifts to Trinity: Spirit, Lord, God
- v.7d: φανέρωσις (phanerōsis) — manifestation, disclosure, making visible; each gift reveals the Spirit's presence
- v.10e: γένη γλωσσῶν (genē glōssōn) — "kinds of tongues"; ecstatic speech or languages given by the Spirit
- v.28f: κυβερνήσεις (kybernēseis) — acts of guidance, administration, steering; from the word for a ship's pilot
- v.31g: καθ' ὑπερβολὴν ὁδόν (kath' hyperbolēn hodon) — "a way beyond all comparison"; this introduces chapter 13
