# 1 Corinthians 5

**1** There's actually a report of sexual immoralityᵃ among you — and it's a kind that doesn't even happen among pagans: a man is sleeping with his father's wife. **2** And you're proud of yourselves? Shouldn't you be grieving instead? Shouldn't the man who did this have been removed from your community?

**3** Here's the thing: even though I'm not physically present, I am with you in spirit. And I've already passed judgment on the one who did this, as if I were right there. **4** When you are gathered together in the name of our Lord Jesus, and my spirit is present with you along with the power of our Lord Jesus, **5** hand this man over to Satanᵇ for the destruction of his flesh, so that his spirit may be saved on the day of the Lord.

**6** Your boasting is not good. Don't you know that a little yeastᶜ works through the whole batch of dough? **7** Get rid of the old yeast so you can be a new batch — as you really are, since you're unleavened. For Christ, our Passover lambᵈ, has been sacrificed. **8** So let's celebrate the festival — not with old yeast, the yeast of malice and wickedness, but with the unleavened bread of sincerity and truth.

**9** I wrote to you in my earlier letterᵉ not to associate with sexually immoral people. **10** I didn't mean the immoral people of this world, or the greedy and swindlers, or idolaters — because then you'd have to leave the world entirely! **11** But what I actually wrote was this: don't associate with anyone who claims to be a believerᶠ but is sexually immoral, greedy, an idolater, verbally abusive, a drunk, or a swindler. Don't even eat with such a person.

**12** What business is it of mine to judge outsiders? Aren't you supposed to judge those who are inside? **13** God will judge those outside. "Remove the wicked person from among you."ᵍ

---

## Notes
- v.1a: πορνεία (porneia) — a broad term for sexual immorality; here refers to an incestuous relationship prohibited in both Jewish law (Lev 18:8) and Roman law
- v.5b: παραδοῦναι τῷ Σατανᾷ (paradounai tō Satana) — "hand over to Satan"; likely means expulsion from the community's protection into the domain where Satan operates, not a magical curse
- v.6c: ζύμη (zymē) — leaven, yeast; a metaphor for how a small corrupting influence can spread through an entire community
- v.7d: πάσχα (pascha) — Passover; Paul connects Christ's sacrifice directly to the Passover lamb, drawing on Exodus imagery
- v.9e: Paul refers to a prior letter (now lost) that preceded our "1 Corinthians"
- v.11f: ἀδελφὸς ὀνομαζόμενος (adelphos onomazomenos) — "one who bears the name of brother"; someone who identifies as a believer
- v.13g: Paul quotes Deuteronomy 17:7, applying Israel's community discipline principle to the church
