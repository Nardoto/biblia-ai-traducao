# 1 Corinthians 11

**1** Follow my example, as I follow the example of Christ.

**2** I praise you because you remember me in everything and hold firmly to the traditionsᵃ I passed on to you. **3** But I want you to understand that the headᵇ of every man is Christ, the head of the woman is the man, and the head of Christ is God. **4** Every man who prays or prophesies with his head covered dishonors his head. **5** But every woman who prays or prophesies with her head uncoveredᶜ dishonors her head — it's the same as having her head shaved. **6** If a woman doesn't cover her head, she might as well cut her hair off. But if it's disgraceful for a woman to cut off her hair or shave her head, then she should cover her head.

**7** A man shouldn't cover his head, since he is the image and gloryᵈ of God. But the woman is the glory of the man. **8** For man didn't come from woman, but woman from man. **9** And man wasn't created for woman, but woman for man. **10** For this reason, and because of the angels, a woman should have a sign of authorityᵉ on her head.

**11** Nevertheless, in the Lord, woman is not independent of man, nor man independent of woman. **12** For just as woman came from man, so also man is born of woman — and everything comes from God. **13** Judge for yourselves: is it proper for a woman to pray to God with her head uncovered? **14** Doesn't nature itself teach you that if a man has long hair, it's a disgrace to him, **15** but if a woman has long hair, it's her glory? For her hair is given to her as a covering. **16** If anyone wants to argue about this — we have no other practice, and neither do the churches of God.

**17** Now, in the instructions that follow, I have no praise for you, because your meetings do more harm than good. **18** In the first place, I hear that when you come together as a church, there are divisionsᶠ among you, and to some degree I believe it. **19** No doubt there have to be differences among you so that those who are genuine may be recognized.

**20** So when you come together, it's not really the Lord's Supper you're eating. **21** Because when you eat, each of you rushes ahead with your own meal. One person goes hungry while another gets drunk! **22** Don't you have homes to eat and drink in? Or do you despise God's church and humiliate those who have nothing? What should I say to you? Should I praise you? I certainly can't praise you for this!

**23** For I received from the Lord what I also passed on to you: the Lord Jesus, on the night he was betrayedᵍ, took bread, **24** and after giving thanks, he broke it and said, "This is my body, which is for you. Do this in remembranceʰ of me." **25** In the same way, after supper he took the cup, saying, "This cup is the new covenantⁱ in my blood. Do this, whenever you drink it, in remembrance of me." **26** For whenever you eat this bread and drink this cup, you proclaim the Lord's death until he comes.

**27** So whoever eats the bread or drinks the cup of the Lord in an unworthy manner will be guilty of sinning against the body and blood of the Lord. **28** Everyone should examine themselves before they eat of the bread and drink from the cup. **29** Because anyone who eats and drinks without recognizing the bodyʲ eats and drinks judgment on themselves. **30** That's why many among you are weak and sick, and a number of you have fallen asleep. **31** But if we examined ourselves, we wouldn't come under judgment. **32** When we are judged by the Lord, we're being disciplined so that we won't be condemned with the world.

**33** So, my brothers and sisters, when you come together to eat, wait for one another. **34** Anyone who is hungry should eat at home, so that when you come together it won't result in judgment. I'll give instructions about the other matters when I come.

## Notes
- v.2a: παραδόσεις (paradoseis) — traditions, things handed down; the oral teachings Paul delivered to them
- v.3b: κεφαλή (kephalē) — "head"; debated whether this means "source/origin" or "authority over"; likely carries both senses
- v.5c: ἀκατακαλύπτῳ τῇ κεφαλῇ (akatakalyptō tē kephalē) — with uncovered head; the specific cultural meaning of head-coverings in Corinth is debated
- v.7d: δόξα (doxa) — glory, honor, splendor, reflection; woman as the "glory" of man may mean his honor or the one who reflects his dignity
- v.10e: ἐξουσίαν (exousian) — authority; surprisingly, Paul says the woman should have authority "on her head," not that she should be "under" authority; the meaning is debated
- v.18f: σχίσματα (schismata) — divisions, tears, splits; the same word used in 1:10
- v.23g: παρεδίδετο (paredideto) — was being betrayed/handed over; the imperfect tense captures the ongoing action of that night
- v.24h: ἀνάμνησιν (anamnēsin) — remembrance, active recall; not mere memory but a re-presentation that makes the past event present
- v.25i: καινὴ διαθήκη (kainē diathēkē) — new covenant; echoing Jeremiah 31:31, Jesus establishes the promised new covenant through his blood
- v.29j: τὸ σῶμα (to sōma) — "the body"; could mean Christ's physical body in the elements, or the church as Christ's body; the social context of the passage suggests the latter
