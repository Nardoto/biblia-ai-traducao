# 1 Corinthians 6

**1** When any of you has a dispute with another believer, how dare you take it to court before the unrighteous instead of before God's people? **2** Don't you know that God's people will judge the world? And if the world is going to be judged by you, aren't you competent to settle trivial cases? **3** Don't you know that we'll judge angels? How much more the matters of everyday life! **4** So if you have disputes about such things, why do you appoint as judges the very people the church doesn't recognize?ᵃ **5** I say this to shame you. Is there really no one among you wise enough to settle a dispute between believers? **6** Instead, one believer takes another to court — and in front of unbelievers at that!

**7** The very fact that you have lawsuits among you means you've already lost. Why not just accept being wronged? Why not just accept being cheated? **8** Instead, you yourselves do wrong and cheat — and you do this to your own brothers and sisters!

**9** Don't you know that the unrighteous will not inherit the kingdom of God? Don't be deceived: neither the sexually immoral, nor idolaters, nor adulterers, nor the effeminateᵇ, nor men who have sex with men, **10** nor thieves, nor the greedy, nor drunkards, nor the verbally abusive, nor swindlers will inherit the kingdom of God. **11** And that's what some of you were. But you were washed, you were made holy, you were made rightᶜ in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ and by the Spirit of our God.

**12** "I have the right to do anything"ᵈ — but not everything is beneficial. "I have the right to do anything" — but I won't be mastered by anything. **13** "Food for the stomach and the stomach for food, and God will do away with both." But the body isn't meant for sexual immorality — it's for the Lord, and the Lord is for the body. **14** God raised the Lord from the dead, and by his power he'll raise us too.

**15** Don't you know that your bodies are membersᵉ of Christ himself? Should I then take the members of Christ and unite them with a prostitute? Absolutely not! **16** Don't you know that whoever is united with a prostitute becomes one body with her? As it says, "The two will become one flesh."ᶠ **17** But whoever is united with the Lord is one spirit with him.

**18** Flee from sexual immorality! Every other sin a person commits is outside the body, but whoever sins sexually sins against their own body. **19** Don't you know that your body is a templeᵍ of the Holy Spirit who is in you, whom you received from God? You are not your own — **20** you were bought at a price. So honor God with your body.

---

## Notes
- v.4a: This verse's syntax is debated; Paul may be asking a rhetorical question or giving an ironic command
- v.9b: μαλακοί (malakoi) — literally "soft ones"; the precise meaning is debated among scholars; could refer to moral weakness, effeminacy, or a specific sexual role
- v.11c: ἐδικαιώθητε (edikaiōthēte) — justified, declared righteous; Paul uses three powerful verbs: washed (ἀπελούσασθε), sanctified (ἡγιάσθητε), justified (ἐδικαιώθητε)
- v.12d: Paul appears to quote Corinthian slogans back at them — "πάντα μοι ἔξεστιν" (panta moi exestin, "all things are permitted to me") — then qualifies them
- v.15e: μέλη (melē) — limbs, body parts, members; the physical body is genuinely connected to Christ
- v.16f: Paul quotes Genesis 2:24, applying the "one flesh" union principle to sexual encounters
- v.19g: ναός (naos) — inner sanctuary; here applied to individual bodies, whereas in 3:16 it referred to the community collectively
