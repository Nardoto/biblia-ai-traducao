# 1 Corinthians 1

**1** This is Paul — called by God's own will to be an apostle of Christ Jesus — along with our brother Sosthenes, **2** writing to God's church in Corinth. You've been set apart in Christ Jesus, called to be holy, together with everyone everywhere who calls on the name of our Lord Jesus Christ — their Lord and ours.

**3** May graceᵃ and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ be yours.

**4** I'm always thanking God for you because of the grace he gave you through Christ Jesus. **5** Through him you've been made rich in every way — in all your speaking and in all your knowledge — **6** since our testimony about Christ was confirmed among you. **7** So you're not lacking any spiritual giftᵇ as you eagerly wait for our Lord Jesus Christ to be revealed. **8** He'll also keep you firm all the way to the end, so that you'll be blameless on the day of our Lord Jesus Christ. **9** God is faithful — and he's the one who called you into fellowshipᶜ with his Son, Jesus Christ our Lord.

**10** Now I'm urgingᵈ you, brothers and sisters, by the name of our Lord Jesus Christ: all of you, agree with one another. Don't let there be divisions among you. Instead, be perfectly united in mind and purpose. **11** Because, my brothers and sisters, Chloe's people have told me that there are quarrels among you. **12** What I mean is this: one of you says, "I follow Paul"; another says, "I follow Apollos"; another, "I follow Cephas"; and still another, "I follow Christ." **13** Is Christ divided? Was Paul crucified for you? Were you baptized in the name of Paul?

**14** I thank God that I didn't baptize any of you except Crispus and Gaius, **15** so nobody can say you were baptized in my name. **16** (Oh wait, I also baptized the household of Stephanas. Beyond that, I don't think I baptized anyone else.) **17** You see, Christ didn't send me to baptize — he sent me to announce the good news. And not with fancy rhetoric either, or the cross of Christ would be emptied of its power.

**18** The message of the crossᵉ is nonsense to those who are perishing, but to us who are being saved, it's the powerᶠ of God. **19** As it's written: "I will destroy the wisdom of the wise; the intelligence of the intelligent I will set aside." **20** Where's the wise person? Where's the scholar? Where's the debater of this age? Hasn't God made the world's wisdom look foolish? **21** Since, in God's wisdom, the world through its wisdom didn't come to know God, God was pleased to save those who believe through the "foolishness" of what we preach. **22** Jews ask for miraculous signs and Greeks search for wisdom, **23** but we preach Christ crucified — a stumbling blockᵍ to Jews and foolishness to Gentiles. **24** But to those who are called, both Jews and Greeks, Christ is the power of God and the wisdom of God. **25** Because God's "foolishness" is wiser than human wisdom, and God's "weakness" is stronger than human strength.

**26** Just look at yourselves when you were called, brothers and sisters. Not many of you were wise by human standards, not many were powerful, not many were from prominent families. **27** But God chose the foolish things of the world to shame the wise. God chose the weak things of the world to shame the strong. **28** God chose the low and despised things of the world — the things that are nothing — to nullify the things that are something, **29** so that nobody could boast before God. **30** It's because of him that you are in Christ Jesus, who became for us wisdom from God — and our righteousness, holiness, and redemption. **31** So, as it's written: "Let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord."

## Notes
- v.3a: χάρις (charis) — God's unmerited favor; paired with εἰρήνη (eirēnē), this greeting blends Jewish and Greco-Roman conventions
- v.7b: χάρισμα (charisma) — a gift freely given by grace; becomes a major theme in chapters 12–14
- v.9c: κοινωνία (koinōnia) — deep sharing, partnership, communion; more than casual association
- v.10d: παρακαλῶ (parakalō) — to urge, appeal, encourage; Paul's pastoral authority expressed as earnest appeal rather than command
- v.18e: λόγος τοῦ σταυροῦ (logos tou staurou) — literally "the word of the cross," the core message about Christ's crucifixion
- v.18f: δύναμις (dynamis) — power, might, ability; God's active, transforming energy
- v.23g: σκάνδαλον (skandalon) — a trap, snare, stumbling block; something that provokes offense or rejection
