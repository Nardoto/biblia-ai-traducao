# 1 Corinthians 7

**1** Now, about the things you wrote to me. You say, "It's good for a man not to touch a woman."ᵃ **2** But because of the temptation to sexual immorality, each man should have his own wife and each woman her own husband. **3** The husband should fulfill his marital duty to his wife, and likewise the wife to her husband. **4** The wife doesn't have authority over her own body — her husband does. And in the same way, the husband doesn't have authority over his own body — his wife does. **5** Don't deprive each other, except by mutual agreement and for a limited time, so you can devote yourselves to prayer. Then come back together, so that Satan won't tempt you because of your lack of self-control.

**6** I'm saying this as a concessionᵇ, not a command. **7** I wish everyone were like me, but each person has their own gift from God — one has this gift, another has that.

**8** To the unmarried and widows I say: it's good for them to stay single, as I am. **9** But if they can't control themselves, they should marry. It's better to marry than to burn with passion.

**10** To the married I give this command — not me, but the Lord: a wife must not separateᶜ from her husband. **11** But if she does, she must remain unmarried or be reconciled to her husband. And a husband must not divorce his wife.

**12** To the rest I say this — I, not the Lord: if a brother has a wife who isn't a believer and she's willing to live with him, he must not divorce her. **13** And if a woman has a husband who isn't a believer and he's willing to live with her, she must not divorce him. **14** Because the unbelieving husband has been made holyᵈ through his wife, and the unbelieving wife has been made holy through the believing husband. Otherwise your children would be unclean, but as it is, they are holy. **15** But if the unbeliever leaves, let them leave. The brother or sister is not bound in such cases. God has called you to peace. **16** How do you know, wife, whether you'll save your husband? Or how do you know, husband, whether you'll save your wife?

**17** Nevertheless, each person should live the life the Lord has assigned to them, just as they were when God called them. This is the rule I establish in all the churches. **18** Was someone already circumcised when they were called? They shouldn't try to reverse it. Was someone uncircumcised when called? They shouldn't be circumcised. **19** Circumcision means nothing, and uncircumcision means nothing. What matters is keeping God's commands. **20** Each person should remain in the situation they were in when God called them. **21** Were you a slave when you were called? Don't let it trouble you — though if you can gain your freedom, by all means do so.ᵉ **22** For the person who was a slave when called by the Lord is the Lord's freed person; likewise, the free person who was called is Christ's slave. **23** You were bought at a price — don't become slaves of human beings. **24** Brothers and sisters, each person should remain with God in whatever situation they were in when they were called.

**25** Now about virgins:ᶠ I don't have a command from the Lord, but I give my opinion as someone who by the Lord's mercy is trustworthy. **26** Because of the present crisisᵍ, I think it's good for a person to remain as they are. **27** Are you bound to a wife? Don't seek to be free. Are you free from a wife? Don't look for one. **28** But if you do marry, you haven't sinned. And if a virgin marries, she hasn't sinned. But those who marry will face troubles in this life, and I'm trying to spare you that.

**29** What I mean, brothers and sisters, is that the time is short.ʰ From now on, those who have wives should live as if they had none; **30** those who mourn, as if they weren't mourning; those who are happy, as if they weren't happy; those who buy things, as if they didn't own them; **31** those who use the things of this world, as if they weren't fully using them. For this world in its present form is passing away.

**32** I want you free from worries. An unmarried man is concerned about the Lord's affairs — how to please the Lord. **33** But a married man is concerned about worldly affairs — how to please his wife — **34** and his interests are divided. An unmarried woman or virgin is concerned about the Lord's affairs, wanting to be devoted to the Lord in both body and spirit. But a married woman is concerned about worldly affairs — how to please her husband. **35** I'm saying this for your own good, not to put restrictions on you, but to promote good order and undivided devotion to the Lord.

**36** If anyone thinks they're acting improperly toward their fiancée,ⁱ and if her passions are strong, and it has to be — let them do as they wish. They're not sinning. Let them get married. **37** But the man who stands firm in his resolve, who isn't under compulsion but has control over his own will, and has decided in his heart to keep her as his fiancée — this man also does well. **38** So the one who marries his fiancée does well, and the one who doesn't marry does even better.

**39** A wife is bound to her husband as long as he lives. But if her husband dies, she's free to marry anyone she wishes — but only in the Lord. **40** In my opinion, she's happier if she stays as she is. And I think I also have the Spirit of God.

## Notes
- v.1a: ἅπτεσθαι γυναικός (haptesthai gynaikos) — "to touch a woman"; likely a Corinthian slogan Paul is quoting, possibly from their letter, not his own teaching
- v.6b: συγγνώμην (syngnōmēn) — concession, allowance; not a command but pastoral flexibility
- v.10c: χωρισθῆναι (chōristhēnai) — to separate, depart; the standard legal term for divorce in the Greco-Roman world
- v.14d: ἡγίασται (hēgiastai) — has been sanctified, set apart; not automatic salvation but a holy influence through the believing spouse
- v.21e: The phrase μᾶλλον χρῆσαι (mallon chrēsai) is ambiguous — it could mean "make the most of your slavery" or "take the opportunity for freedom"; the latter reading is followed here
- v.25f: παρθένων (parthenōn) — virgins; could refer specifically to engaged women, or to unmarried people more broadly
- v.26g: ἐνεστῶσαν ἀνάγκην (enestōsan anankēn) — "the present distress/crisis"; possibly local persecution or eschatological urgency
- v.29h: ὁ καιρὸς συνεσταλμένος (ho kairos synestalmenos) — "the time has been compressed/shortened"; Paul's sense of urgency in light of Christ's return
- v.36i: τὴν παρθένον αὐτοῦ (tēn parthenon autou) — "his virgin"; debated whether this refers to a fiancée, a virgin daughter, or a spiritual companion
