# 2 Corinthians 1

**1** Paul, an apostle of Christ Jesus by God's will, along with our brother Timothy — to God's community in Corinth and to all God's people throughout the region of Achaia: **2** may grace and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ be with you.

**3** All praise to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ! He's the Father of compassion and the God of every kind of comfort. **4** He comforts us in every trouble we face, so that we can comfort others going through any kind of difficulty, using the same comfort we've received from God. **5** Because just as Christ's sufferings overflow into our lives, so through Christ our comfort overflows too. **6** If we're going through hard times, it's for your comfort and salvation. If we're being comforted, it's so you can receive the same comfort that gives you the strength to endure the same sufferings we face. **7** And our hope for you is rock solid, because we know that just as you share in our sufferings, you also share in our comfort.

**8** Brothers and sisters, we don't want you to be in the dark about what happened to us in the province of Asia. We were crushed beyond what we could bear, to the point where we gave up hope of staying alive. **9** In fact, we felt like we'd received a death sentence inside ourselves. But this happened so we wouldn't rely on ourselves but on God, who raises the dead. **10** He rescued us from such a deadly threat, and he'll keep rescuing us. We've placed our hope in him that he'll continue to deliver us, **11** as you also help us through your prayers. That way, many people will give thanks to God on our behalf for the favor granted to us through the prayers of so many.

**12** And here's what we're proud of: our conscience testifies that we've conducted ourselves in this world — and especially in our relationship with you — with the sincerity and purity that come from God, not with worldly wisdom but by God's grace. **13** We're not writing anything you can't read and understand. And I hope you'll come to fully understand, **14** just as you've partly understood already, that we're your reason for pride just as you'll be ours on the day of the Lord Jesus.

**15** With that confidence, I wanted to visit you first, so you'd receive a double blessing. **16** I planned to visit you on my way to Macedonia, then come back to you from Macedonia, and have you send me on my way to Judea. **17** When I made this plan, was I being flippant? Do I make my plans the way the world does, saying "yes, yes" and "no, no" in the same breath? **18** But as God is faithful, our message to you isn't "yes" and "no" at the same time. **19** Because the Son of God, Jesus Christ, who was proclaimed among you by us — by me, Silvanus, and Timothy — wasn't "yes" and "no." In him it's always been "yes." **20** For all of God's promises find their "yes" in him. That's why through him we say "amen" to the glory of God. **21** And it's God who establishes us with you in Christ. He anointed us, **22** sealed us, and placed the Spirit in our hearts as a down payment of what's to come.

**23** I call God as my witness against my own life: it was to spare you that I didn't come back to Corinth. **24** Not that we lord it over your faith — on the contrary, we're partners in your joy, because you stand firm by faith.

---

## Notes

- v.8: The "affliction in Asia" (θλῖψις, thlipsis) refers to an unidentified event — possibly persecution in Ephesus or another severe crisis.
- v.20: "Amen" (ἀμήν) comes from Hebrew meaning "so be it" or "it is true" — Paul connects God's faithfulness to promises fulfilled in Christ.
- v.22: "Down payment" (ἀρραβών, arrabōn) was a commercial term for the first installment guaranteeing the rest — the Spirit is the first payment of the life to come.
