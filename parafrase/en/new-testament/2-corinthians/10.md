# 2 Corinthians 10

**1** I, Paul, personally appeal to you by the meekness and gentleness of Christ — I who am supposedly "timid when face to face but bold when far away." **2** I beg you that when I'm there, I won't have to be as bold as I intend to be toward some who think we operate by worldly standards. **3** For although we live in this world, we don't wage war by the world's standards. **4** The weapons of our warfare aren't human — they're divinely powerful for tearing down strongholds. We tear down arguments **5** and every arrogant claim that sets itself up against the knowledge of God, and we take every thought captive to make it obey Christ. **6** And we're ready to punish every act of disobedience once your obedience is complete.

**7** Look at what's right in front of you! If anyone is convinced they belong to Christ, let them consider again: just as they belong to Christ, so do we. **8** Even if I boast a little more about the authority the Lord gave us for building you up rather than tearing you down, I won't be put to shame. **9** I say this so I won't seem to be trying to scare you with my letters. **10** For someone says, "His letters are weighty and powerful, but in person he's unimpressive and his speaking amounts to nothing." **11** Let that person understand this: what we are by letter when we're absent, we'll be in action when we're present.

**12** We wouldn't dare classify or compare ourselves with some who recommend themselves. They measure themselves by themselves and compare themselves with themselves — and they don't understand a thing. **13** We, however, won't boast beyond limits but within the field God has marked out for us, a field that reaches all the way to you. **14** We're not overextending ourselves as if we hadn't reached you — because we did reach you with the gospel of Christ. **15** We don't boast beyond limits about other people's work. Our hope is that as your faith grows, our area of activity among you will expand far beyond what it is now, **16** so that we can preach the gospel in the regions beyond you, without boasting about what's already been accomplished in someone else's territory. **17** "Let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord." **18** For it's not the one who recommends himself who's approved, but the one the Lord recommends.

---

## Notes

- v.1: Paul's irony is sharp — he quotes his opponents' accusations against him. From chapter 10, the tone shifts dramatically.
- v.4: "Strongholds" (ὀχυρωμάτων, ochyrōmatōn) — a military metaphor for systems of thought that resist the truth.
- v.10: This is a direct quote from Paul's critics — suggesting he wasn't an impressive speaker by Greco-Roman standards.
- v.17: Quotation from Jeremiah 9:24.
