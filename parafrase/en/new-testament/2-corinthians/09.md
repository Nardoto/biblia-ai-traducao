# 2 Corinthians 9

**1** Actually, it's unnecessary for me to write to you about this service to the saints. **2** I know your eagerness, and I've been bragging about you to the Macedonians, telling them that Achaia has been ready since last year. And your enthusiasm has motivated most of them. **3** But I'm sending the brothers so that our boasting about you on this point won't turn out to be empty, and so you'll actually be ready, just as I said you would be. **4** Because if some Macedonians come with me and find you unprepared, we — not to mention you — would be embarrassed by our confidence. **5** So I thought it necessary to ask the brothers to go ahead to you and arrange in advance the generous gift you promised. That way it'll be ready as an act of generosity, not as something grudgingly extracted.

**6** Remember this: whoever sows sparingly will reap sparingly, and whoever sows generously will reap generously. **7** Each person should give what they've decided in their heart, without reluctance or pressure, because God loves a cheerful giver. **8** And God is able to make all grace overflow to you, so that in all things at all times, having all you need, you'll overflow in every good work. **9** As it's written: "He scattered his gifts, he gave to the poor; his righteousness endures forever."

**10** The one who supplies seed to the sower and bread for food will also supply and multiply your seed and increase the harvest of your righteousness. **11** You'll be enriched in every way for all generosity, which through us produces thanksgiving to God. **12** Because this act of service doesn't just supply the needs of the saints — it also overflows in many expressions of thanksgiving to God. **13** Through the proof this service provides, they'll glorify God for your obedient confession of Christ's gospel and for the generosity of your contribution to them and to everyone. **14** And in their prayers for you, their hearts will go out to you because of the surpassing grace God has given you. **15** Thanks be to God for his indescribable gift!

---

## Notes

- v.6: "Sowing and reaping" — Paul uses agricultural language (σπείρω/θερίζω) as a metaphor for generosity, not as a promise of financial returns.
- v.7: "Cheerful giver" (ἱλαρὸν δότην, hilaron dotēn) — ἱλαρός is the root of "hilarious"; giving should bring genuine pleasure.
- v.9: Quotation from Psalm 112:9.
- v.15: "Indescribable gift" (ἀνεκδιηγήτῳ δωρεᾷ) — likely refers to Christ, though it could also encompass the entire cascade of grace Paul has just described.
