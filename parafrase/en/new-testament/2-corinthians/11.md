# 2 Corinthians 11

**1** I wish you'd put up with a little foolishness from me — and in fact, you are putting up with me! **2** I'm jealous for you with a divine jealousy. I promised you in marriage to one husband, to present you as a pure virgin to Christ. **3** But I'm afraid that just as the serpent deceived Eve with his cunning, your minds may be led astray from the simplicity and purity devoted to Christ. **4** For if someone comes along and preaches a different Jesus than the one we preached, or if you receive a different spirit from the one you received, or a different gospel from the one you accepted, you put up with it readily enough!

**5** I consider myself not one bit inferior to those "super-apostles." **6** I may not be a trained speaker, but I certainly have knowledge — and we've made that clear to you in every possible way.

**7** Did I commit a sin by humbling myself to lift you up, by preaching God's gospel to you free of charge? **8** I robbed other churches by accepting support from them in order to serve you. **9** And when I was with you and needed something, I wasn't a burden to anyone. The brothers who came from Macedonia supplied what I lacked. In every way I kept myself from being a burden to you, and I'll keep doing so. **10** As surely as Christ's truth is in me, no one in the regions of Achaia will take this boast away from me. **11** Why? Because I don't love you? God knows I do!

**12** And I'll keep doing what I'm doing, to cut the ground out from under those who want an opportunity to be considered equal to us in the things they boast about. **13** For such people are false apostles, dishonest workers, disguising themselves as apostles of Christ. **14** And no wonder — even Satan disguises himself as an angel of light. **15** So it's not surprising if his servants disguise themselves as servants of righteousness. Their end will match their deeds.

**16** I'll say it again: don't let anyone think I'm a fool. But if you do, then accept me as a fool so I can do a little boasting too. **17** What I'm about to say, I'm not saying as the Lord would — I'm saying it like a fool, in this confident boasting. **18** Since many boast by human standards, I'll boast too. **19** You who are so wise gladly put up with fools! **20** You put up with it when someone enslaves you, when someone devours you, when someone takes advantage of you, when someone puts on airs, when someone slaps you in the face!

**21** To my shame, I confess we were "too weak" for that! But whatever anyone else dares to boast about — I'm speaking as a fool — I dare to boast about it too. **22** Are they Hebrews? So am I. Are they Israelites? So am I. Are they Abraham's descendants? So am I. **23** Are they servants of Christ? I'm out of my mind to talk like this — I'm more of one! With far more labors, far more imprisonments, beaten countless times, facing death again and again. **24** Five times I received from the Jews forty lashes minus one. **25** Three times I was beaten with rods, once I was stoned, three times I was shipwrecked, I spent a night and a day adrift at sea. **26** On frequent journeys, in danger from rivers, in danger from bandits, in danger from my own people, in danger from Gentiles, in danger in the city, in danger in the wilderness, in danger at sea, in danger from false brothers. **27** In toil and hardship, many sleepless nights, in hunger and thirst, often going without food, in cold and without enough clothing.

**28** Beyond all that, there's the daily pressure on me: my concern for all the churches. **29** Who's weak without me feeling weak too? Who stumbles without me burning inside?

**30** If I must boast, I'll boast about the things that show my weakness. **31** The God and Father of the Lord Jesus — blessed be he forever — knows I'm not lying. **32** In Damascus, the governor under King Aretas had the city of the Damascenes guarded in order to arrest me, **33** but I was lowered in a basket through a window in the wall and escaped his grasp.

---

## Notes

- v.2: "Divine jealousy" (θεοῦ ζήλῳ) — Paul sees himself as the "father of the bride" who prepared the bride (the community) for the wedding (Christ's return).
- v.5: "Super-apostles" (ὑπερλίαν ἀποστόλων) — an ironic term for Paul's rivals in Corinth; debated whether they're the Twelve or itinerant preachers.
- v.24: The 39 lashes (forty minus one, per Deut 25:3) indicate Paul still attended synagogues and was punished as a community member.
- v.25-27: Most of these events aren't recorded in Acts, showing we know far less about Paul's life than we think.
- v.32-33: This episode is also recorded in Acts 9:23-25; the basket (σαργάνη) was probably woven rope.
