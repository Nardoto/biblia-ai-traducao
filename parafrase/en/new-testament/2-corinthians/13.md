# 2 Corinthians 13

**1** This will be the third time I'm coming to you. "Every matter must be established by the testimony of two or three witnesses." **2** I already warned you — and I warn you again, as I did when I was there the second time, and now while I'm away — I say to all who sinned earlier and to everyone else: when I return, I won't hold back. **3** Since you demand proof that Christ is speaking through me — he who isn't weak in dealing with you but is powerful among you. **4** For indeed he was crucified in weakness, but he lives by God's power. We too are weak in him, but we'll live with him by God's power in dealing with you.

**5** Examine yourselves to see whether you're in the faith. Test yourselves. Don't you recognize that Jesus Christ is in you? — unless you fail the test. **6** But I hope you'll realize that we haven't failed the test. **7** We pray to God that you won't do anything wrong — not so we'll look like we passed, but so you'll do what's right, even if we look like we've failed. **8** For we can't do anything against the truth, only for the truth. **9** We're glad when we're weak and you're strong. What we pray for is your complete restoration. **10** That's why I'm writing these things while I'm away — so that when I'm there, I won't have to use severity, in keeping with the authority the Lord gave me for building up and not for tearing down.

**11** Finally, brothers and sisters: rejoice, aim for restoration, encourage one another, be of the same mind, live in peace — and the God of love and peace will be with you. **12** Greet one another with a holy kiss. All the saints send you their greetings.

**13** The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, the love of God, and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit be with all of you.

---

## Notes

- v.1: Quotation from Deuteronomy 19:15 — Paul applies the legal principle to the context of his visits.
- v.4: "Crucified in weakness" (ἐσταυρώθη ἐξ ἀσθενείας) — summarizes Paul's central paradox: the cross looks like defeat but is the ultimate expression of God's power.
- v.5: "Examine yourselves" (ἑαυτοὺς πειράζετε) — Paul turns the tables; instead of him proving his authority, they should examine themselves.
- v.13: The most complete Trinitarian blessing in the NT — grace linked to Christ, love to the Father, fellowship to the Spirit. This formula has shaped Christian liturgy for centuries.
