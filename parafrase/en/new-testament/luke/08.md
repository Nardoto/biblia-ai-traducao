# Luke 8

**1** After this, Jesus traveled from one town and village to another, proclaiming and announcing the good news of God's kingdom. The Twelve were with him, **2** along with some women who had been healed of evil spirits and diseases: Mary, called Magdalene, from whom seven demons had gone out; **3** Joanna, the wife of Chuza — Herod's household manager; Susanna; and many others. These women were supporting them out of their own resources.ᵃ

**4** A large crowd was gathering, with people coming to him from town after town. He spoke to them in a parable:

**5** "A farmer went out to sow his seed. As he scattered it, some fell along the path and was trampled, and the birds ate it up. **6** Some fell on rocky ground, and when it sprouted, it withered because it had no moisture. **7** Some fell among thorns, and the thorns grew up alongside it and choked it out. **8** But some fell on good soil, and it grew up and produced a crop — a hundred times what was sown."

After saying this, he called out, "Whoever has ears to hear — listen!"ᵇ

**9** His disciples asked him what this parable meant. **10** He said, "You've been given the ability to understand the secrets of God's kingdom. But for the rest, it comes in parables, so that

'though seeing, they may not perceive,
and though hearing, they may not understand.'ᶜ

**11** "Here's what the parable means: The seed is God's word. **12** The ones along the path are people who hear it, but then the devil comes and takes the message from their hearts so they won't believe and be saved. **13** The ones on rocky ground are people who hear the word and receive it with joy, but they have no root. They believe for a while, but when testing comes, they fall away. **14** The seed that fell among thorns — those are people who hear, but as life goes on, they get choked by worries, wealth, and pleasures, and they never produce mature fruit. **15** But the seed on good soil — those are people who hear the word, hold on to it with a good and honest heart,ᵈ and produce a crop through patient endurance."

**16** "Nobody lights a lamp and then covers it with a bowl or shoves it under a bed. Instead, they put it on a stand so that people coming in can see the light. **17** There's nothing hidden that won't be brought into the open, and nothing concealed that won't be known and come to light. **18** So pay attention to how you listen. Whoever has something will be given more, but whoever has nothing — even what they think they have will be taken from them."

**19** His mother and brothers came to see him, but they couldn't get to him because of the crowd. **20** Someone told him, "Your mother and your brothers are standing outside, wanting to see you."

**21** He replied, "My mother and my brothers are those who hear God's word and do it."

**22** One day, he got into a boat with his disciples and said, "Let's cross over to the other side of the lake." So they set out. **23** As they were sailing, he fell asleep. A violent windstorm swept down on the lake, and the boat was being swamped — they were in real danger. **24** They went and woke him: "Master! Master! We're going to drown!" He got up, rebuked the wind and the raging waves, and they died down. Everything became calm.

**25** "Where is your faith?" he asked them. Shaken and amazed, they said to each other, "Who is this? He gives orders to the winds and the water, and they obey him!"

**26** They sailed on to the region of the Gerasenes,ᵉ which is across from Galilee. **27** When Jesus stepped ashore, a man from the town met him who was possessed by demons. For a long time, he hadn't worn clothes or lived in a house — he lived among the tombs.

**28** When he saw Jesus, he screamed, fell down before him, and shouted at the top of his voice, "What do you want with me, Jesus, Son of the Most High God? I'm begging you — don't torture me!" **29** For Jesus had been commanding the unclean spirit to come out of the man. It had seized him many times, and even though he'd been bound with chains and shackles and kept under guard, he'd snap the restraints and be driven by the demon into desolate places.

**30** Jesus asked him, "What's your name?"

"Legion,"ᶠ he said — because many demons had gone into him.

**31** And they kept begging Jesus not to order them into the abyss.ᵍ

**32** A large herd of pigs was feeding there on the hillside. The demons begged him to let them go into the pigs, and he gave them permission. **33** The demons came out of the man and went into the pigs, and the herd stampeded down the steep bank into the lake and drowned.

**34** When the men tending them saw what had happened, they ran off and reported it in the town and countryside. **35** People came out to see what had happened. They came to Jesus and found the man the demons had left — sitting at Jesus's feet, dressed, and in his right mind. And they were afraid. **36** Those who had seen it told them how the demon-possessed man had been healed. **37** Then the entire population of the Gerasene region asked Jesus to leave, because they were overwhelmed with fear. So he got into the boat and left.

**38** The man from whom the demons had gone out begged to go with him, but Jesus sent him away, saying, **39** "Go home and tell everyone what God has done for you." So the man went away, proclaiming throughout the whole town what Jesus had done for him.

**40** When Jesus returned, a crowd welcomed him — they'd all been waiting for him. **41** Then a man named Jairus came — he was a synagogue leader. He fell at Jesus's feet and begged him to come to his house, **42** because his only daughter, about twelve years old, was dying.

As Jesus was on his way, the crowds nearly crushed him. **43** And a woman was there who had been suffering from bleeding for twelve years.ʰ She'd spent everything she had on doctors but couldn't be healed by anyone. **44** She came up behind him and touched the fringe of his cloak,ⁱ and immediately her bleeding stopped.

**45** "Who touched me?" Jesus asked. Everyone denied it. Peter said, "Master, the crowds are pressing in on you from every side!"

**46** But Jesus said, "Someone touched me — I felt power go out from me."ʲ

**47** The woman realized she couldn't stay hidden. Trembling, she came forward, fell at his feet, and told — in front of everyone — why she had touched him and how she'd been instantly healed. **48** He said to her, "Daughter, your faith has healed you. Go in peace."

**49** While he was still speaking, someone came from the synagogue leader's house and said, "Your daughter has died. Don't bother the teacher anymore."

**50** When Jesus heard this, he said to Jairus, "Don't be afraid. Just believe, and she'll be healed."

**51** When he arrived at the house, he didn't let anyone go in with him except Peter, John, James, and the child's father and mother. **52** Everyone was weeping and mourning for her. "Stop crying," he said. "She isn't dead — she's sleeping."

**53** They laughed at him, knowing she was dead. **54** But he took her by the hand and said, "Child, get up!"ᵏ **55** Her spirit returned, and she stood up at once. He told them to give her something to eat. **56** Her parents were amazed. But he instructed them not to tell anyone what had happened.

## Notes

- v.2-3a: Luke uniquely names women who traveled with Jesus and supported his ministry financially. Joanna's connection to Herod's court (Chuza was Herod's epitropos, estate manager) is a remarkable detail — the gospel reached even the royal household.
- v.8b: "ὁ ἔχων ὦτα ἀκούειν ἀκουέτω" — a Semitic idiom calling for spiritual perception beyond mere hearing.
- v.10c: Quoting Isaiah 6:9. Jesus's parables simultaneously reveal truth to the receptive and conceal it from the unreceptive — not as punishment but as a natural consequence of spiritual posture.
- v.15d: "ἐν καρδίᾳ καλῇ καὶ ἀγαθῇ" (en kardia kalē kai agathē) — "with a good and noble heart." This phrase uses language familiar from Greek moral philosophy, a touch characteristic of Luke.
- v.26e: Manuscript traditions vary between "Gerasenes," "Gadarenes," and "Gergesenes." The location is on the eastern shore of the Sea of Galilee.
- v.30f: "Λεγιών" (Legiōn) — a Roman military term for a unit of about 5,000-6,000 soldiers. The name indicates both the large number of demons and evokes the oppressive Roman military presence.
- v.31g: "ἄβυσσος" (abyssos) — the abyss or bottomless pit, understood as the place of demonic imprisonment. The demons prefer even inhabiting pigs to being sent there.
- v.43h: Luke, the physician, notably omits Mark's comment that she had "suffered much under many doctors" and "grew worse" (Mark 5:26). He simply notes she couldn't be healed.
- v.44i: "κρασπέδου" (kraspedou) — the fringe or tassel of his garment, referring to the tzitzit worn by observant Jews (Numbers 15:38-39).
- v.46j: "δύναμιν ἐξεληλυθυῖαν ἀπ' ἐμοῦ" (dynamin exelēlythuian ap' emou) — "power having gone out from me." The healing was not automatic or mechanical — Jesus was aware of it, making the woman's faith the key element.
- v.54k: "ἡ παῖς, ἔγειρε" (hē pais, egeire) — "child, arise!" Luke translates Jesus's Aramaic command into Greek for his audience, while Mark preserves the original Aramaic "Talitha koum."
