# Luke 7

**1** After he'd finished saying all this to the people who were listening, he entered Capernaum. **2** There, a centurion had a slave who was very dear to him, and the slave was sick and about to die. **3** When the centurion heard about Jesus, he sent some Jewish elders to him, asking him to come and save his slave's life. **4** They came to Jesus and pleaded earnestly: "He deserves this from you — **5** he loves our nation, and he's the one who built our synagogue."ᵃ

**6** So Jesus went with them. But when he wasn't far from the house, the centurion sent friends to tell him, "Lord, don't trouble yourself — I'm not worthy to have you come under my roof. **7** That's why I didn't even consider myself worthy to come to you in person. Just say the word, and my servant will be healed.ᵇ **8** I understand authority — I'm a man under authority myself, with soldiers under me. I tell one, 'Go,' and he goes. I tell another, 'Come,' and he comes. I tell my slave, 'Do this,' and he does it."

**9** When Jesus heard this, he was amazed at him. He turned to the crowd following him and said, "I'm telling you, I haven't found faith this great even in Israel."ᶜ

**10** When the messengers returned to the house, they found the slave in good health.

**11** Soon afterward, Jesus went to a town called Nain, and his disciples and a large crowd went along with him. **12** As he approached the town gate, a dead man was being carried out — the only son of his mother, and she was a widow. A considerable crowd from the town was with her.

**13** When the Lord saw her, his heart went out to her,ᵈ and he said, "Don't cry." **14** He went up and touched the bier,ᵉ and the bearers stopped. He said, "Young man, I'm telling you — get up!" **15** The dead man sat up and began to speak, and Jesus gave him back to his mother.

**16** Awe gripped everyone. They praised God, saying, "A great prophet has appeared among us!" and "God has come to help his people!" **17** This news about Jesus spread throughout all of Judea and the entire surrounding region.

**18** John's disciples reported all these things to him. **19** So John called two of his disciples and sent them to the Lord to ask, "Are you the one who is coming, or should we expect someone else?"ᶠ

**20** When the men reached Jesus, they said, "John the Baptist sent us to you to ask: 'Are you the one who is coming, or should we expect someone else?'"

**21** Right at that moment, Jesus healed many people of diseases, afflictions, and evil spirits, and gave sight to many who were blind. **22** Then he answered them, "Go back and report to John what you've seen and heard: the blind see, the lame walk, those with leprosy are cleansed, the deaf hear, the dead are raised, and the poor are hearing good news.ᵍ **23** And blessed is anyone who doesn't stumble over me."

**24** After John's messengers left, Jesus began speaking to the crowds about John: "What did you go out into the wilderness to see? A reed swaying in the wind? **25** No, really — what did you go out to see? A man dressed in fine clothes? Look, people in splendid clothing and luxury live in royal palaces. **26** So what did you go out to see? A prophet? Yes, I'm telling you — and far more than a prophet. **27** This is the one about whom it's written:

'Look, I'm sending my messenger ahead of you,
who will prepare your way before you.'

**28** "I'm telling you, among those born of women, no one is greater than John. Yet the least person in God's kingdom is greater than he is."

**29** All the people who heard this — even the tax collectors — acknowledged God's justice, because they'd been baptized by John.ʰ **30** But the Pharisees and the legal experts rejected God's plan for themselves, since they hadn't been baptized by him.

**31** "So what should I compare the people of this generation to? What are they like? **32** They're like children sitting in the marketplace, calling out to each other:

'We played the flute for you, and you didn't dance.
We sang a dirge, and you didn't weep.'

**33** "John the Baptist came not eating bread or drinking wine, and you say, 'He has a demon.' **34** The Son of Man came eating and drinking, and you say, 'Look at this man — a glutton and a drunk, a friend of tax collectors and sinners!' **35** But wisdom is vindicated by all her children."ⁱ

**36** One of the Pharisees invited Jesus to eat with him. He went to the Pharisee's house and reclined at the table. **37** A woman in that town who had lived a sinful life learned that Jesus was eating at the Pharisee's house. She brought an alabaster jar of perfume, **38** and standing behind him at his feet, weeping, she began wetting his feet with her tears. She wiped them with her hair, kissed his feet over and over, and poured the perfume on them.ʲ

**39** When the Pharisee who had invited him saw this, he said to himself, "If this man were really a prophet, he'd know who this woman is who's touching him — and what kind of woman she is. She's a sinner!"

**40** Jesus responded, "Simon, I have something to say to you."

"Go ahead, Teacher," he said.

**41** "Two people owed money to a certain lender. One owed five hundred denarii, the other fifty.ᵏ **42** Neither of them could pay it back, so he graciously canceled both debts. Now, which of them will love him more?"

**43** Simon answered, "I suppose the one who had the bigger debt canceled."

"You've judged correctly," Jesus said.

**44** Then, turning toward the woman, he said to Simon, "Do you see this woman? I came into your house — you gave me no water for my feet, but she has wet my feet with her tears and wiped them with her hair. **45** You didn't greet me with a kiss, but she hasn't stopped kissing my feet since I arrived. **46** You didn't anoint my head with oil, but she has poured perfume on my feet. **47** That's why I'm telling you: her many sins have been forgiven — that's clear from how much love she's shown. But the one who is forgiven little loves little."ˡ

**48** Then he said to her, "Your sins are forgiven."

**49** The other guests began saying among themselves, "Who is this, who even forgives sins?"

**50** Jesus said to the woman, "Your faith has saved you. Go in peace."

## Notes

- v.5a: Luke's version uniquely includes the Jewish elders as intermediaries and the detail about the centurion building the synagogue — showing a Gentile who respects Judaism, fitting Luke's theme of worthy Gentiles.
- v.7b: "εἰπὲ λόγῳ" (eipe logō) — "just say with a word." The centurion recognizes Jesus's authority operates through his word alone, without physical presence.
- v.9c: "ἐθαύμασεν" (ethaumasen) — Jesus "marveled" or was amazed. One of the rare occasions where Jesus himself is surprised, highlighting the extraordinary nature of this Gentile's faith.
- v.13d: "ἐσπλαγχνίσθη" (esplanchnisthē) — a visceral word meaning compassion that grips the gut. Luke uses this word at pivotal moments (also 10:33, 15:20). It's Luke's characteristic term for deep divine compassion.
- v.14e: "σοροῦ" (sorou) — the bier or open coffin. By touching it, Jesus would become ritually unclean according to the Law — but instead of contamination flowing to him, life flows from him.
- v.19f: John's question from prison is honest and poignant. Even the great forerunner experienced doubt. Jesus's response doesn't rebuke the question but points to evidence.
- v.22g: Jesus's answer echoes Isaiah 35:5-6 and 61:1, listing the signs of the messianic age. The evidence speaks for itself.
- v.29h: "ἐδικαίωσαν τὸν θεόν" (edikaiōsan ton theon) — "justified God," meaning they acknowledged God's righteous ways by accepting John's baptism. A remarkable phrase unique to Luke.
- v.35i: "ἐδικαιώθη ἡ σοφία ἀπὸ πάντων τῶν τέκνων αὐτῆς" — Wisdom is justified/vindicated by all her children. Unlike Matthew's "by her deeds," Luke says "by her children" — wisdom's validity is proved by those who follow her.
- v.38j: The woman's actions are extravagant and culturally shocking. Letting down her hair in public was considered scandalous. Every detail conveys overwhelming gratitude and devotion.
- v.41k: A denarius was roughly a day's wage for a laborer. So the debts were approximately a year and a half's wages versus about two months' wages — a significant but comprehensible difference.
- v.47l: This verse is often misread. Jesus is not saying love earns forgiveness. The love is evidence of forgiveness already received — the parable makes the sequence clear: cancellation of debt comes first, then love follows.
