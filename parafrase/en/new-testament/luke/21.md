# Luke 21

**1** Jesus looked up and saw the wealthy putting their gifts into the temple treasury. **2** He also saw a poor widow put in two tiny copper coins.ᵃ **3** "I'm telling you the truth," he said, "this poor widow has put in more than all the others. **4** All these people gave their gifts out of their surplus, but she, out of her poverty, put in everything she had to live on."ᵇ

**5** When some were talking about the temple — how it was decorated with beautiful stones and dedicated gifts — he said, **6** "As for all these things you're admiring, the days will come when not one stone will be left on another. Every one will be torn down."

**7** They asked him, "Teacher, when will these things happen? And what will be the sign that they're about to take place?"

**8** He said, "Watch out — don't be deceived. Many will come in my name, saying, 'I am he!' and 'The time is near!' Don't follow them. **9** When you hear about wars and upheavals, don't be terrified. These things have to happen first, but the end won't come right away."

**10** Then he said to them, "Nation will rise against nation, and kingdom against kingdom. **11** There will be massive earthquakes, famines, and plagues in various places, along with terrifying events and great signs from heaven.

**12** "But before all this, they'll seize you and persecute you. They'll hand you over to synagogues and prisons, and you'll be brought before kings and governors because of my name. **13** This will be your opportunity to testify.ᶜ **14** So make up your minds not to prepare your defense ahead of time, **15** because I'll give you words and wisdom that none of your opponents will be able to resist or contradict.

**16** "You'll be betrayed even by parents, brothers, relatives, and friends, and some of you will be put to death. **17** Everyone will hate you because of my name. **18** But not a hair on your head will be lost.ᵈ **19** By your enduranceᵉ you will gain your lives.

**20** "When you see Jerusalem surrounded by armies, then know that its desolation is near.ᶠ **21** At that point, those in Judea should flee to the mountains, those in the city should get out, and those in the countryside shouldn't go into the city. **22** Because these are the days of punishment, fulfilling everything that's been written.

**23** "How terrible it will be in those days for pregnant women and nursing mothers! There will be great distress in the land and wrath against this people. **24** They'll fall by the edge of the sword and be taken as prisoners to all nations. Jerusalem will be trampled by the Gentiles until the times of the Gentiles are fulfilled.ᵍ

**25** "There will be signs in the sun, moon, and stars. On the earth, nations will be in anguish and bewilderment at the roaring and tossing of the sea. **26** People will faint from fear and anxiety about what's coming on the world, because the powers of the heavens will be shaken. **27** Then they'll see the Son of Man coming in a cloud with power and great glory.ʰ **28** When these things begin to happen, stand up straight and lift your heads — because your redemptionⁱ is drawing near."

**29** He told them a parable: "Look at the fig tree, and all the trees. **30** When they sprout leaves, you can see for yourselves and know that summer is near. **31** In the same way, when you see these things happening, know that God's kingdom is near.

**32** "I'm telling you the truth: this generation will not pass away until all these things have taken place.ʲ **33** Heaven and earth will pass away, but my words will never pass away.

**34** "Be careful, or your hearts will be weighed down by overindulgence, drunkenness, and the worries of this life — and that day will close on you suddenly like a trap. **35** Because it will come upon everyone living on the face of the whole earth. **36** Stay alert at all times, praying that you'll have the strength to escape all these things that are going to happen, and to stand before the Son of Man."

**37** During the days, he was teaching in the temple courts. During the nights, he would go out and stay on the hill called the Mount of Olives.ᵏ **38** And all the people would get up early in the morning to come and hear him in the temple.

## Notes

- v.2a: "λεπτὰ δύο" (lepta duo) — two lepta, the smallest coins in circulation. Mark adds "which is a quadrans" for his Roman audience; Luke, characteristically, omits the Roman equivalence.
- v.4b: "πάντα τὸν βίον" (panta ton bion) — literally "her whole livelihood." The word bios means her means of living, not just spare change. The contrast between surplus giving and sacrificial giving is stark.
- v.13c: "ἀποβήσεται ὑμῖν εἰς μαρτύριον" (apobēsetai hymin eis martyrion) — persecution becomes "an opportunity for testimony." The Greek martyrion is the root of "martyr" — testimony that may cost one's life.
- v.18d: The tension between "some will be put to death" (v.16) and "not a hair will perish" (v.18) is deliberately paradoxical. Physical death does not constitute ultimate loss; spiritual preservation is assured.
- v.19e: "ὑπομονῇ" (hypomonē) — endurance, patient steadfastness under pressure. Not passive waiting but active, courageous persistence. A key virtue in Luke's theology.
- v.20f: Luke's version replaces Mark's "abomination of desolation" (Mark 13:14) with the more concrete "Jerusalem surrounded by armies" — making the prophecy unmistakably about the Roman siege. This may reflect Luke writing with awareness of the AD 70 events, or it may be Jesus's more specific phrasing that Luke preserved.
- v.24g: "καιροὶ ἐθνῶν" (kairoi ethnōn) — "the times of the Gentiles." A concept unique to Luke, suggesting a period during which Gentiles have a prominent role in God's plan, after which Jerusalem's status will change. The phrase has generated extensive theological discussion.
- v.27h: An allusion to Daniel 7:13-14. The Son of Man comes with divine authority. Luke's account is briefer than Mark's or Matthew's.
- v.28i: "ἀπολύτρωσις" (apolytrōsis) — redemption, liberation, release from captivity. In the context of cosmic upheaval, this word offers hope: what terrifies the world signals salvation for the faithful.
- v.32j: "ἡ γενεὰ αὕτη" (hē genea hautē) — "this generation." The meaning is debated: it may refer to the generation alive at Jesus's time (fulfilled in AD 70), the generation alive when the end-time events begin, or "generation" in the sense of "type of people" (the faithful or the unfaithful).
- v.37k: Luke mentions the Mount of Olives as Jesus's nightly retreat, setting the stage for the arrest in Gethsemane. This summary bridges the teaching ministry and the passion narrative.
