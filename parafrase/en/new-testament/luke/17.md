# Luke 17

**1** Jesus said to his disciples, "It's inevitable that things will come along that cause people to stumble,ᵃ but how terrible for the person through whom they come! **2** It would be better for them to have a millstone hung around their neck and be thrown into the sea than to cause one of these little ones to stumble. **3** So watch yourselves!

"If your brother or sister sins, rebuke them. And if they repent, forgive them. **4** Even if they sin against you seven times in a single day and come back to you seven times saying, 'I repent,' forgive them."

**5** The apostles said to the Lord, "Increase our faith!"

**6** The Lord replied, "If you had faith the size of a mustard seed, you could say to this mulberry tree,ᵇ 'Be uprooted and planted in the sea,' and it would obey you.

**7** "Suppose one of you has a servant who comes in from plowing or tending sheep. Would you say, 'Come right in and sit down to eat'? **8** Of course not! You'd say, 'Prepare my dinner, get yourself ready, and serve me while I eat and drink. After that, you can eat and drink.' **9** Would you thank the servant for doing what was told? **10** It's the same with you. When you've done everything you were told to do, say, 'We're unworthy servants. We've only done what we were supposed to do.'"ᶜ

**11** On his way to Jerusalem, Jesus was traveling along the border between Samaria and Galilee. **12** As he entered a certain village, ten men with leprosyᵈ met him. They stood at a distance **13** and called out in loud voices, "Jesus, Master, have mercy on us!"

**14** When he saw them, he said, "Go show yourselves to the priests." And as they were on their way, they were cleansed.

**15** One of them, when he saw that he was healed, came back, praising God in a loud voice. **16** He threw himself facedown at Jesus's feet, thanking him. And he was a Samaritan.ᵉ

**17** Jesus responded, "Weren't all ten cleansed? Where are the other nine? **18** Was no one found to come back and give praise to God except this foreigner?"ᶠ **19** Then he said to him, "Get up and go. Your faith has made you well."ᵍ

**20** When the Pharisees asked him when God's kingdom would come, he answered, "God's kingdom isn't coming in ways you can observe.ʰ **21** People won't say, 'Look, here it is!' or 'There it is!' Because God's kingdom is in your midst."ⁱ

**22** Then he said to the disciples, "The days are coming when you'll long to see just one of the days of the Son of Man, but you won't see it. **23** People will tell you, 'Look, there!' or 'Look, here!' Don't go chasing after them. **24** Because the Son of Man on his day will be like lightning that flashes and lights up the sky from one end to the other. **25** But first he must suffer many things and be rejected by this generation.

**26** "Just as it was in Noah's days, so it will be in the days of the Son of Man: **27** People were eating, drinking, marrying, being given in marriage — right up until the day Noah entered the ark. Then the flood came and destroyed them all.

**28** "It will be the same as it was in Lot's days: People were eating, drinking, buying, selling, planting, building. **29** But the day Lot left Sodom, fire and sulfur rained down from heaven and destroyed them all. **30** That's how it will be on the day the Son of Man is revealed.

**31** "On that day, anyone on the rooftop shouldn't go down to get their belongings from the house. Anyone in the field shouldn't turn back. **32** Remember Lot's wife!ʲ **33** Whoever tries to preserve their life will lose it, but whoever loses their life will keep it alive.

**34** "I'm telling you, on that night two people will be in one bed — one will be taken and the other left. **35** Two women will be grinding grain together — one will be taken and the other left."ᵏ

**37** "Where, Lord?" they asked.

He replied, "Where the body is, the vultures will gather."ˡ

## Notes

- v.1a: "σκάνδαλα" (skandala) — stumbling blocks or snares. The word originally meant the trigger of a trap. Jesus acknowledges their inevitability while pronouncing judgment on those who set them.
- v.6b: "συκαμίνῳ" (sykaminō) — a mulberry tree (or sycamine), known for its deep root system, making it especially hard to uproot. Luke's choice differs from Matthew's mountain-moving imagery.
- v.10c: "δοῦλοι ἀχρεῖοί ἐσμεν" (douloi achreioi esmen) — "we are unprofitable/unworthy servants." Not a statement of worthlessness but of proper perspective: obedience is baseline duty, not grounds for self-congratulation. This passage is unique to Luke.
- v.12d: Leprosy sufferers were required to keep their distance from others (Leviticus 13:45-46). The fact that they called from afar shows they knew the rules.
- v.16e: Luke delays the revelation that the grateful man is a Samaritan for maximum dramatic impact — a characteristic literary technique. That a despised Samaritan shows more gratitude than nine presumably Jewish men reinforces Luke's theme.
- v.18f: "ἀλλογενής" (allogenēs) — "foreigner, one of another race." This exact word appeared on warning inscriptions in the Jerusalem temple, forbidding Gentiles from entering the inner courts. Jesus uses it with pointed irony.
- v.19g: "ἡ πίστις σου σέσωκέν σε" (hē pistis sou sesōken se) — "your faith has saved/healed you." The word sōzō means both physical healing and spiritual salvation. All ten were cleansed (katharizō), but only the one who returned in faith was "saved" (sōzō) — a deeper restoration.
- v.20h: "μετὰ παρατηρήσεως" (meta paratērēseōs) — "with observation/watching." The kingdom doesn't arrive with the kind of observable, calculable signs the Pharisees wanted.
- v.21i: "ἐντὸς ὑμῶν" (entos hymōn) — this can mean "within you" (interior, spiritual) or "among you" (in your midst). Given that Jesus is speaking to Pharisees, "among you" (i.e., present in Jesus's person and ministry) is more likely.
- v.32j: Lot's wife looked back (Genesis 19:26). The reference is uniquely Lukan and serves as a two-word parable about divided loyalty at the moment of crisis.
- v.34-35k: Verse 36 ("Two men will be in the field; one will be taken and the other left") is absent from the earliest and best manuscripts of Luke, likely added later from Matthew 24:40.
- v.37l: "ὅπου τὸ σῶμα, ἐκεῖ καὶ οἱ ἀετοὶ ἐπισυναχθήσονται" — a proverbial saying. Vultures (or eagles) naturally gather where there is a corpse. The meaning is that the Son of Man's coming will be as unmistakable and inevitable as vultures circling carrion — no one will need to point it out.
