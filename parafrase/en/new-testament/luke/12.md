# Luke 12

**1** Meanwhile, a crowd of thousands had gathered, so many they were trampling each other. Jesus began speaking first to his disciples: "Watch out for the yeast of the Pharisees — which is hypocrisy.ᵃ **2** There's nothing covered up that won't be uncovered, and nothing hidden that won't become known. **3** Whatever you've said in the dark will be heard in the light, and what you've whispered in private rooms will be proclaimed from the rooftops.

**4** "I'm telling you, my friends — don't be afraid of those who kill the body and after that can do nothing more. **5** Let me tell you who to fear: fear the one who, after killing, has the authority to throw you into Gehenna.ᵇ Yes, I'm telling you — fear him.

**6** "Aren't five sparrows sold for two small coins?ᶜ Yet not a single one of them is forgotten by God. **7** Even the hairs on your head are all counted. Don't be afraid — you're worth more than a whole flock of sparrows.

**8** "I'm telling you, whoever acknowledges me before people, the Son of Man will acknowledge before God's angels. **9** But whoever denies me before people will be denied before God's angels. **10** Everyone who speaks a word against the Son of Man will be forgiven. But whoever blasphemes against the Holy Spirit won't be forgiven.ᵈ

**11** "When they bring you before synagogues, rulers, and authorities, don't worry about how to defend yourselves or what to say. **12** The Holy Spirit will teach you at that very moment what you need to say."

**13** Someone in the crowd said to him, "Teacher, tell my brother to divide the inheritance with me."

**14** Jesus said to him, "Friend, who appointed me a judge or arbitrator over you?" **15** Then he said to them all, "Watch out! Guard yourselves against every form of greed, because a person's life doesn't consist in the abundance of their possessions."

**16** And he told them a parable: "The land of a certain rich man produced an enormous crop. **17** He thought to himself, 'What am I going to do? I don't have room to store all my harvest.' **18** Then he said, 'Here's what I'll do: I'll tear down my barns and build bigger ones, and I'll store all my grain and goods there. **19** Then I'll say to myself, "You've got plenty stored up for years to come. Relax! Eat, drink, and enjoy yourself."'

**20** "But God said to him, 'You fool! This very night your life is being demanded of you.ᵉ And everything you've accumulated — who's going to get it?'

**21** "That's how it is with anyone who stores up wealth for themselves but isn't rich toward God."

**22** Then he said to his disciples, "That's why I'm telling you: don't worry about your life — what you'll eat — or about your body — what you'll wear. **23** Life is more than food, and the body is more than clothing.

**24** "Think about the ravens: they don't sow or reap, they have no storeroom or barn, yet God feeds them. How much more valuable are you than birds! **25** Which of you by worrying can add a single hourᶠ to your life? **26** If you can't even do something that small, why worry about the rest?

**27** "Think about how the wildflowers grow: they don't labor or spin thread. But I'm telling you, not even Solomon in all his splendor was dressed like one of these. **28** If that's how God clothes the grass in the field — here today and thrown into the oven tomorrow — how much more will he clothe you? You have such little faith!

**29** "Don't keep striving for what you'll eat and what you'll drink. Don't be anxious.ᵍ **30** The nations of the world chase after all these things, and your Father knows you need them. **31** Instead, seek his kingdom, and these things will be given to you as well.

**32** "Don't be afraid, little flock, because your Father has been pleased to give you the kingdom.ʰ **33** Sell your possessions and give to those in need. Make yourselves wallets that don't wear out — an inexhaustible treasure in heaven, where no thief comes near and no moth destroys. **34** Because where your treasure is, that's where your heart will be too.

**35** "Stay dressed and ready, and keep your lamps burning, **36** like people waiting for their master to return from a wedding banquet, so that when he comes and knocks, they can open the door for him immediately. **37** How blessed are those servants whom the master finds alert when he arrives! I'm telling you the truth — he'll dress himself to serve, have them recline at the table, and come and wait on them.ⁱ **38** Whether he comes in the second watch or the third watch of the night and finds them ready — how blessed are those servants!

**39** "But understand this: if the homeowner had known what hour the thief was coming, he wouldn't have let his house be broken into. **40** You also must be ready, because the Son of Man is coming at an hour you don't expect."

**41** Peter asked, "Lord, are you telling this parable to us, or to everyone?"

**42** The Lord replied, "Who then is the faithful and wise manager whom the master puts in charge of his household servants, to give them their food allowance at the right time? **43** How blessed is that servant whom the master finds doing exactly that when he arrives! **44** Truly I tell you, the master will put that servant in charge of all his possessions. **45** But if that servant says to himself, 'My master is taking a long time,' and starts beating the other servants, both men and women, and eating and drinking and getting drunk — **46** that servant's master will come on a day he doesn't expect, at an hour he doesn't know, and will cut him off and assign him a place with the unfaithful.

**47** "The servant who knew what the master wanted but didn't get ready or act on it will receive a severe beating. **48** But the one who didn't know and did things deserving punishment will receive a lighter beating. From everyone who has been given much, much will be demanded. And from the one entrusted with much, even more will be asked.ʲ

**49** "I came to cast fire on the earth — and how I wish it were already ablaze! **50** But I have a baptism to undergo, and how distressed I am until it's completed!ᵏ

**51** "Do you think I came to bring peace on earth? No, I'm telling you — division! **52** From now on, five in one household will be divided — three against two and two against three. **53** Father against son and son against father, mother against daughter and daughter against mother, mother-in-law against daughter-in-law and daughter-in-law against mother-in-law."

**54** He also said to the crowds, "When you see a cloud rising in the west, you immediately say, 'A rainstorm is coming,' and it does. **55** And when a south wind blows, you say, 'It's going to be scorching hot,' and it is. **56** You hypocrites! You know how to read the appearance of the earth and sky — so why don't you know how to interpret the present time?

**57** "And why don't you judge for yourselves what's right? **58** As you're going with your adversary to the magistrate, make every effort to settle things on the way. Otherwise, they might drag you before the judge, and the judge hand you over to the officer, and the officer throw you into prison. **59** I'm telling you, you won't get out until you've paid the very last penny."ˡ

## Notes

- v.1a: "ζύμη" (zymē) — yeast/leaven. Jesus explicitly defines the Pharisees' leaven as hypocrisy — the disconnect between outward appearance and inner reality. Luke makes this identification more explicitly than the other Gospels.
- v.5b: "γέεννα" (geenna) — Gehenna, from the Hebrew "Valley of Hinnom" (Ge-Hinnom) south of Jerusalem, where child sacrifices had occurred (2 Kings 23:10). It became a symbol of final judgment.
- v.6c: "ἀσσαρίων δύο" (assariōn duo) — two assaria, very small coins. Matthew says two sparrows for one assarion; Luke says five for two, implying the fifth is thrown in free — making God's care for the "worthless" even more striking.
- v.10d: Blasphemy against the Holy Spirit is distinguished from speaking against the Son of Man. The distinction may relate to persistently rejecting the Spirit's testimony about Jesus, as opposed to honest confusion about his identity.
- v.20e: "τὴν ψυχήν σου ἀπαιτοῦσιν ἀπὸ σοῦ" — "they are demanding your soul/life from you." The plural "they" is impersonal, possibly meaning divine agents or simply "your life is being required."
- v.25f: "πῆχυν" (pēchyn) — a cubit, about 18 inches. It can refer to lifespan ("add a single hour") or height ("add a single cubit"). Given the context of worry about life, "hour" captures the temporal sense.
- v.29g: "μετεωρίζεσθε" (meteōrizesthe) — a rare word meaning to be suspended in anxiety, like a ship tossed on waves. Rendered as "don't be anxious" to capture the unsettled, worried state.
- v.32h: "μικρὸν ποίμνιον" (mikron poimnion) — "little flock." A tender, uniquely Lukan address. The contrast between the smallness of the flock and the vastness of the gift (the kingdom) is deliberate.
- v.37i: A master serving his servants was shocking in ancient culture. This reversal of roles anticipates Jesus washing his disciples' feet (John 13) and serving at the Last Supper (Luke 22:27).
- v.48j: A principle of proportional accountability. Greater knowledge and privilege bring greater responsibility.
- v.50k: "βάπτισμα" (baptisma) — Jesus uses baptism as a metaphor for his coming suffering and death. "Συνέχομαι" (synechomai) — "I am constrained/distressed" — reveals the intense pressure he felt.
- v.59l: "λεπτόν" (lepton) — the smallest coin in circulation, worth about 1/128 of a denarius. The parable urges urgency in reconciliation before judgment becomes unavoidable.
