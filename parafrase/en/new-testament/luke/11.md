# Luke 11

**1** One day, Jesus was praying in a certain place. When he finished, one of his disciples said to him, "Lord, teach us to pray, just like John taught his disciples."ᵃ

**2** He said to them, "When you pray, say:

'Father,ᵇ
may your name be held holy.
May your kingdom come.
**3** Give us each day our daily bread.ᶜ
**4** And forgive us our sins,
because we ourselves forgive everyone who wrongs us.
And don't lead us into testing.'"ᵈ

**5** Then he said to them, "Suppose one of you has a friend, and you go to him at midnight and say, 'Friend, lend me three loaves of bread, **6** because a friend of mine has just arrived from a journey, and I don't have anything to offer him.' **7** And suppose the one inside answers, 'Don't bother me. The door is already locked, and my children and I are in bed. I can't get up and give you anything.'

**8** "I'm telling you, even if he won't get up and give it to you because of friendship, he will get up and give you everything you need because of your bold persistence.ᵉ

**9** "So I say to you: Ask, and it will be given to you. Seek, and you'll find. Knock, and the door will be opened for you. **10** Because everyone who asks receives, the one who seeks finds, and to the one who knocks, the door is opened.

**11** "Which father among you, if your son asks for a fish, would give him a snake instead? **12** Or if he asks for an egg, would give him a scorpion? **13** If you, then, being imperfect as you are, know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more will the Father in heaven give the Holy Spirit to those who ask him!"ᶠ

**14** Jesus was driving out a demon that had made a man unable to speak. When the demon left, the man spoke, and the crowd was amazed. **15** But some of them said, "He drives out demons by Beelzebul,ᵍ the ruler of the demons." **16** Others, testing him, demanded a sign from heaven.

**17** Knowing their thoughts, he said to them, "Every kingdom divided against itself is headed for ruin, and a divided household collapses. **18** If Satan is divided against himself, how can his kingdom stand? I say this because you claim I drive out demons by Beelzebul. **19** Now if I drive out demons by Beelzebul, by whom do your own people drive them out? So they'll be your judges. **20** But if I drive out demons by the finger of God,ʰ then God's kingdom has arrived right here among you.

**21** "When a strong man, fully armed, guards his own estate, his possessions are safe. **22** But when someone stronger attacks and overpowers him, the stronger one takes away the armor he trusted in and distributes his plunder.

**23** "Whoever isn't with me is against me, and whoever doesn't gather with me scatters.

**24** "When an unclean spirit leaves a person, it wanders through waterless places looking for rest. Finding none, it says, 'I'll go back to the house I left.' **25** When it arrives, it finds the place swept clean and put in order. **26** Then it goes and brings seven other spirits more wicked than itself, and they move in and settle there. And that person ends up worse off than before."

**27** While he was saying these things, a woman in the crowd raised her voice and said, "How blessed is the womb that carried you and the breasts that nursed you!"

**28** But he replied, "Even more blessed are those who hear God's word and obey it."ⁱ

**29** As the crowds kept growing, he began to say, "This generation is a wicked generation. It demands a sign, but no sign will be given to it except the sign of Jonah. **30** For just as Jonah became a sign to the people of Nineveh, so the Son of Man will be a sign to this generation. **31** The queen of the South will rise up at the judgment alongside the people of this generation and condemn them, because she came from the ends of the earth to hear Solomon's wisdom — and look, something greater than Solomon is here! **32** The people of Nineveh will stand up at the judgment alongside this generation and condemn it, because they repented at Jonah's preaching — and look, something greater than Jonah is here!

**33** "Nobody lights a lamp and hides it or puts it under a basket. Instead, they set it on a stand, so that those who come in can see the light. **34** Your eye is the lamp of your body.ʲ When your eye is healthy, your whole body is full of light. But when your eye is bad, your body is full of darkness. **35** Be careful, then, that the light within you isn't actually darkness. **36** So if your whole body is full of light, with no part of it in darkness, it will be entirely radiant — like when a lamp shines its light on you."

**37** After he'd finished speaking, a Pharisee invited him to eat at his home. Jesus went in and reclined at the table. **38** The Pharisee was surprised to notice that Jesus didn't wash before the meal.ᵏ

**39** The Lord said to him, "You Pharisees — you clean the outside of the cup and the dish, but inside you're full of greed and wickedness. **40** You fools! Didn't the one who made the outside also make the inside? **41** But give what's inside as charity, and then everything will be clean for you.ˡ

**42** "But how terrible for you Pharisees! You tithe your mint and rue and every garden herb, but you neglect justice and the love of God. You should practice the latter without leaving the former undone.

**43** "How terrible for you Pharisees! You love the best seats in the synagogues and greetings of honor in the marketplaces.

**44** "How terrible for you! You're like unmarked graves — people walk over them without even knowing it."ᵐ

**45** One of the legal experts said to him, "Teacher, when you say these things, you're insulting us too."

**46** He replied, "How terrible for you legal experts as well! You load people down with burdens they can hardly carry, and you yourselves won't lift a finger to help.

**47** "How terrible for you! You build tombs for the prophets, yet it was your ancestors who killed them. **48** So you testify that you approve of what your ancestors did — they killed the prophets, and you build their tombs. **49** That's why the Wisdom of God said, 'I will send them prophets and apostles, and they'll kill some and persecute others,' **50** so that this generation will be held accountable for the blood of all the prophets shed since the foundation of the world — **51** from the blood of Abel to the blood of Zechariah, who was killed between the altar and the sanctuary.ⁿ Yes, I'm telling you, this generation will be held accountable for it all.

**52** "How terrible for you legal experts! You've taken away the key of knowledge. You didn't go in yourselves, and you blocked those who were trying to enter."

**53** When he left there, the scribes and the Pharisees began to oppose him fiercely, pressing him to speak on many topics, **54** lying in wait to catch him in something he might say.

## Notes

- v.1a: Only Luke records the disciples' request for Jesus to teach them to pray, and connects it to observing Jesus at prayer — a distinctively Lukan touch.
- v.2b: Luke's version simply says "Father" (Πάτερ, Pater), while Matthew has "Our Father in heaven." Luke's shorter, more intimate form may be closer to the original Aramaic "Abba."
- v.3c: "ἐπιούσιον" (epiousios) — this word is found almost nowhere else in Greek literature. It may mean "for the coming day," "necessary for existence," or "daily." Its uniqueness makes precise translation challenging.
- v.4d: Luke's version of the Lord's Prayer is notably shorter than Matthew's, lacking "your will be done," "deliver us from evil," and the doxology. Many scholars consider Luke's shorter form closer to the original.
- v.8e: "ἀναίδειαν" (anaideian) — traditionally "importunity" or "shamelessness." It could refer to the petitioner's bold persistence or the sleeper's desire to avoid the shame of not helping. Rendered as "bold persistence" to capture the positive force.
- v.13f: Where Matthew has "good things," Luke specifies "the Holy Spirit" — Luke's theological emphasis on the Spirit as the supreme gift.
- v.15g: "Βεελζεβούλ" (Beelzeboul) — likely derived from "Baal-zebub" (lord of the flies) or "Baal-zebul" (lord of the dwelling/prince). Used as a name for the chief of demons.
- v.20h: "δακτύλῳ θεοῦ" (daktylō theou) — "the finger of God." Luke uses this phrase (echoing Exodus 8:19) where Matthew has "Spirit of God." Both point to divine power, but Luke's phrasing evokes the Exodus narrative.
- v.28i: Jesus doesn't deny the woman's blessing but redirects it from biological relationship to obedience — consistent with Luke 8:21.
- v.34j: "ὀφθαλμός" (ophthalmos) — the eye as lamp is a metaphor for spiritual perception. A "healthy" (ἁπλοῦς, haplous, literally "single/simple") eye sees clearly; a "bad" (πονηρός, ponēros) eye distorts everything.
- v.38k: This was ceremonial hand-washing (ritual purification), not hygiene. Jesus's omission was a deliberate provocation.
- v.41l: "τὰ ἐνόντα δότε ἐλεημοσύνην" — a difficult phrase. It may mean "give as charity what is within" (the contents of the dish), or "give from the heart." The point is that inner generosity, not external ritual, produces true cleanness.
- v.44m: Contact with a grave made a person ritually unclean (Numbers 19:16). Unmarked graves spread defilement unknowingly — a devastating metaphor for hidden spiritual corruption.
- v.51n: Abel is the first murder victim in Genesis; Zechariah son of Jehoiada was killed in the temple court (2 Chronicles 24:20-22), the last murder recorded in the Hebrew Bible's canonical order. Together they span the entire Old Testament.
