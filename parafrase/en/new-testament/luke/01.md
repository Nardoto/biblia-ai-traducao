Here is the paraphrase of Luke 1:

# Luke 1

**1** A lot of people have already tried to put together an account of everything that's happened among us — **2** based on what was handed down by the people who were there from the start and saw it all with their own eyes, the ones who devoted their lives to spreading this message. **3** So I figured, since I've carefully looked into all of this from the very beginning, I'd write it out for you in an orderly way, Theophilus — **4** so you can be confident that everything you've been taught is solid and reliable.

**5** Back when Herod was king of Judea, there was a priest named Zechariah who belonged to the priestly division of Abijah. His wife Elizabeth was also from a priestly family — a descendant of Aaron. **6** They were both genuinely good people in God's eyes, living blamelessly and following all the Lord's commands and regulations. **7** But they had no children. Elizabeth couldn't conceive, and by now they were both getting up there in years.

**8** One day, when Zechariah's division was on duty and he was serving as priest before God, **9** he was chosen by lot — as was the custom — to go into the Lord's sanctuary and burn incense. **10** The whole crowd of worshipers was outside praying during the time the incense was being offered.

**11** Then an angel of the Lord appeared to him, standing on the right side of the incense altar. **12** When Zechariah saw him, he was shaken to the core — fear just gripped him. **13** But the angel said, "Relax, Zechariah. Your prayer has been heard. Your wife Elizabeth is going to have a son, and you're to name him John. **14** You'll be overjoyed — thrilled beyond words — and a lot of other people will celebrate his birth too, **15** because he's going to be someone truly significant in the Lord's eyes. He won't touch wine or any strong drink, and he'll be filled with the Holy Spirit from before he's even born. **16** He'll turn many of the people of Israel back to the Lord their God. **17** He'll go ahead of the Lord with the same spirit and power that Elijah had — reconciling parents with their children, turning the rebellious back to the wisdom of those who do right — getting a people ready for the Lord's arrival."

**18** Zechariah said to the angel, "How can I be sure about this? I'm an old man, and my wife is well past her prime."

**19** The angel replied, "I'm Gabriel. I stand in the very presence of God, and I was sent to speak to you and bring you this good news. **20** But since you didn't believe my words — which will absolutely come true when the time is right — you're going to be unable to speak. Not a word, until the day all of this happens."

**21** Meanwhile, the people outside were waiting for Zechariah, getting confused about why he was taking so long in the sanctuary. **22** When he finally came out, he couldn't speak to them. They realized he must have seen a vision inside, because he kept trying to communicate with gestures but couldn't get any words out.

**23** When his time of service was finished, he went back home. **24** Soon after, his wife Elizabeth became pregnant, and she stayed in seclusion for five months. **25** "The Lord has done this for me," she said. "He looked my way and took away the disgrace I've carried among people."

**26** In the sixth month of Elizabeth's pregnancy, God sent the angel Gabriel to a town in Galilee called Nazareth, **27** to a young woman who was engaged to a man named Joseph, from David's family line. The young woman's name was Mary. **28** The angel came to her and said, "Hello, favored one! The Lord is with you."

**29** Mary was deeply unsettled by these words and kept turning them over in her mind — what kind of greeting was this? **30** The angel told her, "Don't be afraid, Mary. God is showing you extraordinary favor. **31** You're going to become pregnant and have a son, and you're to name him Jesus. **32** He'll be great — he'll be called the Son of the Most High. The Lord God will give him the throne of his ancestor David, **33** and he'll reign over Jacob's descendants forever. His kingdom will never end."

**34** Mary said to the angel, "How is that possible? I've never been with a man."

**35** The angel answered, "The Holy Spirit will come over you, and the power of the Most High will overshadow you. That's why the child born to you will be holy — he'll be called the Son of God. **36** And here's something else: your relative Elizabeth — the one everyone said could never have children — she's six months pregnant, even at her age. **37** Because nothing is impossible for God."

**38** Mary said, "I'm the Lord's servant. Let it happen to me just as you've said." And the angel left.

**39** Not long after, Mary hurried off to a town in the hill country of Judah. **40** She went into Zechariah's house and greeted Elizabeth. **41** The moment Elizabeth heard Mary's greeting, the baby inside her leaped, and Elizabeth was filled with the Holy Spirit. **42** She burst out in a loud voice, "You are the most blessed of all women, and blessed is the child you're carrying! **43** How is it that the mother of my Lord comes to visit me? **44** The instant your greeting reached my ears, the baby in my womb jumped for joy! **45** And how happy is the woman who believed that what the Lord told her would actually come true!"

**46** Then Mary said,

"My whole being celebrates the Lord,
**47** and my spirit is bursting with joy because of God my Savior —
**48** because he noticed me, his humble servant.
And from now on, every generation will call me blessed,
**49** because the Mighty One has done incredible things for me.
His name is holy.
**50** His mercy stretches from one generation to the next
for those who hold him in reverence.
**51** He's flexed his mighty arm
and scattered those who are arrogant to the bone.
**52** He's pulled the powerful down from their thrones
and lifted up the humble.
**53** He's filled the hungry with good things
and sent the rich away with nothing.
**54** He's come through for his servant Israel,
remembering his mercy —
**55** just as he promised our ancestors,
to Abraham and his descendants, forever."

**56** Mary stayed with Elizabeth for about three months, then went back home.

**57** When the time came for Elizabeth to have her baby, she gave birth to a son. **58** Her neighbors and relatives heard how incredibly generous the Lord had been to her, and they celebrated right alongside her.

**59** On the eighth day, they came to circumcise the child, and they were going to name him Zechariah, after his father. **60** But his mother spoke up: "No! His name is John."

**61** They said to her, "But nobody in your family has that name!" **62** So they made signs to his father, asking what he wanted the child to be called. **63** He asked for a writing tablet and wrote, "His name is John." Everyone was stunned. **64** And right then, his mouth opened, his tongue was freed, and he began to speak — praising God.

**65** A sense of awe fell on everyone in the neighborhood, and people throughout the hill country of Judea were talking about all of this. **66** Everyone who heard about it turned it over in their minds, wondering, "What is this child going to become?" Because it was clear that the Lord's hand was on him.

**67** Then his father Zechariah was filled with the Holy Spirit and spoke prophetically:

**68** "Praise the Lord, the God of Israel,
because he has come to his people and set them free!
**69** He has raised up a mighty deliverer for us
from the family of his servant David —
**70** just as he announced long ago
through his holy prophets —
**71** rescue from our enemies
and from the grip of everyone who hates us.
**72** He's showing the mercy he promised our ancestors,
honoring his sacred covenant —
**73** the oath he swore to our father Abraham:
**74** that we'd be rescued from the hands of our enemies
and set free to serve him without fear,
**75** living holy and right before him
every day of our lives.

**76** And you, my child — you'll be called
a prophet of the Most High,
because you'll go ahead of the Lord
to get things ready for him,
**77** giving his people the understanding of salvation
through the forgiveness of their sins —
**78** all because of the tender compassion of our God.
The dawn from heaven is about to break upon us,
**79** shining on those sitting in darkness
and in the shadow of death,
guiding our feet onto the path of peace."

**80** The child grew up and became strong in spirit. He lived out in the wilderness until the day he stepped into the public eye before Israel.

## Notes

- v.1: "πράγματα" (pragmata) — rendered as "everything that's happened" to capture the sense of accomplished events/deeds, not just stories.
- v.17: "ἐν πνεύματι καὶ δυνάμει Ἠλίου" — "with the same spirit and power that Elijah had" captures the intended parallel without implying reincarnation.
- v.28: "κεχαριτωμένη" (kecharitōmenē) — rendered as "favored one" rather than "full of grace" to stay theologically neutral; the perfect passive participle indicates grace received, not an inherent quality.
- v.34: Mary's question "πῶς ἔσται τοῦτο" is one of genuine puzzlement, not doubt (contrast with Zechariah's request for a sign in v.18), hence "How is that possible?" vs. "How can I be sure?"
- v.35: "ἐπισκιάσει" (episkiasei) — "overshadow" echoes the divine presence overshadowing the tabernacle in Exodus 40:35; kept the word as it carries the right weight in English.
- v.46-55: Mary's song (the Magnificat) is rendered in a poetic but still conversational register. The Greek aorist verbs are prophetic — speaking of future actions as already accomplished — preserved by using present perfect ("He's pulled down... He's lifted up...").
- v.78: "ἀνατολὴ ἐξ ὕψους" — literally "rising/dawn from on high." Rendered as "dawn from heaven" to capture the messianic metaphor of light breaking into darkness.
