# Luke 9

**1** Jesus called the Twelve together and gave them power and authority over all demons and to heal diseases. **2** He sent them out to proclaim God's kingdom and to heal the sick. **3** He told them, "Don't take anything for the journey — no staff, no bag, no bread, no money, no extra shirt.ᵃ **4** Whatever house you enter, stay there until you leave that town. **5** If people don't welcome you, shake the dust off your feet as you leave that town — as a testimony against them." **6** So they set out, going from village to village, proclaiming the good news and healing people everywhere.

**7** Now Herod the tetrarch heard about everything that was happening, and he was baffled, because some were saying that John had been raised from the dead, **8** others that Elijah had appeared, and still others that one of the ancient prophets had come back to life. **9** Herod said, "I beheaded John. So who is this I keep hearing about?" And he kept trying to see him.ᵇ

**10** When the apostles returned, they reported to Jesus everything they had done. He took them along and withdrew privately to a town called Bethsaida. **11** But the crowds found out and followed him. He welcomed them, spoke to them about God's kingdom, and healed those who needed healing.

**12** As the day began to wind down, the Twelve came to him and said, "Send the crowd away so they can go to the surrounding villages and countryside to find food and lodging, because we're in a remote place here."

**13** But he told them, "You give them something to eat."

They said, "All we have is five loaves of bread and two fish — unless you want us to go buy food for all these people." **14** There were about five thousand men.

He said to his disciples, "Have them sit down in groups of about fifty." **15** They did, and everyone sat down. **16** Taking the five loaves and the two fish, he looked up to heaven, blessed them, broke them, and kept handing them to the disciples to distribute to the crowd. **17** Everyone ate and was satisfied, and twelve baskets of leftover fragments were collected.ᶜ

**18** Once, while Jesus was praying alone with the disciples nearby,ᵈ he asked them, "Who do the crowds say I am?"

**19** They answered, "Some say John the Baptist; others say Elijah; still others say one of the ancient prophets has come back."

**20** "But what about you?" he said. "Who do you say I am?"

Peter answered, "God's Messiah."ᵉ

**21** Jesus strictly warned them not to tell this to anyone. **22** He said, "The Son of Man must suffer many things, be rejected by the elders, chief priests, and scribes, be killed, and on the third day be raised."ᶠ

**23** Then he said to them all, "If anyone wants to follow me, they must deny themselves, take up their cross daily,ᵍ and follow me. **24** Whoever wants to save their life will lose it, but whoever loses their life for my sake will save it. **25** What good is it for someone to gain the whole world but lose or forfeit themselves? **26** Whoever is ashamed of me and my words — the Son of Man will be ashamed of them when he comes in his glory and in the glory of the Father and of the holy angels. **27** But I'm telling you truthfully, some standing here right now won't taste death before they see God's kingdom."

**28** About eight days after he said these things,ʰ he took Peter, John, and James and went up the mountain to pray. **29** While he was praying, the appearance of his face changed, and his clothes became dazzling white — like a flash of lightning. **30** Suddenly, two men were talking with him — Moses and Elijah, **31** who appeared in gloryⁱ and were discussing his departure,ʲ which he was about to bring to fulfillment in Jerusalem.

**32** Peter and his companions had been heavy with sleep, but when they became fully awake, they saw his glory and the two men standing with him. **33** As the two were leaving Jesus, Peter said to him, "Master, it's good that we're here. Let's put up three shelters — one for you, one for Moses, and one for Elijah." He didn't really know what he was saying.

**34** While he was still speaking, a cloud came and enveloped them, and they were afraid as they entered the cloud. **35** A voice came from the cloud: "This is my Son, the one I've chosen. Listen to him!"ᵏ **36** When the voice had spoken, Jesus was found alone. The disciples kept this to themselves and told no one at that time what they'd seen.

**37** The next day, when they came down from the mountain, a large crowd met him. **38** A man in the crowd called out, "Teacher, I beg you — look at my son! He's my only child! **39** A spirit seizes him and he suddenly screams. It throws him into convulsions with foaming, and it barely ever lets him go — it's destroying him. **40** I begged your disciples to drive it out, but they couldn't."

**41** Jesus replied, "What an unbelieving and twisted generation! How long will I be with you and put up with you? Bring your son here."

**42** Even as the boy was approaching, the demon threw him down and convulsed him. But Jesus rebuked the unclean spirit, healed the boy, and gave him back to his father. **43** Everyone was overwhelmed by the greatness of God.

While they were all marveling at everything Jesus was doing, he said to his disciples, **44** "Let these words sink into your ears: The Son of Man is about to be handed over to human hands." **45** But they didn't understand what he meant. It was hidden from them so they couldn't grasp it, and they were afraid to ask him about it.

**46** An argument broke out among them about which of them was the greatest. **47** Jesus, knowing the reasoning of their hearts, took a little child, had the child stand beside him, **48** and said to them, "Whoever welcomes this little child in my name welcomes me. And whoever welcomes me welcomes the one who sent me. The one who is least among all of you — that person is the greatest."

**49** John said, "Master, we saw someone driving out demons in your name, and we tried to stop him because he's not one of us."

**50** Jesus said, "Don't stop him. Whoever isn't against you is for you."

**51** As the time drew near for him to be taken up,ˡ he resolutely set his face toward Jerusalem.ᵐ

**52** He sent messengers ahead of him. They went into a Samaritan village to get things ready for him, **53** but the people there wouldn't welcome him because he was heading toward Jerusalem. **54** When the disciples James and John saw this, they said, "Lord, do you want us to call fire down from heaven to destroy them?"ⁿ

**55** But he turned and rebuked them. **56** And they went on to another village.

**57** As they were walking along the road, someone said to him, "I'll follow you wherever you go."

**58** Jesus said, "Foxes have dens and birds have nests, but the Son of Man has no place to lay his head."

**59** He said to another person, "Follow me." But they replied, "Lord, first let me go and bury my father."

**60** Jesus told him, "Let the dead bury their own dead. But you — go and proclaim God's kingdom."

**61** Still another said, "I'll follow you, Lord — but first let me go say goodbye to my family."ᵒ

**62** Jesus said, "No one who puts their hand on the plow and keeps looking back is fit for God's kingdom."

## Notes

- v.3a: Luke's list of prohibited items differs slightly from Mark's. Luke forbids even a staff, heightening the sense of total dependence on God's provision.
- v.9b: "ἐζήτει ἰδεῖν αὐτόν" (ezētei idein auton) — Herod "kept seeking to see him." This curiosity foreshadows Herod's eventual meeting with Jesus at his trial (Luke 23:8).
- v.17c: Twelve baskets — one for each of the Twelve apostles. The "κόφινοι" (kophinoi) were distinctively Jewish baskets, symbolizing the abundance of God's provision for Israel.
- v.18d: Luke uniquely notes that Jesus was "praying alone" before this pivotal conversation — consistent with his emphasis on Jesus's prayer life at crucial moments.
- v.20e: "τὸν Χριστὸν τοῦ θεοῦ" (ton Christon tou theou) — "the Messiah/Christ of God." Luke's formulation emphasizes the divine appointment of the Messiah.
- v.22f: "δεῖ" (dei) — "must." This word conveys divine necessity — the suffering isn't accidental but is part of God's plan.
- v.23g: "καθ' ἡμέραν" (kath' hēmeran) — "daily." This is Luke's addition. Cross-bearing isn't a one-time event but a daily posture.
- v.28h: Luke says "about eight days" while Matthew and Mark say "after six days" — a minor variation showing Luke's looser chronological approach, inclusive of the day of speaking and the day of transfiguration.
- v.31i: "ὀφθέντες ἐν δόξῃ" (ophthentes en doxē) — "appeared in glory." Only Luke mentions that Moses and Elijah appeared in glory.
- v.31j: "ἔξοδον" (exodon) — literally "exodus/departure." A profound word choice evoking Israel's exodus from Egypt. Jesus's death, resurrection, and ascension are his own "exodus" — liberation through departure. Unique to Luke.
- v.35k: "ὁ ἐκλελεγμένος" (ho eklelegmenos) — "the chosen one." Luke uses "chosen" rather than "beloved" (as in Matthew/Mark), echoing Isaiah's Servant language (Isaiah 42:1).
- v.51l: "ἀνάλημψις" (analēmpsis) — "taking up," referring to Jesus's ascension. This word encompasses the entire sequence of events culminating in his exaltation. It appears only here in the NT.
- v.51m: "τὸ πρόσωπον ἐστήρισεν" (to prosōpon estērisen) — literally "he set his face firmly." This marks the beginning of Luke's extensive Travel Narrative (9:51-19:27), Jesus's journey toward Jerusalem and the cross.
- v.54n: An allusion to 2 Kings 1:10-12, where Elijah called down fire. James and John miss the point of Jesus's mission entirely.
- v.61-62o: This exchange is unique to Luke. The allusion to Elijah's call of Elisha (1 Kings 19:19-21) is inverted — Elisha was allowed to say goodbye, but Jesus demands even more radical commitment.
