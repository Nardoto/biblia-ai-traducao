# Luke 3

**1** In the fifteenth year of Emperor Tiberius's reign — when Pontius Pilate was governor of Judea, Herod was tetrarch of Galilee, his brother Philip was tetrarch of Iturea and Trachonitis, and Lysanias was tetrarch of Abilene — **2** during the high priesthood of Annas and Caiaphas — the word of God came to John, Zechariah's son, out in the wilderness.ᵃ

**3** He went throughout the whole region around the Jordan, proclaiming a baptism of repentanceᵇ for the forgiveness of sins, **4** just as it's written in the book of the prophet Isaiah:

"A voice crying out in the wilderness:
'Get the Lord's road ready!
Straighten out his paths!
**5** Every valley will be filled in,
every mountain and hill brought low.
The crooked roads will be made straight,
the rough ways made smooth.
**6** And every living person will see God's salvation.'"ᶜ

**7** So John said to the crowds coming out to be baptized by him, "You brood of vipers! Who tipped you off to run from the coming judgment? **8** Produce fruit that shows you've actually changed.ᵈ And don't start telling yourselves, 'We have Abraham as our father.' I'm telling you, God can raise up children for Abraham from these very stones. **9** The axe is already at the root of the trees. Every tree that doesn't produce good fruit is going to be cut down and thrown into the fire."

**10** The crowds asked him, "So what should we do?"

**11** He answered, "If you have two shirts, share with someone who has none. If you have food, do the same."

**12** Even tax collectors came to be baptized. "Teacher," they asked, "what should we do?"

**13** He told them, "Don't collect anything more than what you're authorized to."

**14** Soldiers also asked him, "And what about us — what should we do?" He said, "Don't extort money through intimidation or false accusations. Be content with your pay."

**15** The people were full of anticipation, and everyone was wondering in their hearts whether John might be the Messiah. **16** John answered them all: "I baptize you with water. But someone more powerful than me is coming — I'm not even worthy to untie the strap of his sandals. He will baptize you with the Holy Spirit and fire.ᵉ **17** He's got his winnowing forkᶠ in hand, ready to thoroughly clean out his threshing floor. He'll gather the wheat into his barn, but the chaff he'll burn with fire that never goes out."

**18** With many other urgent appeals, he kept announcing the good news to the people.

**19** But when John confronted Herod the tetrarch about Herodias — his brother's wife — and about all the other evil things Herod had done, **20** Herod added this to everything else: he locked John up in prison.

**21** Now when all the people were being baptized, Jesus was baptized too. And as he was praying, heaven opened up, **22** and the Holy Spirit came down on him in a physical form, like a dove. And a voice came from heaven: "You are my Son, the one I love. I'm completely delighted with you."ᵍ

**23** Jesus was about thirty years old when he began his public work. He was the son (so it was assumed) of Joseph,ʰ
son of Heli, **24** son of Matthat, son of Levi, son of Melchi,
son of Jannai, son of Joseph, **25** son of Mattathias, son of Amos,
son of Nahum, son of Esli, son of Naggai, **26** son of Maath,
son of Mattathias, son of Semein, son of Josech, son of Joda,
**27** son of Joanan, son of Rhesa, son of Zerubbabel, son of Shealtiel,
son of Neri, **28** son of Melchi, son of Addi, son of Cosam,
son of Elmadam, son of Er, **29** son of Joshua, son of Eliezer,
son of Jorim, son of Matthat, son of Levi, **30** son of Simeon,
son of Judah, son of Joseph, son of Jonam, son of Eliakim,
**31** son of Melea, son of Menna, son of Mattatha, son of Nathan,
son of David, **32** son of Jesse, son of Obed, son of Boaz,
son of Salmon, son of Nahshon, **33** son of Amminadab, son of Admin,
son of Arni, son of Hezron, son of Perez, son of Judah,
**34** son of Jacob, son of Isaac, son of Abraham, son of Terah,
son of Nahor, **35** son of Serug, son of Reu, son of Peleg,
son of Eber, son of Shelah, **36** son of Cainan, son of Arphaxad,
son of Shem, son of Noah, son of Lamech, **37** son of Methuselah,
son of Enoch, son of Jared, son of Mahalaleel, son of Cainan,
**38** son of Enosh, son of Seth, son of Adam, son of God.

## Notes

- v.1-2a: Luke's elaborate historical synchronism — naming the emperor, governor, tetrarchs, and high priests — is characteristic of Hellenistic historiography. It dates the beginning of John's ministry to approximately AD 28-29.
- v.3b: "βάπτισμα μετανοίας" (baptisma metanoias) — baptism of repentance. Metanoia means a fundamental change of mind and direction, not merely feeling sorry.
- v.6c: Luke extends the Isaiah 40 quotation further than Matthew or Mark, reaching "all flesh shall see God's salvation" — fitting his universal theme.
- v.8d: "καρποὺς ἀξίους τῆς μετανοίας" (karpous axious tēs metanoias) — fruit worthy of, or consistent with, genuine repentance. Not just feelings, but visible change.
- v.16e: "πνεύματι ἁγίῳ καὶ πυρί" (pneumati hagiō kai pyri) — the Holy Spirit and fire are presented as a single baptism with a dual aspect: purification and empowerment.
- v.17f: "πτύον" (ptyon) — a winnowing fork used to toss grain into the air so the wind separates the heavy wheat from the light chaff. A vivid image of judgment and separation.
- v.22g: "ἐν σοὶ εὐδόκησα" (en soi eudokēsa) — the aorist tense suggests "I have been delighted" or "I chose you with delight," indicating an eternal divine pleasure, not a new decision.
- v.23h: "ὢν υἱός, ὡς ἐνομίζετο, Ἰωσήφ" — "being (as was supposed) the son of Joseph." Luke's genealogy traces back to Adam and God, emphasizing Jesus's connection to all humanity — contrasting Matthew's genealogy which traces to Abraham and David.
