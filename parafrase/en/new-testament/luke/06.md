# Luke 6

**1** One Sabbath, Jesus was walking through some grainfields, and his disciples were picking heads of grain, rubbing them in their hands, and eating them.ᵃ **2** Some of the Pharisees said, "Why are you doing what's not permitted on the Sabbath?"

**3** Jesus answered them, "Haven't you read what David did when he and his companions were hungry? **4** He went into God's house, took the consecrated bread, ate it, and gave some to his men — bread that only the priests are allowed to eat."

**5** Then he told them, "The Son of Man is lord of the Sabbath."ᵇ

**6** On another Sabbath, he went into the synagogue and was teaching. A man was there whose right hand was withered.ᶜ **7** The scribes and Pharisees were watching Jesus closely to see if he'd heal on the Sabbath — looking for something to accuse him of. **8** But he knew their thoughts. He said to the man with the withered hand, "Get up and stand here in the middle." The man got up and stood there.

**9** Then Jesus said to them, "Let me ask you something: Is it lawful on the Sabbath to do good or to do evil? To save a life or to destroy it?"

**10** He looked around at all of them and said to the man, "Stretch out your hand." He did, and his hand was completely restored. **11** But they were filled with mindless rage and started discussing with each other what they might do to Jesus.ᵈ

**12** During those days, he went out to the mountainside to pray, and he spent the entire night in prayer to God.ᵉ **13** When morning came, he called his disciples together and chose twelve of them, whom he also named apostles:ᶠ **14** Simon, whom he named Peter; his brother Andrew; James; John; Philip; Bartholomew; **15** Matthew; Thomas; James son of Alphaeus; Simon called the Zealot; **16** Judas son of James; and Judas Iscariot, who became the traitor.

**17** He came down with them and stood on a level place.ᵍ A large crowd of his disciples was there, along with a huge number of people from all over Judea, Jerusalem, and the coastal region of Tyre and Sidon. **18** They'd come to hear him and to be healed of their diseases. Those troubled by unclean spirits were being cured, **19** and the whole crowd was trying to touch him, because power was coming from him and healing them all.

**20** He looked directly at his disciples and said:

"How blessed are you who are poor,
because God's kingdom belongs to you.
**21** How blessed are you who are hungry right now,
because you'll be filled to satisfaction.
How blessed are you who weep right now,
because you're going to laugh.

**22** "How blessed are you when people hate you, when they shut you out, insult you, and trash your name as evil — all because of the Son of Man. **23** Be glad on that day and leap for joy, because your reward in heaven is great! After all, that's exactly how their ancestors treated the prophets.

**24** "But how terrible for you who are rich,
because you've already received your comfort.
**25** How terrible for you who are well-fed right now,
because you're going to go hungry.
How terrible for you who are laughing now,
because you'll mourn and weep.

**26** "How terrible for you when everyone speaks well of you — that's exactly how their ancestors treated the false prophets.ʰ

**27** "But I'm telling you who are listening: Love your enemies. Do good to those who hate you. **28** Bless those who curse you. Pray for those who mistreat you. **29** If someone strikes you on one cheek, offer the other one too. If someone takes your coat, don't hold back your shirt either. **30** Give to everyone who asks you, and if someone takes what's yours, don't demand it back. **31** Treat others the way you want to be treated.ⁱ

**32** "If you love only those who love you, what credit is that to you? Even sinners love people who love them back. **33** If you do good only to those who do good to you, what credit is that? Sinners do the same. **34** If you lend only to people you expect to get something back from, what credit is that? Even sinners lend to sinners, expecting full repayment. **35** No — love your enemies, do good, and lend without expecting anything in return. Then your reward will be great, and you'll be children of the Most High, because he is kind to the ungrateful and the wicked.ʲ **36** Be merciful, just as your Father is merciful.ᵏ

**37** "Don't judge, and you won't be judged. Don't condemn, and you won't be condemned. Forgive, and you'll be forgiven. **38** Give, and it will be given to you — a generous amount, pressed down, shaken together, running over, poured into your lap.ˡ Because the standard you use for others is the standard that will be used for you."

**39** He also told them a parable: "Can a blind person guide another blind person? Won't they both end up in a ditch? **40** A student isn't above the teacher, but everyone who is fully trained will be like their teacher.

**41** "Why do you notice the splinter in someone else's eye but don't see the plank in your own? **42** How can you say, 'Friend, let me get that splinter out of your eye,' when you can't even see the plank in yours? You hypocrite — first take the plank out of your own eye, and then you'll see clearly enough to deal with the splinter in your friend's eye.

**43** "A good tree doesn't produce rotten fruit, and a rotten tree doesn't produce good fruit. **44** Each tree is known by its own fruit. You don't pick figs from thornbushes, or harvest grapes from brambles. **45** A good person brings good things out of the good stored up in their heart, and an evil person brings evil things out of the evil stored up in theirs. Because the mouth speaks from whatever fills the heart.

**46** "Why do you keep calling me 'Lord, Lord' but don't do what I say? **47** Let me show you what someone is like who comes to me, hears my words, and puts them into practice: **48** They're like a person building a house who dug deep and laid the foundation on bedrock. When a flood came and the river slammed against that house, it couldn't shake it, because it was solidly built. **49** But the one who hears and doesn't act on it is like a person who built a house on the ground with no foundation. The moment the river hit it, it collapsed — and the destruction of that house was total."

## Notes

- v.1a: "ψώχοντες ταῖς χερσίν" (psōchontes tais chersin) — "rubbing them in their hands." A detail unique to Luke, explaining the process for a non-Palestinian audience.
- v.5b: "κύριός ἐστιν τοῦ σαββάτου ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου" — The Son of Man is lord of the Sabbath. An extraordinary authority claim: the one who instituted the Sabbath has the right to interpret it.
- v.6c: Luke specifies it was the "right" hand — another detail consistent with medical precision.
- v.11d: "ἄνοια" (anoia) — literally "mindlessness" or "folly," rendered as "mindless rage." The word suggests irrational fury that clouds judgment.
- v.12e: "διανυκτερεύων" (dianyktereuōn) — a word appearing only here in the NT, meaning to spend the entire night. Luke emphasizes that Jesus prayed all night before choosing the Twelve.
- v.13f: "ἀποστόλους" (apostolous) — "sent ones." Luke alone notes that Jesus gave the Twelve this specific title at the time of their selection.
- v.17g: "ἐπὶ τόπου πεδινοῦ" (epi topou pedinou) — "on a level place." This is Luke's "Sermon on the Plain," parallel to Matthew's "Sermon on the Mount." Whether this is the same or a different occasion is debated.
- v.24-26h: The "woes" are unique to Luke's version. They balance the blessings and create a sharp contrast between present comfort and future reversal — a major Lukan theme.
- v.31i: The "Golden Rule" — stated here in positive form, which goes beyond merely avoiding harm.
- v.35j: "χρηστός ἐστιν ἐπὶ τοὺς ἀχαρίστους καὶ πονηρούς" — God is kind to the ungrateful and wicked. This remarkable statement grounds the ethic of enemy-love in God's own character.
- v.36k: Luke writes "merciful" (οἰκτίρμων, oiktirmōn) where Matthew has "perfect" (τέλειος). Both reflect the Hebrew rachum. Luke's choice emphasizes compassion.
- v.38l: The image is of grain being measured into the fold of a garment — pressed, shaken, and overflowing. A marketplace image of extraordinary generosity.
