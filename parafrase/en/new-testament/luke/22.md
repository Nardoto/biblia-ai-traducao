# Luke 22

**1** Now the Festival of Unleavened Bread, called the Passover, was approaching. **2** The chief priests and the scribes were looking for a way to kill Jesus, but they were afraid of the people.

**3** Then Satan entered Judas, the one called Iscariot, who was one of the Twelve.ᵃ **4** He went and discussed with the chief priests and temple officers how he might hand Jesus over to them. **5** They were delighted and agreed to give him money. **6** He consented and began looking for an opportunity to betray him when no crowd was around.

**7** Then the day of Unleavened Bread arrived — the day the Passover lamb had to be sacrificed. **8** Jesus sent Peter and John ahead, saying, "Go and prepare the Passover meal for us to eat."

**9** "Where do you want us to prepare it?" they asked.

**10** He told them, "Listen — when you enter the city, a man carrying a jar of waterᵇ will meet you. Follow him into the house he enters, **11** and say to the owner of the house, 'The Teacher asks: Where is the guest room where I can eat the Passover with my disciples?' **12** He'll show you a large, furnished upper room. Make the preparations there." **13** They went and found everything just as he had told them, and they prepared the Passover meal.

**14** When the hour came, he reclined at the table with the apostles. **15** He said to them, "I've deeply longed to eat this Passover with you before I suffer.ᶜ **16** Because I'm telling you, I won't eat it again until it finds its fulfillment in God's kingdom."

**17** He took a cup, gave thanks, and said, "Take this and share it among yourselves. **18** I'm telling you, from now on I won't drink the fruit of the vine until God's kingdom comes."

**19** Then he took bread, gave thanks, broke it, and gave it to them, saying, "This is my body, given for you. Do this in remembrance of me."ᵈ **20** In the same way, after the meal he took the cup, saying, "This cup is the new covenant in my blood, which is poured out for you.ᵉ

**21** "But look — the hand of the one who is betraying me is right here with mine on the table. **22** The Son of Man is going as it has been determined. But how terrible for that man who betrays him!" **23** They began questioning each other about which of them it could possibly be who would do this.

**24** A dispute also broke out among them about which of them should be considered the greatest. **25** Jesus said to them, "The kings of the Gentiles lord it over them, and those who exercise authority over them call themselves 'Benefactors.'ᶠ **26** But you — don't be like that. Instead, the greatest among you should become like the youngest, and the leader like the one who serves. **27** Who is greater — the one at the table, or the one serving? Isn't it the one at the table? But I am among you as the one who serves.ᵍ

**28** "You are the ones who have stayed with me through my trials. **29** And I am conferring on you a kingdom, just as my Father conferred one on me — **30** so that you may eat and drink at my table in my kingdom, and sit on thrones judging the twelve tribes of Israel."

**31** "Simon, Simon — Satan has demanded to sift all of you like wheat.ʰ **32** But I have prayed for you, Simon, that your faith won't fail. And when you've turned back, strengthen your brothers."

**33** Simon said, "Lord, I'm ready to go with you to prison and to death!"

**34** Jesus replied, "I'm telling you, Peter — the rooster won't crow today before you deny three times that you know me."

**35** Then he said to them, "When I sent you out without a money bag, travel bag, or sandals — did you lack anything?"

"Nothing," they said.

**36** He told them, "But now, whoever has a money bag should take it, and a travel bag too. And whoever doesn't have a sword should sell their cloak and buy one. **37** Because I'm telling you, what is written must be fulfilled in me: 'He was counted among the lawless.'ⁱ Yes, what is written about me is reaching its conclusion."

**38** They said, "Lord, look — here are two swords."

"That's enough," he said.ʲ

**39** He went out and headed to the Mount of Olives, as was his custom, and the disciples followed him. **40** When he reached the place, he said to them, "Pray that you won't fall into temptation."

**41** He withdrew from them about a stone's throw, knelt down, and began to pray: **42** "Father, if you're willing, take this cup from me. Yet not my will, but yours be done."

**43** An angel from heaven appeared to him, strengthening him. **44** And being in anguish, he prayed more intensely, and his sweat became like drops of blood falling to the ground.ᵏ

**45** When he got up from prayer and went back to the disciples, he found them sleeping — exhausted from grief. **46** "Why are you sleeping?" he said. "Get up and pray so you won't fall into temptation."

**47** While he was still speaking, a crowd arrived, and the man called Judas — one of the Twelve — was leading them. He approached Jesus to kiss him. **48** But Jesus said to him, "Judas, are you betraying the Son of Man with a kiss?"

**49** When those around Jesus saw what was about to happen, they said, "Lord, should we strike with the sword?" **50** One of them struck the high priest's servant and cut off his right ear.

**51** But Jesus said, "Stop! No more of this." And he touched the man's ear and healed him.ˡ

**52** Then Jesus said to the chief priests, temple officers, and elders who had come for him, "Have you come out with swords and clubs as if I were a revolutionary?ᵐ **53** Every day I was with you in the temple courts, and you didn't lay a hand on me. But this is your hour — and the domain of darkness."

**54** They seized him, led him away, and brought him into the high priest's house. Peter followed at a distance. **55** When they'd lit a fire in the middle of the courtyard and sat down together, Peter sat among them. **56** A servant girl saw him sitting in the firelight, stared at him, and said, "This man was with him too."

**57** But he denied it: "Woman, I don't know him."

**58** A little later, someone else saw him and said, "You're one of them too."

Peter said, "Man, I am not!"

**59** About an hour later, another person insisted, "No question — this man was with him too. He's a Galilean!"

**60** Peter said, "Man, I don't know what you're talking about!" And immediately, while he was still speaking, a rooster crowed. **61** The Lord turned and looked straight at Peter.ⁿ And Peter remembered what the Lord had said to him: "Before the rooster crows today, you'll deny me three times." **62** And he went outside and wept bitterly.

**63** The men guarding Jesus began mocking him and beating him. **64** They blindfolded him and demanded, "Prophesy! Who hit you?" **65** And they said many other insulting things to him.

**66** When daybreak came, the council of the elders of the people — both chief priests and scribes — assembled and had him brought before their Sanhedrin. **67** "If you are the Messiah," they said, "tell us."

He replied, "If I tell you, you won't believe me. **68** And if I ask you a question, you won't answer. **69** But from now on, the Son of Man will be seated at the right hand of the power of God."ᵒ

**70** They all said, "So you are the Son of God?"

He answered, "You say that I am."ᵖ

**71** They said, "Why do we need any more testimony? We've heard it from his own mouth!"

## Notes

- v.3a: "εἰσῆλθεν δὲ Σατανᾶς εἰς Ἰούδαν" (eisēlthen de Satanas eis Ioudan) — "Satan entered into Judas." Luke frames the betrayal as both human choice and satanic agency. This connects back to 4:13, where the devil departed "until an opportune time" — that time has now come.
- v.10b: A man carrying a water jar would be unusual — this was typically women's work — making him easy to identify. The arrangement suggests pre-planning.
- v.15c: "ἐπιθυμίᾳ ἐπεθύμησα" (epithymia epethymēsa) — a Semitic intensifier, literally "with desire I have desired." An expression of deep, passionate longing. Jesus's awareness of his coming suffering makes this desire all the more poignant.
- v.19d: The words of institution. "τοῦτό ἐστιν τὸ σῶμά μου" (touto estin to sōma mou) — "this is my body." The "do this in remembrance of me" (τοῦτο ποιεῖτε εἰς τὴν ἐμὴν ἀνάμνησιν) is found only in Luke and 1 Corinthians 11:24-25 among the Gospel accounts.
- v.20e: "ἡ καινὴ διαθήκη ἐν τῷ αἵματί μου" (hē kainē diathēkē en tō haimati mou) — "the new covenant in my blood." This echoes Jeremiah 31:31-34, where God promises a new covenant. The blood language connects to the Passover lamb and the covenant ratification at Sinai (Exodus 24:8).
- v.25f: "Εὐεργέται" (Euergetai) — "Benefactors," a title claimed by Hellenistic rulers. The irony is sharp: they demand the title of benefactor while actually exploiting their subjects.
- v.27g: "ἐγὼ δὲ ἐν μέσῳ ὑμῶν εἰμι ὡς ὁ διακονῶν" — "I am among you as the one who serves." This statement is unique to Luke and forms the theological heart of the Last Supper narrative — Jesus redefines greatness as service.
- v.31h: "ἐξῃτήσατο ὑμᾶς" (exētēsato hymas) — "demanded/asked for you all." The pronoun is plural — Satan wants all the disciples, not just Peter. But Jesus's prayer is for Peter specifically (singular "you" in v.32), whom he expects to lead the others back.
- v.37i: Isaiah 53:12 — the Suffering Servant prophecy. This is the only time in the Synoptic Gospels where Jesus explicitly quotes Isaiah 53 about himself.
- v.38j: "ἱκανόν ἐστιν" (hikanon estin) — "it is enough" or "that's enough." This may be a dismissal of the whole line of conversation rather than an endorsement of armed resistance. The disciples have completely missed his point.
- v.43-44k: These verses are absent from some important manuscripts and may be a later addition, though they are deeply consonant with Luke's portrayal of Jesus's humanity. "Ἱδρὼς ὡσεὶ θρόμβοι αἵματος" (hidrōs hōsei thromboi haimatos) — sweat "like drops/clots of blood." Whether this is a simile (sweat like blood) or a medical condition (hematidrosis, where capillaries burst under extreme stress) is debated.
- v.51l: Only Luke records Jesus healing the servant's ear — consistent with Luke's emphasis on Jesus's compassion, even toward his enemies, in his darkest hour.
- v.52m: "λῃστήν" (lēstēn) — a bandit or insurrectionist, not merely a petty thief. The word implies political revolutionary — which is precisely what Jesus is not.
- v.61n: "ὑπέβλεψεν" — the Lord "looked at" Peter. Only Luke records this devastating glance. A single look communicates more than words — recognition, sorrow, love, and the weight of fulfilled prophecy.
- v.69o: Luke's trial scene differs from Mark's. Jesus doesn't directly answer the Messiah question but makes a Son of Man claim about future enthronement — combining Daniel 7:13 and Psalm 110:1.
- v.70p: "ὑμεῖς λέγετε ὅτι ἐγώ εἰμι" (hymeis legete hoti egō eimi) — "You say that I am." This is affirmative but places the confession in their mouths, making them witnesses to their own verdict.
