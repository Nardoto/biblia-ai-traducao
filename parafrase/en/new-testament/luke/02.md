# Luke 2

**1** Around that time, Emperor Augustusᵃ issued a decree that a census should be taken of the entire Roman world. **2** This was the first census, carried out while Quirinius was governing Syria. **3** So everyone went to register, each to their own ancestral town.

**4** Joseph also traveled up from the town of Nazareth in Galilee to Judea, to the city of David called Bethlehem — because he belonged to David's family line. **5** He went to register with Mary, who was engaged to him and was expecting a child. **6** While they were there, the time came for her to give birth, **7** and she had her firstborn son. She wrapped him snugly in strips of clothᵇ and laid him in a feeding trough,ᶜ because there was no room for them in the guest quarters.ᵈ

**8** In the fields nearby, there were shepherds staying out at night, keeping watch over their flock. **9** An angel of the Lord appeared to them, and the Lord's glory blazed all around them. They were absolutely terrified. **10** But the angel said, "Don't be afraid! I'm bringing you good news — news of tremendous joy for all people. **11** Today, in the city of David, a Savior has been born for you. He is the Messiah,ᵉ the Lord. **12** And here's how you'll know: you'll find a baby wrapped in strips of cloth, lying in a feeding trough."

**13** Then suddenly, a massive army of heaven's angelsᶠ appeared alongside the first angel, praising God and saying,

**14** "Glory to God in the highest realms,
and on earth, peace among those he favors!"ᵍ

**15** After the angels left and returned to heaven, the shepherds said to each other, "Let's go straight to Bethlehem and see this thing that's happened — what the Lord has told us about." **16** They hurried off and found Mary and Joseph, and the baby lying in the feeding trough. **17** When they saw him, they spread the word about everything that had been told to them about this child. **18** Everyone who heard it was amazed by what the shepherds told them. **19** But Mary treasured all these things, turning them over carefully in her heart.ʰ **20** The shepherds went back, praising and glorifying God for everything they'd heard and seen, which was exactly as they'd been told.

**21** When eight days had passed and it was time for the child's circumcision, he was given the name Jesus — the name the angel had given him before he was even conceived.

**22** When the time came for their purification according to the Law of Moses, they brought him up to Jerusalem to present him to the Lord — **23** as it's written in the Lord's law: "Every firstborn male shall be set apart as holy to the Lord" — **24** and to offer a sacrifice in keeping with what the Lord's law says: "a pair of turtledoves or two young pigeons."ⁱ

**25** Now there was a man in Jerusalem named Simeon. He was righteous and devout, waiting expectantly for the comfort of Israel,ʲ and the Holy Spirit rested on him. **26** It had been revealed to him by the Holy Spirit that he wouldn't die before he had seen the Lord's Messiah. **27** Led by the Spirit, he came into the temple courts. When the parents brought the child Jesus in to carry out the customary requirements of the law for him, **28** Simeon took him in his arms, praised God, and said,

**29** "Now, Master, you can let your servant go in peace,
just as you promised —
**30** because my own eyes have seen your salvation,
**31** which you've prepared in full view of every nation:
**32** a light to reveal truth to the Gentiles,
and the shining glory of your people Israel."

**33** The child's father and mother stood there marveling at what was being said about him. **34** Then Simeon blessed them and said to Mary his mother, "Pay attention — this child is destined to cause the falling and rising of many in Israel, and to be a sign that people will reject.ᵏ **35** A sword will pierce your own soul too. All of this will expose what's really going on in people's hearts."

**36** There was also a prophet named Anna, the daughter of Phanuel, from the tribe of Asher. She was very old — she'd been married for seven years, **37** and then had been a widow until she was eighty-four. She never left the temple, worshiping night and day with fasting and prayer. **38** She came up at that very moment, gave thanks to God, and began speaking about the child to everyone who was looking forward to the redemption of Jerusalem.

**39** When Joseph and Mary had completed everything the Lord's law required, they returned to Galilee, to their own town of Nazareth. **40** The child grew and became strong, filled with wisdom, and God's favor rested on him.

**41** Every year, his parents went to Jerusalem for the Passover festival. **42** When he was twelve years old, they went up as usual for the festival. **43** After the celebration was over and they were heading home, the boy Jesus stayed behind in Jerusalem — but his parents didn't realize it. **44** Assuming he was somewhere in the traveling group, they went a full day's journey before they started looking for him among their relatives and friends. **45** When they couldn't find him, they went back to Jerusalem to search for him.

**46** After three days, they finally found him in the temple courts, sitting among the teachers, listening to them and asking them questions. **47** Everyone who heard him was astonished by his understanding and his answers. **48** When his parents saw him, they were stunned. His mother said to him, "Son, why have you done this to us? Your father and I have been worried sick, searching everywhere for you!"

**49** He said to them, "Why were you searching for me? Didn't you know I had to be in my Father's house?"ˡ **50** But they didn't grasp what he meant.

**51** Then he went back to Nazareth with them and was obedient to them. His mother continued to treasure all these things in her heart. **52** And Jesus kept growing — in wisdom, in stature, and in favor with both God and people.

## Notes

- v.1a: "Καῖσαρ Αὐγούστος" (Kaisar Augoustos) — Caesar Augustus, the first Roman emperor. Luke anchors the narrative in world history with characteristic precision.
- v.7b: "σπαργανόω" (sparganoō) — to wrap in swaddling bands, strips of cloth wound tightly around a newborn, a common ancient practice.
- v.7c: "φάτνη" (phatnē) — a manger or feeding trough for animals, not a crib. Rendered literally to preserve the striking humility of the scene.
- v.7d: "κατάλυμα" (katalyma) — the same word used for the upper room in Luke 22:11. It means guest room or lodging, not a commercial inn (which would be "πανδοχεῖον," pandocheion, as in Luke 10:34).
- v.11e: "Χριστὸς Κύριος" (Christos Kyrios) — "Messiah, the Lord." Christos is the Greek translation of the Hebrew Mashiach (Anointed One).
- v.13f: "στρατιᾶς οὐρανίου" (stratias ouraniou) — literally "heavenly army/host," a military term emphasizing the organized multitude of angels.
- v.14g: "ἐν ἀνθρώποις εὐδοκίας" — rendered "among those he favors," following the better-attested genitive reading rather than the nominative "good will toward men."
- v.19h: "συμβάλλουσα ἐν τῇ καρδίᾳ αὐτῆς" (symballousa en tē kardia autēs) — Mary was actively pondering, piecing things together. The verb symballō means to compare, consider, weigh mentally.
- v.24i: The offering of two doves or pigeons instead of a lamb indicates the family's modest means (see Leviticus 12:8).
- v.25j: "παράκλησιν τοῦ Ἰσραήλ" (paraklēsin tou Israēl) — "the comfort/consolation of Israel," a messianic expectation rooted in Isaiah 40:1.
- v.34k: "σημεῖον ἀντιλεγόμενον" (sēmeion antilegomenon) — a sign that is spoken against, contradicted, opposed. The Messiah would be a lightning rod for controversy.
- v.49l: "ἐν τοῖς τοῦ πατρός μου" (en tois tou patros mou) — could mean "in my Father's house" or "about my Father's business." The ambiguity is intentional in the Greek; "house" fits the temple context.
