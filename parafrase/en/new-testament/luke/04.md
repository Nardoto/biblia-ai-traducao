# Luke 4

**1** Jesus, full of the Holy Spirit, returned from the Jordan and was led by the Spirit into the wilderness, **2** where he was tested by the devil for forty days. He ate nothing during that time, and when those days were over, he was starving.

**3** The devil said to him, "If you are the Son of God, tell this stone to become bread."

**4** Jesus answered, "It is written: 'A person doesn't live on bread alone.'"ᵃ

**5** Then the devil took him up and showed him all the kingdoms of the world in a single instant.ᵇ **6** The devil told him, "I'll give you all this authority and splendor — it's been handed over to me, and I give it to whoever I want. **7** So if you worship me, it'll all be yours."

**8** Jesus answered, "It is written: 'Worship the Lord your God and serve him only.'"

**9** Then the devil brought him to Jerusalem, set him on the highest point of the temple, and said, "If you are the Son of God, throw yourself down from here. **10** After all, it is written: 'He will command his angels concerning you, to protect you,' **11** and 'They will carry you in their hands, so you won't even strike your foot against a stone.'"

**12** Jesus answered, "It says: 'Don't put the Lord your God to the test.'"

**13** When the devil had exhausted every kind of temptation, he withdrew from him until a more opportune time.ᶜ

**14** Jesus returned to Galilee in the power of the Spirit, and news about him spread throughout the whole region. **15** He was teaching in their synagogues, and everyone spoke highly of him.

**16** He came to Nazareth, where he'd been raised. On the Sabbath day, he went into the synagogue as was his custom and stood up to read. **17** The scroll of the prophet Isaiah was handed to him. He unrolled it and found the passage that says:

**18** "The Spirit of the Lord is on me,
because he has anointed me
to announce good news to the poor.
He has sent me to proclaim freedom for prisoners
and recovery of sight for the blind,
to release the oppressed,
**19** to declare the year of the Lord's favor."ᵈ

**20** Then he rolled up the scroll, handed it back to the attendant, and sat down. Every eye in the synagogue was fixed on him. **21** He began by telling them, "Today, this scripture has been fulfilled right here as you listened."ᵉ

**22** Everyone was impressed with him and marveled at the gracious words coming from his mouth. But they said, "Wait — isn't this Joseph's son?"

**23** He said to them, "No doubt you'll quote me this proverb: 'Doctor, heal yourself!' You'll say, 'Do here in your hometown what we've heard you did in Capernaum.'" **24** Then he added, "I'm telling you the truth — no prophet is accepted in his hometown.

**25** "Believe me, there were plenty of widows in Israel during Elijah's time, when the sky was shut for three and a half years and a severe famine struck the whole land. **26** Yet Elijah wasn't sent to any of them, but to a widow in Zarephath, in Sidonian territory. **27** And there were plenty of people with leprosy in Israel during the prophet Elisha's time, yet not one of them was healed — only Naaman the Syrian."ᶠ

**28** When they heard this, everyone in the synagogue was furious. **29** They jumped to their feet, drove him out of town, and brought him to the edge of the hill their town was built on, intending to throw him off the cliff. **30** But he walked right through the middle of the crowd and went on his way.

**31** He went down to Capernaum, a town in Galilee, and was teaching them on the Sabbath. **32** They were astonished by his teaching, because his words carried authority.ᵍ

**33** In the synagogue there was a man possessed by the spirit of an unclean demon, and he screamed at the top of his voice, **34** "Ha! What do you want with us, Jesus of Nazareth? Have you come to destroy us? I know who you are — the Holy One of God!"

**35** Jesus rebuked him sharply: "Be quiet! Come out of him!" The demon threw the man down in front of everyone and came out of him without doing him any harm. **36** They were all stunned. They kept saying to each other, "What kind of message is this? He commands unclean spirits with authority and power, and they come out!" **37** And news about him spread to every corner of the surrounding region.

**38** Jesus left the synagogue and went to Simon's house. Simon's mother-in-law was suffering from a severe fever, and they asked Jesus to help her. **39** He stood over her, rebuked the fever, and it left her. Instantly she got up and began serving them.

**40** As the sun was setting, people brought to him everyone who was sick with all kinds of diseases. He laid his hands on each one and healed them. **41** Demons also came out of many, screaming, "You are the Son of God!" But he rebuked them and wouldn't let them speak, because they knew he was the Messiah.

**42** At daybreak, Jesus went out to a secluded place. The crowds searched for him and, when they found him, tried to keep him from leaving. **43** But he told them, "I have to proclaim the good news of God's kingdom to the other towns too — that's what I was sent to do."ʰ **44** And he kept preaching in the synagogues of Judea.ⁱ

## Notes

- v.4a: Luke omits the second half of Deuteronomy 8:3 ("but on every word that comes from the mouth of God"), which Matthew includes. A characteristic Lukan brevity in this particular case.
- v.5b: "ἐν στιγμῇ χρόνου" (en stigmē chronou) — literally "in a point/instant of time." A uniquely Lukan detail suggesting a supernatural, visionary experience.
- v.13c: "ἄχρι καιροῦ" (achri kairou) — "until an opportune moment." The devil's departure is strategic, not permanent — foreshadowing further testing (notably in Gethsemane, Luke 22:3, 53).
- v.18-19d: Isaiah 61:1-2. Jesus stops reading mid-sentence, omitting "the day of vengeance of our God." This deliberate omission emphasizes the mercy of his mission.
- v.21e: "σήμερον πεπλήρωται" (sēmeron peplērōtai) — "today it has been fulfilled." The perfect tense indicates a completed action with ongoing significance. This is one of Luke's most dramatic moments.
- v.25-27f: Jesus's examples of Elijah and Elisha ministering to Gentiles infuriate the crowd because he's implying God's favor extends beyond Israel — a central Lukan theme.
- v.32g: "ἐν ἐξουσίᾳ ἦν ὁ λόγος αὐτοῦ" (en exousia ēn ho logos autou) — "his word was with authority." Unlike the scribes who cited other authorities, Jesus spoke on his own authority.
- v.43h: "εὐαγγελίσασθαι... τὴν βασιλείαν τοῦ θεοῦ" (euangelisasthai... tēn basileian tou theou) — "to proclaim the good news of the kingdom of God." Luke uniquely specifies the content of the proclamation here.
- v.44i: Some manuscripts read "Galilee" instead of "Judea." Luke may be using "Judea" in a broad sense to mean the whole Jewish land.
